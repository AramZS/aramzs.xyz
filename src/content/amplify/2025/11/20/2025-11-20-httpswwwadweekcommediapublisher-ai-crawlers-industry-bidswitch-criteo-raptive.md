<p>When it comes to artificial intelligence, two things are difficult to overstate.</p>
<p>The first is the existential threat the technology has posed to the media industry. In June, The Wall Street Journal called it an “<a href="https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/google-ai-news-publishers-7e687141?gaa_at=eafs&amp;gaa_n=AWEtsqdDrsq4IwURvu4hhQbawiucfDjwBGC5F-fXN_7I8r7wZjKhN3uXN6H-pac8Aqo%3D&amp;gaa_ts=691dcea9&amp;gaa_sig=C2zJQkPqSPwwibcf8USdtoIc-g8nyy19ePXGFwEg1UrHCD_njL5z4UsMoxdzhgdYSZigbZbQdDdemqXyN5lnyQ%3D%3D">armageddon</a>” whose downstream effects on search traffic and programmatic ad revenue have upended the economics of digital publishing.</p>
<p>In May, <a href="https://www.adweek.com/media/business-insider-layoffs-traffic-sensitivity/">Business Insider cut nearly a quarter of its staff</a>, citing AI as the primary reason.</p>
