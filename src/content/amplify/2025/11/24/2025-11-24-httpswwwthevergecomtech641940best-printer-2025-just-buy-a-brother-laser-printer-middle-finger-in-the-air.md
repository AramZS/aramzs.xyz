<h2>The Best Printer You Can Buy Is Still a Boring Brother Laser Printer</h2>
<p><strong>Look, nobody <em>wants</em> to buy a printer in 2025. Let’s just get that out of the way.</strong></p>
<p>Printers are the punchline of the tech world, the beige boxes of frustration sitting in the corner, waiting for the precise moment you <em>actually</em> need them to demand a firmware update, claim they’re out of magenta (when you only print black!), or just... refuse to connect to the Wi-Fi for reasons known only to the chaos gods. We live in an age of instant cloud sync, AI assistants, and phones that fold in half, yet printing a simple return label can still feel like wrestling a bear.</p>
<p>And the worst offenders? Those cheap inkjet printers. You know the ones. They lure you in with a ridiculously low price tag, sometimes even free with a new PC. But it’s a trap. A scam. The real business model isn’t the printer; it’s the ink. Tiny cartridges filled with ridiculously expensive liquid that dries out if you look at it funny, costing more per milliliter than vintage champagne. They push ink subscriptions, DRM’d cartridges, and software designed seemingly only to tell you you’re low on ink <em>again</em>. It’s a racket, plain and simple.</p>
<p><strong>Stop the madness. Seriously. Just stop.</strong></p>
