<ul><li>Russian nationals Anton Perevalov and Tatiana Zaiko were detained by immigration agents despite pending asylum applications and legal work authorization documents.</li><li>Under Trump’s administration, asylum seekers with pending claims are no longer protected from arrest and deportation.</li><li>Their detention has sparked fear throughout Southern California’s Russian immigrant community, many fleeing political persecution or military conscription following Russia’s invasion of Ukraine.</li></ul>
<p>The August sun was already warming the San Fernando Valley when Anton Perevalov dressed in athletic shorts and decided to take an early morning stroll with his miniature pinscher, Ben, while his wife slept.</p>
<p>As he turned right onto Hillcrest Drive — a route he’d taken so many mornings before — an unmarked car stopped in front of him and a man he’d never met emerged and peppered him with questions: “Are you Anton Perevalov?” “Are you a citizen of Russia?”</p>
