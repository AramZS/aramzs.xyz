<p>Have you heard of the Google Goldmine Scoring System? It supposedly looks at your page, your content, and Google gives it a goldminePageScore, title tag factor, body factor, anchor factor, heading factor and more.</p>
<p>Shaun Anderson uncovered this as part of the older <a href="https://www.seroundtable.com/google-search-data-leak-37462.html">Google data leak</a> and said on <a href="https://x.com/hobo_web/status/1975330684823867868">X</a>, "The "Goldmine" system rates title tags. It evaluates "title candidates" (your title, h1, headings, and anchor text) using dozens of signals to calculate goldminePageScore. The highest-scoring candidate is the one most likely to be shown in search results."</p>
<p>He shared this screenshot that says "the core of the evaluation engine. These attributes represent the individual scoring components (Factors) and the final composite scores that determine a title candidate's quality and ranking." He posted a lot more detail about this on <a href="https://www.hobo-web.co.uk/title-tags/">his blog</a> but it lists these attributes:</p>
<ul><li>goldminePageScore</li> <li>goldmineAdjustedScore</li> <li>goldmineTitleTagFactor</li> <li>goldmineBodyFactor</li> <li>goldmineAnchorFactor</li> <li>goldmineHeadingFactor</li></ul>
