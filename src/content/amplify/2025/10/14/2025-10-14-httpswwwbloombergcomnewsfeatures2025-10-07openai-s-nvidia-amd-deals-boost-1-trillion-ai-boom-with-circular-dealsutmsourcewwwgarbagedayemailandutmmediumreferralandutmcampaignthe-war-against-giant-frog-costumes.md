<h1 data-component="headline">OpenAI, Nividia Fuel $1 Trillion AI Market With Web of Circular Deals</h1>
<p>A wave of deals and partnerships are escalating concerns that the trillion-dollar AI boom is being propped up by interconnected business transactions.</p>
<p>By <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AW4BeVx3KeQ/emily-forgash">Emily Forgash</a> and <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AXqQdR8XiCM/agnee-ghosh">Agnee Ghosh</a></p>
<p>October 7, 2025 at 1:00 PM PDT</p>
<p>Two weeks ago, Nvidia Corp. agreed to <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-22/nvidia-to-invest-100-billion-in-openai-in-ai-computing-buildout">invest as much as $100 billion</a> in OpenAI to help the leading AI startup fund a data-center buildout so massive it could power a major city. OpenAI in turn committed to filling those sites with millions of Nvidia chips. The arrangement was <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-23/nvidia-s-massive-openai-deal-fuels-circular-financing-concerns">promptly criticized</a> for its “circular” nature.</p>
<p>This week, undeterred, OpenAI struck a similar deal. The ChatGPT maker on Monday <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-06/openai-signs-amd-chips-deal-worth-tens-of-billions-of-dollars">inked a partnership</a> with Nvidia rival Advanced Micro Devices Inc. to deploy tens of billions of dollars worth of its chips. As part of the tie-up, OpenAI is poised to become one of AMD’s largest shareholders.</p>
