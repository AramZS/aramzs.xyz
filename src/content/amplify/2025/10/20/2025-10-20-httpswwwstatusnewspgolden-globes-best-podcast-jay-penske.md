<p>Earlier this year, the long-running <strong>Hollywood</strong> film and television awards show, the <strong>Golden Globes</strong>, announced it will present an inaugural <strong>Best Podcast</strong> award at the upcoming 83rd annual ceremony. Then, this month, the awards show—owned and operated by <strong>Penske Media Corporation</strong>—unveiled a <a href="https://goldenglobes.com/articles/25-podcasts-announced-as-eligible-for-golden-globes/">slate of 25 podcasts</a> eligible for the new honor. <strong>Helen Hoehne</strong>, the president of the Globes, celebrated the list, declaring that it reflected “the incredible depth, diversity and creativity thriving in the podcasting world today.”</p>
<p>Among the programs selected by Penske’s <strong>Luminate</strong> were podcasting mainstays such as <strong>“Call Her Daddy,” “The Daily,”</strong> and<strong> “The Bill Simmons Show.” </strong>But also included were some questionable selections, like those belonging to <strong>Joe Rogan</strong>, <strong>Megyn Kelly</strong>, and <strong>Theo Von</strong>. And, more surprising, were the inclusions of right-wing extremists <strong>Tucker Carlson</strong> and <strong>Candace Owens</strong>, two figures who have built lucrative empires on polluting the information environment and attacking the very cultural institutions that the Globes purport to celebrate.</p>
