<p><b>Methodology</b></p>
<p>The Post simulated basic, hypothetical parlays based on several assumptions. We assumed that each leg was a bet on a point spread, and these bets were on independent events. Point spread bets have varying odds, but many have roughly -110 odds on each side, which is the scenario The Post considered. A sports bet with those odds and equal amounts wagered on both sides would have a house edge of 4.5 percent, which is considered typical.</p>
<p>Calculating the expected hold percentage â€” the share of the total amount wagered that is kept by the sportsbook â€” is possible only when the probability of winning the parlay is known. The Post assumed bettors had a 50 percent chance of correctly predicting each leg, because multiple experts said most bettors are no better at picking games than if they selected a side at random. In an analysis of all bets, regardless of sport, placed in New Jersey in 2020, Rutgers researchers found that consumers lost 50.3 percent of the time on point spread bets for entire games. The Postâ€™s model also assumed there were no pushes, which happen when the final margin is equal to the point spread and bettors on both sides have their wager returned. Under both those circumstances, bettors would have a 3.1 percent chance of winning a five-leg parlay.</p>
<p>To determine the potential payout, The Post assumed the odds given for a parlay would be equivalent to the multiplied value of the odds of each leg. This is the case when building a parlay on the sites of major operators and according to a parlay explainer <a href="https://sportsbook.draftkings.com/help/how-to-bet-parlay?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">on DraftKingsâ€™ website</a>. Sometimes sportsbooks offer promotions that improve the odds of the parlay.</p>
<p>The Post also analyzed state-level sports betting reports. States publish gambling reports with varying level of detail and only some designate how much money is wagered or revenue is generated on parlays.</p>
<p>The Post analyzed data available as of Oct. 1 from six states: <a href="https://sbg.colorado.gov/sports-betting-monthly-reports?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">Colorado</a>, <a href="https://igb.illinois.gov/sports-wagering/sports-reports.html?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">Illinois</a>, <a href="https://www.in.gov/igc/publications/monthly-revenue/?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">Indiana</a>, <a href="https://lsp.org/about/leadershipsections/bureau-of-investigations/gaming-enforcement-division/gaming-revenue-reports/?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">Louisiana</a>, <a href="https://www.mdgaming.com/maryland-sports-wagering/revenue-reports/all-financial-reports/?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">Maryland</a> and <a href="https://www.njoag.gov/about/divisions-and-offices/division-of-gaming-enforcement-home/financial-and-statistical-information/monthly-press-releases-and-statistical-summaries/?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">New Jersey</a>.
