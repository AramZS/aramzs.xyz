<p><a href="https://www.404media.co/tag/ai-slop/">AI Slop</a> </p>
<p>I am haunted by a pregnant bill in Andrew Cuomo’s new AI-generated attack ad against Zohran Mamdani.</p>
<p>Cuomo posted the ad on his <a href="https://nypost.com/2025/10/24/us-news/cuomo-campaign-whacked-for-cringey-ai-video-claims-accidentally-posted-by-junior-staffer/?ref=404media.co"><u>X account</u></a> that riffed on the famous <em>Schoolhouse Rock! </em>song <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZ8psP4S6BQ&amp;list=RDSZ8psP4S6BQ&amp;start_radio=1&amp;ref=404media.co"><u>“I’m just a bill.”</u></a> In Cuomo’s AI-generated <a href="https://x.com/andrewcuomo/status/1983919479936409617?ref=404media.co"><u>cartoon nightmare</u></a>, Zohran Mamdani lights money on fire while a phone bearing the ChatGPT logo explains, apparently, that Mamdani is not qualified.</p>
<p>The ad bears all the hallmarks of the sloppiest of AI trash: weird artifacting, strange voices that don’t sync with the mouths talking, and inconsistent animation. It feels both surreal and of the moment and completely ancient.</p>
<p>And then there’s the pregnant bill.</p>
<p>The <em>Schoolhouse Rock! </em>Bill is an iconic cartoon character that has been parodied by everyone from <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w8gt-EEpelY&amp;ref=404media.co"><em><u>The Simpsons</u></em></a> to <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nh6Hf5_ZYPI&amp;ref=404media.co"><em><u>Saturday Night Live</u></em></a>. There are thousands, perhaps millions, of pictures of the cartoon bill online, all available to be gobbled up by scrapers and turned into training data for AI.</p>
<p>For some reason, the bill in Cuomo’s ad has thick red lips (<a href="https://www.cnn.com/2013/01/14/politics/schoolhouse-rock-40?ref=404media.co"><u>notably absent</u></a> in the original) and appears to be pregnant. Adding to the discordant AI jank of the image, the pregnancy is only visible when the bill is standing up. Sometimes it’s leaning against the steps and in those shots it has the slim figure characteristic of its inspiration. But when the bill stands it looks positively inflated, almost as if the video generator used to make Cuomo’s ad was trained on MPREG fetish art of the bill and not the original cartoon itself. The thick and luscious red lips are present whether the bill is leaning or standing.</p>
<p>Towards the end of the ad, an anthropomorphic phone with a ChatGPT logo wanders into the scene. Standing next to the pregnant bill, I could not but help but think that the phone is the father of whatever child the bill carried.</p>
<p>My observation led to an argument in the 404 Media Slack channel and opinions were split. “It does not seem pregnant to me,” said Emanuel Maiberg.</p>
<figure><a data-fslightbox="" data-no-swup="" href="https://www.404media.co/content/images/2025/10/CleanShot-2025-10-30-at-10.02.45.png"><img alt="" src="https://www.404media.co/content/images/2025/10/CleanShot-2025-10-30-at-10.02.45.png"/></a></figure>
<p>Jason Koebler, however, came to my defense. He circled the pregnant belly of the cartoon bill and shared it. “Baby is stored in the circle area,” he said.</p>
<p>Perplexed by all this, I reached out to Cuomo’s campaign for an explanation. I wanted a response to the ad and to get his thoughts on AI-generated political content. More importantly, I needed to know their opinion on the pregnancy. “Does that bill look pregnant to you?” I asked. “I think it looks pregnant, but my editors are split. I would love for the Campaign to weigh in.” Out of journalist due diligence, I also reached out to Mamdani’s press office. Neither campaign has responded to my request for it to weigh in on the pregnancy of the AI-generated cartoon bill.</p>
<p>This is not the first time the Cuomo campaign has used AI. An ad in early October featured a <a href="https://ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/politics/2025/10/09/ai-generated-imagery-takes-new-york-politics-by-storm?ref=404media.co"><u>deepfaked Cuomo</u></a> working as a train operator, stock trader, and a stagehand. A week ago, the Cuomo campaign released a long, racist video depicting criminals endorsing Mamdani. Critics called the ad racist. The campaign deleted it shortly after it was posted and <a href="https://nypost.com/2025/10/24/us-news/cuomo-campaign-whacked-for-cringey-ai-video-claims-accidentally-posted-by-junior-staffer/?ref=404media.co"><u>blamed the whole</u></a> thing on a junior staffer.</p>
<p>It is worth noting that Cuomo's AI slop is being deployed most likely because the candidate has been utterly incapable of generating any authentic excitement about his campaign in New York City or on the internet, and he is facing a digitally native, younger candidate who just seems effortlessly Good At the Internet and Posting.</p>
<p>This is, unfortunately, how a lot of politics works in 2025. Desperate campaigns and desperate presidents are in a slop-fueled arms race to make the most ridiculous possible ads and social media content. It looks cheap, is cheap, and is the realm of politicians who are totally out of ideas, but increasingly it feels like slop is the dominant aesthetic of our time.</p>
