<p>An internal Google email said it received many complaints about Israeli government YouTube ads about food access in Gaza, but ruled they could remain online.</p>
<p>In August, two days after the United Nations-backed global hunger monitor declared a <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2025/08/22/gaza-city-famine-ipc/">rapidly spreading famine</a> in the Gaza City region, Israel’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs posted a YouTube video that made an opposite claim: “Cynical politicians and biased media are lying.”</p>
