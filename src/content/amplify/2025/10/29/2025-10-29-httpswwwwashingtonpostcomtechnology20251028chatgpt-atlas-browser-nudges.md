<p>The prodding to try AI products as you watch TV, surf the web or stare at your phone is a classic tactic in technology. It’s annoying, but it works.</p>
<p>If you’ve used ChatGPT in the past few days, you might have seen a nudge to download Atlas, the company’s <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2025/10/22/chatgpt-atlas-browser/">new web-surfing competitor</a> to Google’s Chrome browser.</p>
<p>As you <a href="https://ppc.land/perplexity-announces-comet-plus-with-major-news-publishers/">scroll some news websites</a> now, including The Washington Post and the Los Angeles Times, pop-up screens pitch free access to articles for a limited time — as long as you use the web browser from the artificial-intelligence start-up Perplexity.</p>
