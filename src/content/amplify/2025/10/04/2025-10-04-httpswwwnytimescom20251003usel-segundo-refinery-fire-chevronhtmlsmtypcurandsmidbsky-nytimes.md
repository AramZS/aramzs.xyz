<p>A fireball lit up the sky across the Los Angeles area on Thursday night after an explosion at a Chevron refinery in El Segundo in Los Angeles County.</p>
<p>The explosion at 9:55 p.m. produced a giant orange blaze that could be seen from miles away. It was not immediately clear what caused the blast or whether there were any injuries.</p>
<p>“We’re trying to figure out what type of fire it is,” said Julio Martinez, a spokesman for the El Segundo Police Department.</p>
<p>Chevron could not immediately be reached for comment.</p>
<p>The facility at El Segundo, home to California’s oil and aerospace industries, was California’s second oil refinery when it opened in 1911. The El Segundo plant is California’s second-biggest oil refinery, <a href="https://www.energy.ca.gov/data-reports/energy-almanac/californias-petroleum-market/californias-oil-refineries">according to state records</a>, and represents about 16 percent of refining capacity per day.</p>
