<p>Los Angeles — America’s second most populous city — is getting a new daily newspaper.</p>
<p>News Corp, the parent company of The New York Post and The Wall Street Journal, is expanding westward with the California Post in early 2026, the company <a href="https://nypost.com/2025/08/04/media/start-the-presses-new-york-post-will-expand-to-la-with-launch-of-the-california-post/">announced Monday</a> (expanded announcement <a href="https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/post-heads-west-new-york-post-media-group-launches-the-california-post--869616363.html">here</a>; the release was <a href="https://www.axios.com/2025/08/04/new-york-post-california-los-angeles">first reported by Axios</a>.) The California Post will cover the country’s most populous state in the tabloid style, with a daily print edition.</p>
<p>“Los Angeles and California surely need a daily dose of The Post as an antidote to the jaundiced, jaded journalism that has sadly proliferated,” <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/robert-thomson-52603a8/">Robert Thomson</a>, CEO of News Corp, said in a statement. “We are at a pivotal moment for the city and the state, and there is no doubt that The Post will play a crucial role in engaging and enlightening readers, who are starved of serious reporting and puckish wit.”</p>
<p><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/nick-papps-71b6b631/?originalSubdomain=au">Nick Papps</a>, a longtime News Corp Australia journalist and editor, will lead the California Post as editor-in-chief, and New York Post Group editor-in-chief <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/keith-poole-23745858/">Keith Poole</a> will oversee the tabloids on both coasts.</p>
<p>Los Angeles is the Post’s second largest market, the company said, and nearly 90% of its digital readers live outside New York City. (For similar reasons, The New York Times <a href="https://www.niemanlab.org/2016/06/with-california-today-the-new-york-times-is-testing-expanding-its-newsletter-strategy-cross-country/">launched</a> its weekday newsletter <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/newsletters/california-today">California Today</a> in 2016.)</p>
