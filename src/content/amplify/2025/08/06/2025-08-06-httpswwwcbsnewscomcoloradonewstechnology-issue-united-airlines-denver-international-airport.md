<p>A ground stop that was effect for United Airlines flights bound for Denver International Airport is over, according to <a href="https://nasstatus.faa.gov/">the FAA</a>.</p>
<p>The airline said that they were experiencing a "technology issue" on Wednesday evening and that led to the stoppage. Departures were also being held to several other airports across the country, <a data-invalid-url-rewritten-http="" href="https://www.cbsnews.com/chicago/news/united-airlines-ground-stop-chicago-ohare/">including O'Hare International Airport</a> in Chicago.</p>
<p>The airline said in a statement to expect flight delays throughout the evening.</p>
<p>"We are working with customers to get them to their destinations after a technology disruption on Wednesday evening. The underlying technology issue has been resolved, and, while we expect residual delays, our team is working to restore our normal operations," a statement from United read.</p>
<p>So far the exact nature of the technology issue hasn't been explained.</p>
