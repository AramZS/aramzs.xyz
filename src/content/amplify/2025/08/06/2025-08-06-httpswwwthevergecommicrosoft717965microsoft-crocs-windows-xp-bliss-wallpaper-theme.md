<p>Microsoft isn’t done celebrating its <a href="https://www.theverge.com/news/643336/microsoft-50-years">50th anniversary</a> just yet. Multiple employees tell me that Microsoft has created limited edition Windows XP-themed Crocs. They even come with a Clippy shoe charm if the Bliss wallpaper on your feet wasn’t enough 50-year nostalgia.</p>
<p>The anniversary edition Crocs are currently available for preorder for Microsoft employees, who “get first dibs” ahead of a “worldwide launch,” I’m told. The $80 pair of Crocs include a six-pack of Microsoft Jibbitz that you can attach to your shoes, with nostalgic options like the original MSN logo, the Internet Explorer icon, Clippy, and a mouse pointer.</p>
<p>On top the Crocs feature the iconic blue sky and clouds from the Windows XP wallpaper, with a green sole to mimic the grass. The photograph of the rolling hills and blue skies of California was initially a stock photo before Microsoft acquired the rights and used it as the default wallpaper for millions of Windows XP users. It could be the <a href="https://napavalleyregister.com/star/lifestyles/say-goodbye-to-bliss/article_2c485132-b504-11e3-85ef-0019bb2963f4.html">most viewed photograph</a> in the world, making it an instantly recognizable image for many.</p>
<p>You’ll also get a Bliss drawstring backpack to wear, just in case onlookers were unclear if your footwear was the Windows XP wallpaper or not. Hopefully each pair won’t smell like a dusty beige PC and Internet Explorer doesn’t automatically install once you’re wearing them.</p>
<p>I asked Microsoft about its Windows XP Crocs, but the company refused to comment. I’m expecting to see an official announcement in the coming days.</p>
