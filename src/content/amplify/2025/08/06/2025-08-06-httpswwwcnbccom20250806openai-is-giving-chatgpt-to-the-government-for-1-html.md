<p><a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2025/08/05/openai-talks-with-investors-about-share-sale-at-500-billion-valuation.html">OpenAI</a> on Wednesday <a href="https://openai.com/index/providing-chatgpt-to-the-entire-us-federal-workforce/">announced</a> it will offer its <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2025/08/05/openai-open-weight-meta-mistral-deepseek-ai.html">ChatGPT</a> Enterprise product to U.S. federal agencies for $1 through the next year, making its technology available to the federal executive branch workforce at “essentially no cost.”</p>
<p>The company has been working to deepen its ties to lawmakers and regulators in recent months, and it will open its first office in <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2025/07/21/openai-plans-to-open-first-office-in-dc-next-year.html">Washington</a>, D.C. early next year.</p>
<p>OpenAI said participating agencies will get access to its frontier models through ChatGPT Enterprise, and it will also offer access to features like Advanced Voice Mode for an additional 60-day period.</p>
<p>The company has partnered with the U.S. General Services Administration to launch the initiative.</p>

<p>“Helping government work better – making services faster, easier, and more reliable—is a key way to bring the benefits of AI to everyone,” OpenAI said in a blog post.</p>
<p>In June, OpenAI launched a new offering called <a href="https://openai.com/global-affairs/introducing-openai-for-government/">OpenAI for Government</a> and said it was awarded a contract of up to <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2025/06/16/openai-wins-200-million-us-defense-contract.html">$200 million</a> by the U.S. Department of Defense.</p>
<p>The company is currently engaging in talks with investors about a potential stock sale at a valuation of roughly $500 billion, as CNBC previously reported.</p>
