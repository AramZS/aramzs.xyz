<p>Let us consider the ongoing overhaul of the White House as a metaphor.</p>
<p>A few months ago, back when I was at The Washington Post, I <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20250506165140/https://s2.washingtonpost.com/camp-rw/?trackId=596b02b6ade4e24119ac1a18&amp;s=6818d4c9fd30690aa004794a&amp;utm_campaign=wp_follow_philip_bump&amp;utm_medium=email&amp;utm_source=newsletter&amp;wpisrc=nl-philipbump&amp;linknum=1&amp;linktot=39">compiled a series of photos</a> to show how the Oval Office had been slowly redecorated to better reflect the aesthetic of the then-slightly-newer president. Even by then, the once-spare room had begun to gleam with gilded bric-a-brac and a number of random golden objects.</p>
<p>The result was striking.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54522054095/"><img alt="" sizes="(max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/54522054095_5535c75f9e_k.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/54522054095_5535c75f9e_k.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/54522054095_5535c75f9e_k-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/54522054095_5535c75f9e_k-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/54522054095_5535c75f9e_k-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>May 13, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Oh, that’s not the Oval Office. It is, instead, one of the rooms in a palace in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. Relative to the Oval Office under prior presidents, the room above is over-the-top. Relative to the Oval Office today, the Saudi palace is downright understated.</p>
<p>Feel free to read my commentary on the changes between the pictures below — <strong>or just scroll through to get the effect</strong>.</p>
<h2>The fireplace</h2>
<p>Let’s begin by considering the fireplace that sits at the other end of the office from the president’s desk. This is what that fireplace looked like in October of last year, when Joe Biden was president.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse46/54057062388"><img alt="" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-3.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-3.jpg 1024w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-3-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-3-768x512.jpg 768w"/></a><figcaption>Oct. 9, 2024.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Soon after Trump took office, the area had already gotten an overhaul. The greenery on the mantel was replaced with a variety of golden knick-knacks. The paintings on the wall were moved around and new paintings and mirrors — all with gilded frames — added. A bust of Robert F. Kennedy Sr. was replaced by one of Winston Churchill, placed on a table shaped like a golden eagle, and a number of flags were added.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54321924629"><img alt="" sizes="(max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-2.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-2.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-2-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-2-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-2-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>Feb. 11, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>You can see the changes a bit more easily below. Notice that the mirror on the door at left is too tall and juts up above the door itself.</p>

<p>It was soon joined by other little appliqués on the wall.</p>

<p>Between June and early July, additional appliqués were added to the sides of the fireplace and to the space under the central portrait. This required swapping out the largest center trophy-thing with a less-gold clock. The bust of Martin Luther King Jr. was replaced with one depicting someone that looks like Dwight Eisenhower.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54644478394"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/10.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/10.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/10-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/10-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/10-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>July 9, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Notice, too, that the marble on the fireplace itself was gilded. You can see that better below.</p>

<p>Recently, the clock was swapped back out for the golden trophy-thing. Gold statue-things were also added next to each of the busts.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54708495608"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/12.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/12.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/12-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/12-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/12-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>Aug. 8, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>The slow accretion of gaudiness is obvious when we switch back to the first time Trump entered the White House after his first term ended: when Biden hosted him after Trump won the 2024 election.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse46/54138122219"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/100.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/100.jpg 1024w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/100-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/100-768x512.jpg 768w"/></a><figcaption>Nov. 13, 2024.</figcaption></figure>
<p>The overhaul that followed was dramatic.</p>
<h2>The rest of the room</h2>
<p>That has also been the case in the rest of the room.</p>
<p>When Biden was president, the area around his desk mirrored the area around the fireplace: lightly decorated and (relatively) austere.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse46/52833334485"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-5.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-5.jpg 1024w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-5-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-5-768x512.jpg 768w"/></a><figcaption>April 10, 2023.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Then came Trump. More paintings, more flags.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54327695050"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-4.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-4.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-4-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-4-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-4-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>Feb. 13, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Golden objects and flourishes were added elsewhere, too, as above the doorway below. Shelves on the opposite wall became home to other little golden objects, in the same manner as the mantel over the fireplace.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54326383117"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/3-2.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/3-2.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/3-2-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/3-2-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/3-2-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>Feb. 13, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Trump had <a href="https://people.com/donald-trump-says-declaration-of-independence-is-about-love-and-respect-11727211">a copy of the Declaration of Independence</a> added to the room, which Tim Tebow looked at for a while when he visited.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54426253163"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/4-2.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/4-2.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/4-2-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/4-2-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/4-2-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>April 1, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Notice, in the picture above, that the border ringing the room is unadorned except for some scalloping. A week or so later, it was home to gilded appliqués like those that would eventually adorn the fireplace.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54443675276"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/5-1.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/5-1.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/5-1-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/5-1-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/5-1-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>April 10, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>If you’re curious when all of this work was done, you will not be surprised to learn that Trump spent the weekend between April 1 and April 10 at Mar-a-Lago.</p>
<p>Here’s Dr. Oz admiring the resulting aesthetic.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54460815043"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-4.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-4.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-4-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-4-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-4-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>April 18, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>The accents went further. More gold things on the shelves. The border of the door is now gilded. Golden appliqués were added to the wainscoting.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54537593381"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/7-5.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/7-5.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/7-5-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/7-5-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/7-5-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>May 21, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>By late May, all of the relief elements of the doorway had been gilded, including the fasces above the lintel.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54556206828"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/8-2.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/8-2.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/8-2-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/8-2-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/8-2-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>May 29, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Apparently concerned that the room wasn’t gaudy enough, more appliqués were added, including to the wall next to the bookshelf and to the sections of the door. The architectural elements in the border at the top of the room was also gilded.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54708103170"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/9-3.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/9-3.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/9-3-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/9-3-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/9-3-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>Aug. 6, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Again, compare this with the way the office looked in the last days of Biden’s presidency.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse46/54250695594"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-4.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-4.jpg 1024w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-4-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-4-768x512.jpg 768w"/></a><figcaption>Jan. 3, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
<p>An unrelenting and unsubtle overhaul.</p>
<p>Trump’s made other changes, too. The Rose Garden went from looking like this…</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse46/52263840352/"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-2.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-2.jpg 1024w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-2-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/1-2-768x512.jpg 768w"/></a><figcaption>June 21, 2022.</figcaption></figure>
<p>…to looking like this. White-and-yellow striped umbrellas were added to the tables below, echoing the aesthetic on Mar-a-Lago’s patio.</p>
<figure><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/whitehouse/54707958273"><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 2048px) 100vw, 2048px" src="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-1.jpg" srcset="https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-1.jpg 2048w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-1-300x200.jpg 300w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-1-768x512.jpg 768w, https://www.pbump.net/o/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/6-1-1536x1024.jpg 1536w"/></a><figcaption>Aug. 5, 2025.</figcaption></figure>
