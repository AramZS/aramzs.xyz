<p>February 2022. Boston. The sidewalks had turned to glass.</p>
<p>I slipped hard walking around a pond and spent a week in the dark, nursing a concussion with a blanket over my head. I had moved to Boston a year earlier from the Bay Area and I was still adjusting. The cold felt personal. The smiles were tight. Everything seemed harder than it needed to be.</p>
<p>The first show I watched when I could finally look at a screen again was <a href="https://www.netflix.com/title/81008305?ref=the-flytrap.ghost.io">Inventing Anna</a>. I don’t remember choosing it; I had never heard the name <a href="https://www.instagram.com/theannadelvey/?hl=en&amp;ref=the-flytrap.ghost.io">Anna Delvey</a>, but there the icon was, glowing at the top of Netflix like a cultural assignment I’d missed. I was supposed to be limiting screen time, but I kept watching.</p>
<p>And then Googling.</p>
<p>And then muttering: <em>She just said she had money, and people…believed her?</em></p>
