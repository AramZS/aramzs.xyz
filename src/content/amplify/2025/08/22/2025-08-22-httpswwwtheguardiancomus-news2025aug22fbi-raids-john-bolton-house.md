<h2 data-gu-name="standfirst">Bolton’s home reportedly part of investigation into classified documents as FBI director warns ‘no one is above law’</h2>
<p>The <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/fbi">FBI</a> raided the home of <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/donaldtrump">Donald Trump</a>’s former national security adviser turned critic John Bolton on Friday morning, according to reports.</p>
<p>The federal search of Bolton’s house in the <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/washington-dc">Washington DC</a> area was as part of an investigation involving the handling of classified documents, the Associated Press reported, citing an unnamed source.</p>
<p>FBI director Kash Patel posted a cryptic message on X on Friday morning, saying: “NO ONE is above the law… @FBI agents on mission.”</p>
<p>The raid began around 7am ET and was <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://nypost.com/2025/08/22/us-news/patels-fbi-raids-john-boltons-home-in-high-profile-national-security-probe/">first reported</a> by the New York Post.</p>
