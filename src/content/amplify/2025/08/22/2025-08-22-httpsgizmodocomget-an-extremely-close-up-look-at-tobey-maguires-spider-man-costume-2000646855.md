<p><a href="https://gizmodo.com/sam-raimi-spider-man-trilogy-rerelease-fathom-events-extended-edition-2000620704"><em>Spider-Man 2</em></a> is widely considered one of <a href="https://gizmodo.com/spider-man-2-is-a-perfect-sequel-1844340552">the greatest comic book movies</a> of all time—if not <em>the</em> greatest—and now you can own a piece of Hollywood history, or at least admire it in some very up-close new photos, thanks to an upcoming auction offering Tobey Maguire’s screen-used Spider-suit. As a bonus, the suit was also used in the much less-beloved <a href="https://gizmodo.com/sam-raimis-spider-man-3-is-obsessed-with-itself-in-the-1844396167"><em>Spider-Man 3</em></a>, so it’s also got some genuine Hollywood notoriety attached to it, too.</p>
<p>The suit is yet another jaw-dropping addition to Propstore’s upcoming mega-auction featuring such covetable memorabilia as <a href="https://gizmodo.com/original-darth-vader-lightsaber-auction-star-wars-2000629787">Darth Vader’s lightsaber</a>, <a href="https://gizmodo.com/indiana-jones-whip-auction-propstore-last-crusade-2000611755">Indiana Jones’ whip</a>, and Sauron’s <em>Lord of the Rings</em> helmet, and bidding is not for the faint of heart. The Spider-Man suit is estimated to pull in $100,000-$200,000, but given how rare an item like this is, you have to imagine it could go even higher.</p>
<p>Here’s a bit more information from <a href="https://propstoreauction.com/lot-details/index/catalog/456/lot/172131/1">the auction catalog</a>: “Created by Frontline Design under the supervision of Academy Award-winning costume designer James Acheson, this version of Spider-Man’s costume reflected refinements made for <em>Spider-Man 2</em>, including darker blue fabric, a more streamlined muscle silhouette, slightly reduced eye lenses, and an enlarged chest emblem. The suit consists of a red and dark blue one-piece stretch spandex bodysuit with integrated boots. The interior lining is gusseted and marked ‘3.S,’ a wardrobe notation that links it to the production use of <em>Spider-Man 3</em>. Zippers are discreetly positioned along the sides and lower back for entry.”</p>
<p>Who knew Spider-Man used zippers to get into character? For anyone who’s not, say, the billionaire scion of OsCorp with a particular interest in Spider-Man outfits, Propstore has shared a ton of detailed images so we can all take a gander at the iconic costume.</p>
