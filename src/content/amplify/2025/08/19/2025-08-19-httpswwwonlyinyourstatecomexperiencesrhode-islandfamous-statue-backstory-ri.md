<p>Public art is important to our culture. There are many people who may never be able to go to a gallery or buy art of their own, but due to all of the awesome grants and creators out there, you can find art to admire for free in almost any city or town. Rhode Island has a particularly rich history of public art. The state is absolutely packed with murals, sculptures, and installations of all kinds that will grab your eye in an instant. However, some of our most prevalent public art is also definitely some of our wackiest. The Mr. Potato Head <a data-elementclicked="Body Text Link" data-moduletype="Article Body" href="https://www.onlyinyourstate.com/nature/rhode-island/south-kingstown-sculpture-trail-ri">statues in Rhode Island</a>, for example, have to be seen to be believed, and the story behind them is oh so fascinating.</p>
<p>While driving through the state, there is a good chance you've spotted at least one of these person sized Potato Heads.</p>

<p>At six feet tall they're pretty hard to miss.</p>
<p>You may not have thought too much about them, just believing they're a strange anomaly. However, these Potato Heads have been around for over 20 years now. Actually, there used to be a LOT more.</p>

<p>As luck would have it, the headquarters for Hasbro toys is located in-state, so the Potato Head seemed like a perfect fit!</p>

<p>So, despite the original number of Potato Heads seeming massive for such a small state, we are now left with only eight. It's a sad end to a cute story, but we think these few that remain are here to stay!</p>

<p>These Mr. Potato Head statues in Rhode Island are incredibly unique! And while they may not be everyone's cup of tea, it's sad that we've lost so many over the years when they are so iconic. The remaining <a data-elementclicked="Body Text Link" data-moduletype="Article Body" href="https://www.onlyinyourstate.com/nature/rhode-island/mr-potato-heads-ri">Rhode Island Potato Heads</a> are scattered throughout the state.
