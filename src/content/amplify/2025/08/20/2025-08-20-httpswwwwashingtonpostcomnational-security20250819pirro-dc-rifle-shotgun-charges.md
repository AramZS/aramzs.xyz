<p>D.C.’s top prosecutor said her office would continue charging violent crimes and firearms trafficking that involve those weapons.</p>
<p>Federal prosecutors in D.C. have been instructed not to seek felony charges against people who are carrying rifles or shotguns in the nation’s capital, regardless of the strength of the evidence, according to U.S. Attorney Jeanine Pirro and an email reviewed by The Washington Post.</p>
