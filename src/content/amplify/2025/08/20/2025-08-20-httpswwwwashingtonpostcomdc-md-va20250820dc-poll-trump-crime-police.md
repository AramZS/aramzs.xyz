<p>Though crime continues to be a concern, most residents don’t think Trump’s actions will make D.C. safer, according to a Washington Post-Schar School poll.</p>
<p>Washington, D.C., residents overwhelmingly oppose President Donald <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/dc-md-va/2025/08/18/trump-dc-takeover-crime-immigration/">Trump’s decision last week to take over the D.C. police</a> and order federal law enforcement and the National Guard onto District streets, and 65 percent don’t think his actions will make the city safer from crime, according to a <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/tablet/2025/08/20/aug-14-17-2025-washington-post-schar-school-poll-dc-residents/">Washington Post-Schar School poll</a>.</p>
