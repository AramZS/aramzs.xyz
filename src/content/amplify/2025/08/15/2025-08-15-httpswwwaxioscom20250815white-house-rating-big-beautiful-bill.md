<p>The <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/politics-policy/donald-trump" data-vars-content-id="622e017c-9c79-4b73-ab65-8085293f5d0d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Scoop: White House loyalty rating for companies" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="West Wing" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/politics-policy/donald-trump">West Wing</a> has created a scorecard that rates 553 companies and trade associations on how hard they worked to support and promote President Trump's "One Big Beautiful Bill," a senior White House official tells Axios.</p>
<p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Trump works transactionally, and companies have rushed to pay demonstrative homage. Now, senior aides will have data to consult when considering corporate requests.</p>
<p><strong>Factors in the rating </strong>include social media posts, press releases, video testimonials, ads, attendance at White House events, and other engagement related to "OB3," as the megabill is known internally.</p>
<ul><li>The organizations' support is ranked as strong, moderate or low.</li><li>Axios has learned that "examples of good partners" on the White House list include Uber, DoorDash, United, Delta, AT&amp;T, Cisco, <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.airlines.org/who-we-are/" data-vars-content-id="622e017c-9c79-4b73-ab65-8085293f5d0d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Scoop: White House loyalty rating for companies" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Airlines for America" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.airlines.org/who-we-are/">Airlines for America</a> and the <a data-vars-click-url="https://steelnet.org/about-us/" data-vars-content-id="622e017c-9c79-4b73-ab65-8085293f5d0d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Scoop: White House loyalty rating for companies" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Steel Manufacturers Association" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://steelnet.org/about-us/">Steel Manufacturers Association</a>.</li></ul>
<p><strong>How it works:</strong> The data, which is being circulated to White House senior staff, will be used as a reality check when someone from K Street calls and says, for instance, that they'd "love to catch up — was so great working with you to pass the big, beautiful bill."</p>
<ul><li>The ranking "helps us see who really goes out and helps vs. those who just come in and pay lip service," the official said.</li><li>Separately, a <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2025/06/trump-effect-a-running-list-of-new-u-s-investment-in-president-trumps-second-term/" data-vars-content-id="622e017c-9c79-4b73-ab65-8085293f5d0d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Scoop: White House loyalty rating for companies" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="running list" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2025/06/trump-effect-a-running-list-of-new-u-s-investment-in-president-trumps-second-term/">running list</a> on the White House website tracks announcements of "Trump Effect" investments in U.S. manufacturing, production, and innovation.</li></ul>
<p><strong>What's next:</strong> We're told this is an evolving document, with the organizations' engagement on other presidential initiatives to be added.</p>
<ul><li>"If groups/companies want to start advocating more now for the tax bill or additional administration priorities, we will take that into account in our grading," the official said.</li></ul>
