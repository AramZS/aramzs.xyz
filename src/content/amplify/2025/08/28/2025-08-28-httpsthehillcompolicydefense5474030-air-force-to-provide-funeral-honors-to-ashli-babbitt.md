<p>The U.S. Air Force will provide Jan. 6 rioter <a href="https://thehill.com/people/ashli-babbitt/">Ashli Babbitt </a>with military funeral honors, reversing a Biden-era decision that denied her family’s request, according to a legal group that has represented her family.</p>
<p>Judicial Watch, the conservative legal group, on Wednesday <a href="https://www.judicialwatch.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Hegseth-Babbitt-letter-2025-5.pdf">made public a letter</a> from Under Secretary of the Air Force Matthew Lohmeier, extending an offer to allow the special funeral procedures to take place.</p>
<p>“I understand that the family’s initial request was denied by Air Force leadership in a letter dated February 9, 2021,” Lohmeier wrote in the <a href="https://www.judicialwatch.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Hegseth-Babbitt-letter-2025-5.pdf">Aug. 15 letter</a> shared by Judicial Watch. “However, after reviewing the circumstances of Ashli’s death, and considering the information that has come forward since then, I am persuaded that the previous determination was incorrect.”</p>
<p>He additionally extended an invitation to her mother and husband to meet him at the Pentagon, so he may offer his condolences.</p>
<p>The Hill requested comment from the Pentagon regarding the veracity of the letter.</p>
<figure><p class="rw-outer-content"><span>Some content could not be imported from the original document.</span> <a href="https://imasdk.googleapis.com/js/core/bridge3.717.1_en.html#fid=goog_2096470828">View content ↗ </a></p></figure>
<p>Babbitt, an Air Force veteran, was shot and killed by law enforcement during the Jan. 6, 2021, Capitol riot as she attempted to climb through a barricaded door to the Speaker’s lobby near the House chamber.</p>
<p>A month after the riot, an Air Force representative under former <a href="https://thehill.com/people/joe-biden/">President Biden’s </a>administration informed her family that military funeral honors were denied for her funeral “due to the circumstances preceding her death.”</p>

<p>“As a result, I have determined that military funeral honors would bring discredit upon the Air Force,” Brian Kelly, an Air Force lieutenant general, <a href="https://www.judicialwatch.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Hegseth-Babbitt-letter-2025-5.pdf">wrote in that letter</a>.</p>
<p>An eligible veteran’s honor guard detail consists of at least two members of the armed forces, one of whom is a representative of the veteran’s service branch, <a href="https://www.cem.va.gov/military_funeral_honors.asp">according to the Department of Veterans Affairs</a>. It also includes a ceremony including the playing of taps and the folding and presentation of the American flag to the next of kin.</p>
<p>“Ashli Babbitt’s family is grateful to <a href="https://thehill.com/people/donald-trump/">President Trump,</a> Secretary [Pete] Hegseth and Under Secretary Lohmeier for reversing the Biden Defense Department’s cruel decision to deny Ashli funeral honors as a distinguished veteran of the Air Force,” Judicial Watch President <a href="https://thehill.com/people/tom-fitton/">Tom Fitton </a>said in a statement.</p>
<p>In May, the government agreed to pay Babbitt’s family <a href="https://thehill.com/policy/national-security/5307989-ashli-babbitt-family-government-settlement-jan-6-capitol-riot/?tbref=hp">nearly $5 million</a> as part of a settlement.</p>
<p>It came as the Trump administration has sought to unravel the Biden administration’s crackdown on those who participated in the riot.</p>
<p>Trump pardoned nearly all rioters on his first day back in the White House and fired dozens of prosecutors who worked on criminal cases stemming from the attack.</p>
<p>The Air Force could not be reached for comment.</p>
