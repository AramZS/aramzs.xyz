<h1 data-testid="Heading">China welcomes 183 Brazil coffee sellers in wake of US tariffs</h1>
<p>By <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/authors/ana-mano/">Ana Mano</a></p>
<p>August 3, 202510:44 AM EDTUpdated 7 hours ago</p>

<p>SAO PAULO, Aug 3 (Reuters) - China has approved 183 new Brazilian coffee companies to export products to the Chinese market, according to a social media post of the Chinese embassy in Brazil on Saturday.</p>
<p>The measure, a boon to local exporters after the United States government's announcement of <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/trump-hits-brazil-with-tariffs-sanctions-key-sectors-excluded-2025-07-30/">steep tariffs</a> on Brazilian coffee and other products, took effect on July 30.</p>
<p>The new Chinese export permits are valid for five years, according to the post.</p>
<p>The U.S.'s 50% tariff on some Brazilian products will begin on August 6.</p>
<p>The levy represents a challenge for commodities traders and Brazilian coffee exporters, who need to find alternatives for the roughly <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/trumps-50-brazil-coffee-tariff-expected-rejig-trade-send-more-beans-china-2025-08-01/">8 million bags</a> sold to U.S. coffee processors every year.</p>
<p>China is Brazil's top trade partner overall while the U.S. is a big buyer of Brazilian beef and orange juice, among other products.</p>
<p>In June, Brazilian coffee exports into the U.S. totaled 440,034 60-kilo bags, 7,87 times more than Brazil's sales into China of nearly 56,000 bags that month, according to trade data compiled by industry lobby Cecafe.</p>
<p>The Brazilian ministry of agriculture and Cecafe did not have an immediate comment.</p>
<p>China's customs authority could not be immediately reached as it was outside the business hours.</p>
<p>Brazil supplies about a third of the U.S. coffee demand each year, a trade valued at $4.4 billion in the 12 months ended in June.</p>
