<p>The <a data-analytics-post-depth="20" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/july-jobs-report-unemployment-rate-employment-federal-reserve-interest-rates-2025-8">latest job report</a> confirms what many job seekers have been feeling: The market really is <em>that</em> bad.</p>
<p>For those who have been <a data-analytics-post-depth="20" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/job-seeker-applied-more-than-2000-roles-2024-9">applying to thousands of jobs</a> or slogging through <a data-analytics-post-depth="20" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/people-looking-for-jobs-enduring-many-rounds-of-interviews-2024-7">grueling interview rounds</a> only to get passed over, the numbers aren't likely surprising.</p>
<p>"Things have ebbed and flowed," Stephanie O'Neill, a job seeker based in Los Angeles who has been on the job hunt for 10 months, told Business Insider. "A couple months ago, it seemed like it was picking up, but it appears to be slowing down again."</p>
<p>The July jobs report revealed that 73,000 jobs were added in July, less than the expected 106,000. The unemployment rate also increased to 4.2%, a slight increase from 4.1% in June.</p>
<p>Another standout detail from the report was the "larger than normal" revisions to May and <a data-analytics-post-depth="20" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/june-jobs-report-unemployment-rate-employment-federal-reserve-interest-rates-2025-7">June's job growth</a> — meaning the earlier months were worse than previously thought.</p>
