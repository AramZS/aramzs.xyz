<p>A five-alarm fire tore through the economic establishment Friday after <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/politics-policy/donald-trump" data-vars-content-id="95bd1026-f8a4-4b2d-9737-fea6b176c670" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Trump's authoritarian streak" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="President Trump" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/politics-policy/donald-trump">President Trump</a> ousted the government's top labor statistician, <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/08/01/trump-jobs-labor-statistics" data-vars-content-id="95bd1026-f8a4-4b2d-9737-fea6b176c670" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Trump's authoritarian streak" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="accusing her" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/08/01/trump-jobs-labor-statistics">accusing her</a> — without evidence — of "rigging" a <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/08/01/jobs-report-july-unemployment" data-vars-content-id="95bd1026-f8a4-4b2d-9737-fea6b176c670" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Trump's authoritarian streak" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="weak jobs report" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/08/01/jobs-report-july-unemployment">weak jobs report</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> It's just one glaring example from a week that bore many authoritarian hallmarks — purging dissenters, rewriting history, criminalizing opposition and demanding total institutional loyalty.</p>
<p><strong>The big picture: </strong>The overwhelming, all-consuming nature of Trump-driven news cycles makes it difficult to discern partisan hysteria from true democratic backsliding.</p>
<ul><li>But apply any of these five storylines from the past week to a foreign leader — or even a past U.S. president — and it reads like an authoritarian playbook.</li></ul>
<p><strong>1. Trump fired Bureau of Labor Statistics </strong>commissioner Erika McEntarfer<strong>, </strong>a 20-year government veteran, after BLS announced massive downward revisions for job growth in May and June.</p>
<ul><li>"We're doing so well. I believe the numbers were phony. ... So you know what I did: I fired her," Trump told reporters, without explaining why he believed past jobs reports were credible when they were positive.</li><li>William Beach, who led the BLS during Trump's first term, <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.friendsofbls.org/updates/2025/8/1/statement-on-commissioner-mcentarfers-removal" data-vars-content-id="95bd1026-f8a4-4b2d-9737-fea6b176c670" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Trump's authoritarian streak" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="blasted the firing" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.friendsofbls.org/updates/2025/8/1/statement-on-commissioner-mcentarfers-removal">blasted the firing</a> as "totally groundless" and warned of a "dangerous precedent" of politicized economic data.</li></ul>
<p><strong>2. The stunning move </strong>came in the midst of Trump's months-long campaign to <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/07/13/trump-powell-federal-reserve-hassett" data-vars-content-id="95bd1026-f8a4-4b2d-9737-fea6b176c670" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Trump's authoritarian streak" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="oust Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/07/13/trump-powell-federal-reserve-hassett">oust Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell</a>, or at least pressure him to cut interest rates.</p>
<ul><li>Trump's stream of insults, which <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/07/31/trump-powell-interest-rates-fed" data-vars-content-id="95bd1026-f8a4-4b2d-9737-fea6b176c670" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Trump's authoritarian streak" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="escalated after the Fed held rates steady" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/07/31/trump-powell-interest-rates-fed">escalated after the Fed held rates steady</a> this week, has prompted comparisons to Turkey's disastrous experiment with bringing its central bank under political control.</li></ul>

<p>Firing the head of a key government agency because you don’t like the numbers they report, which come from surveys using long established procedures, is what happens in authoritarian countries not democratic ones. This is surely not the most serious threat to our democracy that <a href="https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump">@realDonaldTrump</a> poses, but it is the one closest to what we economists do. I don’t see how serious members of the economics profession can stay in his administration.<br/></p>
