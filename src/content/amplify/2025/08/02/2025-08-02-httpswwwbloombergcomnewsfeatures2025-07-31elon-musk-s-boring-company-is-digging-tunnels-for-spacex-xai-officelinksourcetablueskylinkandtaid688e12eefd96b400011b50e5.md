<h1>Elon Musk’s Boring Co. Is Turning Into a $900 Million Flop</h1>
<p>Nearly a decade ago, the billionaire said he’d build a hyperloop. Since then, he’s mostly built tunnels for Teslas and his own employees.</p>

<p>By <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ARNT5T6VBW0/kiel-porter">Kiel Porter</a></p>
<p>July 31, 2025 at 6:00 AM PDT</p>
<p>During an all-hands meeting <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/billionaires/profiles/elon-r-musk/">Elon Musk</a> led at the Boring Co. last year, he spent most of the time talking about how Donald Trump’s election would be a boon to SpaceX, according to former employees. There was little mention of his tunneling venture and its prospects, and they said he left the September meeting without visiting Boring’s factory.</p>
<p>That’s a far cry from how Musk first hyped Boring nearly a decade ago. He vowed to build a futuristic, ultra-fast underground hyperloop as a revolutionary solution to the world’s “soul-destroying” traffic problems, raising more than $900 million from the likes of <a data-component="link" href="https://www.boringcompany.com/seriescround">Sequoia Capital</a> and Peter Thiel’s Founders Fund.</p>
