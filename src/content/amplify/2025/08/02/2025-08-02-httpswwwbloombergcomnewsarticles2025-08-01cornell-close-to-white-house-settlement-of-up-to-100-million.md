<p>The White House is eyeing a settlement of as much as $100 million in negotiations with <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/545745Z:US">Cornell University</a> about an agreement that would reinstate <a data-component="link" href="https://statements.cornell.edu/2025/20250618-financial-austerity.cfm">hundreds of millions of dollars</a> in frozen federal research funding, according to people familiar with the matter.</p>
<p>A deal could be announced as soon as next week, said the people, who asked not to be named because the talks are private. One of the issues still under discussion is whether the pact would call for a resolution monitor to supervise how Cornell is carrying out changes required by the US — an arrangement accepted by <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/321034Z:US">Columbia University</a> in a landmark $221 million <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-23/columbia-agrees-to-200-million-fine-to-settle-white-house-fight">deal</a> last month.</p>
