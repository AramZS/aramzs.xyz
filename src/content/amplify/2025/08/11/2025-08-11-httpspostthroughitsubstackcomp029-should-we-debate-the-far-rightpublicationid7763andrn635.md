<p><strong><a href="https://substack.com/@mehdirhasan">Mehdi Hasan</a></strong>, a journalist renowned for confronting power directly and dismantling opponents in political debates, joined <em>Posting Through It</em> to reflect on his <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2S-WJN3L5eo">viral appearance</a> on "Surrounded" — a YouTube debate show that recruited him to argue against 20 "far-right conservatives."</p>
<p>Over nearly two hours, his opponents took turns sitting across from him and espousing racist bigotries, antisemitic conspiracy theories, and anti-democratic beliefs. When the cameras stopped rolling, Mehdi said one participant stood up and declared: "Left or Right, we all hate the Jews."</p>
<p>"No one said a word," Mehdi recalled.</p>
<p>The episode's explosive popularity (10.2 million views on YouTube alone) has reignited debates about "platforming" extremism and whether engaging with the far-right this way serves any constructive purpose. On this episode, we explore those criticisms with Mehdi, discuss his “hygiene tests” for debates, and explore why so many people who try to face off against the far-right end up failing.</p>
