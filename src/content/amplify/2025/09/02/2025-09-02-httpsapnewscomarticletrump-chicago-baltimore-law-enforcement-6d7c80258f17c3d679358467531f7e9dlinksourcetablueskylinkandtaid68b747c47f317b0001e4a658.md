<p>WASHINGTON (AP) — President <a data-gtm-enhancement-style="LinkEnhancementA" href="https://apnews.com/article/venezuela-strike-rubio-trump-0f901b2a30ee20e314bcab1385ffb0c0">Donald Trump</a> said Tuesday that he will direct federal law enforcement intervention to Chicago and Baltimore, despite local officials in both places opposing such moves.</p>
<p>Asked by reporters in the Oval Office if he had decided to send National Guard troops to Chicago, Trump said, “We’re going in,” but added. “I didn’t say when.”</p>
<p>Trump has already sent <a data-gtm-enhancement-style="LinkEnhancementA" href="https://apnews.com/article/trump-surge-dc-crime-courts-judges-cases-12bb76d6de84a8c720fafadc77d860a0">National Guard troops into Washington</a> and federalized the <a data-gtm-enhancement-style="LinkEnhancementA" href="https://apnews.com/article/washington-bowser-trump-dc-police-5d3768539b5155b1a7f4f26ddb54a8c3">nation’s capital’s police force</a>. More recently, he’s said he’s planning similar moves in other cities, particularly those run by Democratic officials.</p>
<p>Trump said he’d like Democratic Illinois Gov. <a data-gtm-enhancement-style="LinkEnhancementA" href="https://apnews.com/article/chicago-violence-crime-pritzker-national-guard-trump-2023e25445c45a3f0f4d3513e8eb2ac4">JB Pritzker</a> to call him and say “send in the troops.” Pritzker has opposed the move.</p>
<p>“If the governor of Illinois would call up, call me up, I would love to do it.” Trump said. “Now, we’re going to do it anyway. We have the right to do it.”</p>
<p>He added that he has an “obligation to protect this country, and that includes Baltimore.”</p>
