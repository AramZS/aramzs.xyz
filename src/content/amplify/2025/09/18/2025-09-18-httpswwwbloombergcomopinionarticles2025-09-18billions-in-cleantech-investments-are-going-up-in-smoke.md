<p>The capriciousness of US energy policy under President Donald Trump increasingly looks like a feature rather than a bug.</p>
<p>A <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-08-22/trump-halts-orsted-wind-project-in-another-blow-to-industry?sref=kOk687Pk">nearly built offshore wind project is suddenly blocked</a> on spurious grounds. A giant transmission project ready to be built <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-23/trump-pulls-4-9-billion-in-financing-for-contested-power-line">loses an agreed federal loan guarantee</a>. Executive orders redefine the very notion of ‘energy’ to exclude certain sources. The objective seems to be not merely to rip up clean technology by the roots but also to salt the soil. The damage is real, even if the objective is ultimately futile.</p>
