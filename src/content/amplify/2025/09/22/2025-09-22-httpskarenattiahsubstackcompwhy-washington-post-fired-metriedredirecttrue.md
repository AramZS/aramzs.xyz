<h3>Read the official termination letter for yourself. </h3>
<p>Ever since I went public on Monday about my firing from the Washington Post, there has been a lot of misinformation about the stated reasons for my firing. A narrative that has taken hold is that I was fired for mentioning Charlie Kirk’s views on Black women.</p>

<p>And because I care about the truth, and <a href="https://www.status.news/p/karen-attiah-fired-washington-post-charlie-kirk">even though this letter is already public</a>, I wanted to clear things up myself.</p>
<p>I was fired by email, without a conversation— after 11 years at the WaPo Opinions section as an editor and then columnist.</p>
<p>The letter, quotes my OTHER posts, not that one about Black women.</p>
<p>Here is the reasoning in the official termination letter that was sent to me last Thursday — which I categorically reject.</p>
<p>You can read it and judge for yourself.</p>
