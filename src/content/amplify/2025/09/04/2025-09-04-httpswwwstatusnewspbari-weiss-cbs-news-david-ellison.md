<p>When <strong>David Ellison</strong> took his victory lap through<strong> New York</strong> and <strong>Los Angeles</strong> after closing the <strong>Paramount</strong>-<strong>Skydance</strong> deal last month, he stuck to a single line whenever reporters pressed him about <strong>Donald Trump’s </strong>boast of a backroom deal to run public service announcements on <strong>CBS</strong>: He didn’t want to “politicize the company.” Again and again, Ellison insisted Paramount would remain above the partisan fray, repeating the talking point until it was clear he had no intention of deviating from the script.</p>
<p>Which is what makes one of his first big moves as Paramount’s new overlord so revealing and hypocritical. On Wednesday, <strong>Puck’s Dylan Byers</strong> <a href="https://puck.news/david-ellison-set-to-acquire-the-free-press/?utm_campaign=the-weiss-price&amp;utm_medium=referral&amp;utm_source=www.status.news">reported that</a> Ellison is on the verge of acquiring <strong>Bari Weiss’</strong> <strong>The Free Press</strong> for “well above” $100 million—an astonishing and absurd sum for a <strong>Substack</strong>-housed opinion site that is only said to generate $10–15 million in annual subscription revenue, with its overhead unknown. Weiss, the stridently pro-<strong>Israel</strong>, proudly anti-“woke” culture warrior, has built her brand on polarizing political commentary—supposedly the type of material Ellison signaled to reporters that he wishes to run away from.</p>
