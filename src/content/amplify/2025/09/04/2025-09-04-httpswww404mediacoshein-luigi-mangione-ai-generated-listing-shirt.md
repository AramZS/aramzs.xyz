<p>A listing on ultra-fast-fashion e-commerce site Shein used an AI-generated image of Luigi Mangione to sell a floral button-down t-shirt.</p>
<p>Mangione—the prime suspect in the December 2024 murder of United Healthcare CEO Brian Thompson—is being held at the Metropolitan Detention Center in Brooklyn, last I checked, and is <em>not </em>modeling for Shein.</p>
<p>I first saw the Mangione Shein listing on the culture and news X account Popcrave, which posted the listing late Tuesday evening.</p>

<p>Shein removed the listing on Wednesday, but someone <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20250903014800/https://m.shein.com/us/Men's-New-Spring/Summer-Short-Sleeve-Blue-Ditsy-Floral-White-Shirt,-Pastoral-Style-Gentleman-Shirt-For-Everyday-Wear,-Family-Matching-Mommy-And-Me-(3-Pieces-Are-Sold-Separately)-p-59358613.html?cdn_rsite=cf&amp;ref=www&amp;rep=dir&amp;ret=mus"><u>saved it on the Internet Archive</u></a> before Shein took it down. "The image in question was provided by a third-party vendor and was removed immediately upon discovery," <a href="https://www.newsweek.com/luigi-mangione-shein-shirt-listing-viral-2123622?ref=404media.co"><u>Shein told <em>Newsweek</em></u></a> in a statement. "We have stringent standards for all listings on our platform. We are conducting a thorough investigation, strengthening our monitoring processes, and will take appropriate action against the vendor in line with our policies." Shein provided the same comment to 404 Media.</p>
<p>The item, sold by the third-party brand Manfinity, had the description “Men's New Spring/Summer Short Sleeve Blue Ditsy Floral White Shirt, Pastoral Style Gentleman Shirt For Everyday Wear, Family Matching Mommy And Me (3 Pieces Are Sold Separately).”</p>
<p>The Manfinity brand makes a lot of Shein stuff using AI-generated models, like <a href="https://us.shein.com/store/home?store_code=6089883462&amp;ici=PageGoodsDetail&amp;main_cate_id=2447&amp;main_goods_id=57898098&amp;rule_poskey=DetailShopItemList&amp;src_identifier=on%3Dstore%60cn%3DManfinity+Sport+PWRUP%60hz%3D0%60ps%3D1_1%60jc%3DthirdPartyStoreHome_6089883462&amp;src_module=DetailBrand&amp;src_tab_page_id=page_goods_detail1756906548470&amp;tab=items&amp;ref=404media.co"><u>these gym bros</u></a> selling PUSH HARDER t-shirts and gym sweats and <a href="https://us.shein.com/Manfinity-Homme-Men-s-Short-Sleeve-T-Shirt-With-Slogan-Print-p-31357634.html?src_identifier=st%3D2%60sc%3Dmanfinity%60sr%3D0%60ps%3D1&amp;src_module=search&amp;src_tab_page_id=page_goods_detail1756906649996&amp;pageListType=4&amp;imgRatio=3-4&amp;pageListType=4&amp;ref=404media.co"><u>this very tough guy</u></a> wearing a “NAH, I’M GOOD” tee. AI-generated models are all over Shein, and seems especially popular with listings featuring <a href="https://us.shein.com/SHEIN-Baby-Boys-2pcs-Set-Hoodie-Sweatshirt-Jacket-With-Letter-Print-And-Pants-With-Letter-Print-Warm-Comfortable-Outfit-p-46891420.html?pageListType=4&amp;imgRatio=3-4&amp;pageListType=4&amp;ref=404media.co"><u>babies</u></a> and <a href="https://us.shein.com/SHEIN-Baby-Boys-Little-Man-Graphic-Print-Comfortable-Round-Neck-Hoodie-Set-p-46755151.html?src_identifier=on%3DCATEGORY_RECOMMEND_COMPONENT%60cn%3DCATEGORY_RECOMMEND_COMPONENT_1%60hz%3D-%60jc%3DitemPicking_017172967%60ps%3D2_2_4&amp;src_module=all&amp;src_tab_page_id=page_risk_crawler_block1756905770606&amp;pageListType=4&amp;imgRatio=3-4&amp;pageListType=4&amp;ref=404media.co"><u>toddlers</u></a>. AI models in fashion are becoming more mainstream; in July, Vogue ran <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgeqe084nn4o?ref=404media.co"><u>advertisements for Guess</u></a> featuring AI-generated women selling the brand’s summer collection.</p>
<p>Last year, <a href="https://fortune.com/2024/04/16/shein-class-action-lawsuit-copyright-infringement-data-scraping-ai-art-alan-giana-china/?ref=404media.co"><u>artists sued Shein</u></a>, alleging the Chinese e-commerce giant scraped the internet using AI and stole their designs, and it’s <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-50817561?ref=404media.co"><u>been well-documented</u></a> that fast fashion sites use bots to identify popular themes and memes from social media to put them on their own listings. <a href="https://www.404media.co/copyright-abuse-is-getting-luigi-mangione-merch-removed-from-the-internet/"><u>Mangione merch</u></a> and anything related to the case—including remixes of the United Healthcare logo and the “Deny, Defend, Depose” line allegedly found on the bullet—went wild in the weeks following Thompson’s murder; Manfinity might have generated what seemed popular on social media (Mangione’s smiling face) and automatically put it on a shirt listing. Based on the archived listing, it worked: A lot of people managed to grab a limited edition Shein Luigi Ditsy Floral before it was removed: According to the archived version of the listing, it was sold out of all sizes except for XXL.</p>
