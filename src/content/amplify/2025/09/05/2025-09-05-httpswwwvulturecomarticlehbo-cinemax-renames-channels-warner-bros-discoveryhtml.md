<p>If there’s one thing we can count on in the streaming era, it’s that Warner Bros. Discovery will <a href="https://www.vulture.com/2023/04/hbo-max-renamed-max-streaming-service-wbd.html">rename</a> and <a href="https://www.vulture.com/article/warner-bros-discovery-spinoff-plan-explained.html">rename</a> and <a href="https://www.vulture.com/article/hbo-max-streaming-name-change.html">un-rename</a> its various streaming and cable properties over and over again. The latest example: Six of the secondary HBO and erstwhile Cinemax channels such as “HBO2” and “ActionMAX” are getting a brand makeover. Here’s the full list of changes announced today:</p>
<p>• HBO2 → <strong>HBO Hits</strong><br/>
• HBO Signature → <strong>HBO Drama</strong><br/>
• HBO Zone →<strong> HBO Movies</strong><br/>
• MoreMAX → <strong>Cinemax Hits</strong><br/>
• ActionMAX → <strong>Cinemax Action</strong></p>
<p>Viewers should see the changes reflected immediately, assuming you were even aware of these channels in the age of cord-cutting and on-demand viewing. (Before today, I had no clue what 5StarMAX was, personally, so in that sense the rebrand has already worked on at least one customer.) These are the stations you likely get access to for free if you subscribed to the primary HBO channel via a cable provider <em>or</em> to HBO Max, where they live in a little livestreaming-options bar in the app.</p>
<p>To Warner Bros. Discovery’s credit, these refreshes aren’t coming out of nowhere. Though a channel HBO2 has been around in one form or another since the early ’90s, it’s somewhat common for ancillary cable brands to get reshuffled every five to seven years in the TV biz; Starz did a similar reorganization of its Encore channels <a href="https://deadline.com/2013/11/starz-reorganizes-encore-channels-to-target-african-americans-baby-boomers-632245/">in 2013</a>. More urgently, though, Warner Bros. Discovery is in the process of <a href="https://www.vulture.com/article/warner-bros-discovery-spinoff-plan-explained.html">splitting itself into two entities</a> and as part of that strategy, recently <a href="https://ottverse.com/hbo-family-and-several-cinemax-channels-to-shut-down-in-august-2025/">nuked a few other channels</a> like HBO Family, ThrillerMax, and OuterMax. This is just another step along <a href="https://www.vulture.com/article/why-max-is-going-back-to-being-called-hbo-max.html">David Zaslav’s retreat</a> from the cable business and from his original vision for WBD.</p>
