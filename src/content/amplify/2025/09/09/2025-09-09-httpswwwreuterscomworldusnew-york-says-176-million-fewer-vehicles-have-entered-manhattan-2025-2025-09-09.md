<h1 data-testid="Heading">New York says 17.6 million fewer vehicles have entered Manhattan in 2025</h1>
<p>By <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/authors/david-shepardson/">David Shepardson</a></p>

<p>Sept 9 (Reuters) - New York officials said on Tuesday that 17.6 million fewer vehicles have entered congested Manhattan since the city launched its <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-may-withhold-environmental-approvals-manhattan-projects-if-state-does-end-2025-04-21/">first-in-the-nation</a> congestion pricing program in January, down 12%.</p>
<p>The program charges most passenger vehicles a toll of $9 during peak periods to enter Manhattan south of 60th Street, in a bid to cut congestion and <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/new-york-city-congestion-program-collected-486-million-january-2025-02-24/">raise funds</a> to improve mass transit.</p>
<p>The Trump administration is <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-judge-bars-trump-killing-new-york-congestion-program-2025-05-28/">trying to kill the program</a> but a judge has put that effort on hold, as well as <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-judge-temporarily-blocks-trump-cutting-funds-over-new-york-congestion-program-2025-05-27/">blocking the federal government</a> from withholding approval or funding for New York projects.</p>
<p><a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/new-york/">New York</a> Governor Kathy Hochul and the Metropolitan Transportation Authority said on Tuesday crossing times into Manhattan in the congestion zone have dropped, making the Brooklyn Bridge 13% faster, Holland Tunnel 36% faster and Lincoln Tunnel 5% faster.</p>
<p>Crashes in the Manhattan congestion zone are down 14%, while traffic injuries are down by 15%, while ridership on buses is up 13% and on subways up 9%.</p>
<p>Hochul has said that funds raised from the program would underpin $15 billion in debt financing for critical mass transit capital improvements.</p>
<p>The USDOT under Democratic President <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/joe-biden/">Joe Biden</a> had <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-withdraws-approval-new-york-citys-congestion-pricing-program-2025-02-19/">approved the congestion program in November</a>, which is monitored via electronic license plate readers. U.S. approval is needed because it involves tolls on federal highways.</p>
<p>The program follows similar ones in London and Singapore.</p>
<p>Opponents including Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy say it takes money from working people and leaves drivers without a free highway option.</p>
<p>Last month, Duffy said USDOT may withhold up to 25% of New York <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-threatens-withhold-funds-over-new-york-subway-worker-safety-issues-2025-08-19/">federal transit funding</a> if the MTA's New York City Transit does not improve safety for subway track maintenance workers.</p>
