<p>In peddling "AI for Social Good" initiatives, technology companies and philanthropies are suggesting that complex political, historical, and social issues can be reduced to technical problems. But given how today's AI systems work, there is no reason we should believe them – and much reason to be suspicious of their claims.</p>
<p>DUBLIN – Amid the growing enthusiasm for integrating AI into critical social infrastructure, philanthropic institutions and tech monopolies have produced a flurry of “AI for Social Good” (AI4SG) initiatives. But while these efforts aspire to address major global challenges like poverty, gender inequality, and climate change, AI systems have been shown to exacerbate these very problems, while concealing others.</p>
