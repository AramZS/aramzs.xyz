<p>Last Thursday, on September 11, <strong>Karen Attiah</strong> was walking out of a martial arts class when she noticed a missed call from a <strong>Washington, D.C.</strong>, number. Before she could figure out who had tried to reach her, an email landed in her inbox. It was from <strong>Wayne Connell</strong>, <strong>The Washington Post’s</strong> head of human resources, and inside was a message that would abruptly put an end to her more than decade-long career at the newspaper: she was being fired.</p>
