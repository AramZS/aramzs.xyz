<p>A new analysis of AI hardware being produced and how it is being used attempts to estimate the vast amount of electricity being consumed by AI.</p>

<p>AI’s energy use already represents as much as 20 percent of global data-center power demand, <a data-event-boundary="click" data-event-click='{"pattern":"ExternalLink"}' data-in-view='{"pattern":"ExternalLink"}' data-include-experiments="true" data-offer-url="https://www.cell.com/joule/fulltext/S2542-4351(25)00142-4" href="https://www.cell.com/joule/fulltext/S2542-4351(25)00142-4">research published Thursday</a> in the journal Joule shows. That demand from <a href="https://wired.com/tag/artificial-intelligence/">AI</a>, the research states, could double by the end of this year, comprising nearly half of all total data-center <a href="https://www.wired.com/tag/electricity/">electricity</a> consumption worldwide, excluding the electricity used for <a href="https://www.wired.com/tag/bitcoin/">bitcoin</a> mining.</p>
