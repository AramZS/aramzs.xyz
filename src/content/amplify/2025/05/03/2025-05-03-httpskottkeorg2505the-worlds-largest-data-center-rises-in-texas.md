<h2>The World’s Largest Data Center Rises in Texas</h2>
<p>Photographer <a href="https://www.stephenvoss.com/">Stephen Voss</a> has been working on a project about data centers and recently travelled to Abilene, Texas to <a href="https://www.stephenvoss.com/blog/2025/5/1/project-stargate-drone-video">document the first data center built</a> as part of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stargate_LLC">Stargate Project</a>. When completed, it will be the largest data center in the world. Here’s a short drone video he took of the project:</p>
<div class="rw-embed-wrapper"><embed src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/fUiI03X6DQc" type="video/mp4"/></div>
<p>“The place was mesmerizing and deeply unsettling,” Voss told me over email. “When finished, it’ll have the power demands of a mid-sized city and is on a piece of land that’s the size of Central Park.”</p>
<p>The video immediately reminded me of <a href="https://www.edwardburtynsky.com/">Edward Burtynsky’s work</a> that documents “the impacts of human industry on the planet”.</p>
