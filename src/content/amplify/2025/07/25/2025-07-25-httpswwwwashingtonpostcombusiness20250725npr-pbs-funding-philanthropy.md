<p>Stations have massive holes to plug. Who will step up?</p>
<p>Margaret McConnell was already staring at a mild deficit on her balance sheet before Congress made it worse — much, much worse.</p>
<p>McConnell, a native of Pennsylvania’s Lehigh Valley, took over as the executive director of the public radio station WDIY in 2024. She said the station, based in Bethlehem, was already struggling to recover from pandemic-era financial losses when she learned the bad but expected news: Lawmakers in Washington had passed President Donald Trump’s rescissions bill, clawing back the <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2025/07/17/pbs-npr-budget-cuts-trump-republicans/50307c9c-632f-11f0-bf70-56d8888ebb94_story.html">$1.1 billion</a> set aside to fund <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2025/07/22/npr-edith-chapin-steps-down-congress-funding/">NPR</a>, <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2025/07/15/pbs-npr-funding-rescission-bill/">PBS</a>, and member stations over the next two years.</p>
