title: G.M. Profit Shrinks on Billion-Dollar Tariff Hit
---
<div><div data-testid="companionColumn-0"><div><p>General Motors said on Tuesday that its profit in the second quarter fell by more than a third, after President Trump’s tariffs cost the company more than $1 billion.</p><p>G.M. was the second automaker in as many days to show the toll that the Trump administration’s trade policies are taking on the industry. <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2025/07/21/business/stellantis-profit-trump-tariffs.html" title="">Stellantis</a>, the maker of Chrysler, Jeep and Ram vehicles, said on Monday that it lost 2.3 billion euros ($2.7 billion) in the first half of the year because of tariffs and other Republican policies.</p><p>G.M.’s profit for the quarter was $1.9 billion, the company said, down from $2.9 billion in the same quarter last year. Sales fell 2 percent, to $47 billion.</p><p>Mary Barra, G.M.’s chief executive, said in a letter to shareholders that the company is investing $4 billion to increase production in the United States of pickups and sport utility vehicles that would be less susceptible to tariffs.</p></div></div><div data-testid="companionColumn-1"><div><p>“We are positioning the business for a profitable, long-term future as we adapt to new trade and tax policies, and a rapidly evolving tech landscape,” Ms. Barra said.</p></div></div></div>
