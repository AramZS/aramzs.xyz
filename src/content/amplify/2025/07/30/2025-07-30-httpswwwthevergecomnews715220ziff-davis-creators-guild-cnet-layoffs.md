<p>Ziff Davis, the media conglomerate that owns outlets like <em>CNET, ZDNET, PCMag, </em>and <em>Mashable </em>is laying off 15 percent of the unionized workforce, or 23 total people.</p>
<p>The majority of layoffs are coming from <em>CNET</em>, where 19 people will lose their jobs — even as Ziff Davis goes on a shopping spree. The layoffs will hit <em>CNET </em>coverage areas like the finance, broadband, and sleep beats, as well as the outlet’s copy desk. A handful of staffers across <em>Lifehacker</em>, <em>Mashable</em>, and <em>ZDNet</em> will also be laid off.</p>
<p>“It’s very clear to us that these cuts aren’t about journalism,” Anna Iovine, unit chair of the Ziff Davis Creators Guild, says. “They’re based on money and greed.” Iovine noted particular concerns around cutting copy editors and fact checkers.</p>
<p>“Eliminating any coverage is really devastating. These journalists, some of them have decades of experience, and we’re losing [that],” Iovine says.</p>
<p>Ziff Davis <a href="https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250703855797/en/Ziff-Davis-Completes-Three-Acquisitions-in-Q2-2025?feedref=JjAwJuNHiystnCoBq_hl-fArZ4S48GqIjCUpbTPJDg_ZRHqeOKdIRBEENMvWuUUmOKZXz2bZRmEQ1BwkpoG4oul5C1rkGQ_h4BkZ40LwsoN4gnxBSGiVu_qLafVdV0IiQPT0fDRifTq3nSfcMpa1LQ%3D%3D">has acquired</a> five other companies <a href="https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250402597262/en/Ziff-Davis-Completes-Two-Acquisitions-in-Q1-2025?feedref=JjAwJuNHiystnCoBq_hl-fArZ4S48GqIjCUpbTPJDg_ZRHqeOKdIRBEENMvWuUUmOKZXz2bZRmEQ1BwkpoG4oul5C1rkGQ_h4BkZ40LwsoN4gnxBSGiVu_qLafVdV0IiQPT0fDRifTq3nSfcMpa1LQ%3D%3D">this year alone</a>, most notably the popular newsletter TheSkimm and the health outlet Well+Good. <em>CNET</em> was <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2024/8/6/24214374/cnet-zeff-davis-acquisition-digital-media-100-million">acquired</a> by Ziff Davis in 2024 for $100 million. Ziff Davis did not immediately respond to a request for comment.</p>
<p><em>CNET</em> has had a tumultuous last few years under its previous owner, Red Ventures. In 2023, the outlet was engulfed in controversy when readers discovered it had been <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2023/1/19/23562966/cnet-ai-written-stories-red-ventures-seo-marketing">quietly publishing stories</a> written by AI that were <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2023/1/25/23571082/cnet-ai-written-stories-errors-corrections-red-ventures">full of errors</a>. In the ensuing weeks and months, <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2023/3/2/23622231/cnet-layoffs-ai-articles-seo-red-ventures"><em>CNET </em>staff were laid off</a>, <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2023/3/2/23622836/cnet-eic-takes-red-ventures-ai-content-job-connie-guglielmo">editor-in-chief Connie Guglielmo stepped down</a> to take a job overseeing AI content, and <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2023/5/16/23723959/cnet-union-red-ventures-tech-editorial-independence-ai-writing">staff at the outlet unionized</a>. After Red Ventures sold the outlet, the union was rolled into the Ziff Davis Creators Guild, represented by the NewsGuild of New York.</p>
<p>“At a time when <em>CNET</em> is still building back its reputation after a damaging AI scandal under Red Ventures, Ziff’s decision to further undermine CNET’s human authority is disturbing,” a statement from the bargaining unit reads.</p>
<p>“Our members are so much more than dollars and cents, even as the capricious management at Ziff Davis tries to treat us as such,” the statement continues. “We won a strong collective bargaining agreement just over a year ago, and we will fight to enforce it so we can preserve our ability to continue producing high-quality work for our readers.”</p>
