<p>The <a data-ga-click="1" data-ga-item="text-link" data-ga-label="$text" data-ga-module="content_body" href="https://mashable.com/search/?query=Arctic">North Pole</a> is the fastest-warming area of our planet. Yet, we know so little about the local ecosystems, the way they interact with the climate, and <a data-ga-click="1" data-ga-item="text-link" data-ga-label="$text" data-ga-module="content_body" href="https://mashable.com/video/pov-collar-camera-footage-polar-bears-arctic">how global warming is impacting them</a>. The French <a data-ga-click="1" data-ga-item="text-link" data-ga-label="$text" data-ga-module="content_body" href="https://fondationtaraocean.org/">Tara Ocean Foundation</a> wants to change that by deploying a spaceship-shaped station in the Central Arctic. The goal is to study the North Pole's ecosystem to document and better understand its relationship to the climate, and how the <a data-ga-click="1" data-ga-item="text-link" data-ga-label="$text" data-ga-module="content_body" href="https://mashable.com/category/climate-change">rising temperatures </a>may change this vulnerable area.</p>
