<p>FBI Director <strong>Kash Patel</strong> may reportedly join Deputy Director <strong>Dan Bongino</strong> in resigning if Attorney General <strong>Pam Bondi</strong> keeps her job.</p>
<p>According to a Friday report from <a href="https://www.axios.com/2025/07/11/epstein-files-dan-bongino-pam-bondi-trump">Axios</a>, Bongino and Bondi clashed over President <strong>Donald Trump’s</strong> administration’s handling of the case of convicted sex trafficker <strong>Jeffrey Epstein</strong>. The Justice Department, led by Bondi, released a joint memo with the FBI announcing that the rumored “Epstein list” naming his associates never really existed. That conclusion contradicted Bondi’s previous claims that the supposed list was on her desk.</p>
<p>As a result, Bongino and Bondi reportedly got into it. That led to Bongino taking off from work on Friday “in protest.” The deputy director, according to <em>The Daily Wire’s</em> <strong>Mary Margaret Olohan</strong>, had also made it clear that <a href="https://www.mediaite.com/politics/kaitlan-collins-reports-dan-bongino-is-telling-people-hes-considering-resigning-over-epstein-fiasco/">he would be leaving his post if Bondi kept hers</a>.</p>
<p>Not long after that reporting, Olohan added that Patel has joined Bongino in his stand against Bondi.</p>
<p>“Source close to DOJ says Kash Patel also wants Pam Bondi gone, and that he’d consider leaving if Bongino leaves,” Olohan said. “Also that there are more frustrations with other documents Bondi hasn’t released.”</p>
