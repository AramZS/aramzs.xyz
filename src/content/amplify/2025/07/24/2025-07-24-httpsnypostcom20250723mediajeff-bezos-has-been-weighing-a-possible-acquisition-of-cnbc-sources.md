<p>Amazon billionaire Jeff Bezos has been weighing a possible acquisition of CNBC, The Post has learned.</p>
<p>The 61-year-old e-commerce magnate has signaled interest to business associates in buying the cable network — home to “Squawk Box” and “Mad Money with Jim Cramer” — after it is<a href="https://nypost.com/2024/10/31/media/msnbc-parent-comcast-weighs-spin-off-of-declining-cable-networks/"> spun off by NBCUniversal parent Comcast later this year</a>, according to a person familiar with Bezos’ thinking.</p>
<p>CNBC would “align well with his interests,” said another source close to Bezos, who noted that the network could serve as a credible “neutral voice” in his media portfolio — a major plus following<a href="https://nypost.com/2024/10/25/media/jeff-bezos-killed-washington-posts-kamala-harris-endorsement-report/"> Bezos’s headaches as owner</a> of the left-leaning Washington Post.</p>

<p>A rep for Bezos declined to comment.</p>
<p>Comcast, meanwhile, plans <a href="https://nypost.com/2024/11/20/media/msnbc-staffers-in-a-panic-as-nbc-parent-comcast-plans-to-spin-off-channel-and-possibly-change-its-name/">to spin off its struggling cable assets</a> — which also include MSNBC, USA Network and E! — by the end of the year. CNBC will become part of a publicly-traded company called Versant, which will be <a href="https://nypost.com/2024/11/20/media/msnbc-staffers-in-a-panic-as-nbc-parent-comcast-plans-to-spin-off-channel-and-possibly-change-its-name/">run by a handful of NBCUniversal execs </a>led by Chief Executive Mark Lazarus.</p>
<p>Sources close to Comcast told The Post that Bezos has not approached the cable giant headed by CEO Brian Roberts.</p>
<p>Another source close the situation said Versant plans to grow CNBC, not sell it.</p>

<p>Versant declined to comment.</p>
<p>It is unclear how much Bezos — currently ranked as the fourth richest person in the world with a net worth of $241 billion, according to Forbes — would be willing to pony up for CNBC.</p>
<p>Comcast does not break out financials for its cable assets but recently said the soon-to-be spun-off Versant generated about $7 billion in revenue last year.</p>
<p>Buying CNBC would be tricky. There is a two-year period in which Versant could not engage in selling major assets like CNBC or even the entire company without major tax implications, sources told The Post.</p>
