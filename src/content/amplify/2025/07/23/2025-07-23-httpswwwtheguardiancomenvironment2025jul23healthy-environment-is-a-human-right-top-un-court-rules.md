<h2 data-gu-name="standfirst">Court’s decision expected to be used in future litigation and to support political negotiations by vulnerable states</h2>
<p>A “clean, healthy and sustainable environment” is a human right, according to judges at the top court of the <a data-component="auto-linked-tag" data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/unitednations">United Nations</a>.</p>
<p>The international court of justice (ICJ) delivered a landmark advisory opinion on Wednesday about countries’ obligations to tackle climate change, and the consequences they may face if they do not.</p>
<p>The non-binding opinion runs to more than 500 pages and is seen as a potential turning point in international climate law. Enshrining a sustainable environment as a human right paves the way for other legal actions, including states returning to the ICJ to hold each other to account, as well as domestic lawsuits.</p>
<p>“The consequences of climate change are severe and far-reaching: they affect both natural ecosystems and human populations. These consequences underscore the urgent and existential threat posed by climate change,” said the ICJ president, Yuji Iwasawa.</p>
<p>The ICJ was <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/mar/30/un-vote-on-climate-justice-pacific-island-change-crisis-united-nations-vanuatu">instructed to produce this document</a> by the UN in 2023, after years of campaigning <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2022/jun/20/from-vanuatu-law-school-to-the-hague-the-fight-to-recognise-climate-harm-in-international-law">by a group of Pacific island law students</a> and diplomacy led by the Pacific island nation of Vanuatu.</p>
<p>During <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/law/2024/dec/11/international-court-of-justice-icj-forced-to-listen-to-climate-victims">two weeks of hearings</a> in The Hague in December, representatives of vulnerable states told judges that a handful of countries <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2024/dec/02/handful-of-countries-responsible-for-climate-crisis-icj-court-told">should be held legally responsible</a> for the continuing impacts of climate change. Meanwhile, the world’s top greenhouse gas emitters <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2024/dec/04/us-climate-crisis-legal-court">denied any legal responsibility</a> beyond the Paris agreement on climate change.</p>

<p>The ICJ is the third of four top courts to publish an advisory opinion on climate change. Earlier this month, the inter-American court of human rights (IACHR) concluded that there is <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2025/jul/03/countries-must-protect-human-right-to-a-stable-climate-court-rules">a human right to a healthy climate</a>. And both the IACHR and the international tribunal for the law of the sea said states <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/article/2024/may/21/greenhouse-gases-are-pollutants-that-kill-marine-life-court-rules">have a legal responsibility</a> to control greenhouse gases.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the African court on human and people’s rights has only just begun the process.</p>
<p>Of all these, the ICJ is the court with the widest jurisdiction and it has the role of harmonising and integrating international law.</p>
<p>Advisory opinions are technically non-binding but are considered authoritative because they summarise existing law rather than create law. They are expected to be used in future litigation and to bolster political negotiations.</p>

<p>Jotham Napat, the prime minister of <a data-component="auto-linked-tag" data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/vanuatu">Vanuatu</a>, said this was a “turning point in the fight for climate justice”.</p>
<p>“This process has elevated the voices of climate-vulnerable nations, driven global awareness, and set the stage for stronger climate action,” said Napat. “This ruling will give us a foundation to build upon, and this moment will inspire continued efforts for stronger action and accountability, inspiring bold efforts to protect our planet.”</p>
