<p>They came by the thousands.</p>
<p>They dressed in black, with T-shirts featuring crucifixes, dragons and demons.</p>
<p>They gathered on Saturday in Birmingham, England, to pay their respects to a figure of almost religious significance in the heavy metal world: Ozzy Osbourne, the <a href="https://loudwire.com/why-ozzy-osbourne-called-prince-of-darkness/">Prince of Darkness</a>.</p>
<p>Since Osbourne and his bandmates Tony Iommi, Bill Ward and Geezer Butler, formed Black Sabbath in Birmingham in 1968, they have been regarded as the fathers of heavy metal.</p>
<p>On Saturday, Osbourne, 76, was at the center of “Back to the Beginning,” a 10-hour concert at the Villa Park soccer stadium that he had said would culminate in Black Sabbath’s final stage appearance.</p>
