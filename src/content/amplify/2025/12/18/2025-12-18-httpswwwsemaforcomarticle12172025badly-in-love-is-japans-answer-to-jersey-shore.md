<p>A new reality series offers a refreshingly bold remix of the stereotypical Japanese dating show.</p>
<p>Where such programs usually begin with two painfully shy people engaging in clipped, overly formal small talk, <em>Badly in Love</em> features two <em>yankī </em>— self-identifying young delinquents, often sporting dyed blonde hair — nearly coming to blows. The series doesn’t let up from there, offering intense but always sincere character portraits of earnest, if occasionally line-crossing contestants as they contend with trauma, regret, and the tribulations of on-screen romance.</p>
<p><em>Badly in Love</em> “feels like Japan’s answer to MTV’s <em>Jersey Shore</em>,” The Japan Times wrote, “<a href="https://www.japantimes.co.jp/culture/2025/12/16/tv-streaming/badly-in-love/">another series that centered a maligned subculture</a> and dared viewers to look closer.”</p>

<p>EU leaders meet Thursday to decide whether to unlock frozen Russian assets to support Ukraine’s war efforts, a momentous decision with political, legal, and military ramifications.</p>
<p>The bloc holds $246 billion in Russian assets, but it <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/dec/17/arguments-frozen-russian-assets-support-ukraine">has been divided over whether to use the funds</a> as a loan to Kyiv. The effort has taken on greater urgency as Washington has stopped providing new aid to Ukraine.</p>
<p>Failure to unlock the assets means Europe “would not have the money to <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2025/12/17/russia-europe-belgium-frozen-assets-intimidation/">sustain Ukraine economically and militarily</a> into 2026 and on,” the head of an Italian think tank argued. But Belgium, where most of the funds are held, is <a href="https://www.lemonde.fr/en/international/article/2025/12/06/belgium-resists-unfreezing-russian-assets-held-in-brussels_6748200_4.html">especially fearful of blowback</a> from Russia in the form of lawsuits and hybrid warfare tactics.</p>

<p>Chinese scientists have prototyped a machine capable of producing powerful AI chips despite Western efforts to prevent such advances, a Reuters investigation revealed.</p>
<p>The extreme ultraviolet lithography system, which etches tiny circuits onto silicon wafers, was built by former engineers of Dutch semiconductor giant ASML <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/china/how-china-built-its-manhattan-project-rival-west-ai-chips-2025-12-17/">who worked under fake names</a> and used parts from older ASML machines; one prominent China watcher suggested it was a “<a href="https://x.com/niubi/status/2001326094428021157">massive security failure</a>” by Dutch authorities.</p>
<p>The project, which aims to produce working chips by 2028, marks a major breakthrough in Beijing’s quest for semiconductor self-sufficiency, described by some as China’s own Manhattan Project. Investors appear bullish on China’s homegrown processors: Shares in a chip startup <a href="https://asia.nikkei.com/business/markets/ipo/chinese-ai-chip-startup-metax-surges-on-trading-debut?utm_campaign=IC_asia_daily_free&amp;utm_source=NA_newsletter&amp;utm_medium=email&amp;utm_content=article_link&amp;del_type=1&amp;pub_date=202512171230000900&amp;seq_num=9&amp;si=82d09493-5037-4e24-a7a7-3333c7c932e6">soared 693% in its trading debut</a> Wednesday.</p>

<p>Indian investors are hungering for e-commerce and food delivery stocks.</p>
<p>Meesho, a platform offering affordable and unbranded retail products, became India’s <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-12-17/meesho-is-india-s-top-performing-big-ipo-of-2025-after-95-rally">best-performing major IPO of 2025</a> after its debut last week. <p>Bhutan is pledging up to 10,000 bitcoin to build a new mega-city, a reflection of how much national governments have embraced the cryptocurrency.</p>
<p>The small Himalayan kingdom has amassed deep bitcoin reserves over the years by <a href="https://www.wsj.com/world/asia/a-remote-himalayan-kingdom-bet-big-on-bitcoin-mining-so-far-it-has-paid-off-a28bc4b8">converting surplus hydropower into digital tokens</a>. The allocation being put toward Gelephu Mindfulness City is valued at roughly $860 million to $1 billion, and marks one of the world’s <a href="https://bitcoinmagazine.com/featured/bhutan-10000-bitcoin-to-build-mega-city">most ambitious sovereign uses of bitcoin</a> for national infrastructure, Bitcoin Magazine wrote.</p>
<p>The new city is part of Bhutan’s efforts to diversify its economy, but the bitcoin strategy carries risk, as a fall in price could dent the country’s coffers.</p>
