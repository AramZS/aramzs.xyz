<h2 data-gu-name="standfirst">About 56,000 people control three times as much wealth as half of humanity. Here’s one way to illustrate that</h2>
<p>Cruising around on private jets, the ultra-rich are the world’s financial elite – but how many people actually occupy this exclusive wealth club? Could they all fit into a floating mega-yacht, or is the group much bigger, possibly the size of a dazzling mega-rich city?</p>
<p>Thanks to an inequality report out on Wednesday, we now have a snapshot of the size of the topmost layer floating above everyone else – the 0.001%.</p>
<p>The report estimates there are 56,000 of these zero-point-zero-zero-one-ers<em>,</em> and if they gathered for a party, the group would fit into the seats of a medium-sized football stadium, such as Arsenal’s Emirates Stadium or the Stadio Olimpico in Rome.</p>
<figure data-alt="Poland’s National Stadium" data-spacefinder-role="inline" data-testid="interactive-element-Poland%E2%80%99s%20National%20Stadium"></figure>
<p>These wealth hoarders are mostly centi-millionaires (meaning they own hundreds of millions of euros), and the average person in the group has close to €1bn.</p>
<p>But why is it important to visualise this? The report’s authors say the comparisons “will help illustrate just how concentrated the very top” is and serve as a reference to allow people to “grasp the magnitude of global inequality”.</p>
<p>To do that, it is helpful to compare their wealth with everyone else’s. Global inequality is well known, and the report shows how the top 10% own 75% of all global wealth.</p>
<figure data-alt="Bar chart" data-spacefinder-role="inline" data-testid="interactive-element-Bar%20chart"></figure>
<p>But one of the starkest findings of the report is that the ultra-rich 0.001% <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/inequality/2025/dec/10/just-0001-hold-three-times-the-wealth-of-poorest-half-of-humanity-report-finds">control three times the wealth of the poorest half of humanity</a>. Not only that, their wealth is growing at a faster rate – close to 5%, compared with 3.4%.</p>
<p>It is very hard to compare these two sections of society, since the 0.001% is a relatively tiny number of 56,000, when compared with the 2.8 billion people in the bottom 50%. It would take 50,000 stadiums to fit them.</p>
<p>But to give an idea of the disparity, the image below shows how big the bottom half is. Each dot represents one stadium of 56,000 people.</p>
<p>These people collectively own just a third of the wealth of the super-rich who would fit in the stadium above.</p>
