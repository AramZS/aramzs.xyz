<p>Google notified users in an email today that, beginning next month, it will stop sending its dark web reports, an opt-in feature that alerted users when Google detected their personal information on the dark web, as reported by <em><a href="https://9to5google.com/2025/12/15/google-dark-web-report-shutting-down/">9to5Google</a></em>. On January 15th, Google will stop scanning for that data, like contact info and home addresses, and any data it collected so far will no longer be available to users starting on February 16th.</p>
<p>The email sent to users who were signed up for the feature explains, “While the report offered general information, feedback showed that it did not provide helpful next steps. We’re making this change to instead focus on tools that give you more clear, actionable steps to protect your information online.”</p>
<p>Dark web reports were initially launched <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2023/3/8/23629530/google-one-vpn-all-plans-dark-web-report">as a perk for Google One subscribers</a> in 2023, then <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2024/7/9/24194970/google-one-free-dark-web-monitoring">expanded to all Google account users</a> in 2024, when it was also moved to Google’s “results about you” page. The feature was only widely available for a little over a year before this shutdown. Google’s email to users receiving the dark web reports suggests using the <a href="https://c.gle/ANnMTca1mYThW13PfKuJe3il0vApmIbgjjR7N9AzjMZc_-OdRhjmUndxjwFkI4pj5By7JDL30J_xOdEcxndYRatGx7tw2XOIniflADtHS9ihLyZunDuRmScJTQXtAwpTTV_YuLaNggLpyF7YzAYYEA">“results about you”</a> page and Google’s <a href="https://c.gle/ANnMTcYQYWYKOP2RmNW_gzbLK_sUahFCeqZMsBz0NFqnO4g4VfhOkEu57pqSddCAc5xyjwDEe82qmDGeNX70xACnVFfqgT1W4OSz1ZIdeJ1RSjQ3FT13Rwxj4YW3mH61-WR-wH8U9gyL9Ke9mc4">Security Checkup</a> feature instead. Of course, there are also plenty of other services, like <a href="https://haveibeenpwned.com/">Have I Been Pwned</a>, that will monitor the dark web for results containing your data.</p>
