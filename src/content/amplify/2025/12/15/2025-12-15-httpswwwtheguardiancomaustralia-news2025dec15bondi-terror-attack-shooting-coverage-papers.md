<h2 data-gu-name="standfirst">Shooting at world-famous beach in which 15 people, plus one alleged gunman, were killed reverberated around the world, with many headlines noting the actions of a hero bystander </h2>
<p>The <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/dec/15/bondi-beach-terror-attack-16-people-including-one-child-died-in-antisemitic-shooting">deadly terrorist attack</a> at Sydney’s Bondi beach, and the heroic acts of bystanders in response to it, were the focus on newspaper front pages across the world on Monday morning, as Australia came to terms with what the prime minister, Anthony Albanese, described as “an act of evil antisemitism”.</p>
<p>The <strong>Guardian</strong> led with “At least 16 dead in terror attack on Jewish festival” accompanied by a picture of ambulance crews assisting a shooting victim. In the top left corner, there is a reference to the <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/dec/14/bystander-tackles-and-wrestles-gun-from-alleged-gunmen-during-bondi-beach-mass-shooting">bystander who wrestled a firearm off one of the alleged gunmen.</a></p>

<p>In the US, the <strong>New York Times</strong> took in reaction from<a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/14/world/australia/hanukkah-shooting-bondi-reaction.html"> US-based rabbis</a> reflecting on how to protect their congregations. Its lead opinion piece questioned <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/14/opinion/bondi-beach-shooting.html">whether more could have been done</a> to protect Australia’s Jewish community. The <strong>Washington Post</strong> ran a piece headlined “Hundreds gathered on Bondi Beach for Hanukkah. Then it turned into <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2025/12/14/australia-bondi-beach-shooting-antisemitism/">a night of terror.</a>” Its lead opinion piece looked at al-Ahmed’s actions, saying <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/2025/12/14/bondi-beach-attack-hero-antisemitism/">“an act of courage exposes cowardice”</a>. The <strong><a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.latimes.com/world-nation/story/2025-12-14/gunmen-kill-at-least-11-people-in-attack-on-jewish-holiday-event-on-sydneys-bondi-beach">LA Times</a></strong> said the shooting followed a wave of antisemitic attacks that had “roiled the country over the last year”.</p>
<p>In Australia, <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.smh.com.au/">the </a><strong><a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.smh.com.au/">Sydney Morning Herald</a></strong> headlined their front page with “Bondi Terror” against a large image of a police officer and a woman in swimwear attending to victims, with the beach and abandoned festival behind them.</p>
