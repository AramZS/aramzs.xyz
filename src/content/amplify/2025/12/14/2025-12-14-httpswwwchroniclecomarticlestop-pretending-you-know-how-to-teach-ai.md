<p>Earlier this year Ohio State University announced an <a data-cms-ai="0" href="https://oaa.osu.edu/ai-fluency">AI Fluency </a>program, promising that, starting with the Class of 2029, every student who graduates from the university will be “fluent in their field of study, and fluent in the application of AI in that field.” Faculty members across the university have been asked to identify applications for generative AI across the disciplines. I thought immediately of the classics: What kind of AI-based task could help students — and perhaps university administrators — investigate Socrates’ paradox from Plato’s Apology: “I neither know nor think I know”?</p>
