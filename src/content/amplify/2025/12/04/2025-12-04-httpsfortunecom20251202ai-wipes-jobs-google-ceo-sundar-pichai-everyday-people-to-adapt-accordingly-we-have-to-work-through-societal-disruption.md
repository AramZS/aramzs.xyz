<p>“AI is the most profound technology humanity is ever working on, and it has potential for extraordinary benefits, and we will have to work through societal disruption,” Pichai <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYx63PKKPvg">told the BBC</a> in a recent interview.</p>
<p>The chief executive has a front-row seat to how AI will shake up the world; last month, <a href="https://fortune.com/company/alphabet/">Google</a> rolled out its latest model, Gemini 3, and received critical praise. The innovation—seen as an improvement on Gemini 2.5, released around eight months ago—<a href="https://fortune.com/2025/11/21/google-cofounder-larry-page-amazon-jeff-bezos-billionaires-list-alphabet-stock-surge-ai-boom/">ignited optimism</a> among investors and analysts, who heralded the chatbot as their “favorite model generally available today.” As the technology continues to advance, Pichai emphasized that it will create new opportunities, while also admitting that some roles will be phased out.</p>
<p>“It will evolve and transition certain jobs,” Pichai continued. “People will need to adapt, and then there will be areas where it will impact some jobs. So, as a society, I think we need to be having those conversations.”</p>
<p>People may believe only certain entry-level roles are being automated, like <a href="https://fortune.com/article/customer-support-ai-cursor-went-rogue/">customer service representatives</a> or <a href="https://fortune.com/2025/06/02/junior-analysts-wall-street-jobs-taken-by-ai/">junior analysts</a>, but Pichai is adamant all roles will be touched by the tech. He even said his own job as CEO is “one of the easier things” for AI to take over one day. There is no industry or high-powered role disconnected from the age of AI—but those who embrace the tools will be the ones who succeed.</p>
<p>“I think people who learn to adopt and adapt to AI will do better,” Pichai continued. “It doesn’t matter whether you want to be a teacher, a doctor—all those professions will be around, but the people who will do well in each of those professions are people who learn how to use these tools.”</p>
<h2>AI is wiping out jobs—but Pichai says kids shouldn’t change course</h2>
<p>Young, up-and-coming professionals may hear Pichai’s prediction and question if they chose the right career path. After all, they’re stepping out of college and into an uncertain labor market.</p>
<p>Job postings across the U.S. <a href="https://fortune.com/2025/11/03/jobs-openings-plunge-thanks-to-ai-gen-z-taking-35k-healthcare-jobs-stanford-report-unemployment-career-advice/">have fallen by</a> about 32% since ChatGPT hit the market, according to data from the Federal Reserve, as businesses deploy AI tools to boost efficiency. The percentage of Gen Z staffers at large public tech companies <a href="https://fortune.com/2025/09/07/silicon-valley-gen-z-tech-industry-jobs-dissappearing-millennials-ai-automation-careers-older-workforce/">has been cut in half</a> within the past two years; once-lucrative career paths, like computer programming, <a href="https://fortune.com/2025/03/17/computer-programming-jobs-lowest-1980-ai/">have hit employment lows</a>; and now, <a href="https://fortune.com/2025/10/31/neo-robot-automation-housekeeper-1x-technologies-humanoid-remote-operator-is-here-blue-collar-jobs-artifical-intelligence/">humanoid robots</a> are even <a href="https://fortune.com/2025/11/25/amazon-layoffs-artificial-intelligence-robots-unemployment-automation/">being designed to take</a> on physical work.</p>
<p>It’s a dire situation that’s forced many aspiring Gen Zers to reconsider if costly college degrees are worth the dwindling payout, or even switch to seemingly more AI-proof blue-collar careers. However, the Google CEO asserted that there is no golden-ticket college major or profession that is guaranteed to be safeguarded from AI; people should pursue whatever career they want, regardless of how the tech changes the job landscape.</p>
<p>“Based on what I see, I wouldn’t change anything of how we’ve always thought,” Pichai said in reference to how parents should advise their kids. “I think there’s going to be a wide variety of disciplines which will end up mattering. I would encourage the next generation to embrace the technology—learn to use it in the context of what you do.”</p>
