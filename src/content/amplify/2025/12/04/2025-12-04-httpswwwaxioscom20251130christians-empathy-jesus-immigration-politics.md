<p>As the U.S. grows more diverse, a quiet civil war is unfolding within <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2024/10/07/christian-nationalism-opponents-trump" data-vars-content-id="cebe2ffc-ec02-43f6-b422-f283d1da217d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Empathy is the new Christian battleground" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="American Christianity" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2024/10/07/christian-nationalism-opponents-trump">American Christianity</a> over who deserves empathy.</p>

<ul><li>They are working to drive out school lessons on empathy and argue in books and sermons that empathy is for the weak or "woke." </li><li>Others say empathy is central to Christianity and the teachings of Jesus.</li><li>This split comes as <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/02/26/us-christianity-decline-pew-study" data-vars-content-id="cebe2ffc-ec02-43f6-b422-f283d1da217d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Empathy is the new Christian battleground" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Christianity and organized religion" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/02/26/us-christianity-decline-pew-study">Christianity and organized religion</a> are shrinking and the U.S. undergoes a profound demographic transformation: no single racial group will hold a majority within two decades.</li></ul>

<p><strong>The big picture: </strong>Around 80% of U.S. adults said empathy was a moral value that underpinned a healthy society, according to the wide-ranging survey by the nonpartisan <a data-vars-click-url="https://prri.org/research/trumps-unprecedented-actions-deepen-asymmetric-divides/#:~:text=Most%20Americans%20agree%20that%20empathy%20is%20a%20moral%20value%20that%20is%20a%20foundation%20of%20a%20healthy%20society%2C%20rather%20than%20a%20dangerous%20emotion%20that%20undermines%20society.%C2%A0" data-vars-content-id="cebe2ffc-ec02-43f6-b422-f283d1da217d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Empathy is the new Christian battleground" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Public Religion Research Institute (PRRI)" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://prri.org/research/trumps-unprecedented-actions-deepen-asymmetric-divides/#:~:text=Most%20Americans%20agree%20that%20empathy%20is%20a%20moral%20value%20that%20is%20a%20foundation%20of%20a%20healthy%20society%2C%20rather%20than%20a%20dangerous%20emotion%20that%20undermines%20society.%C2%A0">Public Religion Research Institute (PRRI)</a> in October.</p>
<ul><li>Only 16% said empathy was a dangerous emotion that undermines "our ability to set up a society that is guided by God's truth," the poll found.</li></ul>
<p><strong>The other side: </strong>"In the Christian tradition, to have anybody argue that a spirit of empathy is somehow a vulnerability... is insane," Father Brendan Busse, pastor of Dolores Mission Catholic Church in Los Angeles, tells Axios.</p>
<ul><li>Busse said the suffering of Jesus on the Cross invokes empathy, as does the call to help "the least of these" in the Gospels. </li></ul>
<ul><li>Empathy drives the mission of <a data-vars-click-url="https://homeboyindustries.org/" data-vars-content-id="cebe2ffc-ec02-43f6-b422-f283d1da217d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Empathy is the new Christian battleground" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Homeboy Industries," data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://homeboyindustries.org/">Homeboy Industries,</a> the Los Angeles-based (and world's largest) gang intervention organization, founded by Catholic priest Greg Boyle.</li><li>The North Carolina-based <a data-vars-click-url="https://breachrepairers.org/" data-vars-content-id="cebe2ffc-ec02-43f6-b422-f283d1da217d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Empathy is the new Christian battleground" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Repairers of the Breach" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://breachrepairers.org/">Repairers of the Breach</a>, founded by Bishop William J. Barber II, uses empathy to fight poverty and economic inequality. </li></ul>
<p><strong>Between the lines</strong>: Between 2021 and early 2023, legislators in <a data-vars-click-url="https://truthout.org/articles/the-right-is-passing-bills-that-ban-the-teaching-of-empathy-and-care-in-schools/" data-vars-content-id="cebe2ffc-ec02-43f6-b422-f283d1da217d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Empathy is the new Christian battleground" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="at least 25 states" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://truthout.org/articles/the-right-is-passing-bills-that-ban-the-teaching-of-empathy-and-care-in-schools/">at least 25 states</a> introduced bills backed by conservatives intended to ban or restrict social-emotional learning (SEL) in K-12 schools.</p>
<ul><li>Social-emotional learning is an educational strategy that teaches empathy, cooperation and social skills.</li><li>All SEL bans have failed to pass following opposition from educators and moderate Christians.</li></ul>
<p><strong>The bottom line: </strong>The Bible gives conflicting messages on empathy, said Dan McClellan, a Biblical scholar and author of "<a data-vars-click-url="https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-bible-says-so-what-we-get-right-and-wrong-about-scripture-s-most-controversial-issues-dan-mcclellan/540f4b8790029211?ean=9781250347466&amp;next=t" data-vars-content-id="cebe2ffc-ec02-43f6-b422-f283d1da217d" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Empathy is the new Christian battleground" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="The Bible Says So:" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-bible-says-so-what-we-get-right-and-wrong-about-scripture-s-most-controversial-issues-dan-mcclellan/540f4b8790029211?ean=9781250347466&amp;next=t">The Bible Says So:</a> What We Get Right (and Wrong) About Scripture's Most Controversial Issues."</p>
<ul><li>On the one hand, McClellan said some stories in scripture speak of empathy only for your ethnic group and disregard others, like Joshua's passages about the walls of Jericho and the killing of innocent children.</li><li>"Then there's a universal empathy, where your empathy extends beyond your own group," McClellan said, pointing to the Beatitudes and Jesus' parable about the Good Samaritan.</li></ul>
