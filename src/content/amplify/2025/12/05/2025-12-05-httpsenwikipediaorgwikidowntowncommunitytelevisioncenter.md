<p>The <b>Downtown Community Television Center</b> or <b>DCTV</b> is a community media center located in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firehouse,_Engine_Company_31">the former Engine Company 31 firehouse</a> on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lafayette_Street_(Manhattan)">Lafayette Street</a> in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civic_Center,_Manhattan">Civic Center</a> of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, New York City. It was founded in 1972 by spouses <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Documentary_film">documentary</a> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Film_director">film director</a> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jon_Alpert">Jon Alpert</a> and Keiko Tsuno.</p>
<h2>Mission</h2>
<p>According to their website,<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Downtown_Community_Television_Center/#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> DCTV "introduc[es] members of the community to the basics of electronic media through hundreds of free or low-cost production courses and access to broadcast-quality production equipment." DCTV conducts classes enabling people from less privileged backgrounds to learn to create video productions and operates studios available to them for low cost. These programs are funded in part by earnings from DCTV's own documentary films which have won 16 national <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmy">Emmy</a> awards and many other honors.</p>
<h2>Facilities</h2>
<p>DCTV is based in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firehouse,_Engine_Company_31">Firehouse, Engine Company 31</a>, a landmarked firehouse at 87 Lafayette Street in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, constructed in 1895 and purchased by DCTV in the 1980s.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Downtown_Community_Television_Center/#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It offers unique and versatile multi-use venue rentals.</p>
<h2>Firehouse Cinema</h2>
<p>In 2022, DCTV opened Firehouse Cinema as a documentary-only, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Art_film">art house</a> movie theater.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Downtown_Community_Television_Center/#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The cinema screens first-run films as well as specialty programming throughout the year.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Downtown_Community_Television_Center/#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Dara Messinger serves as the director of programming.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Downtown_Community_Television_Center/#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2>Programs</h2>
<p>By providing professional training and tools, access to critical equipment and public exhibition space in Manhattan, DCTV gives youth, aspiring and emerging filmmakers and communities of all backgrounds greater voice and platforms through documentary film.</p>
<p>PRO-TV (Professional Youth Media Training) is a program designed to train students from New York City's underprivileged communities in media arts, using state-of-the-art equipment.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Downtown_Community_Television_Center/#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
