<p>Industry insiders are gearing up for weight-loss drugs to shake up the consumer economy as they seep into every sector — including apparel, restaurants, grocery, gyms and travel. They are causing a “psychological shift” for the people taking them, said Ali Furman, a partner in PwC’s U.S. consumer markets division. As their bodies change, she said, so are their decisions on how to spend their time and money.</p>
<p>In just over a year, the percentage of U.S. adults taking drugs such as Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro and Zepbound <a href="https://news.gallup.com/poll/696599/obesity-rate-declining.aspx?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">more than doubled</a> to 12.4 percent, according to Gallup. The survey also reported that the obesity rate fell from almost 40 percent in 2022 to 37 percent in 2025. Some companies are already responding by acquiring health food brands, renovating hotel gyms and changing lunch menus. But that’s only scratching the surface, said Diana Melencio, a partner at XRC Ventures, an early-stage venture capital firm.</p>
<p>“It's revolutionary,” she said. “GLP-1s will play a huge role in society.”</p>
<p>Take a ride with us to see how these drugs are reshaping the consumer economy and what companies are doing to adapt.</p>

<h2 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="1DNYJ21Z6149DBYQ2C4WJ1YERG" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Pharmacies</h2>
<p>The drugs come with some uncomfortable or harmful side effects, including indigestion, nausea and loss of hair and muscle mass. As a result, sales for supplements and vitamin-rich beauty products have surged. <a href="https://health.clevelandclinic.org/ozempic-babies?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">Some studies</a> suggest that the medication might also boost fertility. Purchases of pregnancy test kits among a cohort of GLP-1 users surged 148 percent over the course of one year, according to NielsenIQ (NIQ).</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="05KUTEDVVD76NFW7YCM9KF883R" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Supplements</h3>
<p>Electrolyte supplements, hair-growth products and anti-nausea medications have skyrocketed among GLP-1 users. Some brands are marketing new product lines aimed at consumers who take the medications, and industry experts expect more companies to jump in. There are risks, however, of some supplement companies making <a href="https://www.classaction.org/media/robins-v-lemme-inc.pdf?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">misleading claims</a> of replicating GLP-1 shots. “The U.S. has pretty loose standards when it comes to the vitamins, minerals and supplements sector,” Melencio said.</p>
<h2 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="0RZHKADN5N2GH22W0GM4CW8JKW" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Grocery stores</h2>
<p>With the elimination of “food noise,” which can lead to bingeing and distracting thoughts about eating, GLP-1 users are cutting back on snacks and spending more on healthy items.</p>
<p>Compared with non-GLP-1 households, GLP-1 users reduced their spending by 10 percent over a year across 100 categories including groceries, quick-service restaurants and tobacco, according to the data company Numerator. While the fallout could hurt the snack food industry, some companies are innovating and acquiring health food brands. They’re focused on high-protein items, which support muscle mass, are highly satiating and boost metabolism, as well as quick and healthy frozen meals.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="2VZYVAXTBN7H56FTZAXWAJ7RE4" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">More fresh produce</h3>
<p>GLP-1 users have reported craving a crunchy carrot and tart kiwi over a salty chip. Over a 13-week period last year, they increased their spending on fresh fruit by 14 percent and on vegetables by 38 percent compared with the year before, according to NIQ.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="M88GRH62TH4GD0KMR9CPBZK8R0" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Healthy snacks</h3>
<p>Sales of chips, baked goods and packaged cookies per consumer fell an average of 6.7 to 11 percent in their first six months on the medication, according to a Numerator and Cornell University <a href="https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5073929&amp;itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">report</a>. To offset these losses, packaged-goods brands are developing food and beverage products, such as protein-packed frozen meals and marketing them as “GLP-1 friendly.” Food labs are also testing products that help counteract the drugs’ side effects by including extra protein, fiber, vitamins or antioxidants.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="FYR47K7HT11XBF8Z0CCWMH5NF8" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Nonalcoholic beverages</h3>
<p>Studies show that GLP-1 drugs can suppress alcohol cravings among heavy drinkers. Users spent 14.5 percent less in the category after starting the treatment, while nonalcoholic wine and beer purchases among the group ballooned by 1,158 and 935 percent, respectively, according to NIQ. Meanwhile, high-protein drinks and probiotic soda brands that promote gut health — which are also popular among non-GLP-1 users — are seeing significant growth.</p>
<h2 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="XMB805JQ1X2FV7VUQAT2X42N3R" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Strip malls</h2>
<p>Weight-loss drugs create an <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2024/09/08/weight-loss-drugs-retail-food/?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">opportunity for clothing brands</a> as consumers refresh their wardrobe while they shed pounds. GLP-1 users are also gaining confidence and boosting their spending on beauty products and new clothes to go out in, said Kelly Pederson, a partner in global retail leadership at PwC.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="ERYNG2MTHN0D1092U8C2R9RXAC" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Smaller sizes</h3>
<p>Sales are up for smaller-size clothing for men and women, according to a recent <a href="https://www.impactanalytics.co/e-books-and-reports/glp1-size-curve-report-2025?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">study</a> from Impact Analytics. Demand for women’s tops in extra small and small sizes rose two percentage points from 2022 to 2024 and were down two percentage points for large and extra large sizes. Analysts say it will probably be more difficult to find larger sizes in stores, with retailers shifting most of that inventory to online only.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="UJMU7V03FD4UD9BZ54JNDC05QM" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Formalwear and sporting goods</h3>
<p>Meanwhile, formalwear sales surged 80 percent and sporting goods jumped 24 percent in the first six months of 2025 compared with the same period last year, according to credit and debit card data from Consumer Edge. This “could indicate a need [or] desire to buy new clothing and accessories after undergoing a positive life change,” said Michael Gunther, Consumer Edge’s head of insights.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="3P75N39J3N7GK37NUE14C1DNXC" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Thrift</h3>
<p>There are also more affordable and eco-friendly ways to spend on apparel. Consignment and thrift shop spending among GLP-1 users surged 80 percent, according to Consumer Edge, as consumers look to save as they go down sizes. Meanwhile, resale outlets are seeing an uptick in larger sizes for donations and consigning.</p>
<h2 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="CDDJ714JKX32K9PC2RMNR35QY8" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Fast food and restaurants</h2>
<p>Grocery data indicates that consumers taking GLP-1s are spending more time cooking at home instead of dining out. In the six months after starting the medications, users reduced their spending on dining out by 8.6 percent, according to Numerator and Cornell University.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="JQ8YJBPB491H11JV0BM5EPAVAC" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Healthier options</h3>
<p>Weight-loss drug users are changing how they visit fast-food restaurants, both at late-night drive-throughs and in the morning. In addition, fast-food restaurants saw a slight decline in breakfast sales, according to Leo Feler, chief economist at Numerator, while dinner sales saw a 6 percent decline. GLP-1 users are also forgoing pizza in favor of garden salads and chicken entrées, Feler said.</p>
<p>While it’s unclear how vast the effect will be on the restaurant industry, some chains are introducing GLP-1 menu options that advertise high protein and are marketing healthier options. Some chains are experimenting with snack boards and smaller portions at a reduced price.</p>
<h2 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="HYTM42GMW95VN7TMKCYEYKYJKR" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Gyms</h2>
<p>With the risk of losing muscle mass, GLP-1 users are encouraged to exercise and strength train. They’re also spending on items linked to a more active lifestyle, like wearable electronics, which saw sales rise 29 percent over six months among the group, according to Consumer Edge.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="XKVFZAJ0A56JX7PT7YC149C9N4" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Training programs</h3>
<p>Gyms have been gearing up to meet the moment, with some tailoring programs for members taking the medications. Equinox has a “GLP-1 protocol,” with dedicated lifestyle and training coaches, while Planet Fitness has curated workout guides on its website. Industry experts say this trend will grow, with more gyms advertising their services to members on the drugs or looking to transition off them and maintain their weight loss.</p>
<h3 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="X251BCQYXX1MNBQV8KPMWJTYR4" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Clinics</h3>
<p>Some gyms are opening health clinics, creating easier access for members to get a prescription. Life Time, a health club chain, has opened clinics that include personal trainers and doctors who can prescribe the medications.</p>
<h2 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="PF709VXC1N0J7D6BGVQ5489JCR" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Travel</h2>
<p>With extra energy and confidence, GLP-1 users may invest more in adventures outside the home. <a href="https://www.pwc.com/us/en/services/consulting/business-model-reinvention/glp-1-trends-and-impact-on-business-models.html?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">PwC research</a> observed a 15 percent increase in GLP-1 users who reported having a more positive body image. This, analysts believe, could eventually increase spending in entertainment areas, such as theaters, live sporting events, concerts and amusement parks. The travel industry could also get a boost, with people feeling more comfortable on airplanes and more enthusiastic about trips involving walking and hiking. Wellness tourism is also on the rise.</p>
<p>PwC’s Furman is telling clients to get ahead of the trend and market health-focused vacation packages and fitness amenities. GLP-1 users are more open to taking active vacations rather than sitting by the pool or ocean. There’s less emphasis on juice cleanses and more on workout classes and healthy food options. Some hotels have begun upgrading their gyms, she said. On the flip side, casinos could see business decline because the drug may reduce the urge to <a href="https://www.bu.edu/articles/2025/diabetes-drugs-also-curb-addictions/?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">gamble</a>.</p>
<h2 data-component="Heading" data-contentid="ZG5UXZDKGD3XFA7YCZ9MFP2GK0" data-file="custom-template-components/src/Heading/Heading.js" data-style="stitches">Complications</h2>
<p>While the potential for these drugs to become truly mainstream is massive, there’s still a barrier to entry. The cost of the drugs is prohibitive for many Americans, reaching as much as $6,000 a year even at a discounted rate. Those with Type 2 diabetes, obesity, heart disease, sleep apnea and high blood pressure and cholesterol can qualify for insurance coverage. But some have reported battling their insurers to get a reasonable price.</p>
<p>Some Americans could get a reprieve on prices. Last month, the White House <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/health/2025/11/06/ozempic-wegovy-trump-price-medicare-deal/?itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">announced</a> a deal with the drugmakers that will lower the price of weight-loss drugs for some patients covered by Medicare and Medicaid.</p>
<p>Methodology</p>
<p>The figures cited are based on several surveys that collect consumer spending data. Numerator’s figures tracked GLP-1 households’ year-over-year purchases from July 2024 through July 2025, compared with non-GLP-1 households. Consumer Edge collected transaction data over the first six months of 2025 from those who made credit or debit card payments to either Lilly Direct or Brello Health and compared their data with the overall population. The NIQ report cites data from a year ending Sept. 28, 2024. The Numerator and Cornell University <a href="https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5073929&amp;itid=lk_inline_enhanced-template">report</a> tracks spending from July 2022 to September 2024 as well as patterns during the first six months after people started the drug.</p>
