<p>The U.S. is undergoing its fastest religious shift in modern history, marked by a rapid increase in the <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/11/13/gallup-us-religion-plunge-shift-global-declines" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="religiously unaffiliated" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/11/13/gallup-us-religion-plunge-shift-global-declines">religiously unaffiliated</a> and numerous <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/10/03/us-churches-close-religious-shift-christians" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="church closures" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/10/03/us-churches-close-religious-shift-christians">church closures</a> nationwide.</p>
<p><strong>Why it matters: </strong>The great unchurching of America comes as identity and reality are increasingly shaped by non-institutional spiritual sources — YouTube mystics, <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.boshemiamagazine.com/blog/tik-tok-tarot-jennifer-cartwright" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="TikTok tarot" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.boshemiamagazine.com/blog/tik-tok-tarot-jennifer-cartwright">TikTok tarot</a>, <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.youtube.com/@poetryofreality" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="digital skeptics" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.youtube.com/@poetryofreality">digital skeptics</a>, folk saints and <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/11/12/christian-ai-chatbot-jesus-god-satan-churches" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="AI-generated prayer bots" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/11/12/christian-ai-chatbot-jesus-god-satan-churches">AI-generated prayer bots</a>.</p>
<ul><li>It's a tectonic transformation that has profound implications for race, civic identity, political persuasion and the ability to govern a fracturing moral landscape.</li></ul>
<p><strong>By the numbers: </strong>Nearly three in 10 American adults today identify as religiously unaffiliated — a 33% jump since 2013, according to the nonpartisan <a data-vars-click-url="https://prri.org/spotlight/2024-prri-census-of-american-religion/" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Public Religion Research Institute (PRRI)" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://prri.org/spotlight/2024-prri-census-of-american-religion/">Public Religion Research Institute (PRRI)</a>.</p>
<ul><li>That's quicker than almost any major religious shift in modern U.S. history, and it's happening across racial groups, an Axios analysis found. </li><li>About four in ten Americans ages 18 to 29 identify as religiously unaffiliated (38%), an increase from 32% in 2013, PRRI said.</li></ul>
<p><strong>Driving the news:</strong> The religious shift has made it harder for political parties and candidates to reach voters, Sisto Abeyta, a Democratic political consultant based in New Mexico with the Nevada-based firm <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.tri-strategies.com/" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="TriStrategies" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.tri-strategies.com/">TriStrategies</a>, tells Axios.</p>
<ul><li>"We have to find (religiously unaffiliated voters), engage them and answer their skeptical questions, rather than just go to a church and pass out campaign literature," Abeyta said. "And they're growing in numbers."</li><li>It costs campaigns about $1.40 to reach out to a single religiously unaffiliated voter, compared to $ 0.45 per faith-based voter, he said.</li></ul>
<p><strong>Zoom in: </strong>Around 57% of Americans today seldom or never attend religious services, a jump from 40% in 2000, per a recent <a data-vars-click-url="https://news.gallup.com/poll/1690/Religion.aspx" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Gallup survey" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://news.gallup.com/poll/1690/Religion.aspx">Gallup survey</a>.</p>
<ul><li>An unprecedented 15,000 <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/10/03/us-churches-close-religious-shift-christians" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="churches" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/10/03/us-churches-close-religious-shift-christians">churches</a> are expected to shut their doors this year, far more than the few thousand expected to open, according to denominational reports and church consultants.</li><li><a data-vars-click-url="https://nickblevins.com/blog/2020/09/09/church-retention-whats-a-healthy-retention-percentage/" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Leading experts say" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://nickblevins.com/blog/2020/09/09/church-retention-whats-a-healthy-retention-percentage/">Leading experts say</a> that — before the pandemic — a church should expect to lose about 15% of its members year over year — a rate of 1.2 million leaving churches annually nationally. That number has likely accelerated since then.</li></ul>
<p><strong>Between the lines: </strong>The racial makeup of Christians within each party is vastly different, and that is shaping the influence of faith voters on Democrats' and Republicans' platforms.</p>
<ul><li>Within the Republican party, the largest group is white Christians (68%) and only 12% identify as religiously unaffiliated, per PRRI.</li><li>Among Democrats, Christians of color (35%) and religiously unaffiliated (34%) make up the bulk of the party.</li></ul>
<p><strong>Zoom out: </strong>The shift in religious activity also is leaving behind a trail of "church graveyards," or empty buildings that are now difficult to sell or have been abandoned.</p>
<ul><li>These churches once served as community gathering places for Alcoholics Anonymous meetings, voting precincts, or town halls, leaving a void.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Megachurches show signs of stability but not enough to reverse overall declines.</li></ul>

<p><strong>Reality check: </strong>There is no widespread academic or mainstream evidence of a large-scale national religious awakening, PRRI CEO Melissa Deckman tells Axios.</p>
<ul><li>Despite anecdotal and <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2025/12/09/young-adults-religion-campuses-kirk/" data-vars-content-id="6ccd9efe-1116-4f33-80c3-c4b5f3b533b2" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="The great unchurching of America" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="media reports" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2025/12/09/young-adults-religion-campuses-kirk/">media reports</a> about Gen Z men returning to church, there's little evidence it's happening beyond scattered examples to reverse the overall decline, she said.</li></ul>
<p><strong>The bottom line: </strong>The old religious map is disappearing.</p>
<ul><li>The new one — digital, more secular, decentralized and deeply fragmented — is only beginning to take shape.</li></ul>
