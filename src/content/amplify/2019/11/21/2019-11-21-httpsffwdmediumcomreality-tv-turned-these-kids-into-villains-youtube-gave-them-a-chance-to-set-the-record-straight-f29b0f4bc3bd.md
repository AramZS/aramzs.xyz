<h2 data-selectable-paragraph=""><em>Portrayed as brats on ‘structured reality’ shows, former reality TV kids are changing the narrative on YouTube</em></h2>
<p>In January 2012, two million Americans watched 14-year-old Payton Ackerman turn into a villain. Looking cherubic with her sleek brunette hair securely scraped back and adorned with a white bow, in an instant Payton’s bright red lips formed into a grimace as she <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-YMzicqAHmQ">argued with her dance teacher</a>, Abby Lee Miller, on Lifetime’s <em>Dance Moms</em>.</p>
<p>“I thought I was better than most of the people,” the teenager declared, seemingly insulting her fellow stars on the reality TV show, which follows young dancers as they compete for prizes. The audience turned on the teen — and they didn’t forget. “I really hate Payton,” reads <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YVkusi_G8P4">the top comment</a> on a clip of the dancer uploaded to YouTube six years after the episode was first broadcast.</p>
