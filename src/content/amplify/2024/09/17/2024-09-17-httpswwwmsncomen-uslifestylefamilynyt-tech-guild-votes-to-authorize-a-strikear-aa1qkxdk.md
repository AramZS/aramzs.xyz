<figure><img data-document-id="cms/api/amp/image/AA1qkOyS" data-reference="image" src="https://img-s-msn-com.akamaized.net/tenant/amp/entityid/AA1qkOyS.img"/><figcaption>NYT Tech Guild votes to authorize a strike</figcaption></figure><p>The <a href="https://www.axios.com/2022/03/04/nyt-tech-workers-vote-unionize?utm_medium=partner&amp;utm_source=microsoft-start&amp;utm_content=link&amp;utm_campaign=subs-partner-msfot-dailyessentials">New York Times Tech Guild</a>, which represents more than 600 staffers, on Tuesday voted to authorize a strike in protest of stalled contract negotiations with The Times' management, sources confirmed to Axios. </p><p><strong>Why it matters</strong>: The guild, which was formed in 2022, has yet to secure a contract after more than two years of bargaining. </p><p><strong>Driving the news</strong>: Of the union's 622 workers, 89% participated in the strike authorization vote Tuesday and an overwhelming majority supported the vote to strike.</p><p><strong>Between the lines</strong>: It's unclear when the guild plans to strike, but with the elections coming up, any time in the near future would be problematic for The Times. </p><ul><li>The Wirecutter union held a <a href="https://www.wirecutterunion.com/">five-day walkout</a> over the Black Friday holiday weekend in 2021 and <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2021/12/14/business/media/wirecutter-union-contract.html">struck a deal</a> with management days later. (It <a href="https://www.nyguild.org/post/unionized-wirecutter-editorial-workers-win-new-contract-9610#:~:text=A%20new%20wage%20floor%20of,10%20continuous%20years%20of%20employment">re-signed</a> another three-year contract earlier this year.) </li><li>The Times' main editorial union held a one-day <a href="https://www.axios.com/2022/12/08/new-york-times-staffers-walkout-union-talks-fail?utm_medium=partner&amp;utm_source=microsoft-start&amp;utm_content=link&amp;utm_campaign=subs-partner-msfot-dailyessentials">walkout</a> in December 2022 and struck a contract deal after more than two years of bargaining in May 2023. </li></ul><p><strong>Catch up quick</strong>: Tech workers voted <a href="https://nytimesguild.org/tech-vote-count">in favor of unionizing</a> with the NewsGuild in early 2022. </p><ul><li>Kathy Zhang, a senior analytics manager with The Times and the guild's unit chair, said, "Management has really dragged its feet when it comes to bargaining," between the union formation and now.</li><li>A spokesperson for The New York Times did not comment. </li></ul><p><strong>The big picture</strong>: The tech guild represents the largest union of tech workers with bargaining rights in the country, per the NewsGuild. </p><ul><li>As such, union organizers are looking to set a precedent for other tech unions via its contract negotiations with The Times. </li><li>Beyond fighting for wages, it's also bargaining for various workplace policies, including <a href="https://www.axios.com/2023/10/30/nyt-tech-workers-walk-out?utm_medium=partner&amp;utm_source=microsoft-start&amp;utm_content=link&amp;utm_campaign=subs-partner-msfot-dailyessentials">remote work rights</a>, and unique just cause protections.</li></ul><div><p><i><a href="https://www.axios.com/signup/am-pm?utm_source=microsoft-start&amp;utm_medium=partner&amp;utm_campaign=subs-partner-msoft-dailyessentials">Get the rundown of the biggest stories of the day with Axios Daily Essentials.</a></i></p></div>
