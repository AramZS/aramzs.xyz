<p>The owner of the shuttered Three Mile Island nuclear plant in Pennsylvania will invest $1.6 billion to revive it, agreeing to sell all the output to Microsoft Corp. as the tech titan seeks carbon-free electricity for data centers to power the artificial intelligence boom.</p>
<p>Constellation Energy Corp., the biggest US operator of reactors, expects Three Mile Island to go back into service in 2028, according to a statement Friday. While one of the site’s two units permanently closed almost a half-century ago after the <a data-component="link" href="https://www.nrc.gov/reading-rm/doc-collections/fact-sheets/3mile-isle.html">worst US nuclear accident</a>, Constellation is planning to reopen the other reactor, which <a data-component="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2019-08-15/nuclear-plant-where-u-s-flirted-with-its-own-chernobyl-to-close">shut in 2019</a> because it couldn’t compete economically.</p>
