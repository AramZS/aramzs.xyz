<p>New Jersey and New York City commuters are facing extensive delays in and out of New York Penn Station Friday, with intensifying ripple effects, after an Amtrak work train car on one of the hub's tracks caught fire.</p>
<p>The FDNY says it was called to the Midtown scene on 31st Street, between Seventh and Eighth avenues, around 1:30 a.m. More than 100 personnel responded. Five transit workers reported injuries, two of whom were taken to a hospital. The others were evaluated at the scene.</p>
<p>Although, NJ Transit service in and out of NY Penn Station has resumed shortly after noon Friday, it is operating with up to 60-minute delays. NJ Transit warns that cancellations should be expected.</p>
<p>Cross-honoring and diversion programs were in effect as the situation developed.</p>

<p>MTA officials say it appears two work trains collided, impacting the electrical system and igniting the fire in one of the Hudson River Tunnels. It was knocked down well before 6 a.m., but impacts crippled service through the first half of the day. Amtrak said it hoped to lift its suspension until noon.</p>
<p>Limited service was operating between Washington (WAS) and New York (NYP) and New York (NYP) and Boston (BOS) shortly before then.</p>
<p>The Long Island Rail Road was suspended at NY Penn Station for a time as well, but full service resumed by around 7 a.m., Friday. Equipment issues were blamed for some ongoing cancellations.</p>
<p>Video from outside Penn Station showed smoke billowing in the pre-dawn hours, as emergency personnel stood by with stretchers awaiting any potential victims.</p>
<p>Amtrak is investigating the cause of the fire.</p>
<p>"We sincerely apologize for the inconvenience this may cause," the agency has said.</p>
<p>An Amtrak worker was later seen telling an astronomically long line of impacted riders that the agency would offer automatic refunds within two or three days.</p>
