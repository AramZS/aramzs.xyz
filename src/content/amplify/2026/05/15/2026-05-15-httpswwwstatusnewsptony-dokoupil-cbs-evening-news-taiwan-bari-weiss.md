<p>On Thursday evening, <strong>Tony Dokoupil </strong>once again anchored the <strong>“CBS Evening News”</strong> alone from <strong>Taiwan</strong> after failing to secure a visa to broadcast from <strong>Beijing</strong>, where<strong> Donald Trump</strong> is meeting with <strong>Chinese</strong> leader <strong>Xi Jinping </strong>on a high-stakes foreign trip. The logistical blunder has become an embarrassment for the <strong>Bari Weiss</strong>-led network, leaving CBS as the only major broadcast news network without an anchor on the ground in the Chinese capital.</p>
<p>And yet, the situation—<em>already humiliating enough</em>—managed to get even worse over the last 24 hours, <em>Status</em> has learned. Indeed, eagle-eyed viewers may have noticed that when Dokoupil anchored Thursday’s broadcast, he was in a very different location than the night before. While he anchored his program from a <strong>Taipei</strong> hotel balcony on Wednesday, Dokoupil was stationed in <strong>Liberty Square</strong> on Thursday.</p>
