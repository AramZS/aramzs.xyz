<p><em>This is the </em><a href="https://indieweb.org/IndieWeb_Fiction_Carnival?ref=werd.io"><em>IndieWeb Fiction Carnival</em></a><em> call for submissions for May, 2026. Everybody is welcome to participate.</em></p>
<p>I'm a long-term member of the indieweb community, and a speculative fiction writer, so I was <em>delighted</em> to see the IndieWeb Fiction Carnival appear. Previous prompts include <a href="https://sarajaksa.eu/2026/03/indieweb-fiction-carnival-april-2026-call-for-submission-platonic-soulmates/?ref=werd.io"><em>platonic soulmates</em></a> (for which I entered my story <a href="https://outmap.org/sharing-is-caring/?ref=werd.io"><em>Sharing is Caring</em></a>), <a href="https://www.dozenialdragons.me.uk/2026/04/03/iwfc_march_26_round-up/?ref=werd.io"><em>silvered eyes</em></a>, and <a href="https://zacharykai.net/notes/ifcfeb26end?ref=werd.io"><em>we've got to dream past it</em></a>.</p>
<p>This month, I've signed up to host.</p>
<p>This month's prompt is <em>sticks and stones will break my bones</em>.</p>
<p>The old rhyme <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sticks_and_Stones?ref=werd.io">dates back to at least 1830</a>:</p>
<blockquote>Sticks and stones may break my bones<br/>But words will never hurt me.</blockquote>
<p>Use this prompt however you like. Perhaps your protagonist has to learn to be impervious to a bully. Maybe it's ironic and words are more powerful than they appear. Or something else entirely might be going on.</p>
<p>I'm excited to read what you come up with.</p>
<h3>Submissions</h3>
<p>The carnival is open to anybody.</p>
<p>To submit a story, you first need to write or create any kind of fiction that responds to the prompt in a way that makes sense to you. Then, publish it openly on an independent website that you control.</p>
<p>Once you've done that, either:</p>
<ul><li>Send a webmention to this page.</li></ul>
<p>Your deadline is midnight on May 31, in whatever your local timezone is.</p>
<p>I'll post a roundup during the first week of June.</p>
<p>Happy writing!</p>
