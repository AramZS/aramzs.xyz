<div> <a data-type="company" href="https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/PZZA">Papa John’s International</a> <a href="https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/PZZA">PZZA -4.84% 🔽</a> said budget-conscious customers have traded down to smaller pizzas and passed on sides and desserts in the first quarter, weighing on sales and profit. </div><p></p>
<p>Chief Executive Todd Penegor said the pizza chain is aiming to address the issue by improving its value perception and stepping up innovation to attract new customers, sell more premium pizzas and boost add-on purchases.</p>
<p>Shares fell 4.7%, to $32.21, shortly after Thursday’s opening bell, extending their nearly 20% decline since the beginning of the year.</p>
<p>“We are focused on continuing our transformation work to be the best pizza makers in the business,” Penegor said on a call with analysts.</p>
<p>By and large, though, pizza businesses are struggling.</p>
<p>Pizzerias—which were once the second-most common U.S. restaurant type—are now outnumbered by coffee shops and Mexican food eateries. Sales growth at pizza restaurants has lagged behind the broader fast-food market for years, The Wall Street Journal previously reported.</p>
<p>Domino’s Pizza Chief Executive Russell Weiner last month said the company was operating amid an increasingly intense and competitive macroeconomic environment, as pizza chains engage in price wars with each other and other fast food vendors. The chain cut its U.S. same-store sales outlook for the year, after sales softened in March as consumers pulled back on eating out.</p>
<p>Papa John’s is working to close hundreds of domestic stores, slash menu items and cut corporate jobs as it seeks to turn around its business. The company is continuing to navigate a weak consumer backdrop and has to increase promotions to drive sales.</p>
<p>Comparable sales, which account for store openings and closings, fell 6.4% in North America during the recent quarter. Penegor said the decline was in line with expectations and partially offset by stronger trends globally, as international comparable sales rose 3.6%.</p>
<p>Looking ahead, the company will continue to launch new products, following the successful rollouts of pan pizzas and oven-toasted sandwiches earlier this year, Penegor said. Papa John’s will also look for additional cost-savings, driven by supply-chain savings and operational efficiencies, he added.</p>
<p>Papa John’s maintained its outlook for systemwide restaurant sales to be flat to down in the low-single-digits this year. Comparable sales are projected to be down 2% to 4% in North America, offset by an estimated 2%-to-4% gain across international markets.</p>
<p>For the first quarter, Papa John’s posted a profit of $7.26 million, or 21 cents a share, down from $9.22 million, or 27 cents a share, a year earlier. Adjusted earnings of 32 cents a share missed analysts’ expectations for 37 cents a share, according to FactSet.</p>
<p>Revenue fell 7.7% to $478.6 million and came in below Wall Street models for $485.5 million.</p>
