<p>We love theming!!! Sometimes the hardest part is finding that perfect background image to really set off your manifesto.</p>
<p>Here's a few of my favorite image libraries to root around in for inspiration!</p>
<p>A library of textures generally used for making computer renders! These files are HUGE so make sure you download in lowest res for docs that load faster.</p>
<p>They come in a .zip that you will need to unpack to get to the texture (the zip also contains things like bump that you don't need)</p>
<p>These are great for repeated tiled image backgrounds!</p>
<p>An early 2000's vibe library of textures built on Neocities. Super fun, crunchy, and loud.</p>
<p>A repository of works in the public domain by Cosmos. Tons of very cool images packaged into a very cool site!</p>
<p>The Biodiversity Heritage Library has a huge collection of prints of the natural world!</p>
<p>Ghibli's official repository of stills from their films! It's organized by film. Just click the film you'd like to get to a page of stills.</p>
<p>A collection of decorative end papers. So pretty, so trippy!</p>
<p>The Getty Museum offers a large part of its collection online. Unfortunately it's a little difficult to navigate. I suggest filtering my "Object Type" and scrolling to find "Painting" or something similar. Perhaps "Illuminated Manuscript"?</p>
<p>The V&amp;A has an incredible online collection available to everyone AND easy to browse! For really cool repeating patterns, I recommend browsing the Wallpaper or Tiles section.</p>
<p>Great visual search engine that draws from a half dozen museum &amp; archive collections; good for finding, say, public domain images of leaves :)</p>
<p>Artvee has a lot of great public domain art as well!</p>
<p>And the Public Domain Image Archive has thousands of great images free for anyone to use!</p>
<p>Pallets of Patterns</p>
<p>Great pattern generator with an extensive collection of editable templates, and easy to customize colors!</p>
<p>Note the default png download may not tile seamlessly (dimensions are different for each pattern) — the trick: download the svg, open in text editor and check dimensions, multiply by the 'scale' factor (2 by default), then enter those dimensions for the png download!</p>
<p>Generate an emoji pattern!</p>
<p>And finally, a cool old-school pixel editor web app for creating simple tileable patterns.</p>
