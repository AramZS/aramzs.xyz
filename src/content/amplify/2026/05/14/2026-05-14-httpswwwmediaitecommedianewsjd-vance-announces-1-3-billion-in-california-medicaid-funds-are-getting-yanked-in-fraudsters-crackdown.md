<p>Vice President<strong> JD Vance</strong> announced on Wednesday he is deferring $1.3 billion in Medicaid reimbursements for California over concerns “fraudsters” have manipulated<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/media/ripe-for-fraud-cbs-reporter-shocked-by-500-california-hospice-offices-in-3-mile-stretch/"> the state’s hospice</a> and other health programs.</p>
<p>“The simple reason is because the state of California has not taken fraud very seriously,” Vance said during a press conference.</p>
<p>Vance said both the California taxpayer and the American taxpayer in every other part of the country has been “defrauded because California isn’t taking the program seriously.”</p>
<p>The vice president said another widespread scam in The Golden State is medical providers prescribing unnecessary prescriptions.</p>
<p>“You assume that your doctor is doing the right thing. But these fraudulent health care providers are getting rich by giving people medications they don’t even need,” Vance said. “It’s a defrauding of the American taxpayer.”</p>
<p>Vance’s announcement comes after his anti-fraud task force has been looking into schemes allegedly taking advantage of the government. The press conference comes a few weeks after the administration announced 447 hospice facilities and 23 home health agencies were suspended for fraudulent billing, as the <a href="https://nypost.com/2026/05/13/us-news/feds-defer-1-3b-in-california-medicaid-funding-amid-healthcare-fraud-crackdown/"><em>New York Post</em></a> reported.</p>
<p>The Trump Administration previously <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/25/politics/trump-vance-minnesota-medicaid">halted $250 million</a> in Medicaid funds for Minnesota over similar concerns the state’s health system is being wrecked by schemers. Federal prosecutors claimed late last year that<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/opinion/cnn-and-ms-now-ignore-9-billion-industrial-scale-minnesota-fraud-claims/"> more than $9 billion in taxpayer dough</a> in Minnesota across a variety of government programs.</p>
