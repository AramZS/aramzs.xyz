<p><em>Sports Illustrated</em> deleted an author — and his entire archive of articles — from its website following allegations of AI plagiarism.</p>
<p>Last week, the sports news site <a href="https://www.sportico.com/business/sports-betting/2026/kalshi-parlays-retail-bettor-losses-rfq-1234894471/"><em>Sportico </em>published an article</a> featuring an original analysis of parlay bets made via the prediction market Kalshi. Two days later, on May 15, <em>Sports Illustrated </em>published an article — titled “<a href="https://archive.ph/x7TXT">Who is really winning on Kalshi parlays according to the data</a>” — that regurgitated the same figures, without ever attributing the analysis to <em>Sportico</em>, as would be the normal and ethical thing to do.</p>
<p>The <em>Sports Illustrated </em>piece only mentioned the other sports outlet when repeating a quote given to <em>Sportico </em>for a related article published back in 2025 — a quote that <em>Sportico</em>, tellingly, had called back to in its more recent piece.</p>
<p><em>Sportico</em> editor Dan Bernstein, who bylined the original piece, <a href="https://x.com/dan_bernstein_/status/2056162970112065735">took to X on Sunday</a> to accuse <em>Sports Illustrated </em>of plagiarism, suggesting in the post that AI may have been used to generate the piece in question.</p>
<p>“The husk of the <em>Sports Illustrated </em>brand is stealing entire stories from people without credit, seemingly using AI,” wrote Bernstein. “This becomes very obvious when it’s stealing data only you’ve reported!”</p>
<p>Soon after Bernstein’s allegations were made public, <em>Sports Illustrated </em>deleted the article in question, which was attributed to a writer named Parker Loverich. Profiles associated with Loverich quickly disappeared from LinkedIn and X. Loverich didn’t immediately respond to a request for comment.</p>
<p>After this story initially ran, <em>Sports Illustrated</em> said the deleted content had been produced by an “independent publisher.”</p>
<p>“Parker Loverich is a real reporter,” it said in a <a href="https://awfulannouncing.com/si/sports-illustrated-deletes-author-profile-prediction-market-ai-plagiarism.html">statement to <em>Awful Announcing</em></a>. “The predictions market On SI site was managed by an independent publisher who is expected to abide by <em>Sports Illustrated</em>’s editorial guidelines. <em>Sports Illustrated</em> became aware of a violation of those guidelines in regards to the use of AI and immediately took steps internally to address this violation, including cutting ties with the publisher.”</p>
<p>Loverich’s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20260513160041/https://www.si.com/betting/prediction-market/authors/parker-loverich">entire profile</a> — along with all of his articles — were scrubbed from <em>Sports Illustrated</em>‘s website.</p>
<p>Speaking to <em>Futurism</em>, Bernstein shared that it was “frustrating” to see his work plagiarized by a high-trafficked brand like <em>Sports Illustrated</em>.</p>
<p>“If someone was plagiarizing my work and no one was actually seeing it, then it would be semi-annoying,” said Bernstein. “But the idea of a plagiarized version showing up in Google over my own version, or someone seeing the plagiarized version and then citing the plagiarized version instead of my own, that’s kind of frustrating.”</p>
<p>The company’s hasty move to scrub an entire writer and their backlog from <em>Sports Illustrated</em>‘s site is significant in light of the iconic sports brand’s not-too-distant history of AI scandal. Back in 2023, <a href="https://futurism.com/sports-illustrated-ai-generated-writers"><em>Futurism </em>published an investigation revealing that <em>Sports Illustrated</em></a>, then owned by The Arena Group, had published a huge number of product review articles bylined by completely fabricated writers with AI-generated profile pictures.</p>
<p>The Arena Group said the articles had been published to <em>Sports Illustrated</em>‘s site by a <a href="https://futurism.com/advon-ai-content">third-party content provider called AdVon Commerce</a>; former employees of AdVon told <em>Futurism </em>that the company was secretly using AI to generate large amounts of content. When we reached out to the Arena Group about the content, the entire section of the <em>Sports Illustrated </em>site where the articles had appeared was swiftly deleted.</p>
<p>Following <em>Futurism</em>‘s reporting, several Arena Group <a href="https://futurism.com/sports-illustrated-publisher-shakeup-ai">executives</a> were fired, including the <a href="https://futurism.com/sports-illustrated-publisher-fires-ceo">company’s then-CEO</a>. The Arena Group soon <a href="https://futurism.com/the-byte/company-published-ai-sports-illustrated-magazine">lost control</a> of <em>Sports Illustrated </em>entirely, and the historic brand ended up in the portfolio of Minute Media.</p>
<p>Asked about Minute Media’s hurried response to scrub the writer’s history without a formal response to the plagiarism allegation, Bernstein added that “it would be nice” if the owner of the brand “took some sort of responsibility.”</p>
<p>“Just because this account was deleted, I would not be surprised if this is happening elsewhere, or if this is going to happen again,” said Bernstein. “It feels like it’s more of a systemic problem than this one incident.”</p>
