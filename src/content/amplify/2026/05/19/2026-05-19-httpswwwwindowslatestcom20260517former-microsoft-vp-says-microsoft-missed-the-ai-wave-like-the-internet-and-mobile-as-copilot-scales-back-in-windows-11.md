<p>Microsoft’s aggressive, multi-billion-dollar push into artificial intelligence was supposed to be a flawless victory. The integration of Copilot into Windows 11, Microsoft 365, and GitHub was designed to usher in a <a href="https://www.windowslatest.com/2025/10/17/microsoft-confirms-windows-11-is-turning-into-agentic-os/">new era of agentic computing</a>. Yet, beneath the polished keynote presentations and massive infrastructure investments, a dramatically different reality is what we saw.</p>
<p>As first reported by Windows Latest, according to a highly respected former Microsoft executive, the company’s AI strategy is fundamentally failing to connect with real users, spurring calls for a massive internal “factory reset.”</p>
<p>The executive in question is <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/matvelloso/">Mat Velloso</a>, who was most recently the Vice President of Product for the Developer Platform at Meta’s Superintelligence Labs. He also led AI developer products at Google DeepMind (including the Gemini API and Google AI Studio). But before his stints at Google and Meta, Velloso spent over 12 years at Microsoft, where he served as a Partner Director managing AI innovation in Windows and, interestingly, spent <strong>four years as the Technical Advisor to Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella. </strong></p>
<p>When someone with Velloso’s resume, having observed the AI arms race from the highest levels of Microsoft, Google, and Meta, says Microsoft has “missed the AI wave,” it can rip the lid off the deep tensions within Redmond.</p>
<h2>Ex-Microsoft exec says Microsoft missed the AI wave</h2>
<figure><img alt="Copilot on Windows 11" sizes="(max-width: 1280px) 100vw, 1280px" src="https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Copilot-on-Windows-11-｜-Meet-the-Computer-You-Can-Talk-To-mp4-image.png" srcset="https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Copilot-on-Windows-11-｜-Meet-the-Computer-You-Can-Talk-To-mp4-image.png 1280w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Copilot-on-Windows-11-｜-Meet-the-Computer-You-Can-Talk-To-mp4-image-300x169.png 300w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Copilot-on-Windows-11-｜-Meet-the-Computer-You-Can-Talk-To-mp4-image-1024x576.png 1024w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Copilot-on-Windows-11-｜-Meet-the-Computer-You-Can-Talk-To-mp4-image-768x432.png 768w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Copilot-on-Windows-11-｜-Meet-the-Computer-You-Can-Talk-To-mp4-image-696x392.png 696w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Copilot-on-Windows-11-｜-Meet-the-Computer-You-Can-Talk-To-mp4-image-1068x601.png 1068w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Copilot-on-Windows-11-｜-Meet-the-Computer-You-Can-Talk-To-mp4-image-747x420.png 747w"/><figcaption>Copilot on Windows 11</figcaption></figure>
<p>Microsoft’s behavior over the last few months is nothing short of shocking. Both the Windows and Xbox divisions suddenly started prioritizing user feedback and implementing requested features after years of ignoring them.</p>
<p>It’s also not a small task to assemble and organize OEMs, ODMs, and chipset vendors in an event like <a href="https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/05/15/microsofts-windows-11-quality-reset-now-targets-bad-drivers-behind-crashes-overheating-and-poor-battery-life/">WinHEC</a> that had its last occurrence almost a decade ago (2018).</p>
<p>Explaining this sudden pivot to listening to customers, Velloso remarked that despite making Bing the company’s biggest AI bet, it failed to capture a single percentage point of search market share from Google. More damning is the state of Copilot.</p>
<p>According to Velloso, less than 3% of paying users actively use Copilot, even though <a href="https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/04/18/microsoft-confirms-ai-agents-are-still-coming-to-the-windows-11-taskbar-as-it-prepares-for-public-rollout/">Microsoft has pre-deployed it directly into the Windows 11 taskbar</a> and across the Office suite.</p>
<figure><img alt="Microsoft 365 Copilot app on Windows 11" sizes="(max-width: 1044px) 100vw, 1044px" src="https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Microsoft-365-Copilot-app-on-Windows-11.jpg" srcset="https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Microsoft-365-Copilot-app-on-Windows-11.jpg 1044w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Microsoft-365-Copilot-app-on-Windows-11-300x264.jpg 300w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Microsoft-365-Copilot-app-on-Windows-11-1024x900.jpg 1024w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Microsoft-365-Copilot-app-on-Windows-11-768x675.jpg 768w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Microsoft-365-Copilot-app-on-Windows-11-696x612.jpg 696w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Microsoft-365-Copilot-app-on-Windows-11-478x420.jpg 478w"/><figcaption>Microsoft 365 Copilot app on Windows 11</figcaption></figure>
<p>Out of Microsoft’s 450 million Microsoft 365 user base, the company has only <a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/events/fy-2026/earnings-fy-2026-q2#:~:text=15%20million%20paid%20Microsoft%20365%20Copilot%20seats">managed</a> to convert roughly 15 million paid Copilot seats. This means a staggering 96.7% of users are rejecting the premium AI features, yielding just a 3.3% paid adoption rate. When viewed against Microsoft’s <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/28/technology/microsoft-earnings-ai-expenditures.html">estimated</a> $37.5 billion quarterly AI spending, this is an alarmingly low adoption rate.</p>
<p>But it’s not just software; Velloso also called out the current state of AI hardware. Over the past year, Microsoft has heavily pushed OEMs to include Neural Processing Units (NPUs) in their latest laptops to power advanced Windows 11 capabilities. We have tracked Microsoft’s push for NPU-powered AI features in Windows 11, but as <a href="https://x.com/matvelloso/status/2042282503906730278?s=20">Velloso noted</a>, OEMs invested heavily in NPUs only to find out that <em>“nobody cares because not a single valuable usecase was built for those in Windows/Office.”</em></p>
<p>Furthermore, he highlighted that GitHub, a platform that should be thriving as the centerpiece of the AI coding revolution, has seen its Service Level Agreement (SLA) reliability <a href="https://mrshu.github.io/github-statuses/">drop below 90%</a>. Combine this with rising Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and shareholders beginning to ask difficult questions, Microsoft was forced to start listening to customers because the AI bet isn’t paying off as smoothly as anticipated.</p>
<h2>Microsoft’s executive exodus</h2>
<p>All this friction appears to be taking a toll on Microsoft’s leadership. Recently, news broke that Julia Liuson, the highly respected head of Microsoft’s Developer Division (DevDiv), <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/microsoft-developer-tools-head-julia-liuson-retires.html">was retiring after 34 years with the software giant</a>.</p>
<p>While official channels framed this as a standard retirement, Mat Velloso critiqued the news, saying, <em>“Looks like Microsoft just went from hit refresh to hit factory reset.”</em> He also listed a massive string of high-profile departures and reassignments across the company, including leaders from Xbox, GitHub, AI Infrastructure, Teams, and OneNote.</p>
<p>This public commentary drew the ire of Frank X. Shaw, Microsoft’s Lead Communications executive. Shaw <a href="https://x.com/fxshaw/status/2042316970708861088">replied</a> to Velloso, defending the departing executives and accusing Velloso of jamming a “negative frame” onto normal corporate retirements.</p>
<p>All Velloso had to do was point out the harsh financial realities that the market is currently digesting. He moved from Microsoft to Google in early 2024, and while Google’s shares surged by roughly 230%, Microsoft’s stock growth remained essentially flat at 0%.</p>
<p><em>“I suppose the whole market is also wrong about this,”</em> Velloso fired back. <em>“But what do I know? I only saw both companies, how they operate, their strengths and their flaws. Microsoft missed the internet wave, the mobile wave and <strong>now it missed the AI wave</strong>. It is what happens when you keep doing the same things expecting different results.”</em></p>
<figure><img alt="Mat Velloso" sizes="(max-width: 519px) 100vw, 519px" src="https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Mat-Velloso.jpg" srcset="https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Mat-Velloso.jpg 888w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Mat-Velloso-197x300.jpg 197w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Mat-Velloso-673x1024.jpg 673w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Mat-Velloso-768x1169.jpg 768w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Mat-Velloso-696x1060.jpg 696w, https://www.windowslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Mat-Velloso-276x420.jpg 276w"/><figcaption>Mat Velloso</figcaption></figure>
<p>He urged <a href="https://x.com/matvelloso/status/2042318623122079943?s=20">Microsoft’s leadership to stop “gaslighting,”</a> deflecting blame, and burying skeletons, arguing that hitting a complete internal <em>“factory reset”</em> is the only pragmatic way to fix the hard problems plaguing the operating system and enterprise software stack.</p>
<h2>OpenAI is cutting out the middleman (Microsoft)</h2>
<p>Apart from the internal challenges, Microsoft is increasingly being affected by its closest allies. The company has staked its entire generative AI future on its multi-billion-dollar partnership with OpenAI. However, OpenAI is rapidly building out its own enterprise infrastructure, threatening Microsoft’s historic dominance in the corporate sector.</p>
<p>Just days ago, OpenAI officially launched the “<a href="https://openai.com/index/openai-launches-the-deployment-company/">OpenAI Deployment Company</a>” (DeployCo), a new business unit backed by over $4 billion in initial investment from global firms. This new venture features 150 “Forward Deployed Engineers” (FDEs) tasked with embedding directly into Fortune 500 companies to help them build and deploy custom AI solutions.</p>
<p>Historically, this hands-on, enterprise-level consulting was Microsoft’s bread and butter. As <a href="https://x.com/matvelloso/status/2053881988529139765?s=20">Velloso reminisced</a> about his early career as a consultant, he noted that Microsoft’s incredible penetration into large enterprises was built on <em>“armies of people spending time, listening, understanding business goals and solving them with technology in every industry vertical.”</em></p>
<p>Now, top AI labs like OpenAI are replicating this same playbook. They no longer want to delegate enterprise deployment to hyperscalers like Microsoft Azure. By cutting out the middleman and owning the direct relationship with businesses, OpenAI is stepping directly into the highly lucrative services layer where the real enterprise AI dollars are headed. This presents a huge structural threat to Microsoft’s long-term enterprise revenue, especially as users continue to push back against forced Copilot web app integrations.</p>
<h2>Why Microsoft is far from dead: “The moat is unbreakable”</h2>
<p>Despite these severe criticisms, Velloso defended his former employer against apocalyptic tech media narratives. When a prominent tech publication recently claimed that <em>“AI is killing Microsoft”</em> and compared their current trajectory to the disastrous 2008 era, Velloso stepped in to shut the narrative down.</p>
<p><em>“Nope, they are not dying,”</em> Velloso stated. <em>“I know I criticize them a lot and that’s because I care, but boy if you think Microsoft is dying you haven’t watched how many times they recovered from problems.”</em></p>
<p>He <a href="https://x.com/matvelloso/status/2042351725060018591?s=20">pointed out</a> that while AI startups and labs might be building flashy deployment companies, completely replacing legacy enterprise software is incredibly difficult. When asked about companies claiming they can fully automate software businesses, Velloso recommended talking to Fortune 500 CIOs to see how realistic that really is.</p>
<p><em>“There’s a reason why all the top AI labs are hiring large consulting teams,”</em> he explained. <em>“The last mile is the hardest and Microsoft has the best distribution for that. Their moat is unbreakable.”</em></p>
<p>This is the <strong>ultimate paradox of Microsoft in 2026</strong>. They are simultaneously struggling to convince users to adopt their new AI features, yet their foundational grip on the enterprise ecosystem is still solid. Millions of businesses depend on Active Directory, Azure, Office, and Windows. While it is easy to spin up an AI agent in Python, integrating it securely into a legacy corporate environment requires the kind of massive distribution network that Microsoft has spent four decades building.</p>
<h2>A necessary wake-up call</h2>

<p>All the “new” features in Windows 11, including the much-awaited <a href="https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/05/16/microsoft-rolls-out-windows-11s-movable-and-smaller-taskbar-5-years-after-killing-it-hands-on-experience/">movable taskbar</a> and <a href="https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/05/16/microsoft-is-making-windows-11s-start-menu-resizable-after-years-of-complaints-adds-small-and-large-options/">resizable Start menu</a>, are a result of everything that went wrong in the company and leadership finally doing something about it.</p>
<p>When a company realizes that pushing a 3.3% adoption-rate AI chatbot down users’ throats isn’t translating to stock growth or user satisfaction, they are compelled to reconsider their strategy.</p>
<p>We are finally seeing the results of this “factory reset” in real time. Microsoft is dismantling sluggish web wrappers, <a href="https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/05/15/microsoft-commits-to-native-ui-for-windows-11-as-users-push-back-against-web-app-slop/">committing to native UI performance</a> for Windows 11 apps, <a href="https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/05/15/microsoft-is-killing-bad-drivers-in-windows-update-admits-windows-11-pcs-have-been-breaking-for-years/">overhauling driver quality standards</a>, and explicitly prioritizing long-ignored user feedback.</p>
<p>Microsoft’s pivot back to platform fundamentals proves that while AI might be the future, you cannot build that future on a crumbling foundation. The market forced <a href="https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/03/21/microsoft-confirms-a-major-windows-11-update-with-faster-explorer-reduced-copilot-and-more/">Microsoft to listen</a>, and for frustrated Windows users, that is arguably the best thing that could have possibly happened.</p>
