<p>Last month, thousands of <strong>Hollywood</strong> actors, directors, and producers signed an open letter vehemently opposing <strong>Paramount’s</strong> proposed merger with <strong>Warner Bros. Discovery</strong>. The letter focused largely on the consequences the tie-up would have for the entertainment industry and consumer choice.</p>
<p>But while it argued that competition is “essential” to a “healthy democracy,” it did not grapple with one of the deal’s most significant implications for the public: putting<strong> CBS News</strong> and <strong>CNN</strong>, two of the most recognizable news organizations in the country, under the control of <strong>David Ellison</strong>, who has conspicuously cozied up to <strong>Donald Trump</strong>.</p>
