<h1>Trusted by<br/>calm creators</h1>
<p>I purchased this specifically to connect to my Tempest weather station. So I could see what the temperature is and what the weather is likely to be today without having to unlock my cell phone or look at my computer. I have it attached to my refrigerator door and anyone can see with a glance. Perfect.</p>
<p>I purchased this to use as a Tempest weather console and it has been wonderful. It's not real-time, but it really does not need to be. Is it backlit? Nope, but I have found that to not be a problem unless I am walking around in the dark and want to know the weather. It's simple, easy to set up, and lacks bells and whistles, which makes it elegant and wonderful. Likely will buy the next version and move this to another room.</p>
<p>The flexibility to change, add, remove, and edit what’s displayed on the TRMNL is amazing. Whenever I need a refresh or just want to cut down on phone time, it’s right there on my desk, showing my calendar, to-do's, YouTube stats, and package deliveries at a glance.</p>
<p>I love the concept and it works well. There have been a few hiccups, but every time Ryan has been very quick to respond and he'll resolve the issue. They even changed cache policies for me when I had a need for fresher data. The very few issues I've had were not only taken care of quickly, but the customer service more than made up for any inconvenience. I've already sold a few of my friends on these, and I'd recommend one to you as well.</p>
<p>Firstly the product packaging was spot on and well presented. Setting up using the existing integrations was a breeze. Specifically, Google Calendar, Strava and Home Assistant integrations. I'd highly recommend this smart e-ink display to anyone looking to keep their family (or themselves) informed of whatever it is you're tracking.</p>
<p>So easy to setup with my home wifi and I was up and running inside 5 minutes. Included options were great and I found one that I paid for and it loaded quickly and easily. I love that despite having a quad display setup of which a few elements update 5x per day the battery barely drops</p>
<p>Love the e-ink display and the white frame. Classic and timeless. Quite easy to setup with the large variety of plugin and recipe options from the site: shared calendars, quotes and custom messages, clocks, xkcd comics, weather, and more. Super easy to setup and even easier to customize. Highly recommend the three-pack so you can put them all over the house/office. The mirror other device is a great way to ensure that all devices share the same content. Can't wait to explore the API and build a few custom plugins and recipes.</p>
<p>Haven't been able to play with it too much yet, but so far I do like it a lot. We have the family calendar visible on it and it's so easy to take a glimpse of it. I am waiting to get the Home Assistant integration, because that opens up another channel to get all relevant information on it. Our electricity pricing is fluctuating in Finland every hour (now changing to every 15m) and it would be nice to see the daily curve on the TRMNL. Another one is the bus schedule for the next few hours. All this I can pull to Home Assistant and therefore it would be an easy gateway to TRMNL also. Anyways, great product!</p>
<p>I am very excited to finally be able to play with TRMNL and I have a lot of fun ideas that would make my life so much nicer 👀 Currently I'm just using the built-in plugin for Google Calendar which albeit limited it actually had every function I was needing, I like being able to just look at a glance to see if I have an event and with the week view it progresses to the right so I always know the first column is current day 💜 Also very excited for a bigger version, I need more TRMNL's in my life! There also needs to be more community made plugins, I'm surprised there aren't more crypto-related ones 😏</p>
<p>Make sure you know what problem this is solving for you before buying. Pay attention when you pull your phone out around the house. What information are you looking for? When are you looking for it? That is the information that you should put on this display and at that given time. No need to pull your phone out of your pocket and get sucked into social media, messaging, etc. This also gives you an opportunity to be more deliberate about your life. Rather than going to the same grocery store every time, have price comparisons between local grocery stores displayed next to your car keys.</p>
<p>The whole experience has been great. Setup, updates, management have all been amazing. I love this for family schedules, dinners, etc - but then one in my office for work planning, goals, news, notifications. I use my phone less and don't feel like I'm missing things. I'll be getting more.</p>
<p>super easy to set up, battery life is incredible. I'm enjoying having comics on it, which was a use case I would never have considered if it wasn't for the (already) rich tapestry of options you have to display. in addition, the community on discord has been stellar as i look at a BYOD screen of my own that will take advantage of this ecosystem.</p>
<p>One of the most appealing aspects of this device is how easy it is to set up a highly customizable E-Ink dashboard. There’s no need for soldering or server setup, allowing you to start using it right away. You can effortlessly create playlists from the plugin list, and by adding the Developer Edition option, you can customize your own screens using HTML markup and APIs. It supports both polling and WebHook methods for data retrieval, offering great versatility. Additionally, a dedicated design system ensures that custom screens maintain a cohesive look with existing plugins. Overall, this device makes it incredibly easy to create a sleek and well-designed dashboard with minimal effort.</p>
<p>Incredible craftsmanship. While I've not had much time with it do to a mix of work and life things popping up I was getting started with it earlier and appreciated the simplicity for startup. Using the WiFi portal method for setup is genius. And the quality of the device is great. It's not entirely solid, there is a small very tiny feeling of fragility. But it's really well manufactured. I'm curious what the material texture on the outside is called or how that's achieved. It makes it feel soft. Nice to hold.</p>
<p>Fantastic and open little device. I love the product and ecosystem built around it as I don't have a lot of time to 'hack' it right now. I was able to get up and running with several basic screens that have been helpful in my daily life.I also love that it's open so, god forbid, the company goes away one day the display will still be useful and I can easily host my own server or repurpose it for another project. Every product should be this way and, in my opinion, it adds a huge layer of value!</p>
<p>I had been on the hunt for an e-ink calendar - they were all prohibitively expensive, or exceedingly complex, or relied on some very fishy app connection. I mean I think I'm a pretty clever guy but I am not capable of the coding and programming needed for so many of the DIY options.Saw TRMNL. Bought TRMNL. It's perfect. Sits on my desk, tells me weather and my calendar, and updates only when it needs to.Despite what marketing tells us - we don't actually need every device to do every thing. TRMNL does what it says it will, does it well, and without extraneous nonsense. Perfect.</p>
<p>Once you have setup your TRMNL, it is pretty straightforward to add plugins from the dashboard and add them to your playlist where these will be displayed.As a minimalist it is easy for me to look at my items at glance and not be bothered by other distractions on my phone: if you do some time blocking on your calendar then I highly recommend adding your calendar through the plugin to see all upcoming events.Beyond that the widgets for weather and custom text are my favourite</p>
<p>I backed TRMNL on Kickstarter, and I was pleasantly surprised to receive my order today (10/17/24) which is a month and a half ahead of the expected delivery date of December 2024. Setup was easy, the device came fully charged, and I've even started tinkering around with creating custom plugins already. I don't know if people realize how insanely difficult it is to bring a hardware product to market, and Kickstarter is rife with crowdfunding efforts where the supporters were burned. This is not one of them, pretty obviously. The actual display is really high quality--if I didn't know better, I would assume it was a fully overseas-manufactured and assembled device!Kudos to Ryan and his team for bringing this thing together. I'm impressed. Try it, I think you'll like it.</p>
