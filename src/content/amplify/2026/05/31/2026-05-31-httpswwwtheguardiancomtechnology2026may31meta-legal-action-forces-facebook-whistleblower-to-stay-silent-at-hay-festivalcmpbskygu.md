<h2 data-gu-name="standfirst">Sarah Wynn-Williams did not speak on stage on her lawyer’s advice amid tech firm’s sanction</h2>
<p>Facebook whistleblower Sarah Wynn-Williams was forced to sit in silence on stage at an event at <a data-component="auto-linked-tag" data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/books/guardian-hay-festival">Hay festival</a>, after lawyers advised her not to speak because of ongoing legal action brought by Meta.</p>
<p>Wynn-Williams, whose bestselling memoir, Careless People, details her years working at <a data-component="auto-linked-tag" data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/technology/facebook">Facebook</a>, was due to appear in conversation with the investigative journalist Carole Cadwalladr and academic Tim Wu.</p>
<p>Instead, Wynn-Williams sat on stage for the duration of the hour-long discussion between Cadwalladr and Wu, without speaking or responding. She was unable even to nod or shake her head.</p>
<p>Introducing the panel, Cadwalladr said: “I think this might be a Hay first, in which we have an author in a hostage situation. Blink once if you can hear us, Sarah, twice if [Mark] Zuckerberg is an asshole.”</p>
<p>At the end of the event, Wynn-Williams received a standing ovation from the audience, during which she was moved to tears.</p>
<p>Describing the situation, Cadwalladr said: “I think we can say that Facebook is triggered.”</p>
<p>Wynn-Williams, a former Facebook executive, has faced mounting legal restrictions since the publication last year of Careless People, which contains allegations about Meta’s internal culture and decision-making, including claims relating to political influence, the company’s approach to China and concerns about the wellbeing of its child users. <a data-component="auto-linked-tag" data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/technology/meta">Meta</a> has strongly disputed the book’s claims.</p>
<p>Hay’s programme director Helen Bagnall told the audience that the moment was “an important act of solidarity for the silenced”.</p>
<p>Meta, which owns Facebook and Instagram, secured an emergency legal order on the eve of publication preventing her from publicly discussing aspects of the book, and she faces fines of $50,000 (£37,000) each time she breaches the order. The financial and legal pressure has <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2025/sep/21/meta-expose-author-sarah-wynn-williams-faces-bankruptcy-after-ban-on-criticising-company">reportedly threatened her with bankruptcy</a>.</p>
<p>Cadwalladr described the spectacle as “trolling-like behaviour” by Meta. “This is not how you conduct crisis comms. Crisis comms would just be simply to ignore this and deprive it of oxygen. This is a kind of trolling-like behaviour against their enemies.”</p>
<p>Speaking on stage, Wu condemned the restrictions on Wynn-Williams’ participation.</p>

<p>“This is censorship,” he told the audience. “This is a demonstration that some of the worst abuses in our time are not confined to kings, emperors, governments … but to a class of companies that have assumed the sovereign affect, and seek to assert their power the same way that some of those despotic nation states do.”</p>
<p>During the event, Cadwalladr read a letter from Wynn-Williams’ lawyers outlining the company’s latest legal claims. The letter stated that, in March 2026, Meta filed a sanctions motion alleging that Wynn-Williams violates the emergency arbitration order “any time she appears in public in a place where she should know that her book is available for sale and her presence might draw attention to it”.</p>
<p>According to the letter, Meta’s motion specifically cited her appearance at the Hay festival as “an example of conduct that should be formally sanctioned”.</p>
<p>It also referred to the identities of her fellow panellists. Meta argued that Cadwalladr was a journalist “primarily known for her negative coverage of Meta”, while Wu was described as “another known critic”.</p>
<p>Following the letter, Hay festival withdrew Careless People from sale while she was speaking at the festival, so as to not breach Meta’s injunction.</p>
