<h1 data-testid="Heading">Exclusive: Iran attacks wipe out 17% of Qatar’s LNG capacity for up to five years, QatarEnergy CEO says</h1>
<p>By <a data-testid="AuthorNameLink" href="https://www.reuters.com/authors/maha-el-dahan/">Maha El Dahan</a>, <a data-testid="AuthorNameLink" href="https://www.reuters.com/authors/andrew-mills/">Andrew Mills</a> and <a data-testid="AuthorNameLink" href="https://www.reuters.com/authors/yousef-saba/">Yousef Saba</a></p>

<p>DUBAI/DOHA, March 19 (Reuters) - Iranian attacks ‌have knocked out 17% of Qatar's liquefied natural gas (LNG) export capacity, causing an estimated $20 billion in lost annual revenue and threatening supplies to Europe and Asia, QatarEnergy's CEO and state minister for energy affairs told Reuters on Thursday.</p>
<p>Saad al-Kaabi said two of Qatar's 14 LNG trains and one of its two gas-to-liquids (GTL) facilities were damaged in ​the unprecedented strikes. The repairs will sideline 12.8 million tons per year of LNG for three to five years, he said ​in an interview.</p>
<p>"I never in my wildest dreams would have thought that Qatar would be - Qatar and the region - ⁠in such an attack, especially from a brotherly Muslim country in the month of Ramadan, attacking us in this way," Kaabi said.</p>
<p>Hours earlier ​Iran had aimed a <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/wrapup1-iran-targets-energy-facilities-across-gulf-after-israel-struck-its-key-2026-03-19/">series of attacks</a> at Gulf oil and gas facilities after Israeli attacks on its own gas infrastructure.</p>
<p>State-owned QatarEnergy will have to ​declare force majeure on long-term contracts for up to five years for LNG supplies bound for Italy, Belgium, South Korea, and China due to the two damaged trains, Kaabi said.</p>
<p>"I mean, these are long-term contracts that we have to declare force majeure. We already declared, but that was a shorter term. Now it's whatever the period ​is," he said.</p>
<h2 data-testid="Heading">EXXONMOBIL IMPACT AND BYPRODUCTS</h2>
<p>QatarEnergy had declared force majeure on its entire output of LNG, after earlier attacks on its Ras Laffan production ​hub, which came under fire again on Wednesday.</p>
<p>"For production to restart, first we need hostilities to cease," he said.</p>
<p>U.S. oil major ExxonMobil <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/XOM.N">(XOM.N)</a> is a partner in ‌the damaged ⁠LNG facilities, while Shell <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/SHEL.L">(SHEL.L)</a> is a partner in the damaged GTL facility, which will take up to a year to repair.</p>
<p>Texas-based ExxonMobil holds a 34% stake in LNG train S4 and a 30% stake in train S6, Kaabi said.</p>
<p>Train S4 impacts supplies to Italy's Edison <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/EDNn.MI">(EDNn.MI)</a> and EDFT in Belgium, while Train S6 impacts South Korea's KOGAS, EDFT and Shell in China.</p>
<p>The scale of the damage from the attacks has set the region back 10 ​to 20 years, he said.</p>
<p>"And of ​course, this is a safe ⁠haven for a lot of people, to have a safe place to stay and so on. And that image, I think, has been shaken."</p>
<p>The fallout extends well beyond LNG. Qatar's exports of condensate will drop by ​around 24%, while liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) will fall 13%. Helium output will fall 14%, and naphtha and ​sulphur will both drop ⁠by 6%.</p>
<p>Those losses have implications ranging from LPG used in restaurants in India to South Korea's chipmakers which use helium.</p>
<p>The damaged units cost approximately $26 billion to build, Kaabi said.</p>
<p>No work is currently taking place on Qatar's massive North Field expansion project, which could be delayed for more than a year, he ⁠said.</p>
<p>"If Israel ​attacked Iran, it's between Iran and Israel. It has nothing to do with us ​and the region," he said.</p>
<p>"And so now, in addition to that, I'm saying that everybody in the world, whether it's Israel, whether it's the U.S., whether it's any other country, ​everybody should stay away from oil and gas facilities."</p>
