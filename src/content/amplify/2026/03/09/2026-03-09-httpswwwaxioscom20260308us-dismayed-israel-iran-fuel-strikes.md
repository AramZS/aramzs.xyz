<p><strong>Why it matters: </strong>The <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2026/02/28/trump-iran-war-iraq-venezuela" data-vars-content-id="bfd9210c-8b0a-4b1f-8ca4-eb8983a87a73" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Scoop: U.S. dismayed by Israel's Iran fuel strikes, sources say" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="U.S." data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2026/02/28/trump-iran-war-iraq-venezuela">U.S.</a> is concerned Israeli strikes on infrastructure that serves ordinary Iranians could backfire strategically, rallying Iranian society to support the regime and <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2026/03/08/trump-oil-prices-iran-fear-strait-hormuz" data-vars-content-id="bfd9210c-8b0a-4b1f-8ca4-eb8983a87a73" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Scoop: U.S. dismayed by Israel's Iran fuel strikes, sources say" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="driving up oil prices" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2026/03/08/trump-oil-prices-iran-fear-strait-hormuz">driving up oil prices</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Driving the news:</strong> The Israeli air force's Saturday strikes created large fires in Tehran, igniting flames visible for miles and blanketing <a data-vars-click-url="https://time.com/7383099/iran-news-oil-strikes-tehran/" data-vars-content-id="bfd9210c-8b0a-4b1f-8ca4-eb8983a87a73" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Scoop: U.S. dismayed by Israel's Iran fuel strikes, sources say" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="the capital in heavy smoke" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://time.com/7383099/iran-news-oil-strikes-tehran/">the capital in heavy smoke</a>.</p>
<ul><li>The IDF claimed in a statement that the fuel depots "are used by the Iranian regime to supply fuel to different consumers including its military organs." </li><li>An Israeli military official said the strikes were intended in part to tell Iran to stop targeting Israeli civilian infrastructure.</li></ul>
<p><strong>Behind the scenes:</strong> Israeli and U.S. officials said the IDF notified the U.S. military ahead of the strikes.</p>
<ul><li>But a U.S. official said that the U.S. military was surprised by how wide-ranging they were. </li><li>"We don't think it was a good idea," a senior U.S. official said. </li><li>An Israeli official said the U.S. message to Israel was "WTF". </li><li>The White House and the IDF didn't comment. </li></ul>
<p><strong>The big picture:</strong> While the facilities that were struck are not oil production facilities, U.S. officials are concerned the footage of burning depots could spook oil markets and push energy prices even higher.</p>
<p><strong>The other side:</strong> The spokesman for Iran's Khatam al-Anbiya headquarters, which oversees the military operations, warned Saturday that if attacks on Iran's oil infrastructure continue, Tehran may respond with similar strikes across the region.</p>
<ul><li>He added that Iran so far hasn't targeted regional fuel and energy infrastructure and threatened that if Iran does, oil prices could hit $200 a barrel.</li><li>Iranian parliament speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, one of the most senior officials in the regime, warned that if attacks on infrastructure continue, Iran will retaliate "without delay." </li></ul>
<p><strong>What's next:</strong> A U.S. official says the disagreement and what the U.S. expects in the war is expected to be addressed at senior political levels between the two allies.</p>
