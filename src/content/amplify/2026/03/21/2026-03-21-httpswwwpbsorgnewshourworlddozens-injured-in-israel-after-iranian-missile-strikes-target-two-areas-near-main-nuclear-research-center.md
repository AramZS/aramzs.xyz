<p>CAIRO (AP) — Iran struck two communities near Israel's main nuclear research center late Saturday, leaving buildings shattered and at least seven people seriously injured, hours after Tehran's main nuclear enrichment site was hit as the war spun into a dangerous new direction at the start of its fourth week.</p>
<p>It was the first time Israel's nuclear research center has been targeted in the fighting. Israel's military said it was not able to intercept missiles that hit the southern cities of Dimona and Arad, the largest near the center in Israel's sparsely populated Negev desert.</p>
<p>"This is a very difficult evening," Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said, adding that more emergency resources were being sent to the scene.</p>
<p>"The war is not close to ending," Israel's army chief, Gen. Eyal Zamir, said earlier in the day.</p>
<p>Iran also targeted the joint U.K.-U.S. Diego Garcia military base in the Indian Ocean about 2,500 miles (4,000 kilometers) away, suggesting that Tehran has missiles that can go farther than previously acknowledged — or that it had used its space program for an improvised launch.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/as-attacks-on-oil-sites-continue-trump-dismisses-ceasefire-says-iran-is-finished"><strong>WATCH:</strong> As attacks on oil sites continue, Trump dismisses ceasefire, says Iran is 'finished'</a></p>
<p>The U.S. and Israel have offered shifting rationales for the war, from hoping to foment an uprising that topples Iran's leadership to eliminating its nuclear and missile programs and its support for armed proxies. There have been no signs of an uprising, while internet restrictions limit information from Iran.</p>
<p>The war's effects are felt far beyond the Middle East, raising food and fuel prices.</p>
<p>It is not clear how much damage Iran has sustained in the U.S. and Israeli strikes that began Feb. 28 — or even who is truly in charge. Supreme Leader Ayatollah Mojtaba Khamenei has not been seen in public since being named to the role.</p>
<h2>Israel had denied responsibility for attack on Natanz</h2>
<p>Footage from Israel's emergency service showed a large crater next to what appeared to be apartment buildings with outer walls sheared away. The missile appeared to have struck an open area.</p>
<p>Rescue workers said the direct hit in Arad caused widespread damage across at least 10 apartment buildings, three of them badly damaged and in danger of collapsing. At least 64 people were taken to hospitals.</p>
<p>Dimona is about 20 kilometers (12 miles) west of the nuclear research center and Arad around 35 kilometers (21 miles) north.</p>
<p>Israel is believed to be the only Middle East nation with nuclear weapons, though its leaders refuse to confirm or deny their existence. The U.N. nuclear watchdog said on X it had not received reports of damage to the Israeli center or abnormal radiation levels.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/iran-intensifies-attacks-on-gulf-energy-sites-after-israel-struck-its-key-gas-field"><strong>READ MORE:</strong> Iran intensifies attacks on Gulf energy sites after Israel struck its key gas field</a></p>
<p>"If the Israeli regime is unable to intercept missiles in the heavily protected Dimona area, it is, operationally, a sign of entering a new phase of the battle," Iran's Parliament Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf said on X before word of the Arad strike spread.</p>
<p>Israel earlier Saturday denied responsibility for the strike on the Natanz nuclear facility, nearly 220 kilometers (135 miles) southeast of Tehran. The Iranian judiciary's official news agency, Mizan, said there was no leakage.</p>
<p>The International Atomic Energy Agency has said the bulk of Iran's estimated 970 pounds (440 kilograms) of enriched uranium is elsewhere, beneath the rubble at its Isfahan facility. It said on X it was looking into the strike.</p>
<p>The Pentagon declined to comment on the strike on Natanz, which was also hit in the first week of the war and in the 12-day war last June. Russian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Maria Zakharova said such strikes posed a "real risk of catastrophic disaster throughout the Middle East."</p>
<p>Iran retaliated hours later.</p>
<h2>U.S. can use Diego Garcia base to protect Strait of Hormuz</h2>
<p>U.K. officials did not give details of the strike that targeted the Diego Garcia base Friday, which was unsuccessful. Britain's Ministry of Defense described Iran as "lashing out across the region."</p>
<p>It's unclear how close the missiles came to the island. Iran previously asserted that it has limited its missile range to below 2,000 kilometers (over 1,200 miles).</p>
<p><a href="https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/nato-pulls-security-advisory-mission-out-of-iraq-after-iranian-attacks-on-european-bases"><strong>READ MORE:</strong> NATO pulls security advisory mission out of Iraq after Iranian attacks on European bases</a></p>
<p>But military experts said Iran may have used its space launch vehicle for an improvised firing. "If you've got a space program, you've got a ballistic missile program," said Steve Prest, a retired Royal Navy commodore.</p>
<p>Israel's army chief, however, said Iran had fired "a two-stage intercontinental ballistic missile." There was no statement from Iran.</p>
<p>Britain has not participated in U.S.-Israeli attacks but has allowed U.S. bombers to use its bases to attack Iran's missile sites. On Friday, the U.K. government said bombers could use Diego Garcia to attack sites used to target ships in the Strait of Hormuz.</p>
<h2>Global pressure increases to get shipping back on track</h2>
<p>As Iran threatens shipping on the Strait of Hormuz, the United Arab Emirates joined 21 other countries including the U.K., Germany, France and Japan in expressing "readiness to contribute to appropriate efforts to ensure safe passage."</p>
<p>The Trump administration announced it was temporarily lifting sanctions on Iranian oil that was already loaded on ships as of Friday, but that does not increase oil production, a central factor in surging prices. The oil ministry of Iran, which has evaded sanctions for years, replied that it "essentially has no crude oil left in floating storage."</p>
<p>The head of U.S. Central Command, Adm. Brad Cooper, asserted that Iran's ability to attack vessels on the strait had been "degraded." He said 5,000-pound (2,270-kilogram) bombs were dropped earlier in the week on an underground facility along Iran's coast used to store anti-ship cruise missiles and mobile missile launchers.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/eu-leaders-balk-at-joining-middle-east-fight-grapple-with-high-energy-prices"><strong>READ MORE:</strong> EU leaders balk at joining Middle East fight, grapple with high energy prices</a></p>
<p>The U.S. is deploying three more amphibious assault ships and roughly 2,500 additional Marines to the Middle East, an official told The Associated Press. Two other U.S. officials confirmed that ships were deploying, without saying where they were headed. All three spoke on condition of anonymity to discuss the operations.</p>
<p>Gulf countries reported more attacks. A missile alert sounded Saturday night in Dubai. Saudi Arabia said it downed 20 drones in its east, home to major oil installations.</p>
<p>Iran's death toll in the war has surpassed 1,500, the state broadcaster reported, citing the health ministry. In Israel, 15 people have been killed by Iranian missiles and four others have died in the occupied West Bank. At least 13 U.S. military members have been killed, along with well over a dozen civilians in Gulf nations.</p>
<h2>Israeli troops and Hezbollah militants clash in Lebanon</h2>
<p>Israel's military said it was conducting a "targeted ground operation" in southern Lebanon and at least four militants were killed. Hezbollah said its fighters clashed with troops in the southern village of Khiam.</p>
<p>Israeli strikes targeting Hezbollah have killed more than 1,000 people and displaced more than 1 million, according to the Lebanese government. Hezbollah's civilian assets also have been targeted.</p>
