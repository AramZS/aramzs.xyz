<body><figure><img alt="US President Donald Trump during the NATO summit in The Hague on June 25." src="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/iBmYV.FVpqE8/v1/-1x-1.webp"/><figcaption><p>US President Donald Trump during the NATO summit in The Hague on June 25.</p> / Photographer: Simon Wohlfahrt/Bloomberg</figcaption></figure><div><p>President <a href="">Donald Trump</a> denounced NATO as “COWARDS,” ratcheting up his rhetoric against the military alliance over its refusal to help the US reopen the Strait of Hormuz, the critical waterway blocked since the start of the Iran war.</p><p>Trump has lashed out at US allies and major trading partners for failing to heed his calls to provide warships to help tankers and other vessels safely transit the narrow passage, a chokepoint for about a fifth of the world’s oil supply. The situation has spurred volatility in energy markets and sent oil and gas prices soaring.</p><p>“Without the U.S.A., NATO IS A PAPER TIGER!”  Trump said Friday on social media. “They didn’t want to join the fight to stop a Nuclear Powered Iran. Now that fight is Militarily WON, with very little danger for them, they complain about the high oil prices they are forced to pay, but don’t want to help open the Strait of Hormuz, a simple military maneuver that is the single reason for the high oil prices.”</p><p>Trump has insisted that the risks to providing military help are low even as the war — now three weeks long — shows no signs of easing and with Iran continuing retaliatory attacks on neighboring states.</p><p>“So easy for them to do, with so little risk,” Trump said of NATO. “COWARDS, and we will REMEMBER!”</p><p>Trump’s comments highlight the challenge for NATO, which he has long assailed as not contributing enough toward collective security, as it struggles to keep the US president on its side over another geopolitical flashpoint — Russia’s war in Ukraine. Trump has pressed to bring that conflict to an end, at times sidelining European allies in diplomacy and pushing concessions on Kyiv that have alarmed NATO members.</p></div><div><ol></ol></div></body>
