<p>Bari Weiss is about to <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/cbs-news-layoffs-plans-dozens-employees-bari-weiss-paramount-skydance-2026-3">swing the ax at CBS News</a>, according to a new report.</p>
<p>The polarizing top editor plans to lay off dozens of staffers imminently as she <a href="https://www.rawstory.com/cbs-news-2676307385/">reshapes the storied broadcast network</a>, Business Insider reported Thursday. And she has telegraphed the cuts for weeks.</p>
<p>At a late-January all-hands meeting, Weiss told CBS News employees that a "tsunami of technological change" was coming and warned that <a href="https://www.rawstory.com/cbs-news-trump-admin/">transformation wouldn't be painless</a>.</p>
<p>"I can't stand up here and tell you that in a moment of incredible transformation that that's not going to mean transformation of our workforce," Weiss told staffers.</p>
<p>Weiss has been blunt about her diagnosis of the network's problems. CBS News has long trailed broadcast rivals ABC and NBC in ratings, and she said <a href="https://www.rawstory.com/cbs-evening-news-2676269014/">clinging to a broadcast-first strategy</a> would be a death sentence.</p>

<p>The cuts follow eleven buyouts at "CBS Evening News" last month as the show underwent an anchor change, with former "CBS Mornings" co-host Tony Dokoupil taking over. That program has seen ratings plummet to perilously low levels.</p>
<p>Weiss, a former New York Times editor who founded anti-establishment news site The Free Press, was a controversial hire by Paramount Skydance CEO David Ellison from the start. Her decision to delay a story critical of President Donald Trump's deportation efforts while Paramount pursued a merger with Warner Bros. Discovery drew fierce backlash inside and outside the network.</p>
