<p>WASHINGTON, March 27 (Reuters) - Iran-linked hackers have broken into ​FBI Director Kash Patel's personal email inbox, publishing photographs of the director and other documents to the internet, the hackers and the ‌bureau said on Friday.</p>
<p>On their website, the hacker group Handala Hack Team said Patel "will now find his name among the list of successfully hacked victims." The hackers published a series of personal photographs of Patel sniffing and smoking cigars, riding in an antique convertible, and making a face while taking a picture of himself in the mirror with a large bottle ​of rum.</p>

<p>The FBI confirmed Patel's emails had been targeted. In a statement, bureau spokesman Ben Williamson said, "we have taken all necessary steps to mitigate ​potential risks associated with this activity" and that the data involved was "historical in nature and involves no government information."</p>
<p>Handala, which ⁠presents itself as a group of pro-Palestinian vigilante hackers, is considered by Western researchers to be one of <a href="https://www.reuters.com/technology/iran-linked-hackers-restore-website-after-us-seizes-domains-2026-03-20/">several personas used by Iranian government cyberintelligence units</a>. Handala ​recently claimed the hack of Michigan-based medical devices and services provider Stryker <a href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/SYK.N">(SYK.N), opens new tab</a> on March 11, saying they had deleted a massive trove of company data.</p>

<p>Handala did not ​return messages. Reuters could not access its website late on Friday.</p>
<p>Alongside the photographs of Patel, the hackers published a sample of more than 300 emails, which appear to show a mix of personal and work correspondence dating between 2010 and 2019.</p>
<p>Reuters was not able to independently authenticate the Patel messages, but the personal Gmail address that Handala claims to have broken ​into matches the address linked to Patel in previous data breaches preserved by the dark web intelligence firm District 4 Labs. Alphabet <a href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/GOOGL.O">(GOOGL.O), opens new tab</a> -owned Google, which runs Gmail, ​did not respond to a request for comment.</p>
<h2>'MAKE THEM FEEL VULNERABLE'</h2>
<p>Iran-linked hackers - who initially kept a low profile after the United States and Israel launched coordinated strikes against the Islamic ‌Republic last ⁠month - have increasingly boasted of their cyber operations as the conflict drags on.</p>
<p>In addition to the hack against Stryker, Handala on Thursday claimed to have published the personal data of dozens of defense company Lockheed Martin employees stationed in the Middle East. In a statement, Lockheed Martin <a href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/LMT.N">(LMT.N), opens new tab</a> said it was aware of the reports and had policies and procedures in place "to mitigate cyber threats to our business."</p>
<p>Gil Messing, chief of staff at Israeli cybersecurity company Check Point, said the hack-and-leak operation ​against Patel was part of Iran's strategy ​to embarrass U.S. officials and "make ⁠them feel vulnerable."</p>
<p>The Iranians, he said, are "firing whatever they have."</p>
<p>It is not unusual for foreign hackers to target senior officials' personal emails, and breaches and leaks both happen periodically. Hackers <a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/world/us/clinton-campaign-chief-podesta-fbi-probing-hack-of-his-emails-idUSKCN12C01U/">famously broke into Hillary Clinton campaign chairman</a> John Podesta's personal Gmail account ​ahead of the 2016 election and published much of the data to the WikiLeaks site. In 2015, teenage hackers ​broke into then-CIA ⁠director John Brennan's <a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/world/us-politics/fbi-secret-service-probe-hack-of-cia-chiefs-private-emails-idUSKCN0SG2B8/">personal AOL account</a> and leaked data about U.S. intelligence officials.</p>
<p>Relatively unsophisticated breaches of this nature are in line with <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/intelligence-assessment-warns-iranian-attacks-us-following-khameneis-death-2026-03-02/">a</a> <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/intelligence-assessment-warns-iranian-attacks-us-following-khameneis-death-2026-03-02/">U.S. intelligence assessment reviewed by Reuters</a> on March 2. The assessment said Iran and its proxies could respond to the killing of Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei with low-level hacks against U.S. digital networks.</p>
<p>Iran-linked hackers may ⁠have other ​emails in reserve.</p>
<p>Last year, another group operating under the pseudonym "Robert" <a href="https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/iran-linked-hackers-threaten-release-trump-aides-emails-2025-06-30/">told Reuters</a> it was considering disclosing 100 ​gigabytes of data stolen from White House chief of staff Susie Wiles and other figures close to U.S. President Donald Trump.</p>
<p>Reuters has not been able to verify the claim and the group has ​not responded to messages in several months.</p>
