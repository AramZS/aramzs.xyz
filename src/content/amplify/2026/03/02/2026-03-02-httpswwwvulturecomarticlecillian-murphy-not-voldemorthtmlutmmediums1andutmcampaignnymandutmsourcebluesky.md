<p>It makes sense that after thirteen years of playing Tommy Shelby on <em>Peaky Blinders</em>, a show with an extremely dedicated and outspoken fanbase, that Cillian Murphy would not exactly be champing at the bit to join yet another long-winded television franchise with a vocal hoard of fans.</p>
<p>After months of rumors that he was going to step into the role of Voldemort in the forthcoming <a href="https://www.vulture.com/article/harry-potter-tv-series.html">HBO adaptation of J.K. Rowling’s <em>Harry Potter</em> novels</a>, the actor officially <a href="https://www.thetimes.com/culture/film/article/cillian-murphy-im-turning-50-what-do-i-do-now-d79lgsgnk?gaa_at=eafs&amp;gaa_n=AWEtsqf4qIz7QSlG9XwCkqyVhrC4wbibKf6MGM9Ex20JhQFIlPzp7J4HRgNqjxnWMUw%3D&amp;gaa_ts=69a58dce&amp;gaa_sig=EgRQ-bdlxQ6DJ5ppOgrgoQuOzsEOegobIFQz6I6XV4va8ZTvIcsLtIr6OotdfHHC6CBGkr8vHuLQY-26YTwqlA%3D%3D">denied those rumors</a>. “I’m categorically not” were his exact words, which is very easy to imagine him saying aloud in his stern but playful brogue. He also claimed to be completely unaware of those rumors, which means he lives in a much purer state of mind than any of us could have ever imagined. “I respond to the fallibility of everyone and the fact most people are trying to do the right thing,” he told <em>The Times</em>, which also clarifies, maybe, why he isn’t playing Voldemort. Imagine trying to think of Voldemort as a guy trying to do the right thing.</p>
<p>While it’s a relief that Murphy isn’t going to be involved, some other handsome-but-slightly-strange-looking actor is almost certainly circling the role as we speak.</p>
