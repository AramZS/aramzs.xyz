<h1>Iran Brings Europe Into Range With Missiles Fired at Diego Garcia</h1>
<h2>Attack on base 2,500 miles away signals more-aggressive military posture for regime</h2>
<p>By <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/shelby-holliday">Shelby Holliday</a> and <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/dov-lieber">Dov Lieber</a></p>
<p>March 21, 2026 5:58 pm ET</p>
<img alt="Aerial view of Diego Garcia in the Chagos Island group." src="https://images.wsj.net/im-37934696?width=700&amp;height=541"/>
<p>Diego Garcia is a U.S.-U.K. military base in the Indian Ocean. U.S. Navy/AP</p>
<p>When Iranian missile crews rolled two of their largest weapons out of hiding and launched them at the U.S.-U.K. Diego Garcia military base 2,500 miles away, they revealed the Islamic Republic had longer-range missiles than many analysts had realized—and a leadership that was no longer interested in hiding them.</p>
<p>The attack in the early hours Friday was Iran’s first-ever use of <a href="https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-israel-war-updates-2026/card/iran-targeted-diego-garcia-base-with-ballistic-missiles-rb7MdZW1CfwRTauDYHOt?mod=article_inline">intermediate-range ballistic missiles</a>, ones that fly far enough to hit much of Europe.</p>
<p>As recently as last month, Iran’s leaders were insisting they had limited their missile ranges to the equivalent of half the distance to Diego Garcia.</p>
<p>Friday’s missiles missed their target—one failed in flight and the other disappeared after a Navy destroyer launched SM-3 interceptors at them, U.S. officials said. But Tehran’s ability to fire them demonstrated a more-aggressive posture by the regime and progress on the capacity to strike far beyond the Middle East.</p>
<p>“It shows how far the decision-making process is moving towards the extreme,” said Danny Citrinowicz, who formerly headed the Iran desk for Israeli military intelligence.</p>
<p>Now, by taking aim at Diego Garcia, a <a href="https://www.wsj.com/politics/national-security/republicans-press-trump-to-kill-u-k-plan-for-diego-garcia-military-base-af56ec26?mod=article_inline">U.S.-U.K. military base</a> about as far away as London or Paris, Tehran has created a new security reality for Europe and parts of the Pacific.</p>
<p>European countries have long worried that Iran might upgrade its existing missile arsenal to increase its range and target them.</p>
<p>Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi warned Friday on social media that the U.K. is putting British lives at risk by allowing its bases to be used in the war.</p>
<p>Following the attack, the U.K. government granted America greater access to its bases around the world to strike targets in Iran and prevent the regime from firing missiles or threatening international shipping.</p>

<p>The U.S. military had taken seriously the risk of an Iranian missile strike and positioned a guided-missile destroyer near the base, U.S. officials said. The destroyer fired SM-3 interceptors at the incoming Iranian missiles, they said.</p>
<p>“There’s sort of no going back,” Lewis said. “This is just a fact: Iran has IRBMs.”</p>
<p>The complexity of reworking an existing warhead or missile means Iran likely prepared for the attack long before the war, said Farzin Nadimi, an Iran-focused senior fellow with the Washington Institute, a U.S.-based think tank.</p>
<p>With protests, an economic crisis, the killing of top leaders and military pressure pushing the regime toward collapse, they decided on a <a href="https://www.wsj.com/world/middle-east/iran-defense-strategy-khamenei-fe9aeaf9?mod=article_inline">more-aggressive approach</a> this time in hopes of deterring the U.S. and Israel from attacking them again.</p>
<p>The decision raises the risk that Iran’s leaders might have a change of heart regarding their nuclear program as well, he said.</p>
