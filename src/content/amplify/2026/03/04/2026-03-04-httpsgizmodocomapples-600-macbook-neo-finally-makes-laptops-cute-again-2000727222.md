<p>As much as the base MacBook Air has been Apple’s relatively affordable laptop, Apple has so far ignored pleas for a truly cheap device built for internet browsing and lightweight tasks. Now, Apple has a truly cheap Mac called the MacBook Neo, with the same macOS Tahoe operating system as every one of its other laptops. It also comes in a calming suite of colors and has a chip made first for mobile—starting at $599.</p>
<p>Just as expected, the MacBook Neo comes in a few pleasing hues: indigo (blue), citrus (yellow), blush (pink), and silver. If you remember the <a href="https://gizmodo.com/most-hated-macbook-could-return-without-the-same-compromises-2000681448">long-defunct 12-inch MacBook</a>, you may be wondering what’s truly different this time. It mostly comes down to the chip. Instead of an Apple M-series processor, like the <a href="https://gizmodo.com/apple-macbook-air-m4-review-feeling-ever-so-blue-2000574154">M4 in the MacBook Air</a> and the M5 in the most recent <a href="https://gizmodo.com/14-inch-macbook-pro-m5-review-new-soul-in-an-old-body-2000677593">MacBook Pro,</a> the cheaper MacBook Neo makes use of an A18 Pro chip. That’s the same CPU found in the <a href="https://gizmodo.com/iphone-16-pro-review-2000499940">iPhone 16 Pro</a>. The new Mac also comes packed with only 8GB of RAM and a 256GB SSD<strong>. </strong>While the base model costs $599, a model with Touch ID costs $699.</p>

<p>As somebody who still misses the <a href="https://gizmodo.com/apple-iphone-14-plus-yellow-color-banana-phone-15-scree-1850196853">banana-shaded iPhone 14</a> from back in 2023, the similarly colored Mac may be my favorite shade of the bunch. The new device also harkens back to the <a href="https://gizmodo.com/the-new-ultra-skinny-macbook-heres-your-next-apple-lap-1689943298">12-inch MacBook</a> that first launched in 2015. That 12-inch design (along with the <a href="https://gizmodo.com/apples-keyboard-nightmare-is-finally-over-1843242184">hated Butterfly keyboard</a>) <a href="https://gizmodo.com/apple-12-inch-macbook-obsolete-retired-discontinued-new-1850500566">finally perished</a> in 2023. The new laptop has a 13<strong>–</strong>inch IPS LCD display.</p>

<p>Apple will be competing against other low-cost PCs and—most importantly—the Chromebook market. Google’s low-power, cloud-centric notebooks haven’t ever made a sweeping case for themselves due to limited available apps. The new MacBook has more potential simply due to the number of compatible apps already available on Apple’s existing ARM-based architecture. We still have to see for ourselves how it can run existing apps, such as the <a href="https://gizmodo.com/apples-creator-studio-review-best-case-to-bury-your-adobe-subscription-2000714651">Apple Creator Studio</a> or more intensive 3D applications.</p>
