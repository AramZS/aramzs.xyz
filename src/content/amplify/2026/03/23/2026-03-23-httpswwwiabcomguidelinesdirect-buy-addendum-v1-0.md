<p>The Interactive Advertising Bureau (IAB) has released an updated, modular legal framework designed to modernize how digital advertising agreements are structured, negotiated, and executed.</p>
<p><strong>This release includes:</strong></p>
<ul> <li><a href="https://www.iab.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IAB_Direct_Buy_Addendum_to_General_Terms_and_Conditions_v1.0_Public_Comment_Clean_February_2026.pdf"><strong>Direct Buy Addendum v1.0 (Draft in Public Comment)</strong></a><br/> Standardized terms for direct digital media buys.</li> <li><a href="https://www.iab.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IAB_General_Terms_v1.1_Public_Comment_February_2026.pdf"><strong>General Terms v1.1</strong></a><br/> A common legal baseline document for all types of digital advertising agreements.</li> <li><a href="https://www.iab.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IAB_Order_Specific_Terms_v1.0_Public_Comment_February_2026.pdf"><strong>Order-Specific Terms for IOs v1.0</strong></a><br/> A set of clauses on an as needed basis for addressing direct buy deal-specific terms such as cancellation, sponsorships, custom content, and upfront deals.</li> <li><a href="https://www.iab.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Appendix_1-_-Definitions_v1.1_Public_Comment_February_2026.pdf"><strong>Appendix 1 – Definitions v1.1 </strong></a><br/> Centralized definitions to promote consistency and reduce interpretive risk across agreements.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> The General Terms are not a standalone contract and must be used in conjunction with an applicable addendum.</p>
<p>Together, these documents establish a shared legal foundation for direct digital advertising transactions while preserving flexibility across buying models, formats, and technologies.</p>

<h3><strong>Why This Matters</strong></h3>
<p>The digital advertising ecosystem has changed faster than the contracts used to govern it.</p>
<p>Since the last major industry update in 2010, streaming and CTV, programmatic automation, AI-driven optimization, first-party data strategies, and new privacy regulations have fundamentally reshaped how advertising is bought, sold, measured, and delivered.</p>
<p>In response, companies have relied on layered custom amendments and bespoke language—creating inconsistency, longer negotiations, and operational friction.</p>
<p>This framework replaces that fragmentation with a <strong>clear, modular legal foundation</strong> that can evolve alongside the industry.</p>
<h3><strong>Who This Is For</strong></h3>
<p>This framework is designed for the full direct-buy ecosystem, including:</p>
<ul> <li>Advertisers and brands</li> <li>Media Agencies and/or teams</li> <li>Publishers and media owners</li> <li>Ad operations, legal, and finance teams</li> </ul>
<p>If you touch contracts, IOs, billing, or campaign execution, this framework is meant to reduce friction—not add to it.</p>
<h3><strong>How to Use These Terms</strong></h3>
<p>The IAB General Terms, Addenda, and Appendices are <strong>optional and flexible</strong>. Companies can adopt them in a way that best fits their contracting model.</p>
<p><strong>Common approaches include:</strong></p>
<p><strong>1. Use the framework collectively, as written</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Apply the <strong>General Terms + applicable Addendum + Appendix 1</strong> at the <strong>MSA (or equivalent) level</strong></li> <li>Use <strong>Special Terms for IOs</strong> on an <strong>IO-by-IO basis</strong></li> </ul>
<p><strong>OR</strong></p>
<p><strong>2. Incorporate selected terms into existing contracts</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Extract provisions or definitions and integrate them into current MSAs, addenda, or templates</li> </ul>
<h3><strong>How This Framework Will Evolve</strong></h3>
<p>The <strong>General Terms are designed not to evolve frequently</strong>, but as IAB develops additional <strong>Specific Terms Addenda</strong>, the General Terms may be updated to ensure alignment across the framework.</p>
<p>IAB plans to develop addenda for:</p>
<ul> <li><strong>Programmatic Platforms</strong></li> <li><strong>Measurement Providers</strong></li> </ul>
<p>This work is planned for <strong>2026–2027</strong>.</p>
<h3><strong>Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)</strong></h3>
<p><strong>What is included in this public comment release?</strong><br/>
 The Direct Buy Addendum v1.0, General Terms v1.1, Order-Specific Terms for IOs v1.0, and Appendix 1 – Definitions.</p>
<p><strong>How long is the public comment period?</strong><br/>
 45 days, from February 12 through March 31, 2026.</p>
<p><strong>How do I submit comments?</strong><br/>
 Email feedback to <strong><a href="mailto:terms-comments@iab.com">terms-comments@iab.com</a></strong>. Comments may include suggested edits, questions, or usability feedback.</p>
<p><strong>Are these terms mandatory?</strong><br/>
 No. Use of the General Terms, Addenda, and Appendices is voluntary. Companies determine whether and how to adopt them.</p>
<p><strong>Can the General Terms be used on their own?</strong><br/>
 No. The General Terms are not a standalone agreement and must be used with an applicable addendum to be operational.</p>
<p><strong>Do these documents replace existing contracts?</strong><br/>
 No. Existing agreements remain in effect. The framework may be used for new agreements, renewals, or selectively incorporated at the discretion of the parties.</p>
<p><strong>Who should review and comment?</strong><br/>
 Advertisers, agencies, publishers, platforms, ad tech providers, measurement companies, and legal, finance, and ad operations teams involved in digital advertising agreements.</p>
<p><strong>Will the General Terms change in the future?</strong><br/>
 Yes. The General Terms are designed to evolve as additional Specific Terms Addenda are developed. Updates may be informed by industry demand and feedback through IAB’s Terms &amp; Conditions Task Force to ensure the framework remains aligned with real-world use cases and market needs.</p>
<p><strong>Can Addenda be updated or revised over time?</strong><br/>
 Yes. Specific Terms Addenda may also be updated as industry practices, technologies, and business models evolve. Changes may be driven by market adoption, implementation feedback, or industry demand surfaced through the Terms &amp; Conditions Task Force. This ensures both the General Terms and Addenda remain practical, current, and fit for purpose.</p>
<p><strong>What addenda are planned next?</strong><br/>
 IAB plans to develop addenda for Programmatic Platforms and Measurement Providers in 2026–2027.</p>
