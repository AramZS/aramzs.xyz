<p>In late <strong>November</strong>, <strong>Karoline Leavitt</strong> strode into the briefing room, holding her young son in her arms. A few steps behind Leavitt was <strong>“Waddle”</strong> the turkey, who was set to receive the traditional presidential pardon, but first made a stop in the briefing room. “We have Waddle,” Leavitt exclaimed to the room full of reporters. “Come on in, Waddle!”</p>
<p>The turkey obliged, taking its spot next to the lectern in the White House briefing room as the tone in the usually serious room took on a playful tone. Reporters jokingly asked the turkey a series of questions. “Waddle, why are you getting a pardon? What did you do wrong?” one journalist asked.</p>
<p>Amid the fun, photographers snapped dozens of images of the scene. Among those many pictures of the <strong>Thanksgiving</strong> spectacle was one in particular that incensed the <strong>White House</strong>—setting off a remarkable chain of events, with the image ultimately being pulled from the <strong>AFP</strong> and <strong>Getty Images </strong>wire photo libraries, <em>Status</em> has learned.</p>
