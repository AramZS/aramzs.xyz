It’s not clear if American forces played a role. <p><p>Today was one of the most horrifying days of my life as an academic. Walking through Iran University of Science and Technology, a top-ranked public university in Iran, I was struck by the devastation. Only last month, this campus was alive with students, bustling between classrooms. Now, parts of the campus lie in ruins, classrooms shattered, hallways choked with dust and shattered glass.</p><p>I saw the offices of professors burned. A newly renovated building, where students gathered for programs, for socializing, for life, destructed.</p><p>One student, tearfully, told me: “My professor’s office was still burning a little. That’s where I used to wait for office hours. To ask questions. To appeal my grade.”</p><p>This is the same university that launched Iran’s Omid and Zafar 2 satellites, symbols of homegrown technological achievement. A week ago, one of its professors was assassinated. Yesterday, they bombed it.</p><p>From sanctions to targeted killings, to the bombing of research centers and universities, there’s a clear pattern: de-development &amp; de-industrialisation/ the systematic dismantling of a nation’s indigenous development, its industrial base, its capacity to stand on its own.</p><p>We will never forget that as the American and Zionist war criminals blatantly target universities, schools, hospitals/  assassinating professors and killing children, and after 2 years or genocice, western intellectuals are still debating whether or not to pass a symbolic, non-enforceable BDS resolution.</p><p>Photos taken by me, full report incoming.  </p></p>

<p>There are other social media descriptions of the attack, such as <a href="https://x.com/iribnews_irib/status/2038072397199491125">this X post</a> by the Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting.</p>
<p>I also found <a href="https://wanaen.com/isfahan-university-of-technology-hit-again-in-reported-attack/">one report</a> of another attack on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isfahan_University_of_Technology">the Isfahan University of Technology</a>, apparently a second strike on that campus.</p>
<p>One reason given for the the Iran University of Science and Technology attack is that the Iranian military allegedly used the campus for nuclear weapons research, <a href="https://www.universityworldnews.com/post.php?story=20260329123345440">according to University World News</a>. Another potential reason is that Iranian security forces may be staging on educational campuses, <a href="https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/iran-news/article-891362">according to the Jerusalem Post</a>. This is part of a new trend of hitting post-secondary institutions, as Israeli strikes hit two other Tehran campuses, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malek-Ashtar_University_of_Technology">Malek Ashtar University</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imam_Hossein_University">Imam Hossein Universit</a>y, <a href="https://www.universityworldnews.com/post.php?story=20260324200214750">five days ago</a>. The given reason there was “their military use by Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), as well as a space research centre.”</p>
<p>Iranian responses to the Iran University of Science and Technology strike came quickly. <a href="https://www.tasnimnews.ir/en/news/2026/03/29/3551636/attacks-on-universities-expose-true-aim-of-us-israeli-war-on-iran-spokesman">A Tasnim post</a> quotes Iranian Foreign Ministry spokeperson Esmaeil Baqaei on the attack, offering an interpretation of it:</p>
<blockquote><p>“In fact, the American-Israeli aggression against Iran continues to reveal its true objective: to cripple our country’s scientific foundation and cultural heritage by systematically targeting universities, research centers, historical monuments, and prominent scientists… Countering Iran’ ‘nuclear program’ and ‘imminent threat’ were nothing but vicious pretexts — mere fabrications designed to conceal their real intention.”</p></blockquote>
<p>Then the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) stated that Israeli and American universities in the region are now legitimate targets for Iranian responses, <a href="https://www.tasnimnews.ir/en/news/2026/03/29/3551408/iran-says-us-universities-in-region-legitimate-targets-after-enemy-attack-on-iranian-university">as per another Tasnim post</a>. I’ll quote the statement in full in case it gets taken down or altered:</p>
<blockquote><p>Warning to the criminal rulers of the US!</p> <p>The invading American-Zionist forces, by bombing Tehran University of Science and Technology, have once again targeted Iranian universities in their bombings.</p> <p>The unwise rulers of the White House should know that from now on, all universities of the occupying regime and American universities in the West Asia region will be legitimate targets for us until two universities are struck in retaliation for the destroyed Iranian universities.</p> <p>We advise all employees, professors, and students of American universities in the region, as well as residents in the surrounding areas, to stay at least one kilometer away from the mentioned universities to protect their lives.</p> <p>If the US administration wants its universities in the region not be among the two targeted for retaliation in this stage, must by 12 noon on Monday, March 30, Tehran time, issue an official statement condemning the bombing of universities.</p> <p>And if it wants its universities in the region to not be hit thereafter, it must prevent its savage allied forces from attacking universities and research centers; otherwise, the threat remains valid and will be carried out.</p> <p>And there is no victory except from Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Wise. (Quran 3:126)</p></blockquote>
<p>That’s a pretty clear threat to attack two universities in the region, unless the Trump administration apologizes, which seems unlikely.</p>
<p>Further, the Jerusalem Post <a href="https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/iran-news/article-891480">reports</a> that “[a]ccording to two Iranian civilians who talked with <em>The Post</em>, security forces have begun assembling in locations such as schools and universities in an apparent effort to deter attacks.”</p>
