<h2 data-gu-name="standfirst">Australian company’s restructuring plan to push into artificial intelligence and enterprise sales</h2>
<p>Australian software giant <a data-component="auto-linked-tag" data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/technology/atlassian">Atlassian</a> has announced it is laying off around 10% of its workforce, or roughly 1,600 positions, as part of a restructuring plan to push into artificial intelligence and enterprise sales.</p>
<p>Shares of the company rose more than 4% in extended trading on the Nasdaq.</p>
<p>The company said it expects to incur total pre-tax charges between US$225 million and US$236 million related to the layoffs and office space reductions.</p>
<p>The move comes as the company seeks to “rebalance” its resources to focus on the “future of teamwork in the AI era,” according to a regulatory filing.</p>
