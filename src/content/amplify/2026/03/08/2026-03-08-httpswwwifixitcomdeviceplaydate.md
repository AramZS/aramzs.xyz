<p>Released in late 2021 with orders shipping in 2022, the Panic Playdate is marketed as a retro, tiny handheld game system, promising to feel familiar while offering something new. Sporting the usual A and B buttons and a D-pad, the Playdate also makes use of a unique side-mounted flip-out crank mechanism that expands the possibilities of in-game controls, allowing you to “precisely dial in the action.”</p>
<p>The Playdate includes 24 free black-and-white games, a 1-year warranty, a color-matched USB-C to USB-A charging cable, and starts at $179.</p>
<p>Developed by Panic in conjunction with Teenage Engineering, the Playdate has the model number PDU-Y-01.</p>
<p><strong>CPU</strong>: 180 MHz ARM Cortex M7F (with SDK support for Lua &amp; C)</p>
<p><strong>Memory</strong>: 16 MB, 32 KB L1 cache</p>
<p><strong>Storage</strong>: 4 GB flash</p>
<p><strong>Battery:</strong> 2.74 Wh (14 days on standby clock, 8 hours active)</p>
<p><strong>Display:</strong> 2.7 inch, 400 x 200 1-bit black &amp; white</p>
<p><strong>Sound</strong>:</p>
<ul><li>Integrated mono speaker</li><li>Condenser mic + TRRS mic in</li></ul>
<p><strong>Ports:</strong></p>
<ul><li>3.5 mm headphone jack</li><li>USB-C power port</li></ul>
<p><strong>Connectivity:</strong> 802.11bgn 2.4 GHz W-Fi, Bluetooth</p>
<p><strong>Inputs:</strong></p>
<ul><li>A &amp; B buttons</li><li>D-pad</li><li>Sleep &amp; menu buttons</li><li>Crank</li></ul>
<p><strong>Dimensions:</strong> 76 x 74 x 9 mm (2.99 x 2.91 x 0.35 in)</p>
<p><strong>Misc. Features:</strong> 3-axis accelerometer</p>
