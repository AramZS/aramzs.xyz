<p>Guys, I hate to break it to you, but did you know that Donald Trump is doing bad stuff? I know — I'm as surprised as you are. But, hold on, let me explain.</p>
<p>Joe Rogan, that sentient Monster energy drink, spent part of his career hosting <em>Fear Factor,</em> where he made people eat shit; now he’s on a podcast spewing shit. <strong>But that Kermit the Frog-sounding over-roasted turkey apparently just learned how to read,</strong> <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/11/politics/joe-rogan-trump-support-epstein-iran"><strong>because he’s kind of mad at Trump about the war in Iran.</strong></a> <strong>He feels furious. He feels betrayed.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Besides using critical thinking, how could Rogan have predicted that the president who indiscriminately</strong> <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2018/jan/23/us-air-wars-trump"><strong>used air strikes against many countries during his first term</strong></a> <strong>would indiscriminately bomb countries during his second term?</strong></p>
<p>Rogan is also mad about the deportations. How could anyone have foreseen that a man who called Mexicans rapists during his first presidential campaign would support such violent deportations of immigrants during his second presidential term? <strong>How does 1 + 1 really add up to 2? Joe Rogan doesn’t know, and he’s mad about it.</strong></p>
<p>And listen, Trump being exactly who he said he was changes everything. The entire foundation of Rogan’s identity is now ruined. I <strong>mean, what’s next? Are we going to tell him that the 2020 election wasn’t stolen, Hillary Clinton isn’t lurking in the basement of a pizza restaurant trying to steal babies, turning your body into chuck roast isn’t a turn-on for women, and the FIFA Peace Prize was all a lie?!</strong></p>
<p>Of course, what else do you expect from a man who believes that 30 seconds <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=naIegDE5JxU">on Google makes him more knowledgeable about apes than an actual primatologist?</a> <strong>What do you expect from a guy who became the face of horse dewormer during a worldwide pandemic, and whose podcast is</strong> <strong><em>Fixer Upper</em></strong> <strong>but for racist comedians looking to rehab their image?</strong></p>
<p><strong>Coming soon to HGTV:</strong> <strong><em>Straight Eye for the Racist Guy</em></strong> <strong>, hosted by Joe Rogan.</strong></p>
<p>Rogan began his career as a street magician, but the biggest magic trick he pulled was convincing people to listen to him. And he’s convinced a lot of people to listen. Mostly people who wear sunglasses on the back of their heads and drive Cybertrucks and call women females and owe $5,000 to DraftKings. In Iowa, we just call them divorced dads on Bumble.</p>
<p>There is a lot of talk about Rogan souring on the Trump administration over tariffs, the war in Iran, and the Epstein files. Last month he called the FBI’s claim that there is no evidence Epstein had clients “ <a href="https://archive.is/o/RKsMe/https://youtu.be/0sMrvv53e9Y?si=hM37nvn_y_1cnGm8&amp;t=8992">the gaslightiest gaslighting sh*t I’ve ever heard in my life</a>.”</p>
<p>Rogan had Kash Patel on his podcast nine months ago. Patel assured him that the FBI was all on the up-and-up and nothing bad was happening at all. And Rogan completely believed him. Patel’s whole argument was, “Trust me, bro.” And Rogan, the man who won't believe in vaccine science, believed that. Now he feels betrayed. <strong>At this point, if you believe Kash Patel, no man nor god can help you.</strong></p>
<p>But I am not convinced Rogan will repent. <strong>First of all, that would require introspection, and can you really introspect when your brain is mostly turkey jerky? Can you think that deeply when your body has the material composition of an owl pellet? Hard to say.</strong></p>

<h3>Something I am enjoying</h3>
<p>It’s spring concert season, and that means this past week I got to see both my kids perform in their school band concerts. Both my children love music and are wonderful musicians. And with the caveat that genetics plays a role (my mom is a musician, and their dad has perfect pitch), I give full credit to their piano teacher, Ms. Lyn. We found Ms. Lyn when my daughter was 7, after we’d bounced between a couple of piano teachers. One teacher was a college student who kept canceling lessons. Another was an older woman who pulled my daughter’s hair to get her to pay attention. This teacher is so infamously awful that when I told my therapist about her, she was like, “That woman pulled my hair when I was 6 years old, too!” I remember telling Lyn that I didn’t care if my kids were concert pianists, I just wanted them to love music. That was it. To be fans of music.</p>
<p>And my kids are fans of music, thanks to Ms. Lyn, who, with her chickens and her smile, has made eight years of music lessons fly by. And also, sorry to brag, but my oldest can play at least four different instruments and is in the top band in her high school as a freshman. (It’s not called “the top band” but I love saying “SHE’S IN THE TOP BAND!” and watching her roll her eyes.) And my son, at his concert, was just such a joy to watch. He smiled the whole time he played the drums and sang, bobbing his head with the music. In my completely unbiased opinion, no kid had more pizzazz.</p>
<p>And then, I got to take them to the symphony, where we saw the orchestra play along to Looney Tunes cartoons. And my kids told me which instruments were which, and we learned about Looney Tunes composer <a href="https://slate.com/culture/2003/12/the-man-behind-the-looney-tunes.html?pay=1773257065670&amp;support_journalism=please">Carl Stalling and how, during the 1940s, Hollywood studios had their own orchestras.</a></p>
<p>And it’s so rewarding to see that after all those years of theater tickets, concert tickets, and music lessons, I actually do have two music lovers. And even though it means I have birthed two more people in this world, in addition to my mother, who will tell me my singing is flat, we can all love music together.</p>
<p>And also, Looney Tunes.</p>

<h3>Something I am enjoying</h3>
<p>It’s spring concert season, and that means this past week I got to see both my kids perform in their school band concerts. Both my children love music and are wonderful musicians. And with the caveat that genetics plays a role (my mom is a musician, and their dad has perfect pitch), I give full credit to their piano teacher, Ms. Lyn. We found Ms. Lyn when my daughter was 7, after we’d bounced between a couple of piano teachers. One teacher was a college student who kept canceling lessons. Another was an older woman who pulled my daughter’s hair to get her to pay attention. This teacher is so infamously awful that when I told my therapist about her, she was like, “That woman pulled my hair when I was 6 years old, too!” I remember telling Lyn that I didn’t care if my kids were concert pianists, I just wanted them to love music. That was it. To be fans of music.</p>
<p>And my kids are fans of music, thanks to Ms. Lyn, who, with her chickens and her smile, has made eight years of music lessons fly by. And also, sorry to brag, but my oldest can play at least four different instruments and is in the top band in her high school as a freshman. (It’s not called “the top band” but I love saying “SHE’S IN THE TOP BAND!” and watching her roll her eyes.) And my son, at his concert, was just such a joy to watch. He smiled the whole time he played the drums and sang, bobbing his head with the music. In my completely unbiased opinion, no kid had more pizzazz.</p>
<p>And then, I got to take them to the symphony, where we saw the orchestra play along to Looney Tunes cartoons. And my kids told me which instruments were which, and we learned about Looney Tunes composer <a href="https://slate.com/culture/2003/12/the-man-behind-the-looney-tunes.html?pay=1773257065670&amp;support_journalism=please">Carl Stalling and how, during the 1940s, Hollywood studios had their own orchestras.</a></p>
<p>And it’s so rewarding to see that after all those years of theater tickets, concert tickets, and music lessons, I actually do have two music lovers. And even though it means I have birthed two more people in this world, in addition to my mother, who will tell me my singing is flat, we can all love music together.</p>
<p>And also, Looney Tunes.</p>
