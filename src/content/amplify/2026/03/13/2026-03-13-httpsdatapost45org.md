<figure><img src="https://data.post45.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/jan-antonin-kolar-lRoX0shwjUQ-unsplash-scaled.jpg"/> <h1>Post45 data collective</h1> <h2>The Post45 Data Collective peer reviews and houses post-1945 literary data on an open-access website designed, hosted, and maintained by Emory University’s Center for Digital Scholarship.</h2> <a href="https://data.post45.org/our-data/">  VIEW OUR DATA  </a> <a href="https://data.post45.org/#peer-review">  HOW TO SUBMIT  </a> </figure>
<p>Submissions must adhere to the following <a href="https://data.post45.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/P45_Data_PeerReview.pdf">criteria</a> and must be accompanied by a 800-1000 word paper that addresses the following questions, as appropriate.</p>
<p><i>The language for these criteria was drawn from Katherine Bode, Jennifer Doty, Lauren F. Klein, Melanie Walsh, Cultural Analytics, Journal of Open Humanities Data, and “Datasheets for Datasets” by Timnit Gebru et. al. </i></p>
<p>How is the data relevant to post-1945 scholarship? Who might it be useful for? What could it be used for? Please suggest at least three specific uses.</p>
<p>For what purpose was the dataset created? Was there a gap that needed to be filled? Has the data been used already? Does similar or overlapping data exist publicly? If so, please describe.</p>
<p>The Post45 Data Collective standardizes the citation of datasets to make it easier for researchers to publish their data and get credit as well as <strong>recognition</strong> for their work. When you create a dataset in the Post45 Data Collective, As an open-access framework and research data repository the Post45 Data Collective is committed to helping researchers, journals, and organizations make humanities data accessible, reusable, and open (when possible), which includes implementing community accepted standards for data publication.</p>
<p>The citation standard defined here offers proper recognition to authors as well as permanent identification through the use of global, persistent identifiers in place of URLs, which can change frequently.</p>
