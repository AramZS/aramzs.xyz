<p>When<strong> Bari Weiss</strong> took the reins of<strong> CBS News </strong>in October, her message was clear: that the public had lost trust in the network and that major change was needed to win viewers back and return it to its glory days. But through a series of actions over her turbulent six-month tenure as editor in chief, it appears the proud anti-woke warrior has instead helped destroy that trust—something that is now laid bare in the network’s rapidly shrinking ratings. Indeed, new ratings data obtained by <em>Status</em> isn’t just bad for Weiss. It is catastrophic.</p>
<p>With the first quarter set to wrap at week’s end, Weiss’ relaunched<strong> “CBS Evening News” </strong>with <strong>Tony Dokoupil</strong> is on track for its lowest-rated first quarter of the 21st century in both total viewers and the advertiser-coveted 25-54 demographic, according to preliminary <strong>Nielsen</strong> ratings obtained by <em>Status</em>.
