<p>For weeks, American travelers have contended with sometimes staggering wait times and winding security lines thanks to a congressional funding impasse that forced the closure of the Department of Homeland Security. Thousands of Transportation Security Administration employees have been working without without pay for more than a month, prompting mass staff callouts at the nation’s major airports.</p>
<p>Now, as the shutdown enters its 37th day with no apparent end in sight, agents from Immigration and Customs Enforcement began arriving to bolster staffing at airports across the country. Over the weekend, President <a href="https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/will-donald-trump-be-able-to-end-his-iran-war.html">Donald Trump</a> announced plans to deploy immigration agents to U.S. airports beginning Monday, blaming Democrats for the more than a monthlong closure of DHS. “What they have done to the Department of Homeland Security, our fantastic TSA Officers, and, most importantly, the great people of our Country, is an absolute disgrace. If the Democrats do not allow for Just and Proper Security at our Airports, and elsewhere throughout our Country, ICE will do the job far better than ever done before!” he wrote on<a href="https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116268396341038197"> TruthSocial</a>.</p>
<p>As passengers set out to catch their flights Monday morning, images and videos began to circulate of armed ICE agents in bulletproof vests stationed throughout various airports. The<a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/22/us/politics/ice-airports-homan-trump.html?smid=url-share"> New York <em>Times</em></a> reports that the federal officers are set to be deployed to at least 14 different airports, including John F. Kennedy International and La Guardia Airports in New York, Chicago-O’Hare International Airport, Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport, and Philadelphia International Airport. On Monday, border czar <a href="https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/homan-pulls-700-ice-cbp-agents-from-minnesota-drawdown.html">Tom Homan</a> indicated that number will rise, <a href="https://x.com/KitMaherCNN/status/2036117017808273566">telling reporters</a>, “There will be more.”</p>
<p>In one image captured by Reuters, a group of ICE agents can be seen standing near a TSA checkpoint in Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, the busiest airport in the country, which has been experiencing wait times of at least three hours in recent days.</p>

<p>Trump <a href="https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116278600245530358">said</a> he would “greatly appreciate” if the immigration agents should refrain from wearing masks while conducting their airport duties, a distinct shift from his support of the federal officers covering the faces during their deployments in American cities and while conducting enforcement operations. The agency has come under fire throughout Trump’s second term following allegations of aggressive tactics and the killing of two Minneapolis residents and U.S. citizens, <a href="https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/fbi-investigation-minneapolis-ice-shooting-renee-good-hopelessly-compromised.html">Renee Good </a>and <a href="https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/alex-pretti-died-for-the-idea-of-america.html">Alex Pretti</a>, by ICE agents after the administration surged personnel to Minnesota as part of a targeted operation.</p>
<p>But as Trump touted the agents’ readiness, <a href="https://www.cbsnews.com/news/dhs-officials-trump-ice-agents-airport-security-tsa/">CBS News</a> reported Sunday that the president’s order caught officials at ICE off guard, forcing them to rush to act upon his directive. In an interview on <a href="https://x.com/atrupar/status/2035751095520100617">Fox News</a>, Homan signaled that, while planning was still under way, agents would likely be assisting with security functions at the airports and not typical TSA actions like X-ray screenings.</p>
<p>“There’s a lot of things that TSA does that doesn’t require specialized training. Like, you have exit lanes where people leave the airports. Now, people try to enter those exit lanes; that’s a security breach. Well, an ICE agent can maintain those exits and that allows a TSA officer to go back to screening to move people through quicker,” Homan said. “ICE can check identification before people enter the screening area.”</p>
<p>The number of TSA employees calling out of work amid weeks of non-payment has steadily grown with DHS reporting that more than 3,450 TSA workers called out across the country Sunday. The agency said major airports experienced significant call out rates that day including 42.3 percent at Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport 39.1 percent at George Bush Intercontinental Airport in Houston, Texas as well as 38.1 percent of TSA employees at Baltimore/Washington International Airport in Maryland, per <a href="https://x.com/NBCNews/status/2036108739908943932">NBC News</a>.</p>
<p>But the head of the federal union that represents TSA workers condemned Trump’s plan to using ICE agents to fill gaps left by the ongoing staffing shortages, saying the immigration officers lack the training needed for the TSA’s unique role.</p>
<p>“ICE agents are not trained or certified in aviation security. TSA officers spend months learning to detect explosives, weapons, and threats specifically designed to evade detection at checkpoints — skills that require specialized instruction, hands-on practice, and ongoing recertification. You cannot improvise that,” Everett Kelley, the national president of the American Federation of Government Employees, said in a<a href="https://www.afge.org/publication/afge-president-everett-kelley-slams-president-trumps-threat-to-deploy-ice-to-airports-to-perform-passenger-security-functions/"> statement </a>Sunday. “Putting untrained personnel at security checkpoints does not fill a gap. It creates one.”</p>
<p>The Trump administration’s deployment of ICE agents is likely a tactical move intended to up the pressure on Democrats in Congress who have opposed the funding of DHS as they seek more reforms to federal immigration operations. But while <a href="https://x.com/SenSchumer/status/2035786498818146724">Democratic leadership</a> has condemned the use of immigration agents in airports, it’s unclear if the decision is likely to move the needle as funding negotiations continue.</p>
<p><a href="https://punchbowl.news/archive/32326-am/">Punchbowl News </a>reported that Senate Majority Leader <a href="https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/unlike-mcconnell-thune-will-be-trumps-willing-accomplice.html">John Thune</a> proposed funding all aspects of DHS beside ICE with the goal of addressing concerns about that agency in a separate reconciliation bill, telling the president that both Democrats and Republicans were open to supporting such a measure, which would resume TSA paychecks. But the outlet’s sources say Trump rejected the idea, urging Republicans to keep fighting Democrats over the matter and to skip their upcoming recess to do so. The president is also <a href="https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116275668825285445">calling</a> on Republicans to pass the SAVE America Act, a controversial voter identification bill, before making any potential deals with Democrats. Thune, however, has <a href="https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116275668825285445">expressed reservations</a> with this strategy, suggesting the voting bill is unlikely to pass anytime soon.</p>
<p>It’s not yet known how long ICE agents will be patrolling American airports, but the president is already considering additional steps. “I want to thank ICE because they stepped in so strongly. They’ll do great. And if that’s not enough, I’ll bring in the National Guard,” Trump<a href="https://x.com/atrupar/status/2036082865759309940"> told </a>reporters Monday. “We’re not gonna have the Democrats destroy our country.”</p>
