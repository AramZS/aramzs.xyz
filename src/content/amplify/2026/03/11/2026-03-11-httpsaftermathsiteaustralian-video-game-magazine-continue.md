<p>The sudden<a href="https://aftermath.site/kotaku-australia-closed-layoffs/"> <u>closure of Kotaku AU in 2024</u></a>, along with other shutterings, layoffs and downsizing, has left video games media in Australia reeling. A country that<a href="https://ncm.org.au/knowledge/australia-online-the-rise-and-fall-of-local-video-game-websites?ref=aftermath.site"> <u>was once home to multiple magazines, websites and communities</u></a> finds itself facing 2026 with almost no local, specialised written coverage <em>at all</em>.</p>
<p>In an attempt to do something about that, a couple of veterans (<strong>disclaimer</strong>: we know both of them, as you're about to see) have decided to skip launching a website and go right back to the source: a video games magazine. Called CONTINUE, <a href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/continuemagazine/continue-a-defiant-new-videogames-magazine?ref=aftermath.site">it's currently up on Kickstarter looking for enough money to get at least one issue together</a>, pay for illustration and provide <em>some </em>kind of space for local games media (and criticism especially) to get the kind of exposure and reception it deserves.</p>
<figure><div class="rw-embed-wrapper"><embed src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/j_fQtpu33ys?feature=oembed" type="video/mp4"/></div></figure>
<p>Overseen by former Kotaku Australia boss Mark Serrels and local journalist Jackson Ryan (<a href="https://aftermath.site/gameshub-clickout-media-seo-gambling-ai/"><u>who wrote this month's incredible 8-months-long investigation into Clickout Media, which began with the demise of an Australian games site</u></a>), the first issue will feature work from both Serrels and Ryan, along with<a href="https://www.youtube.com/charalanahzard?ref=aftermath.site"> <u>Alanah Pearce</u></a>,<a href="https://ally-hennessy-art.com/?ref=aftermath.site"> <u>Ally McLean</u></a>,<a href="https://dangolding.com/?ref=aftermath.site"> <u>Dan Golding</u></a>,<a href="https://www.instagram.com/r.ubyinnes/?ref=aftermath.site"> <u>Ruby McInnes</u></a> and more.</p>
<p>"With CONTINUE we want to create a magazine that sits at the intersection between the immense and incomparable <a href="https://apwot.com/?ref=aftermath.site"><em><u>A Profound Waste of Time</u></em></a> and the excellent reportage found in magazines like <a href="https://www.themonthly.com.au/?ref=aftermath.site"><em><u>The Monthly</u></em></a> or <a href="https://www.newyorker.com/?ref=aftermath.site"><em><u>The New Yorker</u></em></a><em><u>"</u></em>, Ryan says of the project. And while the magazine will come from Australia and feature Australian writers (and artists), the ultimate goal is that it won't be <em>just </em>about Australian stuff. <strong>"</strong>We won't do traditional reviews or explainers and there won't be much in the way of industry news”, Ryan says. “Our focus is on story<strong>. </strong>We want to focus on Australian games and Australian developers and while our first issue focuses on our home, games are global — we'll be chasing stories from across the planet."</p>
<p>You can <a href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/continuemagazine/continue-a-defiant-new-videogames-magazine?ref=aftermath.site">check out the Kickstarter page for the magazine here</a>.</p>
