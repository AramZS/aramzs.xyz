<h1>Justice Department Probes Iran’s Use of Binance to Evade Sanctions</h1>
<h2>Investigation focuses on money flowing through crypto platform to network backing terror groups, including Yemen’s Houthi militants</h2>
<p>By <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/angus-berwick">Angus Berwick</a> and <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/patricia-kowsmann">Patricia Kowsmann</a></p>
<p>March 11, 2026 7:00 am ET</p>
<figure>
<img alt="Binance Holdings Ltd. booth during the Australian Crypto Convention in Sydney." src="https://images.wsj.net/im-98715046?width=700&amp;height=467"/>
<figcaption>Binance is the world’s largest crypto exchange. Brent Lewin/Bloomberg News</figcaption>
</figure>
<p>The Justice Department is investigating Iran’s use of Binance to evade U.S. sanctions.</p>
<p>The probe follows the crypto exchange’s <a href="https://www.wsj.com/finance/currencies/binance-iran-sanctions-financing-staff-b1648133?mod=article_inline">dismantling of an internal investigation</a> into more than $1 billion that flowed through the platform to a network funding Iran-backed terror groups, according to company documents and people familiar with the matter.</p>
<p>Officials have contacted people with knowledge of the Iranian transactions to seek interviews and gather evidence, some of the people said. The Wall Street Journal couldn’t determine whether the Justice Department is investigating Binance itself for potential misconduct, or solely the customers on its platform.</p>
<p>The inquiry puts the world’s largest crypto exchange back in the legal spotlight after its founder Changpeng Zhao, known as CZ, <a href="https://www.wsj.com/finance/currencies/trump-binance-changpeng-zhao-pardon-7509bd63?mod=article_inline">received a pardon</a> from President Trump in October.</p>
<p>Binance <a href="https://www.wsj.com/finance/inside-binances-guilty-plea-and-the-biggest-fine-in-crypto-history-e959fca0?mod=article_inline">pleaded guilty in 2023</a> to violating U.S. anti-money-laundering and sanctions laws, paying a $4.3 billion fine and agreeing to operate under U.S. oversight. Zhao pleaded guilty to a related charge and spent four months in jail.</p>
<p>The Treasury Department-appointed monitor overseeing the company’s compliance program also recently requested that the exchange provide information about the Iranian transactions, including about a business partner that sent much of the money, according to people familiar with the requests.</p>
