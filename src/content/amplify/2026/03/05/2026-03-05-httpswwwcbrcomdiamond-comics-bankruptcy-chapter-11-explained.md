<p><a href="https://www.cbr.com/tag/diamond-comic-distributors/">Diamond Comic Distributors, Inc.</a> has officially filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy.</p>
<p>Per <a href="https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250114676220/en/Diamond-Comic-Distributors-Files-Voluntary-Petition-for-Relief-Under-Chapter-11?fbclid=IwZXh0bgNhZW0CMTAAAR0LBCAj3eUdv2gG5cI91-7keQ0C3X4Y9bAN1UppTyfjHDGAp2HhCywWmCU_aem_XKo8Ua59C2fpU3qu5qrynw">Business Wire</a>, Diamond Comic Distributors, Inc., one of North America's largest comic book distributors, has filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy. The petition for bankruptcy seeks the United States Bankruptcy Court for the District of Maryland to facilitate the restructuring of the business, which has received a $39 million bid from an affiliate of Universal Distribution for the Alliance Game Distributors. Currently, Diamond is still owned by Geppi Family Enterprises, who also own Alliance Game Distributors, Diamond Book Distributors, Diamond UK, Diamond Select Toys, Gemstone Publishing, E. Gerber Products, Diamond International Galleries, Hake's Americana &amp; Collectibles, Morphy's Auctions, <em>Baltimore</em> magazine, and its own branded entertainment museum.</p>

<p>This news comes on the heels of a slate of publishers leaving their exclusivity agreements with the company behind for a wider share of distributors across North America. The most recent company to do so, BOOM! Studios, cut their exclusivity contract with Diamond not long after Image, Marvel, DC, IDW, and Dark Horse all made the same change. Diamond Comic Distributors President Chuck Parker released a letter to various comic book stores and publishers confirming the news of the company's bankruptcy filing, as well as their intent to sell the company and its sister companies to Universal in the following months. The letter, as per <a href="https://bleedingcool.com/comics/diamond-comic-distributors-declares-bankruptcy/#google_vignette">Bleeding Cool</a>, reads:</p>
<p>"Dear Valued Customer, I'm writing to share one of the most challenging messages of my career. Earlier today, Diamond made the difficult but necessary decision to file for Chapter 11 bankruptcy. This step is intended to stabilize our financial foundation and protect the most vital aspects of our business. This decision was not made lightly, and I understand that this news may be as difficult to hear as it is for me to share. The Diamond leadership team and I have worked tirelessly to avoid this outcome but the financial challenges we face have left us with no other viable option. Chapter 11 will provide us with the opportunity to restructure and address our financial obligations. This process will likely include the sale of key assets of Diamond Comic Distributors. We have already received a commitment from Universal Distribution to purchase Alliance Game Distributors coupled with a signed Letter of Intent to purchase Diamond UK, and we remain committed to pursuing offers for all Diamond lines of business. What does this mean for you? I want to reassure you that, with secured financing in place to support our operations, Diamond Comic Distributors is committed to continuing business as usual throughout this process. Our plan is to continue fulfilling orders in a complete and timely fashion and we are working hard to minimize any disruptions to your orders. We will be as transparent as possible during this process and will keep you informed of significant developments. If you have any questions, please reference the Retailer FAQs below, also available on our Retailer FAQs page on the Retailer Services Website. With further inquiries, as always, you can submit a ticket via our online Retailer Support Center or contact us at customersupport@diamondcomics.com. Thank you for your support. We are committed to your business and ensuring that your needs are met as we navigate this process."</p>

<p>Diamond's declaration of bankruptcy was heralded by the closure of the company's Plattsburgh distribution center in early December, which came with little to no warning. Between the fact that the Plattsburgh location served as a central piece of Diamond's infrastructure and that the closure came just weeks before the height of the holiday season, publishers, store owners, and readers were all left with cause for concern. <a href="https://www.cbr.com/diamond-comics-delays-explained/">At the time, Diamond's Chris Powell stated</a>, "This process is not working as it needs to, obviously, but modifying it takes time... We must make changes and test them with live data and shipments while trying to minimize the impact on retailers."</p>
