<body><figure><img alt="The Stargate AI data center in Abilene, Texas." src="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/iDiXu.lWSg1c/v0/-1x-1.webp"/><figcaption>The Stargate AI data center in Abilene, Texas. / Photographer: Kyle Grillot/Bloomberg</figcaption></figure><div><p><a href="bbg://securities/ORCL%20US%20Equity/FA">Oracle Corp.</a> and <a href="">OpenAI</a> have scrapped plans to expand a flagship artificial intelligence data center in Texas after negotiations dragged over financing and OpenAI’s changing needs.</p><p>The collapsed talks created an opening for <a href="bbg://securities/META%20US%20Equity/FA">Meta Platforms Inc.</a> to step in and consider leasing the planned expansion site in Abilene, Texas, from developer Crusoe, according to people familiar with the matter. <a href="">Nvidia Corp.</a>, the leading AI chipmaker, helped facilitate Meta’s discussions with the developer, said the people, who asked not to be identified because the talks are private.</p><p>The shifting plans underscore the complexity of building out AI data centers, which are expected to cost in the tens of billions of dollars and require cooperation from a wide swath of partners.</p><p>The campus being developed by <a href="">Crusoe</a> in Abilene is part of the highly publicized Stargate project, which was announced last year at the White House with President <a href="">Donald Trump</a>. While the 1,000-acre site continues to be built, and several parts are up and running, Oracle and OpenAI elected not to go forward with tentative plans to lease a large expansion, the people said.</p><p>Oracle and OpenAI are using Nvidia’s AI semiconductors at the Stargate site. With Crusoe seeking a tenant, Nvidia became involved to ensure its products would still fill the expanded data center rather than that of rival <a href="">Advanced Micro Devices Inc.</a>, said the people. Nvidia paid a $150 million deposit to Crusoe and began helping court Meta as a tenant for the expansion, the people said.</p><p>Oracle <a href="https://openai.com/index/stargate-advances-with-partnership-with-oracle/">agreed</a> last July to develop 4.5 gigawatts of data center capacity for OpenAI. That deal remains on track, and the companies have announced a number of projects in other locations, such as one near Detroit owned by Related Digital.</p><p>Intense computing power needed to train and deploy AI models has led to a boom in data center projects of unprecedented scale. Oracle has transformed its business to focus on filling these demands for clients such as OpenAI and Elon Musk’s xAI. Social networking giant Meta has been <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-28/meta-says-2026-spending-will-blow-past-analysts-estimates">spending lavishly on AI infrastructure</a> for its products, and has projected capital expenditures of as much as $135 billion in 2026 alone. Nvidia is the leading maker of chips for AI workloads.</p><p>The Crusoe-owned data center in Abilene is <a href="https://arrington.house.gov/uploadedfiles/final_oracle_oai_data_center_fact_sheet_092225b.pdf">among</a> the highest-profile yet announced. Oracle has been rapidly filling buildings on the site with servers, which are used by OpenAI to train and deploy its products. Oracle, Crusoe, and OpenAI had been talking since the middle of 2025 about expanding the facility from 1.2 gigawatts to about 2.0 gigawatts. A gigawatt is akin to the capacity from one nuclear reactor and can provide electricity at any one point to roughly 750,000 houses.</p><p>The negotiations stretched on and were complicated by financing and OpenAI’s often-changing demand forecasting before they fell apart, the people said.</p><p>Read More: <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2025-05-20/inside-stargate-ai-data-center-from-openai-and-softbank">Inside the First Stargate AI Data Center</a></p><p>In addition, relations between Oracle and Crusoe have been strained by reliability issues at the site. Earlier this year, data center buildings went offline for days due to winter weather affecting some of the liquid cooling machinery, according to people familiar with the incident.</p><p>Both companies said their relationship remains strong and the Oracle-leased site in Abilene is progressing quickly.</p><p>“We are very proud of our relationship and our progress in bringing capacity online,” Oracle said in a statement.</p><p>“Together, Crusoe and Oracle are operating in lockstep to deliver one of the world’s largest AI factories in Abilene,” Crusoe said in a statement. “Our collaboration can deliver massive-scale infrastructure faster than anyone else in the industry.” </p><p>Meta and OpenAI declined to comment. Nvidia didn’t respond to requests for comment.</p><p>Negotiations between Meta and Crusoe for the expansion of the Abilene location remain active and could change, the people said. Meta currently is working on several large data centers in <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-16/blue-owl-seals-largest-private-capital-deal-for-meta-s-ai-growth">Louisiana</a> and <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-11/meta-to-spend-more-than-10-billion-on-indiana-based-data-center">Indiana</a>. Last month, Meta <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/meta-to-spend-tens-of-billions-of-dollars-on-amd-gear-buy-stock">struck a deal</a> to deploy 6 gigawatts worth of gear from AMD.</p></div><div><ol></ol></div></body>
