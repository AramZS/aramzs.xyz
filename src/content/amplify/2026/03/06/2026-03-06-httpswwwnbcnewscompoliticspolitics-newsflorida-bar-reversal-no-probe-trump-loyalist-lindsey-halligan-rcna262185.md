  In reversal, Florida Bar says it is not investigating Trump loyalist Lindsey
  Halligan

<p>The Florida Bar said Friday it is not investigating former Justice Department attorney <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/justice-department/lindsey-halligan-not-employed-justice-department-us-attorney-rcna255969">Lindsey Halligan</a>, walking back an earlier statement saying a <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/justice-department/trump-loyalist-lindsey-halligan-faces-probe-florida-bar-actions-doj-rcna262004">probe was underway</a>.</p>

<p>In early February, a Florida Bar official responded to a request for a probe into the Trump loyalist over her time in the administration by writing, “We already have an investigation pending.”</p>
<p>A day after NBC News and other outlets reported on the letter Thursday, the Florida Bar said that while there is a “monitoring file,” there is no investigation into Halligan.</p>
<p>“In response to an inquiry from a complainant, The Florida Bar wrote a letter to the complainant erroneously stating that there is a pending Bar investigation of member Lindsey Halligan,” a Florida Bar spokeswoman said in a statement Friday. “There is no such pending Bar investigation of Lindsay Halligan.”</p>
<p>The bar said it received a complaint against Halligan and then followed what it called standard practice by “monitoring the ongoing legal proceedings underlying the complaint.”</p>
<p>The complaint to the Florida Bar came from the Campaign for Accountability, a nonprofit watchdog group. The group said that by falsely claiming to be a U.S. attorney, Halligan committed a variety of ethical violations.</p>
<p>Halligan, who had no prosecutorial experience before leading <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/justice-department/judge-dismisses-cases-james-comey-letitia-james-finding-prosecutor-was-rcna244775">unsuccessful cases</a> against former FBI Director James Comey and New York Attorney General Letitia James, <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/justice-department/judge-posts-job-opening-top-prosecutor-spot-doj-claims-lindsey-halliga-rcna254982">left her role </a>as interim U.S. attorney for the Eastern District of Virginia in January after a judge said she <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/justice-department/judge-posts-job-opening-top-prosecutor-spot-doj-claims-lindsey-halliga-rcna254982">was “masquerading” as the district’s top federal prosecutor.</a></p>

<p>The Campaign for Accountability <a href="https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/26222400-cfa-fl-va-bar-complaint-lindsey-halligan/">filed complaints </a>with both the Florida and the Virginia bars in November, and it <a href="https://campaignforaccountability.org/watchdog-renews-lindsey-halligan-bar-complaint-following-court-rulings/">followed up </a>with the Florida Bar last month.</p>
<p>Attorney General Pam Bondi, who previously served as Florida’s attorney general, criticized the earlier news reports of an investigation.</p>
<p>“The Florida Bar ‘investigation’ of Lindsey Halligan is totally fake news,” she said Friday <a href="https://x.com/AGPamBondi/status/2030036812618490203?s=20">in a post on X</a>. “Lindsey not only did nothing wrong — she did a great job!”</p>
<p>Asked for comment on the Florida Bar’s reversal, Halligan responded to NBC News Friday by writing, “Where’s my apology?”</p>
<p>She also sent a screenshot of a <a href="https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump">Trump's Truth Social</a> account where he reposted Bondi's defense of Halligan.</p>
