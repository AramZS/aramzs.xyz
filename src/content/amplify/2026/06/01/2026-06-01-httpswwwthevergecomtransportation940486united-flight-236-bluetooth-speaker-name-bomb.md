<p>United flight 236 from Newark to Palma de Mallorca on Saturday night was forced to <a href="https://x.com/flightradar24/status/2060877268570771693">turn around</a> just an hour after takeoff due to <a href="https://x.com/Dirt_Diver_24_7/status/2060965996907876464?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E2060965996907876464%7Ctwgr%5Ee5be10b3ef7f4157491accf21d8d3598bd28c84a%7Ctwcon%5Es1_&amp;ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fviewfromthewing.com%2Funited-flight-to-spain-turns-back-to-newark-after-teens-bluetooth-speaker-named-bomb%2F">security concerns</a> around a Bluetooth signal. <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/unitedairlines/comments/1tsk81w/ua_236_the_bluetooth_flight/">Multiple</a> <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/unitedairlines/comments/1tse6mq/ua_flight_turn_bluetooth_off_or_were_turning/">Redditors</a> claimed to be on the flight and reported that the crew repeatedly requested passengers to turn off their Bluetooth. According to one poster, the crew issued a one-minute warning, saying that two devices were still active.</p>
<p>One Redditor reported flight attendants making comments like, “This little joke is ruining it for everyone.”</p>
<p>An archived recording from Air Traffic Control (embedded below) confirms that the root of the issue was the name of a discoverable Bluetooth speaker.</p>
<blockquote><p>“There’s a security detail out there. Someone had a Bluetooth speaker, and they named it a certain four-letter word. So they have to inspect the whole aircraft, including the cargo area, and the passengers have to evacuate.”</p></blockquote>
<figure><audio controls=""> <source src="https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/05/KEWR-Ramp-Company-May-31-2026-0200Z.mp3" type="audio/mp3"/> </audio></figure>
<p>While the recording does not explicitly confirm the <a href="https://airlive.net/emergency/2026/05/31/united-flight-turned-around-over-atlantic-as-a-boy-named-his-bluetooth-device-bomb/">speculation</a> that the Bluetooth name in question was “<a href="https://viewfromthewing.com/united-flight-to-spain-turns-back-to-newark-after-teens-bluetooth-speaker-named-bomb/">bomb</a>,” it would certainly make sense given the response from the crew and security personnel on the ground. It also serves as a friendly reminder that what you think is a <a href="https://www.theverge.com/circuitbreaker/2016/12/22/14063842/samsung-galaxy-note-7-virgin-america-flight-sfo">clever WiFi</a> or Bluetooth name probably isn’t.</p>
