<p>On Monday evening, <strong>Bill Owens</strong>, the well-respected former executive producer of <strong>“60 Minutes”</strong> who resigned from his position last year citing corporate interference, boarded the <strong>Cornucopia Majesty</strong>, a 210-foot mega-yacht docked on the west side of <strong>Manhattan</strong>, not too far from <strong>CBS News’ New York </strong>offices. Owens, dressed in a grey suit with a light blue shirt, sans tie, was on the vessel to accept the <strong>Gabe Pressman Truth to Power Award</strong> at the <strong>New York Press Club’s </strong>annual dinner.</p>
<p>"These remarks were written last night, and then <strong>Scott Pelley</strong> happened today," Owens quipped at the outset of his acceptance speech.</p>
