<p>First on LateNighter: Preliminary ratings are in for <a href="https://latenighter.com/tag/byron-allen/">Byron Allen</a>’s 11:35 p.m. <em><a href="https://latenighter.com/tag/comics-unleashed/">Comics Unleashed</a></em> debut on CBS Friday night. As for how the show performed… well, it depends on how you look at it.</p>
<p>According to initial panel-only Nielsen Live+Same Day ratings data, the first half-hour of the show, which was a first-run episode, drew a national audience average of 995,000 total viewers and 116,000 viewers in the 18–49 demo.</p>
<p>The show’s second half-hour—a repeat from September 2025—drew 600,000 total viewers and 55,000 viewers in the demo.</p>
<p>Against the prior night’s <em><a href="https://latenighter.com/tag/the-late-show-with-stephen-colbert/">Late Show with Stephen Colbert</a></em> finale—which logged a series high of 6.7 million total viewers and 1.1 million demo viewers by the same ratings measure—<em>Comics Unleashed</em> was down 85% in total viewers and-95% among younger viewers. Versus the previous week’s <em>Late Show</em> repeat in the same slot, the show fell -46% in total viewers and 65% in the demo.</p>
<p>The show’s debut also wasn’t able to beat its 11:35 competitors. A first-run episode of NBC’s <em><a href="https://latenighter.com/tag/the-tonight-show-starring-jimmy-fallon/">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></em>—which aired a half-hour later than usual on the East Coast due to an NBA overrun earlier in the evening—averaged 1.5 million total viewers Friday night and 319,000 viewers in the demo, while a repeat of ABC’s <em><a href="https://latenighter.com/tag/jimmy-kimmel-live/">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></em> drew 1.6 million total viewers and 208,000 in the demo.</p>
<p>That’s the national picture. Locally, Allen Media Group is telling a different story.</p>
<p>According to ratings data released by Allen’s company, <em>Comics Unleashed</em> outperformed Fallon and/or Kimmel in more than two dozen local markets Friday night, giving Allen a more favorable set of numbers to tout as his long-running comedy showcase begins its new CBS run.</p>

<p>Whether the show’s ratings performance was affected by Friday being Allen’s 11:35 debut, from Kimmel being in repeats and Fallon airing later than usual, or some combination of the above remains to be seen. Also still to be seen is how many viewers <em>Comics Unleashed</em> and its competitors add once delayed-viewing data comes in over the next several days.</p>
<p>It’s worth noting that <em>Comics Unleashed</em> is produced at a fraction of the cost of a traditional late-night show, and is built on an entirely different financial model. Allen pays CBS for the time and sells the commercial spots on the show to advertisers directly.</p>
<p>Though Friday’s episode marked the 11:35 p.m. debut for <em>Comics Unleashed</em> on CBS, viewers saw an otherwise normal episode of the long-running series. Allen <a href="https://latenighter.com/news/byron-allen-comics-unleashed-cbs-1135-debut/">did mark the occasion</a> during the show’s first commercial break, appearing from the show’s set to pay tribute to the late-night figures who came before him—and to call out one of his sponsors, Procter &amp; Gamble, which appeared to subsidize the message.</p>
<p>Allen Media Group’s 12:37am entry, <em><a href="https://latenighter.com/tag/funny-you-should-ask/">Funny You Should Ask</a></em>, also made its official CBS debut Friday, coming in behind a time-delayed <em><a href="https://latenighter.com/tag/late-night-with-seth-meyers/">Late Night with Seth Meyers</a></em>.</p>
