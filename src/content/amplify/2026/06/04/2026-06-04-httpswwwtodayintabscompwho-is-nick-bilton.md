<p>Yesterday CBS’s Temu Leni Riefenstahl <a href="https://www.status.news/p/60-minutes-bari-weiss-firings-cecilia-vega-sharyn-alfonsi"><strong>Bari Weiss</strong></a><a href="https://www.status.news/p/60-minutes-bari-weiss-firings-cecilia-vega-sharyn-alfonsi"> fired everyone at 60 Minutes</a> who had <a href="https://www.showbiz411.com/2026/05/28/cbs-lets-bari-weiss-gut-60-minutes-like-a-fish-officially-fires-sharyn-alfonsi-replaces-executive-producer-with-writer-from-the-idol">demonstrated they were willing to stand up to her and her paymasters</a> <strong>David Ellison</strong>, his daddy <strong>Larry Ellison</strong>, and the Ellisons’ daddy <strong>Donald Trump</strong>. Then Weiss <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/28/business/media/nick-bilton-60-minutes-bari-weiss.html">announced her new pick to lead the show: </a><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/28/business/media/nick-bilton-60-minutes-bari-weiss.html"><strong>Nick Bilton</strong></a>.</p>
<p>The two reactions to this news were either <em>“who?”</em> or <em>“him?”</em> depending on how closely you followed tech journalism in the twenty-teens. If you weren’t following it, good call! Luckily you know a guy who was following it and regrettably that is me. Let’s visit the archives.</p>

<h2>Who is Nick Bilton?</h2>
<p>Bilton was born at the <em>New York Times</em> and grew up as “a design editor in the newsroom and a researcher in the research and development labs,” which are real jobs <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Bilton">according to Wikipedia</a>, before he became a <em>Times </em>technology columnist writing asinine takes like “<a href="https://archive.nytimes.com/bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/07/16/the-pros-and-cons-of-a-surveillance-society/">what if Trayvon Martin had been wearing Google glasses when he was murdered</a>” and “<a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2015/03/19/style/could-wearable-computers-be-as-harmful-as-cigarettes.html?smid=tw-nytstyles&amp;_r=1">is your smartwatch giving you CANCER</a>?” The latter was <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2015/3/18/8252087/cell-phones-cancer-risk-tumor-bilton-new-york-times">roasted so hard by </a><a href="https://www.theverge.com/2015/3/18/8252087/cell-phones-cancer-risk-tumor-bilton-new-york-times"><em>The Verge</em></a><a href="https://www.theverge.com/2015/3/18/8252087/cell-phones-cancer-risk-tumor-bilton-new-york-times">’s </a><a href="https://www.theverge.com/2015/3/18/8252087/cell-phones-cancer-risk-tumor-bilton-new-york-times"><strong>Russell Brandom</strong></a> that it accreted a full page of editorial notes and corrections walking back the entire premise of the piece.</p>
<p>In 2013 Bilton <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/nov/13/hatching-twitter-nick-bilton-review">published a book about the founding of Twitter</a> and an anonymous hater collected some of the clunkiest sentences <a href="https://nickcantwrite.tumblr.com/">in a Tumblr titled “Nick Can’t Write.”</a> Bilton was a pioneer of the kind of baffling metaphor that now plagues so much A.I. writing, <a href="https://nickcantwrite.tumblr.com/post/69477708284/crowded-car">such as</a>:</p>
<p>❝</p>
<p>It was an altogether impossible task, like asking a mechanic to figure out how to replace the engine of a moving car filled with 1.4 million passengers.</p>
<p>In “<a href="https://www.fastcompany.com/3038110/today-in-tabs-the-seventh-stage-of-grief-is-tabs">Nick Bilton’s series of technology columns for idiots</a>” he was also ahead of his time in producing the kind of aggressively vapid A.I. doom-marketing that has since become omnipresent. <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2014/11/06/fashion/artificial-intelligence-as-a-threat.html">In a 2014 piece about the risks of out of control machine super-intelligence, he wrote</a>:</p>
<p>❝</p>
<p>Before we get into what could possibly go wrong, let me first explain what artificial intelligence is. Actually, skip that. I’ll let someone else explain it: Grab an iPhone and ask Siri about the weather or stocks. Or tell her “I’m drunk.” Her answers are artificially intelligent.</p>
<p>Ok! You might expect him nail down a little more specifically what he means by “A.I.” but that’s not Bilton’s vibe. Instead he barrels ahead with speculation about a hypothetical cancer-curing medical robot, “self-replicating nanobots,” and autonomous weapons, apparently unaware that the first real victims of A.I. would turn out to be <a href="https://www.todayintabs.com/p/the-kind-of-newsletter-that-made-benches-become-men">writers of baffling metaphor</a>.</p>
<p>In 2016 Bilton left his childhood home, <em>The New York Times</em>, and went off to <em>Vanity Fair</em> where they let him <a href="https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2016/09/nick-bilton-on-theranos">pretend he discovered the Theranos fraud</a> that <strong>John Carreyrou</strong> actually discovered, and ask “<a href="https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2017/01/will-mark-zuckerberg-be-our-next-president">Will Mark Zuckerberg Be Our Next President?</a>” You can tell that’s a serious question because the first sentence of the subhed is: “It’s a serious question.” <a href="https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2023/09/artificial-intelligence-industry-future">He returned to A.I. again in 2023 seemingly for the first time, asking the same questions he had asked in 2014</a> but this time about technologies “most of which have been realized in just under six months.” Which six months did he mean, of the intervening decade? Who knows. Bilton’s post-<em>Times</em> style got so florid and overwrought that <a href="https://www.todayintabs.com/p/why-is-nick-bilton-so-obsessed-with">while I was writing about it in </a><a href="https://www.todayintabs.com/p/why-is-nick-bilton-so-obsessed-with"><em>Tabs</em></a>:</p>
<p>❝</p>
<p><strong>Edward Bulwer-Lytton</strong> just sent me a telegram from hell that says: “WOW THIS GUY SUCKS AT WRITING STOP.”</p>
<p>There may only be a single person about whom me, <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/jeffjarvis.bsky.social/post/3mmwlpvoguk25">hyperglocal thinkfluencer </a><a href="https://bsky.app/profile/jeffjarvis.bsky.social/post/3mmwlpvoguk25"><strong>Professor Jeff Jarvis</strong></a>, and <a href="https://www.gawkerarchives.com/nick-bilton-is-the-new-worst-columnist-at-the-new-york-1617069889">circa 2014 </a><a href="https://www.gawkerarchives.com/nick-bilton-is-the-new-worst-columnist-at-the-new-york-1617069889"><strong>Leah Finnegan</strong></a> would all say “wow, that guy sucks” and it’s Nick Bilton. He was the kind of tech commentator anti-talent who makes a smooth and remunerative career for himself by being enthusiastic about gadgets while seeming think-y and writing at a low fourth grade level. Sometimes these guys ascend to their proper heaven and become venture capitalists, <a href="https://techcrunch.com/author/josh-constine/">like </a><a href="https://techcrunch.com/author/josh-constine/"><strong>Josh Constine</strong></a>. Sometimes they stick it out in print media and torment us for decades, <a href="https://defector.com/toward-a-theory-of-kevin-roose">like </a><a href="https://defector.com/toward-a-theory-of-kevin-roose"><strong>Kevin Roose</strong></a>. Not smart enough for the former or likable enough for the latter, instead Nick Bilton went into TV.</p>
<h2>Nick’s Television Experience</h2>
<p><strong>Michael Calderone</strong>, Bilton’s former editor at <em>Vanity Fair</em> now at <em>New York Magazine</em>, reported that Bilton has “<a href="https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/how-nick-bilton-took-the-helm-at-60-minutes.html">a decade of experience in documentaries</a>.” I guess! In <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/28/business/media/nick-bilton-60-minutes-bari-weiss.html">the </a><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/28/business/media/nick-bilton-60-minutes-bari-weiss.html"><em>New York Times</em></a><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/28/business/media/nick-bilton-60-minutes-bari-weiss.html"><strong>Benjamin Mullin</strong></a><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/28/business/media/nick-bilton-60-minutes-bari-weiss.html"> and </a><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/28/business/media/nick-bilton-60-minutes-bari-weiss.html"><strong>Michael Grynbaum</strong></a><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/28/business/media/nick-bilton-60-minutes-bari-weiss.html"> summarized his film career this way</a>:</p>
<p>❝</p>
<p>Since [2016], he has worked in documentaries, writing and directing “Fake Famous,” an HBO film about aspiring social media influencers, and serving as a producer on “The Inventor: Out for Blood in Silicon Valley,” a film directed by Alex Gibney about the disgraced Theranos founder Elizabeth Holmes.</p>
<p>And he <a href="https://www.semafor.com/article/05/28/2026/the-completely-new-60-minutes">told </a><em><a href="https://www.semafor.com/article/05/28/2026/the-completely-new-60-minutes">Semafor</a></em><a href="https://www.semafor.com/article/05/28/2026/the-completely-new-60-minutes">’s </a><strong><a href="https://www.semafor.com/article/05/28/2026/the-completely-new-60-minutes">Max Tani</a></strong><a href="https://www.semafor.com/article/05/28/2026/the-completely-new-60-minutes"> much the same thing</a>:</p>
<p>❝</p>
<p>The original idea behind <em>60 Minutes</em>, [Bilton] said, was to create a series of short documentaries, and he’s produced documentaries in recent years for HBO and Netflix on topics from cryptocurrency to social media.</p>
<p>This is all true, as far as it goes. Bilton has been <a href="https://www.newyorker.com/culture/on-television/fake-famous-and-the-tedium-of-influencer-culture">involved in a couple of documentary projects in the last few years</a>. But oddly I haven’t seen him mention his highest profile television credit: staff writer for all five episodes of <strong>Sam Levinson</strong> and <strong>Abel</strong> “The Weeknd” <strong>Tesfaye</strong>’s egregious 2023 flop “<a href="https://www.imdb.com/title/tt14954666/fullcredits/?ref_=tt_cst_sm">The Idol</a>.” The <em>Guardian</em> called it “<a href="https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2023/jul/03/the-idol-finale-review-one-of-the-worst-programmes-ever-made">one of the worst shows ever made</a>,” and the <em>Hollywood Reporter</em> said it was “<a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-reviews/the-idol-finale-review-1235528174/">dogged by a thin plot and an incoherent narrative</a>.” <em>Rolling Stone</em> <a href="https://www.rollingstone.com/tv-movies/tv-movie-recaps/the-idol-finale-hbo-cringe-ends-the-weeknd-lily-rose-depp-sam-levinson-1234782380/">reported that a member of the production described the show as</a>: “like any rape fantasy that any toxic man would have,” and eventually <a href="https://deadline.com/2025/05/the-weeknd-the-idol-show-hbo-negative-reviews-half-baked-1236402627/">even The Weeknd himself admitted it was very bad</a>.</p>
<p>Bilton’s new job at CBS leaves the status of his upcoming collaboration with <strong>Martin Scorsese</strong> and <strong>Dwayne</strong> “The Rock” <strong>Johnson</strong> in “<a href="https://www.imdb.com/title/tt35891507/?ref_=nm_flmg_job_1_accord_1_unrel_cdt_t_1">Untitled Hawaii-Set Crime Drama</a>” up in the air unless Scorsese can find another writer to take over—perhaps a rhesus macaque with a severe brain injury, or Claude.</p>

<p>“<a href="https://x.com/nickbilton/status/2060028458793615646">Walking into this building and putting my name on this job is the honor of my career</a>,” wrote Bilton in his characteristically almost-but-not-quite-coherent introductory message to 60 Minutes’ staff. “There are more red flags flying over this memo than a Soviet military parade” <a href="https://www.hamiltonnolan.com/p/if-you-take-the-weasel-job-then-you">observed </a><a href="https://www.hamiltonnolan.com/p/if-you-take-the-weasel-job-then-you"><strong>Hamilton Nolan</strong></a>, who points out that the only reason someone so unqualified gets hired for a job like this is as a hatchet man, just like Bari Weiss was herself. In a parenthetical Nolan also writes:</p>
<p>❝</p>
<p>I wouldn’t worry too much about CBS becoming another Fox News. Roger Ailes, who created Fox News, was a genius—an evil genius, but a genius nonetheless in the practice of manipulating the medium of television for political ends. Bari Weiss is not this sort of genius. Rather than being transformed into a sophisticated right wing propaganda operation, it is more likely that CBS News is just made dumb and pointless.</p>
<p>Weiss has <a href="https://www.todayintabs.com/p/where-is-everybody">already made the CBS Evening News dumb and pointless</a>, and <a href="https://latenighter.com/news/ratings/comics-unleashed-cbs-1135-debut-ratings/">CBS has replaced </a><a href="https://latenighter.com/news/ratings/comics-unleashed-cbs-1135-debut-ratings/"><strong>Stephen Colbert</strong></a><a href="https://latenighter.com/news/ratings/comics-unleashed-cbs-1135-debut-ratings/"> with something dumb and pointless</a>, so I don’t think it’s particularly speculative to say Weiss is now hard at work doing the same to 60 Minutes. <strong><a href="https://www.patreon.com/posts/new-worst-at-new-159428401?utm_campaign=patron_engagement&amp;utm_source=post_link&amp;post_id=159428401&amp;utm_id=a9330e8e-e251-41ac-bc7e-e64d56007a37&amp;utm_medium=email">Max Read</a></strong><a href="https://www.patreon.com/posts/new-worst-at-new-159428401?utm_campaign=patron_engagement&amp;utm_source=post_link&amp;post_id=159428401&amp;utm_id=a9330e8e-e251-41ac-bc7e-e64d56007a37&amp;utm_medium=email"> pointed out the essentially confusing nature of the hire</a>: “Has the 60 Minutes well has been so poisoned that Bilton was the biggest name they could convince take the job on the terms offered? Or do they actually think he will do a good job on the merits, such as they are?” As the adage goes: “A’s hire A’s, and B’s hire C’s.” Bari Weisses hire Nick Biltons. It’s hard to imagine what kind of people Nick Bilton will hire.</p>
