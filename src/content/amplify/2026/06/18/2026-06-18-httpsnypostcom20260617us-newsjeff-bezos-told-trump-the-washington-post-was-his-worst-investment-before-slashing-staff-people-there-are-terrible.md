<p>WASHINGTON — Amazon founder Jeff Bezos called the Washington Post his worst investment in a conversation with President Trump months before <a href="https://nypost.com/2026/02/04/media/washington-post-begins-hundreds-of-newsroom-layoffs-as-jeff-bezos-ignores-reporters-pleas-report/">gutting the newsroom</a>, according to a new book by New York Times journalists Jonathan Swan and Maggie Haberman.</p>
<p>“The people there are terrible,” Bezos told Trump over dinner in December 2024, according to an excerpt obtained by The Post ahead of the June 23 release of “<a data-aps-asc-tag="nypost-20" data-aps-asin="1668067242" data-aps-status="read" data-ga4-event='{"event_name":"ux_engagement","source_unit":"amazon","source_position":94}' data-wrapped-template="https://r.nypostlink.com?btn_ref=org-19984c113c692001&amp;btn_url" href="https://r.nypostlink.com?btn_ref=org-19984c113c692001&amp;btn_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FRegime-Change-Inside-Imperial-Presidency%2Fdp%2F1668067242%3Ftag%3Dnypost-20%26asc_refurl%3Dhttps%3A%2F%2Fnypost.com%2F2026%2F06%2F17%2Fus-news%2Fjeff-bezos-told-trump-the-washington-post-was-his-worst-investment-before-slashing-staff-people-there-are-terrible%2F%26asc_source%3Dweb">Regime Change: Inside the Imperial Presidency of Donald Trump.</a>“</p>
<p>“They don’t listen. My other companies, they listen,” Bezos said, focusing his ire at the business side of the publication after losing <a href="https://www.wsj.com/business/media/washington-post-losses-topped-100-million-in-2025-85076aae">more than $100 million</a> that year.</p>
