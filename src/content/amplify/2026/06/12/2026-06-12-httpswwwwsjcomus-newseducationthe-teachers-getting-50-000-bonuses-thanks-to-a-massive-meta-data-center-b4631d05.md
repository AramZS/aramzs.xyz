<h1>The Teachers Getting $50,000 Bonuses Thanks to a Massive Meta Data Center</h1>
<h2>Jump in sales tax receipts in Louisiana parish provides new talking point in debate over AI construction boom</h2>
<p>By <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/meghan-bobrowsky">Meghan Bobrowsky</a> and <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/ben-eisen">Ben Eisen</a></p>
<p>June 11, 2026 9:16 pm ET</p>
<p><img alt="Construction of Meta's AI data center at Holly Ridge." src="https://images.wsj.net/im-72565311?width=700&amp;height=525"/>Construction at Meta’s AI data center in Louisiana’s Richland Parish in December.Greg Hilburn/USA Today Network/Reuters</p>
<p>A school district in Louisiana says some of its teachers will receive bonuses of more than $50,000 this year thanks to increased tax revenue linked to a <a href="https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/META">Meta Platforms</a> data-center construction project.</p>
<p>Local officials said the windfall for teachers, funded by a portion of the parish’s sales tax, reflects an influx of economic activity that is helping to revitalize a slumping region. The parish brought in $42.9 million in sales and use tax for the first nine months of the current fiscal year, more than double last year’s total, according to the Northwest Louisiana Finance Authority. Meta also separately made a $22.4 million tax payment to the parish in May, it said.</p>
