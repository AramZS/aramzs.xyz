<h2>What Else Did Scientists Find?</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/02/technology/scientists-find-way-to-supercharge-dangerous-computer-worms-with-ai.html">Scientists Find Way to Supercharge Dangerous Computer ‘Worms’ With A.I.</a><br/>
—<strong>Cade Metz</strong>, <em>New York Times.</em> Ok, but why?</p>

—CBS News. Ok, <em>but why????</em></p>

—<strong>Michelle Starr</strong>, <em>ScienceAlert</em>. Yes, great, this always turns out well in fiction.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.anl.gov/article/scientists-find-an-elusive-vibration-a-higgs-mode-changing-a-crystals-symmetry">Scientists find an elusive vibration—a Higgs mode—changing a crystal’s symmetry</a><br/>
—<strong>Savannah Mitchem</strong>, Argonne National Laboratory. Who among us hasn’t cycled through all the “elusive vibrations” searching for that perfect “Higgs mode,” iykiwm(aityd)?</p>
<p><a href="https://mymodernmet.com/wifi-accurately-identitifies-humans/">Scientists Find That Ordinary Wi-Fi Routers Can Identify People With Near-Perfect Accuracy</a><br/>
—<strong>Linnea Pejcha</strong>, <em>My Modern Met</em>. This isn’t “identifying people” like connecting to your device and identifying your digital footprint. The wi-fi radio is <em>looking at you</em>. There’s something living inside the cloud.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.reuters.com/business/environment/scientists-find-new-species-dragonfly-grasshopper-fluorescent-spider-2026-06-03/">Scientists find new species of dragonfly, grasshopper and a fluorescent spider</a><br/>
—<strong>Tim Cocks</strong>, <em>Reuters. </em><a href="https://www.thewildernessproject.org/news/cassai-life-atlas">The Wilderness Project can keep looking for things</a>. Everyone else, please chill.</p>
