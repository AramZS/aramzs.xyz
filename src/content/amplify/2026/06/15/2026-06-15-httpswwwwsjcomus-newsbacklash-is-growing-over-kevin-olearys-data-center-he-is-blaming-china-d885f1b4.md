<h1>Backlash Is Growing Over Kevin O’Leary’s Data Center. He Is Blaming China.</h1>
<h2>‘Shark Tank’ panelist blasts his detractors on TV and social media as tools of Beijing. Some locals in Utah say the tactic is backfiring.</h2>
<img alt="Kevin O'Leary attending the 83rd annual Golden Globe Awards, fists clenched, with a wide, happy grin on his face." src="https://images.wsj.net/im-01591224?width=1280&amp;height=853"/>
<p>By <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/will-parker">Will Parker</a></p>
<p>June 14, 2026 11:00 am ET</p>
<p>BOX ELDER COUNTY, <a href="https://www.wsj.com/topics/place/utah">Utah</a>—Kevin O’Leary is trying to build one of America’s largest <a href="https://www.wsj.com/topics/industry/data-centers">data centers</a>. That is, he said, if he can stop the Chinese government from interfering.</p>
<p>O’Leary aims to develop a natural-gas and data-center project covering thousands of acres in the Hansel Valley. For weeks, he has encountered furious pushback from people across the state, largely over the potential impact on the Great Salt Lake, which has been receding for years.</p>
