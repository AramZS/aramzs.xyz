<p>To: Bari Weiss</p>
<p>RE: Good luck, babe!</p>
<p>I honestly cannot believe you’ve willingly decided to go into the worst kind of job that exists: management at a dying company.</p>
<blockquote><p>This is the glass cliff to end all glass cliffs</p></blockquote>
<p>Managing sucks! It sucks even when you <em>like</em> the people you’re managing and it’s a low-stress position! And I’m sure I don’t have to tell you: running CBS News is not a low-stress position. You are going to get blamed by everyone above you for decisions that are made by people below you, and you are going to get blamed by people below you for the decisions that are made by people above you. You’re also going to get blamed for your own decisions, just for kicks. You have elected to take a job where the primary purpose is for you to eat shit and own the death of broadcast TV news, a thing that is going to die no matter what you do. Nice work!</p>
<p>This is the glass cliff to end all glass cliffs. You’re Marissa Mayer at Yahoo without the Googler street cred. You’re Nancy Dubuc at Vice without the string of hit TV shows. You’re Linda Yaccarino at Twitter without the advertiser relationships. You have been hired as a sop to a Trump administration that is actively hostile to the <em>actual </em>free press, and you will be made to oversee wave after wave of layoffs until you quit or get fired and the entire news division is shut down in a final spasm of cost-cutting after the next inescapable media merger.</p>
<p>No one can save CBS News, because it was made for a media ecosystem that is now dead. Broadcast television is slowly circling the drain, its aging audience drifting toward the great inevitable. Younger people are getting their news from TikTok and Instagram in ever-greater numbers, and they trust institutional media less than ever. CBS in particular — that’s your new company, now — has <a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-news/tv-viewers-oldest-audiences-1235910778/">the oldest audience in primetime TV</a>. These folks aren’t <em>The Free Press</em>’ assiduously courted “classical liberals,” either. It’s normie grandparents, a bunch of whom are planning to appear at the next No Kings rally. I might invoke Edward R. Murrow, but they remember him. They don’t have any goodwill toward you. They don’t know who you are at all. And they are not going to defend you when you screw up.</p>
<p>You are now stuck claiming your goal is to win back younger audiences, which you cannot do, while your <em>real</em> job is to manage decline. There is no way to win here, only slightly better ways to lose.</p>
<blockquote><p>You also have to manage <em>Talent</em></p></blockquote>
<p>Now, it’s possible that you’re delusional enough to think that <a href="https://www.theverge.com/tech/787051/larry-ellison-tiktok-quest-to-run-the-world">Larry Ellison’s takeover of TikTok </a>will give you a tailwind. I mean, <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2025/10/04/larry-david-ellison-media-trump/">your owner is Ellison’s son</a>, after all, and why wouldn’t the Ellison family support each other? You should have known the moment you saw Rupert Murdoch on the investor list that wasn’t going to happen — after all, did MySpace help Fox News? Or did News Corp catastrophically fumble an asset it didn’t understand?</p>
<p>On a business level, your problem with TikTok is this: Platforms exist to erode institutions like CBS News. You cannot solve your problems with improved distribution on TikTok. The things that work on television for 60-year-olds do not work on TikTok for 14-year-olds, first of all. But more importantly, TikTokers monetize their content by directly integrating advertising and brands directly into their content, a strategy in immediate conflict with the values and ethics of a storied news organization like CBS News. Even if you somehow manage to crack TikTok’s algorithm, you still won’t make enough revenue to survive without trashing the very soul of the institution you’re purporting to save.</p>
<p>It’s actually even worse than that. You also have to manage <em>Talent</em>. Famous TV Talent, the people your audience actually knows and likes, and who will eat you alive if they think you’ve screwed them over. In order for that to work, you actually need to completely disappear and let the Talent be the face of the network, and quite frankly nothing about your history suggests you’re capable of that. And one of the main problems with Talent, <em>especially</em> Famous TV Talent, is that they are egomaniacal monsters who love getting paid and throwing hissy fits, and hate being told what to do. You think you had a bad time at <em>The New York Times</em>? Baby, you have no idea what a bad time is. You’ll find out when you try to tell Norah O’Donnell anything at all about her reporting. If you’re lucky, she’ll just shake her Emmys at you when she tells you to fuck off. You think Gayle King or David Martin are going to sit through a lecture from you? Like for real? Come on. Jane Pauley has already <a href="https://www.adweek.com/tvnewser/jane-pauley-deborah-norville-today-succession/">caused one media firestorm</a> by changing jobs. Are you ready for this?</p>
<p>Managing requires certain kinds of soft skills, ones I am not confident you possess. They weren’t necessary in your cushy <em>Wall Street Journal</em> op-ed job, or your cushier <em>New York Times</em> op-ed job. They were barely required at the publication you invented, <em>The Free Press</em>. So now you’re the head honcho at CBS News. Let’s say you decide to skip levels to directly edit a <em>60 Minutes </em>story. It doesn’t even have to be a controversial story to make all hell break loose — because you have neither the credibility nor the relationships required to take this kind of work on. And what’s more, you’ve got a news division composed exclusively of ambitious piranhas below you — not your handpicked cronies, <a href="https://www.thefp.com/p/my-favorite-actress-is-not-human-tilly-norwood-artificial-intelligence">like Tyler “I wish to see Hollywood virgins” Cowen. </a>These people have <em>decades</em> in television, and you have a newsletter and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20200715235820/https://www.bariweiss.com/resignation-letter">a history of throwing your colleagues under the bus.</a></p>
<blockquote><p>To make matters worse, you have never reported, and they all know it</p></blockquote>
<p>The media reporters are already circling you like a pack of wolves. Like, take <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/patrickhruby.bsky.social/post/3m2jyypzsqk2m">your opening memo to the news division</a>, the one about “the same core journalistic values that defined this profession from the beginning,” which for some reason includes using “all the tools of the digital era.” Not only could it have been better edited, and based on actual journalistic history, but you might have held off on insinuating you intend to incorporate AI slop into the work process until sometime after your first day. Consider all the people you now lead who have actual reporting experience. Do you think they are going to do something other than call their favorite media reporter the moment you interject yourself into a story or impose some nonsensical AI “bias meter” review?</p>
<p>To make matters worse, you have never reported, and they all know it. You don’t know anything about the challenges or judgment calls required for complicated stories. You’re an op-ed writer who <a href="https://www.esquire.com/news-politics/a14428464/gop-never-trump/">rode Bret Stephens’ coattails</a> into <em>The New York Times</em> newsroom, then quit in a huff because they wouldn’t fire you for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20200715235820/https://www.bariweiss.com/resignation-letter">whatever it was you wanted to pretend to be a free speech martyr for</a>. (You might want to <a href="https://www.bariweiss.com/resignation-letter">pay for your personal website</a>, by the way. It’s expired.) The closest you came to doing anything like reporting was the flop “Twitter Files” work, which exposed… um, <a href="https://www.theverge.com/23551060/elon-musk-twitter-takeover-layoffs-workplace-salute-emoji">people having banal conversations</a>. In the context of the Twitter Files, this was a win <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2022/12/2/23490863/elon-musk-twitter-expose-hunter-biden-flop-doxxed-multiple-people">since you didn’t actively dox anyone</a>.</p>
<p>Honestly, I thought your experience with Elon Musk might have prepared you for what a bad idea this was, since, you know, he got mad at you when <a href="https://www.theverge.com/23871530/elon-musk-biography-twitter-acquisition">you asked the wrong questions about China</a>. Sure, you’ve made a career out of kissing the right asses to move ahead, but you must have learned by now that means you will be disposed of whenever it’s convenient.</p>
<p>You had such a good gig going, too. Your entire pitch at <em>The Free Press</em> — lol, lmao — was that you were standing boldly against big bad corporate media that simply wouldn’t tell the truth! This was an easy pitch — albeit one made by every Substacker from Taylor Lorenz to Ken Klippenstein to Naomi Wolf — and, judging by your audience, a fairly lucrative one. You didn’t even have to get stories right! Remember when your columnist Coleman Hughes published a column about George Floyd that was so wrong it got dogwalked not once but <a href="https://radleybalko.substack.com/p/the-retconning-of-george-floyd"><em>three</em></a> <a href="https://radleybalko.substack.com/p/the-retconning-of-george-floyd-part"><em>times</em></a> <a href="https://radleybalko.substack.com/p/the-retconning-of-george-floyd-part-382">by Radley Balko</a>? And <a href="https://radleybalko.substack.com/p/the-retconning-of-george-floyd-a">you personally got dogwalked a fourth time</a>? And your response was that Balko <a href="https://x.com/bariweiss/status/1763620005105590724">should do your podcast</a>?</p>
<blockquote><p>Any insufficiently aggressive reporting on the situation in Gaza is ultimately going to be blamed on you, Ms. Zionist Fanatic</p></blockquote>
<p>No one but the terminally online remembers that little kerfuffle, because the stakes were small potatoes. But you’re on TV now, babe. Do you remember when CBS News’ Dan Rather <a href="https://blog.smu.edu/2004-election/killian-documents/">didn’t adequately verify some documents purporting to be about George W. Bush’s military service</a>, and it turned into a national controversy that lasted for weeks? That’s what you have to look forward to, especially if you personally sign off on compromised reporting.</p>
<p>And that controversy is coming for you at warp speed. More than half of America <a href="https://apnews.com/article/israel-gaza-hamas-palestinians-poll-0cd426a364260582ac6c1bc79ac8922f">thinks Israel has “gone too far” in Gaza</a>, and any insufficiently aggressive reporting on the situation in Gaza is ultimately going to be blamed on you, Ms. <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/10/opinion/israel-lara-alqasem-bds.html">Zionist Fanatic</a>. Plus, CBS News isn’t going to move the needle on <a href="https://www.politico.com/news/2025/10/03/israel-gaza-poll-young-americans-00593217">young people’s attitudes toward Israel because</a> they don’t watch it. This specifically is an area of news where there are no winning moves for you because of your proudly worn biases. You don’t even have to be involved in a bum story to get blamed for it, because getting blamed for reporters’ stories <em>is the most important part of your job.</em></p>
<p>Though reporters are near and dear to my heart, they are not your worst problem. Your worst problem is, somehow, Brendan Carr.</p>
<p>I don’t mean that he’s going to personally come for <em>you</em>, Bari. I mean that you’ve obviously <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2025/07/24/business/media/fcc-skydance-merger-paramount.html">been put in charge of CBS News as a way of appeasing the Trump administration</a> — something to allow the merger to go through. Carr blessed the Skydance acquisition after CBS paid $16 million to President Donald Trump in what was definitely not a bribe, <em>and</em> didn’t renew <em>Late Show </em>host Stephen Colbert’s contract. Skydance promised Carr an anti-DEI, pro “diversity of viewpoints” nanny in the News division. That nanny is <a href="https://apnews.com/article/paramount-trump-ombudsman-journalism-cbs-news-56e68f08389e30b49360d73c018f8e7c">a Republican donor</a> who somehow has even less journalistic experience than you do.</p>
<p>So that kind of puts you in a precarious position, babe. The moment that deal is closed, you’re disposable. And there are just so many ways to dispose of you! Every day will be a thrilling new adventure. Will a Truth Social post end your career? Will you accidentally publish AI slop? Is there going to be a mass quit, leaving you with a gaping hole in your programming? Or will a MAGA-pilled influencer inevitably goad Musk into an advertiser harassment campaign that ends your career? Perhaps there will be a newsroom scandal I have not adequately foreseen! This is a deck that’s stacked against you, and you haven’t got the skills to finesse your way through the deal.</p>
<p>Congrats on that <a href="https://www.wsj.com/business/media/paramount-buys-bari-weisss-the-free-press-for-150-million-737a94eb?gaa_at=eafs&amp;gaa_n=ASWzDAhLuFfsk92_Ah1FVcRTWtLVGOIBtiqfMnydjmd1DgxQZcGFYsgafH2U&amp;gaa_ts=68e4120d&amp;gaa_sig=Pezce6YBbZBoRmjZFG6ebbXazMRfKM9_PuNXmZOhbGCLtPY3Z74h9whprpU_KWhFl8vnlyRdf8wOVMSVVZ6-Xg%3D%3D">$150 million payout</a> for <em>The Free Press</em>. Someone else owns it now.</p>
