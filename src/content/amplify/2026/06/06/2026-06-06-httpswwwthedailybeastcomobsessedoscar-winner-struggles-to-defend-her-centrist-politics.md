title: Oscar Winner Struggles to Defend Her ‘Centrist’ Politics
---
<div><article><p>Gwyneth Paltrow struggled to clarify her political views in a new interview, saying she does not align with either major party.</p><p>“I’m pretty centrist,” Paltrow, 53, said on a new episode of <i>The Goop Podcast. </i>She noted that her husband, Brad Falchuk—whom she described as “so progressive”—thinks she is a Republican.</p><div><p>“I don’t feel anything right now, to be totally honest with you,” she said of her own political beliefs. “I feel like I’m completely an independent.”</p></div><figure><img alt="Cast member Gwyneth Paltrow and her husband Brad Falchuk attend width="800"/><figcaption><span>Paltrow said that her husband is "progressive." </span><span>Mario Anzuoni/REUTERS/Mario Anzuoni </span></figcaption></figure><p>The <i>Marty Supreme</i> actress was joined on the podcast by Trae Stephens, co-founder of AI defense company Anduril, who said the United States’ “problems” cannot be “fixed” without “open, respectful dialogue.”</p><p>“I couldn’t agree with you more,” Paltrow told Stephens, adding that in her view the country has become “so binary.”</p><p>“I am trying to, in my journey through being an American right now, trying to, I don’t know, I guess sort of weave together lots of different points of view, and also to get out of that place of, like, righteousness and anger and fear,” the Santa Monica, California-raised actress
