<h2>Opening summary</h2>
<p><strong>The UK is set to get some respite from this week’s record-breaking heat</strong> over the weekend, but much of the rest of <a data-component="auto-linked-tag" data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/europe-news">Europe</a> <strong>remains in the grip of an intense heatwave as it shifts eastwards.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Germany</strong> provisionally recorded its highest ever temperature yesterday with <strong>41.3C near the city of Saarbrücken</strong> close to the French border. Forecasters say the hottest conditions <strong>are now spreading into central and eastern Europe,</strong> including <strong>Poland</strong> and the <strong>Balkans</strong>, over the weekend.</p>
<p>The Ironman European Championship taking place tomorrow in Frankfurt has been forced to adapt to the extreme conditions, with organisers shortening both the cycling and running courses because of the heat.</p>

<p>“The ‌heatwave is going to peak at the weekend, well over 40C in some parts of Germany,” Karsten Brandt, a meteorologist ‌at weather forecasting site Donnerwetter.de, told Reuters.</p>
<p><strong>France</strong> has borne the brunt of the heatwave, with temperatures exceeding well above 40C in some regions. The sweltering conditions have contributed to <a data-link-name="in body link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jun/26/europe-heatwave-shift-east-child-deaths-france-cities-heat-stress">a growing number of heat-related fatalities</a> in recent days, including dozens of drownings as people sought relief from the heat. Others have died after being left in hot vehicles or from heat stress.</p>
<p><strong>The Pride march scheduled to take place in Paris over the weekend was postponed</strong> after police asked organisers to move the event to ease pressure on emergency services responding to the heatwave.</p>
<p>In contrast, organisers of the <strong>Budapest Pride</strong> march <strong>say the event will go ahead as planned this weekend</strong> despite temperatures forecast to reach 38C. They say the march is intended to send a message to Hungary’s new government after last year’s ban under former leader Viktor Orbán, underscoring continued public support for LGBTQ+ rights.</p>

<p>“This year’s Pride will send an important signal to the new government: these issues are still relevant, and we still face exclusion in many areas of life,” Hungarian transgender activist Pippin Nadori told AFP.</p>
<p>The <strong>UK</strong> provisionally broke its June temperature record for a third consecutive day yesterday, with <strong>37.3C reached at Santon Downham in Suffolk.</strong> Until this week, the June record was 35.6C from the summer of 1976.</p>
<p>Andy Page, chief forecaster at the Met Office, said the UK will see a gradual shift in conditions over the weekend, with south-east England holding on to the warmest weather the longest. He added that an amber extreme heat warning remains in place for much of today, although temperatures are expected to peak in the low 30s.</p>
