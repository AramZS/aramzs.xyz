<p>A month ago, I heard that the now vacant Shoreham Nuclear Power Plant was available as a filming location, and I immediately set up a tour. It’d certainly be the first nuclear power plant to have in my files, and I was absolutely fascinated to see what still remained inside.</p>
<figure><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" src="https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.28-PM.png?w=1194&amp;ssl=1" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.28-PM.png?w=1194&amp;ssl=1 1194w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.28-PM.png?resize=300%2C198&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.28-PM.png?resize=1024%2C678&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.28-PM.png?resize=768%2C508&amp;ssl=1 768w"/><figcaption>Construction began on Shoreham’s GE Mark II Boiling Water Reactor in 1973 and finished 12 years later in 1985, when it received its testing license and began operating at 5% capacity. <p>Public opposition had been growing steadily during this period, in large part due to Three Mile Island’s partial meltdown in 1979 and the Chernobyl tragedy in 1986. The state and county eventually sided with the opposition and refused to approve the plant’s emergency escape route plan, which prevented it from obtaining an operating license.</p>
<figure><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" src="https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.47-PM.png?w=1190&amp;ssl=1" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.47-PM.png?w=1190&amp;ssl=1 1190w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.47-PM.png?resize=300%2C198&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.47-PM.png?resize=1024%2C675&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.47-PM.png?resize=768%2C506&amp;ssl=1 768w"/></figure>
<p>In 1992, the $6 Billion facility was sold to the state for $1 (the cost was passed onto LI tax payers as a 3% surcharge on electric bills). The two-year decommissioning process commenced, the first time in US history that a licensed commercial nuclear reactor would be dismantled.</p>
<figure><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" src="https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.55-PM.png?w=1186&amp;ssl=1" srcset="https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.55-PM.png?w=1186&amp;ssl=1 1186w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.55-PM.png?resize=300%2C197&amp;ssl=1 300w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.55-PM.png?resize=1024%2C673&amp;ssl=1 1024w, https://i0.wp.com/nickcarr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Screenshot-2026-06-18-at-11.56.55-PM.png?resize=768%2C505&amp;ssl=1 768w"/></figure>
<p>The process was completed in 1994 following the removal of 5 million pounds of radioactive waste and 560 irradiated fuel rod assemblies. The plant has been vacant and dormant ever since.</p>
