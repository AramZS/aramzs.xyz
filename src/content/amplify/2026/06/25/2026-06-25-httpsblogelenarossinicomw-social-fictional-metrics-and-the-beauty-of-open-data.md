<p>The new social network <a href="https://blog.elenarossini.com/tag/w-social/">W Social</a> - which purports to be a healthier alternative to Elon Musk’s X, “made in Europe, for the world” - would be a fascinating case study for future business school seminars. Its debut has been messy, filled with controversies - and yet, it has been embraced by high profile personalities from the worlds of politics and journalism. I am about to cover its biggest scandal yet, which puts into question W Social’s core promise: “Trust your feed”.</p>
<h3>A quick recap</h3>
<p><a href="https://blog.elenarossini.com/tag/w-social/">W Social</a> was announced out of nowhere at Davos this past January. The media frenzy around its soft launch was awe-inducing: European decentralized networks like <a href="https://joinmastodon.org/?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">Mastodon</a> or <a href="https://eurosky.tech/?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">Eurosky</a> never received a fraction of the attention W Social commanded - even if at the time W Social didn’t have a single user and kept details about its technical infrastructure secret. People quickly found <a href="https://blog.elenarossini.com/w-social-uncovered-the-reality-behind-the-hype/">evidence that W Social was a hard fork of the open network Bluesky</a>, based on the AT protocol. Weeks before its beta launch <a href="https://blog.elenarossini.com/w-social-public-institutions-and-the-theater-of-european-digital-sovereignty/">it quietly went closed-source</a> - at the same time as it was embraced by the crème de la crème of European institutions, politicians and NGOs for fostering “European digital sovereignty”.</p>
<p>I have published <a href="https://blog.elenarossini.com/tag/w-social/">a series of exposés about W Social</a> - including the scoop last week that the network had quietly gone closed-source. My research has been widely quoted in <a href="https://netzpolitik.org/2026/soziale-netzwerke-warum-w-social-mehr-kalter-kaffee-als-heisser-scheiss-ist/?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">German</a>, <a href="https://www.republik.ch/2026/06/24/ctrl-braucht-es-social-media-made-in-europe?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">Swiss</a>, <a href="https://www.frandroid.com/culture-tech/web/3147439_w-social-le-reseau-social-europeen-et-open-source-qui-ne-letait-pas-vraiment?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">French</a> and <a href="https://www.nrc.nl/nieuws/2026/06/18/hoe-het-nieuwe-sociale-medium-w-social-de-europese-commissie-wist-te-verleiden-a4930476?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">Dutch</a> media. The tide is beginning to turn, with more critical eyes observing W Social’s operations. If you want to catch up on all my articles about W Social so far, you can find them here:</p>
<figure><a href="https://blog.elenarossini.com/tag/w-social/"><img alt="" src="https://blog.elenarossini.com/content/images/icon/ELE-avatar-oink-33e52720-cf33-4563-866d-dde3ace7cc40.jpg"/>Elena RossiniElena Rossini<img alt="" src="https://blog.elenarossini.com/content/images/thumbnail/WSocial-followup-cover-1-dce99944-e60c-4053-949d-d34a3a872f59.jpg"/></a></figure>
<p>Having become an unwitting W Social expert, I can reveal there are several scoops that I have yet to publish concerning falsehoods and critical security issues; I am receiving tips on a daily basis. Today, I’ll cover W Social’s misrepresentations about their network’s metrics.</p>
<h2>Doctored Numbers on W Social's Homepage</h2>
<p>A source tipped me off about the discrepancy between the metrics displayed on <a href="https://wsocial.news/?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">W Social</a>’s landing page - which shows posts from its network as floating cards - and the real numbers of interactions when one checks the original messages on ATproto.</p>
<p>My source said:</p>
<blockquote>The number for the comments seemed suspicious to me so I checked them against the Bluesky app. They apparently boosted the comments counts to make it appear like there is A LOT of discussion on W. This is so pathetic.</blockquote>
<p>I checked the screenshots the source shared with me and then I went to verify the landing page of W Social and compared user metrics against the same posts on <a href="https://bsky.app/?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">bsky.app</a>. It’s true. The number of comments on posts by the likes of W Social CEO Anna Zeiter and the president of the European Commission Ursula von der Leyen are completely fabricated, doctored to appear higher (I have the distinct feeling that a W Social intern is about to be blamed for this incident.)</p>
<p><a href="https://mu.social/profile/anna.wsocial.eu/post/3moz6lvu7a22h?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">A post by Anna Zeiter</a> about her recent appearance at the Cannes Lions conference shows 22 comments on W Social’s homepage; <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/anna.wsocial.eu/post/3moz6lvu7a22h?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">in reality they is only one</a> (as seen on ATproto):</p>

<p>These are only two recent examples, but <strong><em>most</em> posts showcased on W Social’s landing page have completely fabricated metrics</strong>.</p>
<p>W Social’s misrepresentations are concerning and at odds with the fact that W has become a central network of communication for the <a href="https://mu.social/profile/ec.europa.eu/post/3moiostzv2s22?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">European Commission</a>, the <a href="https://mu.social/profile/europarl.europa.eu/post/3moioo7edu22z?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">European Parliament</a> and the <a href="https://mu.social/profile/ecb.europa.eu?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">European Central Bank</a>. European Commission president Ursula von der Leyen <a href="https://blog.elenarossini.com/w-social-public-institutions-and-the-theater-of-european-digital-sovereignty/">took time out from her G7 meetings to advertise the launch of W Social</a> last week - causing a bit of an uproar.</p>

<h2>Trust Your Feed? Trusted European Platforms?</h2>
<p>The irony is that W Social’s tagline displayed in a huge headline on its landing page is “Trust your feed”.</p>
<figure><img alt="a screenshot of W Social's homepage with the big title: &quot;Trust Your Feed&quot; and floating posts under it" sizes="(min-width: 1200px) 1200px" src="https://blog.elenarossini.com/content/images/2026/06/8-TrustYourFeed.jpg" srcset="https://blog.elenarossini.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/06/8-TrustYourFeed.jpg 600w, https://blog.elenarossini.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/06/8-TrustYourFeed.jpg 1000w, https://blog.elenarossini.com/content/images/2026/06/8-TrustYourFeed.jpg 1200w"/><figcaption>a screenshot of W Social's homepage with the big title: "Trust Your Feed" and floating posts under it</figcaption></figure>
<p>Copy on its homepage states:</p>
<blockquote>We believe in the need for a global, trusted social media platform owned, run, and hosted in Europe. W is built on verified human users, transparency, privacy, and free speech.</blockquote>
<p>“Trust” and “values” are often evoked by W Social marketing materials. And the company has been featured in multiple presentations about “<a href="https://stars4media.eu/trusted-european-platforms-democracy-shield-replacing-toxic-networks-like-x-twitter-july-2025/?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">Trusted European Platforms</a>.”</p>

<p>W Social advisor and investor <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/christopheleclercq.wsocial.eu?ref=blog.elenarossini.com">Christophe Leclercq</a> has created this label, which aims to help promote European platforms. The cover page of the project’s pitch deck describes the initiative this way:</p>
<p><em>A TEPs LABEL TO HELP NEW SOCIAL MEDIA FROM EUROPE<br/>
- selecting most promising ventures: trusted &amp; staying in European hands<br/>
- helping them cooperate to get to critical mass<br/>
- opportunities for news media groups and for impact investors</em></p>
