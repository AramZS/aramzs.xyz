<p>Last week New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani and a bunch of his "democratic socialist" allies won <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jun/28/zohran-mamdani-new-democratic-politics?ref=karlbode.com">several key primaries</a> in the city. The victories kicked <a href="https://rollcall.com/2026/06/23/goldman-espaillat-lose-new-york-primaries-to-mamdani-backed-challengers/?ref=karlbode.com">several Democratic party incumbents to the curb</a>, instantaneously triggering a massive wave of extraction class hyperventilation echoed across the corporate press.</p>
<p>Despite being a handful of primary victories in the deep blue areas of New York, it immediately triggered a wave of headlines about how "socialism" was now dangerously close to devouring the country alive:</p>

<p>To be clear, the U.S. political discourse has been so polluted by <a href="https://www.damemagazine.com/2023/03/06/america-propaganda-disinformation/?ref=karlbode.com">decades of corporate and right wing propaganda</a> and substandard education, that I don't think even well-intentioned Americans operate with any coherent, grounded understanding of political reality or policy a good 80 percent of the time.</p>
<p>So when you mention "<a href="https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/egalitarian?ref=karlbode.com">socialism</a>," most Americans have been well conditioned to immediately think of AK-47 wielding revolutionaries and bread lines, not public libraries, fire departments, <a href="https://ilsr.org/article/community-broadband-networks/new-map-displays-nationwide-trends-in-municipal-networks/?ref=karlbode.com">community-owned broadband networks</a>, or regulators holding companies accountable for Listeria-riddled arugula.</p>
<p>The U.S. is such a corrupt corporatocracy, most "democratic <a href="https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/socialism?ref=karlbode.com">socialism</a>" in practice usually involves deeply under-funded progressive movements desperately clawing uphill for the barest of common-sense reforms – a tiny fraction of which ever come to fruition due to overwhelming financial and political ratfucking by the rich.</p>
<p>Which is to say you probably shouldn't hold your breath if you're waiting for the plebs to <em>seize the means of production</em>. We're a glorified shopping mall (admittedly with some nice mountains) pretending to be a country, singularly obsessed with unchecked wealth accumulation above <em>all else</em>.</p>
<p>Even then, Trump corruption has been <em>so extreme</em> – and the failure of the Democratic party to adapt and fight back has been <em>so pathetic</em> – and the corporate press coverage <em>so enabling and awful</em> – that the lazy veneer has been stripped away, leaving the obvious rot beneath in full view to young voters.</p>
<p>Enter <em>scary socialism</em> (haunted_house_ghost_sounds.wav).</p>
<p>Most of the democratic socialists that won in New York City aren't remotely radical; they're pushing for things like free child care, equitable taxation of extremely rich people, swapping out a few cops for social workers, and avoiding the industrialized <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/24/middleeast/israel-targeting-children-report-un-intl?ref=karlbode.com">U.S. taxpayer-funded mass murder of overseas toddlers</a>.</p>
<p>Most of their positions are both popular and ordinary. Perhaps <a href="https://karlbode.com/corruption-killed-the-u-s-government-and-the-press-was-complicit/">we should have regulators that function</a>? Mayhap we could have a CEO or two that isn't a <a href="https://karlbode.com/the-press-is-still-propping-up-elon-musks-supergenius-engineer-mythology/">grotesque, comic book racist supervillain</a>? What if we heavily taxed obscenely rich people and used the money to <a href="https://infrastructurereportcard.org/cat-item/bridges-infrastructure/?ref=karlbode.com">repair U.S. bridges</a> and feed children?</p>
<p>Most of these aren't just <strong>not</strong> extreme positions, they're <em>majority held</em>.</p>
<p>But corporate media ownership very much likes the broken status quo, the corruption, and the regulatory capture. Said ownership would very much like this gravy train to keep rolling, and they <em>very much see progressive populist reform as a bigger threat to their interests than democracy-ending authoritarianism</em>.</p>
<p>This is endlessly reflected in press coverage, and they've never really been subtle about it. Propaganda-driven, right wing fake populism like MAGA is framed as "authentic middle America." Genuine, grass roots progressive populism coming from anywhere left of center is framed as extreme, unreliable, and incompetent.</p>
<p>If you flash back to 2010 or so, you might recall how the Tea Party (a <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2010/oct/13/tea-party-billionaire-koch-brothers?ref=karlbode.com">billionaire-backed</a> artificial right wing "populist" marketing rebranding of post-George W. Bush Republicanism that ultimately birthed MAGA) was heralded by the press as an <a href="https://fair.org/home/journalists-9829-tea-party/?ref=karlbode.com">authentic grass roots movement that should be taken very seriously</a>.</p>
<p>Now that a populist left wing movement is gaining momentum on the back of frustration with fascism, the corporate press is painting a very different picture.</p>
<p>Prominent CNN pseudo-journalist Van Jones, for example, took to Elon Musk's <a href="https://karlbode.com/data-shows-elon-musks-x-quickly-rots-your-brain/">right wing propaganda website</a> to unsubtly imply that Mamdani supporters mostly just hate America and free enterprise. You can clearly see he sees the authentic excitement building to his left as an enemy to be bested in combat:</p>

<p>Fading comedian and centrist Democrat Bill Maher also made <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/bill-maher-dire-midterm-election-warning-dems-really-crazy-socialists-win-primaries?ref=karlbode.com">press headlines</a> maligning the "crazy" democratic socialists of New York who are somehow failing at their jobs despite not having started them yet:</p>
<blockquote>"Democrats just elected three very far-left democratic socialists in New York who are crazy — like outright really crazy — so like, how are they gonna blow it? I don't know but they seem to be well on their way."</blockquote>
<p>It's very clear that corporations, Republicans, and centrist Democrats alike are very upset that Mamdani's easy charm is helping rebrand a political movement they only foundationally understood through the lens of Reagan-era bogeymen and a media utterly <a href="https://karlbode.com/the-press-really-doesnt-want-aoc-to-be-president/">dedicated to undermining populist, progressive reform</a>.</p>
<p>The mothball arm of the Democratic party responded to the NYC wins with this weird <a href="https://www.thepromisetoamerica.com/?ref=karlbode.com">"Promise to America" letter</a>, which featured 12 centrist House Democrats proudly declaring "we are capitalist, not socialist," before wading into a minefield of platitudes about "innovation, entrepreneurship, and ownership."</p>
<p>It's not clear who the letter is actually for. Their corporate donors, perhaps?</p>
<p>Asked to comment on the letter, one centrist Democrat think tanker again lamented the (nonexistent) "extremism" of Democratic socialism to <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/26/us/politics/moderate-democrats-capitalist-socialism.html?ref=karlbode.com"><em>the Times</em></a><em>:</em></p>
<blockquote>Matt Bennett, a co-founder of Third Way, a centrist Democratic think tank, said the outcomes in New York were “dangerous” for Democrats nationally.</blockquote>
<blockquote>“What we’ve seen Republicans do very successfully before is weaponize the craziest ideas of the activist left,” he said. “And now the ammunition they’ve got is much, much more powerful.”</blockquote>
<p>While every movement has its weirdos, there's really very little about the actual <a href="https://www.dsausa.org/?ref=karlbode.com">Democratic Socialists of America platform</a> that's extreme. And trying to base your party identity around subjects that won't be framed as "extreme" by bad faith MAGA fascists is like trying to stay dry at a water park.</p>
<p>Readers probably know that you can expect a lot more of this anti-socialist hyperventilation as we head into the midterms. Democrats will, as per tradition, spend more time fighting the left flank of their party than they will the authoritarians actively trying to dismantle democracy.</p>
<p>It's because the extraction class very much prefer <em>decorative democracy</em>. They spent generations crafting an illusion where your electoral choices involved either far right corporation-coddling authoritarian christofascism, or center-right corporation-coddling platitudes punctuated by annual Earth day celebrations.</p>
<p>But the historic corruption of Donald Trump has exposed the machinery of the con. It made it painfully clear that the Republican party is unforgivably corrupt, racist, and soulless, and that the Democratic party is feckless, <em>largely decorative, in no way structurally load bearing, and in desperate need of a rebuild</em>.</p>
<p>It's pretty clear that if America is to survive fascism, it's going to come from a good faith effort to rebuild institutions by honest, progressive reformers. You can call it whatever you want, but it's abundantly clear that the nation's richest assholes – and the <a href="https://karlbode.com/cbs-just-cant-stop-kissing-donald-trumps-ass/">media they increasingly control</a> – see it as their biggest threat.</p>
