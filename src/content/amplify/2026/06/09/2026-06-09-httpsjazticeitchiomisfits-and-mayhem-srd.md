<p><em><strong>Misfits &amp; Mayhem</strong></em> is a TTRPG mechanics engine I developed that is meant to be easily customizable to any setting or story. The mechanics are <strong>modular</strong>, meaning everything outside the baseline rules can be included or excluded based on the type of story you want to tell. It is derived from the <em>Powered by Kids on Bikes</em> system and uses many of the same mechanics, but in my mind, it's distinct enough to warrant its own name and SRD.</p>
<p><strong>The <em>Misfits &amp; Mayhem SRD</em> is best used to develop and play the following types of games:</strong></p>
<ul><li>Games that <strong>focus mostly on story</strong> (roughly two thirds of gameplay should be narrative roleplay and one third should be combat or combat-like scenarios)</li><li>Games where <strong>each Player Character is the main character of their own story</strong> (and one of the main characters of the overarching story itself)</li><li>Games with <strong>unique, homebrew, or highly specific settings</strong> that don't fit neatly into a single genre or pre-existing TTRPG</li><li>Games where <strong>supernatural abilities tend to be more flexible and narrative</strong>, rather than granular and specific like D&amp;D 5e spells/magic</li><li>Games where <strong>you'll be rolling a lot in tense or unknown situations</strong>, resulting in actions that are somewhat important individually but mostly serve to combine together into the eventual climax of the scene</li><li>Games where <strong>combat and other violent altercations are intense, fast-paced, and deadly</strong> situations where every move matters... and could be your last</li><li>Games where <strong>the GM's primary purpose is to be a storyteller, narrator, and lore/world/magic system creator</strong>, rather than someone constantly fiddling with and understanding complex mechanics</li><li>Games where <strong>a player's mastery of the system (e.g. min-maxing) is not only rewarding but even encouraged</strong>, especially if said system mastery is used to contribute to the narrative</li></ul>
<p>Importantly, this document does not contain any pre-made characters, worldbuilding prompts, or adventure modules. It’s simply the mechanics that can be used to broadly simulate a wide range of possible situations. Basically, <strong>this is the equivalent of a video game physics engine</strong>; to make it a full game and story, you and your table will have to come up with the story and world content yourselves!</p>
<p><strong>To play a game using the <em>Misfits &amp; Mayhem SRD</em>, you will need:</strong></p>
<ul><li>A set of polyhedral dice (d20, d12, d10, d8, d6, d4)</li><li>Tokens (like glass beads or poker chips)</li><li>1 game master</li><li>3-5 players</li><li>The PDFs included here</li><li>The time, space, and energy to tell a collaborative story</li></ul>
<p>The document itself is 29 pages and ~22,000 words long, but <strong>the baseline mechanics (including the introduction, character creation, and GM tools sections) are just on pages 1-9</strong>. Everything after that is a modular mechanic that can be included or excluded based on what type of game you want to play. Printable character and vehicle sheets are included in the download, and virtual templates in Google Documents are available as well. Additional mechanics expansions are planned for future installments of the SRD.</p>
<h3>If you play any games using this SRD and notice mechanical inconsistencies or problems, please leave a comment below and let me know! I will periodically update the document after playtesting new versions with consideration for feedback.</h3>
