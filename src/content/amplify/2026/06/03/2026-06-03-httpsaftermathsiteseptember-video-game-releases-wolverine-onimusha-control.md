title: 'OK, But Do We Really Think All Those Games Are Gonna Come Out In September?'

<figure><figcaption>Remedy </figcaption></figure>
<p>After a few of the many, many trailers shown during <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvyezhN16IU&amp;ref=aftermath.site"><u>yesterday’s PlayStation State Of Play</u></a>, a theme began to emerge: September. It seems that everyone in the games industry suddenly remembered the 21st night of September and all the others besides, with a whopping ten major releases set for the humble month and another six on the way during its annual sequel, October. Obviously, publishers are terrified of <em>Grand Theft Auto VI</em>, the <a href="https://aftermath.site/grand-theft-auto-vi-games-industry/"><u>sleeping behemoth</u></a> that wakes in November. But surely somebody’s gonna blink before it’s all said and done, right?</p>
<p>Here’s the full list of major games now scheduled to come out during September:</p>
<ul><li><em>The Blood Of The Dawnwalker</em> – September 3</li><li><em>Halloween: The Game</em> – September 8</li><li><em>Phantom Blade Zero</em> – September 9</li><li><em>Marvel’s Wolverine</em> – September 15</li><li><em>Trails In The Sky 2nd Chapter </em>– September 17</li><li><em>Warhammer 40,000: Dawn of War IV</em> – September 17</li><li><em>Dune Awakening </em>(console) – September 22</li><li><em>Control Resonant</em> – September 24</li><li><em>Silent Hill: Townfall</em> – September 24</li><li><em>Onimusha: Way of the Sword</em> – September 25</li></ul>
<p>And just for fun, here’s a selection from October, too:</p>
<ul><li><em>Rayman Legends Retold</em> – October 1</li><li><em>Dynasty Warriors 3 Remastered</em> – October 1</li><li><em>Ace Combat 8: Wings of Theve</em> – October 2 </li><li><em>Star Wars: Galactic Racer</em> – October 6</li><li><em>Planet Zoo 2 </em>– October 13</li><li><em>Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4</em> – October 23</li></ul>
<p>Now, many of these games cater to different audiences, and you don’t have to buy every game—even ones you’re looking forward to—<a href="https://bsky.app/profile/rowan.monster/post/3mndt734bdk24?ref=aftermath.site"><u>on the day they come out</u></a>. In fact, it often makes more sense to wait for a sale, especially when you’re dealing with triple-A price tags.</p>
<p>Still, this is the kind of pileup we haven’t seen in years, and I would have to imagine that at least a few of these games wind up getting delayed into the following months—or to early 2027, to once again avoid a potentially disastrous clash with <em>GTA VI</em>. Further, I cannot help but consider the current industry climate; if any one of these games falls short of—or, in some cases, even meets—sales expectations, layoffs and other grim knock-on effects are likely. However, given the shortsightedness with which companies are incentivized to operate, delays could produce similar outcomes. Not ideal no matter how you slice it!</p>
<p>Perhaps one especially bold publisher will move their game into November, a wintry dead zone outside of <em>GTA VI</em>. I’m not counting on it, but you know what they say about desperate times.</p>
