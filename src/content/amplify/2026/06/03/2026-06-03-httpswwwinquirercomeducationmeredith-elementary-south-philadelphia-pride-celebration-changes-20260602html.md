<p>A <a data-link-type="article-body" href="https://www.inquirer.com/topic/south-philly">South Philadelphia</a> school’s Pride Day event was amended after a prominent conservative social media account put Meredith Elementary’s celebration on blast on social media.</p>
<p>Meredith Elementary, in Queen Village, celebrates Pride Month with a day of activities, typically culminating in all students and staff walking the perimeter of the school, waving flags.</p>
<p>Principal Nina Brevard, in a letter sent to families Monday, said that Tuesday’s parade route was being shifted to the schoolyard and inside the building, a decision made by the school’s Pride Committee and administration, as well as Philadelphia School District leadership.</p>
<p>“While we remain committed to supporting an inclusive and respectful school environment, the safety and well-being of our students and staff remain our top priority,” Brevard wrote. “This decision was made to ensure a positive and safe experience for all members of our school community.”</p>
<p>The district’s Office of School Safety also would “increase the presence of school safety officers in collaboration with the <a data-link-type="article-body" href="https://www.inquirer.com/topic/philadelphia-police-department">Philadelphia Police Department</a>,” Brevard wrote.</p>
<p>Family members who had planned to come view and cheer the parade were still permitted to do so, the principal said, but were asked to stay on the building’s perimeter.</p>
<p>“Despite this shift, we still anticipate a day of celebration and inclusion,” Brevard wrote, adding that all Pride Day events are not mandatory and parents can opt their children out.</p>
<p>The conservative social media account LibsofTikTok, which has 4.7 million followers, posted about the Meredith event on Sunday.</p>
<p>Meredith, the account said, is holding a Pride parade “for kindergartners. Little kids will be given pride stickers and pronoun pins to wear by staff to march for trans pride.”</p>
<p>The account did not note that those students whose parents wanted them opted out would not be participating.</p>
<p>LibsofTikTok suggested supporters call the school to voice displeasure. A conservative writer also tagged the school district, saying: “Philadelphia voters did not vote for this. Pull the program. Fire the principal.”</p>
<p>No protesters appeared at the parade itself, which drew several dozen family members standing on the sidewalk outside the black schoolyard fence in a joyful, protective scene. Two city police officers and a marked police car were stationed across the street from the school.</p>
<p>Students inside the Meredith yard waved Pride flags and rainbow fans inside the yard, cheering.</p>
<p>Philadelphia has been a <a data-link-type="article-body" href="https://www.inquirer.com/education/gender-neutral-bathrooms-philadelphia-schools-lgbtq-students-20210712.html">leader in rights </a>for LGBTQ+ students; its groundbreaking policy 252 was among the first in the U.S. requiring that schools use gender-neutral language whenever possible and allowing students the right to use their <a data-link-type="article-body" href="https://www.inquirer.com/education/philadelphia-school-district-transgender-student-coronavirus-remote-learning-20200513.html">preferred names and pronouns</a> without parental approval or evidence of medical transition. It also <a data-link-type="article-body" href="https://www.inquirer.com/education/piaa-transgender-athletes-policy-pennsylvania-funding-philadelphia-20250225.html">permits students to participate on sports teams</a> that match their gender identity.</p>
