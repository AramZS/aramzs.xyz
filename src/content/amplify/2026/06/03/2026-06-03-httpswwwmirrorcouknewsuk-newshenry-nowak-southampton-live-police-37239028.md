<p>Fresh protests are expected on Wednesday after groups gathered outside Southampton Central Police Station a day earlier following the murder of 18-year-old student Henry Nowak. Missiles were later thrown at police near to where he was attacked.</p>
<p>Vickrum Digwa, 23, was this week convicted of murdering <a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/uk-news/henry-nowak-police-officers-southampton-37238497">Mr Nowak</a> with a "Sikh dagger" in Southampton in December 2025. Digwa lied to police at the scene and claimed he had been the victim of a racist attack.</p>
<p>In body-worn police footage from the incident, Mr Nowak - who was initially <a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/uk-news/henry-nowak-police-officer-resigns-37238937">arrested</a> himself - can be heard repeatedly saying: “I’ve been stabbed,” to which an officer replies: “Don’t think you have, mate.” <a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/keir-starmer">Sir Keir Starmer</a> said the footage made him feel sick.</p>
<p>Following the protest outside Southampton Central Police Station, a group walked across town to the area of St Denys close to where the murder took place. Chairs, cans and flares were thrown at police in riot gear. Stand up to Racism condemned "fascists and far right activists" who turned the demonstrations violent.</p>
<p>It comes after the father of Stephen Lawrence, an 18-year-old black British student who was murdered in a racially motivated attack in 1993, <a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/uk-news/henry-nowak-video-neville-lawrence-37238985">said history was "repeating itself" following Mr Nowak's death</a>.</p>
