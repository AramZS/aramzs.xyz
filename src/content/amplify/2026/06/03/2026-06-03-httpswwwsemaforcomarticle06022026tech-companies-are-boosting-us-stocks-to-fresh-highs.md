<p>Tech companies boosted US stocks to fresh highs on Tuesday, even as warnings bubbled up over the AI boom’s outsized grip on markets.</p>
<p>Despite geopolitical turbulence — a rocky energy market in particular — there is “<a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/goldman-ceo-david-solomon-greed-mode-ai-firms-ipos.html">more greed than there is fear</a>,” Goldman Sachs’ CEO said Tuesday.</p>
<p>But outside AI, stocks are far weaker than headline indexes suggest, and investors are underestimating the risk that carries, a market strategist argued, since “the profit cycle and the market narrative are all <a href="https://www.ft.com/content/d62da0d0-41ab-4d04-86d6-548d90629aaf?syn-25a6b1a6=1">leaning on the same narrow pillar</a>.”</p>
<p>Companies have yet to show real returns on their AI investments, Goldman’s head of equity research said on a podcast. “That doesn’t mean it’s never going to happen. It <a href="https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/goldman-sachs-exchanges/the-ai-investment-boom-when-will-it-pay-off">just means the stakes are higher</a>.”</p>
