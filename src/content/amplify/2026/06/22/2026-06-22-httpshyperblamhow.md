<h1><figure><img alt="Hyperblam: write music, not javascript" src="https://hyperblam.how/static/images/make-music-rough.svg"/><figcaption>Hyperblam: write music, not javascript</figcaption></figure></h1>
<p><strong>HYPERBLAM</strong> lets you make music with HTML. It’s a declarative implementation of the <a href="https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/Web_Audio_API">Web Audio API</a> and is completely dependency free.</p>
<p>Create <strong>pedal boards</strong>, <strong>drum machines</strong>, <strong>sampled instruments</strong>. And don’t write <em>a single line of JavaScript</em> in the process. Unless you really want to.</p>
<ul> <li> <h2>Declarative</h2> <p>Custom elements handle everything from sampling to sequencing</p> </li> <li> <h2>Independent</h2> <p>Free of monolithic libraries and proprietary technologies</p> </li> <li> <p>Use as many effects, LFOs, and auxiliary envelopes as you want</p> </li> <li> <h2>Flexible</h2> <p>Create polymetric beats, generative drones, or event-based sound effects for your game</p> </li> <li> <h2>Probablistic</h2> <p>Emulate improvisation using multiple random factors and modulations</p> </li> <li> <h2>No AI</h2> <p>This is for creating new sounds, not stealing old ones and making them worse</p> </li> </ul>
<p><strong>Pretty neat!</strong> Why not take a look at some <a href="https://hyperblam.how/examples">live examples</a> or study the in-depth <a href="https://hyperblam.how/guides">guides</a>.</p>
