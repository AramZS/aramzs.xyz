<h1>Microsoft’s Satya Nadella: We Can’t Let AI Giants Eat the Economy</h1>
<h2>In interview, Microsoft’s CEO offers a blistering critique of AI power balance and calls for earning society’s permission</h2>
<p>By</p>
<p>Bradley Olson and Tina Li</p>
<p>June 21, 2026 9:00 pm ET</p>
<p>Satya Nadella helped usher in the AI boom. Now he has a tough message for the companies leading it.</p>
<p>The chief executive of <a href="https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/MSFT">Microsoft</a> is joining a growing effort to take on artificial-intelligence giants <a href="https://www.wsj.com/topics/subject/openai">OpenAI</a> and Anthropic, outlining in an interview his vision for the next wave of <a href="https://www.wsj.com/finance/stocks/all-the-money-flooding-into-ai-is-a-giant-warning-sign-6e08e3ea?mod=article_inline">the AI boom</a>, one involving cheaper models, more user control and political messaging that wins the public’s trust.</p>
<p>Nadella offered a blistering critique of how the race for AI supremacy has taken shape, with a small group of companies capturing the value of the world-changing technology as they make dire prophecies about safety risks and <a href="https://www.wsj.com/economy/jobs/layoffs-2026-tracker-784ea69f?mod=article_inline">job losses</a>, insisting they need vast resources for limitless expansion.</p>
<p>“You can’t say, hey, all white-collar jobs are gone and this could even be a weapon and we will use all the power to build data centers,” Nadella told The Wall Street Journal. The public, he predicted, wouldn’t tolerate just a few models and companies “doing all of the learning for the world.”</p>
<p>While he didn’t directly name OpenAI, Anthropic or <a href="https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/GOOGL">Alphabet’s</a> Google—the three companies building the most advanced proprietary models—he made clear that Microsoft is seeking to steer the AI race away from a future dictated and controlled by frontier model-builders.</p>
<p>In a matter of weeks, Microsoft has rolled out a suite of low-cost models meant to drive prices down for customers bracing from the <a href="https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/corporate-america-is-starting-to-ration-ai-as-cost-skyrockets-1eb99d7a?mod=article_inline">sticker shock of skyrocketing AI bills</a>. The company released Copilot Cowork, an autonomous AI “agent” allowing users to choose various AI models, including cheaper ones, as they complete long-running tasks.</p>
