<p>• <strong>Starmer set to step down:</strong> Keir Starmer has <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/22/world/live-news/keir-starmer-uk-pm?post-id=cmqoybmn10006356swfy5bf74">announced he will resign</a> amid mounting pressure from his own lawmakers, paving the way for a seventh British prime minister in a decade.</p>
<p>• <strong>Potential successor: </strong>Andy Burnham, the former mayor of Greater Manchester, <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/22/world/live-news/keir-starmer-uk-pm?post-id=cmqoza8f1000j356sbzmzwe63">looks likely to replace Starmer</a> within weeks. Another potential challenger, former health minister Wes Streeting, <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/22/world/live-news/keir-starmer-uk-pm?post-id=cmqp1xt3v000a3b6sfgor2peq">today backed Burnham</a> and ruled himself out.</p>
<p>• <strong>Political upheaval</strong>: Starmer’s announcement comes just two years after his center-left Labour party swept into power with a landslide majority – and <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/22/business/brexit-anniversary-uk-economy-impact-intl">almost 10 years to the day</a> since Britain voted to leave the European Union, plunging the country into a decade worth of political instability.</p>

<p>Despite winning a landslide election in 2024, the British public soured on Prime Minister Keir Starmer almost as soon as he took office.</p>
<p>Attacked from the right over his perceived failure to control illegal immigration, from the left over unpopular economic policies – and by many across the political spectrum over his lack of charisma and political vision – Starmer’s position had been deteriorating for months.</p>
<p>Last month’s local election results – which saw Labour lose more than 1,400 seats across English councils and control of the Welsh parliament, where it had been the largest party for decades – further eroded Starmer’s authority, appearing to convince dozens of Labour lawmakers that he is not capable of winning the next general election, due by the summer of 2029.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, Burnham returned to parliament by convincingly winning a by-election on Thursday against the hard-right populist party Reform UK, who currently lead most national opinion polls.</p>
<p>That created a stark contrast between the two men, and offered electoral weight to Burnham’s argument that he is better placed than Starmer to defeat Reform in a general election.</p>

<p>UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer became emotional towards the end of his speech as he thanked his wife, Lady Victoria Starmer for “being a rock by my side in good times and bad.”</p>
<p>“When I leave the biggest job in the country, I shall spend more time on the most important job, being the best husband I can to my fantastic wife Vic,” he said, his voice breaking.</p>
<p>“And being the best dad I can to my beautiful children who are my pride and joy.”</p>

<p>Keir Starmer announced Monday he is stepping down as Britain’s prime minister, yielding to intense pressure from his own lawmakers and setting the country on the path for a sixth leader in seven years.</p>
<p>“The question my party is asking now is whether I am best placed to lead us into the next general election,” he said.</p>
<p>He continued, “I have heard the answer of my parliamentary party to that question, and I accept that answer with good grace. Every decision I’ve taken has been about putting the country I love first. That is why will resign as leader of the Labour Party. I have spoken to His Majesty the King this morning to inform him of my decision.”</p>
