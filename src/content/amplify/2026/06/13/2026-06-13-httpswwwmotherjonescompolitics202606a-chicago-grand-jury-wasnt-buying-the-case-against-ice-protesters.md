<p>The grand jury transcripts from the “Broadview Six” case, in which the federal government tried to charge six Chicagoans with felony conspiracy for their participation in an anti-ICE protest, were released this week, offering a rare look inside an aggressive federal prosecution.</p>
<p>The Broadview case <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jun/08/chicago-broadview-six-trump-administration">collapsed</a> in late May amid <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/may/22/chicago-ice-protesters-charges-dropped-broadview-six">prosecutorial-misconduct allegations</a>, a month after the harshest charges against the protesters were dropped. The transcripts show unnamed jurors repeatedly pressing prosecutor Sherri Mecklenburg on why defendants faced assault and conspiracy charges when the ICE agent whose vehicle they blocked was unharmed.</p>
<p>“This person wasn’t harmed, but by extension impeding and assaulting his vehicle, that constitutes simple assault?” one juror asked. “The law doesn’t require that you actually touch him,” Mecklenburg said.</p>
<p>The juror then asked whether the ICE agent had the right to drive into the protesters. “So if the person comes and stands in front of my car, do I have the right to drive against him?” the juror asked. Mecklenburg brushed it off. “That didn’t happen.”</p>
<p>“It happened,” the juror responded. “He moved.” In <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GjwEMr8Zr1g">video from the protest</a>, the ICE agent’s car can, indeed, be seen <a href="https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.ilnd.488593/gov.uscourts.ilnd.488593.87.0.pdf">driving towards</a> the crowd of protesters.</p>
<p>In another interaction, Mecklenburg explained the requirements of a charge of “conspiracy to impede or injure a federal officer,” which the Trump administration has <a href="https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2026/05/spokane-3-washington-ice-conspiracy-conviction-court-rally-dhs-protest/">repeatedly brought</a> against ICE protesters.</p>
<p>“Are you actually presenting any new actual facts or just a different viewpoint on your side?” an unnamed grand juror asked.</p>
<p>“Okay. I’m feeling the skepticism already. Are you going to be able to listen with an open mind? Tell me the truth,” Mecklenburg said.</p>
<p>“I heard this case like last week, and I thought it was a crock of shit then and I still think it is,” the juror said. Prosecutors required multiple attempts across three separate days to secure an indictment—and a visit from Chicago’s U.S. Attorney Andrew Boutros, who gave a speech to the grand jury on the importance of impartiality, according to a <a href="https://www.justice.gov/usao-ndil/media/1443716/dl?inline">special report</a> released by his office.</p>
<p>The Broadview case is part of a larger federal effort to silence dissent. Last September, Donald Trump <a href="https://www.justice.gov/ag/media/1415691/dl">explicitly directed</a> federal prosecutors to target ICE protesters, telling US attorneys’ offices to “charge all such persons with the highest provable offense available under the law.” Some prosecutors <a href="https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2026/05/spokane-3-washington-ice-conspiracy-conviction-court-rally-dhs-protest/">resigned rather than comply</a>. Others followed orders: in Chicago, in <a href="https://www.pbs.org/newshour/nation/federal-prosecutors-charge-3-activists-with-doxing-of-ice-agent-in-los-angeles">Los Angeles</a>, and in Washington State, prosecutors came for ICE protesters.</p>
<p>“The right does have a bloodlust to imprison dissenters,” Kat Abughazaleh, a former congressional candidate <a href="https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2025/10/kat-abughazaleh-chicago-trump-indictment-doj-political-perscution-ice/">and <em>Mother Jones</em> contributor</a>, said in an interview May 27. “I and a bunch of other people got hit by a car while exercising our First Amendment rights, and then the federal government tried to charge us with conspiracy.”</p>
<p>The conspiracy charges could have put the six defendants, who are all <a href="https://chicago.suntimes.com/immigration/2026/06/09/broadview-six-grand-jury-midway-blitz-boutros-trump">involved in local Democratic politics</a>, in jail for the better part of a decade, all for standing in the way of one ICE vehicle. “The conspiracy charge got dropped about a month ago <a href="https://www.cbsnews.com/chicago/news/broadview-six-fallout-us-attorney-grand-jury-reforms/">when we asked to see the unredacted grand jury transcripts</a>,” Abughazaleh said.</p>
<p>“The government was embarrassed, just as they were embarrassed that <a href="https://capitolnewsillinois.com/news/charges-dismissed-for-2-of-broadview-6-ice-facility-protesters/">ICE shot Joselyn Walsh, my co-defendant’s, guitar</a>. And they should be embarrassed. This is absolutely pathetic behavior from supposedly the strongest government in the world.”</p>
<p>In Spokane, Washington, three people were found guilty last month of the same charge the Broadview protesters were charged with: “conspiracy to impede or injure a federal officer.” Cases against ICE protesters in <a href="https://www.keranews.org/criminal-justice/2026-04-01/prairieland-ice-shooting-antifa-trial-trump-justice-department-politics-texas">Texas</a> and <a href="https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/30-more-people-indicted-over-anti-ice-protest-at-minnesota-church-bondi-says">Minnesota</a> are ongoing.</p>
<p>“I think the goal is to make an example out of us,” Abughazaleh said.</p>
