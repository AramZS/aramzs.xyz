<h1>Anthropic Halts Access to Top AI Models After U.S. Ban on Foreign Use</h1>
<h2>All Fable 5 and Mythos 5 users lose access after Trump administration declares the models security risks</h2>
<p>By <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/amrith-ramkumar">Amrith Ramkumar</a> and <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/robert-mcmillan">Robert McMillan</a></p>
<p>The Trump administration is banning foreign governments, companies and individuals from using Anthropic’s <a href="https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/trump-ai-anthropic-mythos-regulation-2378971f?mod=article_inline">most powerful artificial-intelligence tools</a>, prompting the company to shut off access to everyone to comply with the new rule.</p>
<p>Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick sent Anthropic Chief Executive Dario Amodei a letter Friday stating that the company’s <a href="https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/anthropic-claude-fable-ai-model-f41fb5d7?mod=article_inline">latest Fable 5</a> and Mythos 5 models are now subject to export restrictions, an administration official said. That means usage by customers outside the U.S. and foreign nationals within the U.S. is prohibited.</p>
<p>The broad scope of the restrictions led the company to limit all access to the two new, powerful models because many foreign governments, companies and individuals, including some foreign-born Anthropic employees, all fall under the rule, the company said in a statement explaining its decision to close off all access to the models. Its other tools won’t be affected for customers, Anthropic said in a statement. “The letter did not provide specific details of [the government’s] national-security concern,” the company said.</p>
<p>The move is one of the most powerful examples yet of U.S. government intervention in the AI race. Anthropic and its competitors are <a href="https://www.wsj.com/tech/anthropic-ipo-paperwork-9a48c35e?mod=article_inline">racing to roll out new tools</a> to customers ahead of highly anticipated initial public offerings, and slowdowns and regulatory barriers can cost the companies billions of dollars.</p>
<p>Governments and companies around the world have been using Mythos to identify and patch software vulnerabilities. Friday’s halt will pause those efforts and could have far-reaching implications on cybersecurity depending on how long it lasts. Critical industries from finance to energy have been deploying the model. Anthropic released Fable, a next-generation “Mythos-class” model to the general public <a href="https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/anthropic-fable-restrictions-ai-developers-cd9bf57c?mod=article_inline">with guardrails</a> that remove dangerous capabilities earlier this week.</p>
<p>The company and the Trump administration have been <a href="https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/anthropic-amodei-hegseth-ai-c12ee0df?mod=article_inline">feuding for months</a> over the guardrails dictating how the Pentagon uses Anthropic’s AI tools, and Friday’s action could escalate the battle between the two sides, analysts said. Administration officials and advisers including venture capitalist David Sacks have accused the company of <a href="https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/ai-anthropic-dario-amodei-david-sacks-9c1a771c?mod=article_inline">so-called doomerism</a> by warning of AI risks and stoking fear, then continuing to release more powerful models.</p>
