<p>BOSTON, June 12 (Reuters) - A federal judge ordered the Trump administration ​on Friday to reinstall exhibits and signs on topics like slavery ‌and <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/climate-change/">climate change</a> that it had removed from parks and monuments nationwide because they "do not align with its preferred narrative."</p>
<p>U.S. District Judge Angel Kelley in Boston <a data-testid="Link" href="https://fingfx.thomsonreuters.com/gfx/legaldocs/zjvqgqorxvx/parks.pdf">issued a preliminary injunction</a> at the ​behest of groups representing park conservationists, historians and scientists, who argued ​that the U.S. Department of the Interior has been engaged in ⁠a "sustained campaign to erase history and undermine science."</p>
<p>Removing these signs not only undermines “the ​integrity of the National Parks; it sets a dangerous precedent of censorship and ​sanitization,” Kelley said.</p>
<p>Kelley said she was ordering the government to restore the signs within 21 days, "by the 250th anniversary to properly honor the remarkable achievements of the United States."</p>
<p>Attorneys for ​the plaintiffs - the National Parks Conservation Association, American Association for State and Local ​History and four other groups - did not immediately respond to a request for comment, nor ‌did ⁠an Interior Department spokesperson.</p>
<p>U.S. President <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/donald-trump/">Donald Trump</a> signed an executive order in March 2025 targeting what he called a "revisionist movement" that portrayed <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/">the U.S.</a> as "inherently racist, sexist, oppressive, or otherwise irredeemably flawed."</p>
<p>Trump's order directed the Interior Department to make changes to parks, ​monuments and memorials ​to address any "false ⁠revision of history" that the White House said had occurred in recent years.</p>
<p>The plaintiffs <a data-testid="Link" href="https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/us-groups-sue-block-trump-effort-rid-parks-history-science-information-2026-02-17/">had argued</a> that the Interior Department was ​removing signs and exhibits from parks in violation of ​congressional mandates ⁠governing how more than 430 national park sites should be operated, and had adopted an unlawful policy lacking any reasoned explanation for why various signs and exhibits must ⁠be ​removed.</p>
<p>An Interior Department spokesperson previously said the policy ​required the country’s parks to “tell the full and accurate story of American history."</p>
