<p>Shares of Elon Musk's SpaceX tech conglomerate plunged 16% Monday to close below their price on June 12, the date of its massive initial public offering.</p>
<p>It was the third straight trading day of declines for a company that just 10 days ago orchestrated the largest IPO ever.</p>
<p>At Monday's closing price of $154.60, average investors who bought SpaceX shares on the open market after its debut have now seen most of their gains disappear, market data shows.</p>
<p>Shares closed at $160.90 on June 12, the day the stock began trading on the Nasdaq exchange. On June 16, they hit a high of $201.80 per share.</p>
<p>When trading ended Monday afternoon, the shares were down about 23% from their peak a week earlier.</p>
<p>The company itself — and <a data-rapid_p="2" data-ylk="slk:holders of its previously privately held shares;elm:context_link;itc:0;sec:content-canvas;" href="https://www.nbcnews.com/business/markets/meet-regular-people-making-money-spacex-ipo-rcna349847">holders of its previously privately held shares</a> — made more than $85 billion in the initial public offering. And with a market cap of about $2 trillion, SpaceX remains more valuable than either Walmart or Facebook parent Meta.</p>
<figure><p class="rw-outer-content"><span>Some content could not be imported from the original document.</span> <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/news/embedded-video/mmvo265021509628">View content ↗ </a></p></figure>
<p>SpaceX's sell-off came amid a broader market drawdown Monday in which shares of Google parent, Alphabet, notched their worst one-day performance in over a year.</p>
<p>The broad S&amp;P 500 index closed down 0.43%, while the tech-heavy Nasdaq fell 1.3%.</p>
<p><a data-rapid_p="3" data-ylk="slk:Oil prices declined Monday;elm:context_link;itc:0;sec:content-canvas;" href="https://www.nbcnews.com/business/energy/oil-prices-hormuz-sanctions-waiver-rcna351144">Oil prices declined Monday</a> to lows not seen since March amid progress in talks between the U.S. and Iran.</p>
<p>But that was little comfort to tech investors, who are concerned that short-term inflation will increase the debt burden of mega-cap companies that have borrowed large amounts of money to fund their artificial intelligence infrastructure.</p>
<p>Bloomberg News reported Monday that SpaceX was looking to raise at least $20 billion through a bond sale.</p>
<p>The move spooked investors who are "wary of the substantial cash required to fund technological ambitions," Jose Torres, a senior economist at Interactive Brokers financial group, said in a note to clients Monday afternoon.</p>
<p>A host of consumer-focused stocks also declined significantly Monday, including Amazon, Chipotle, McDonald's, Home Depot and Netflix.</p>
