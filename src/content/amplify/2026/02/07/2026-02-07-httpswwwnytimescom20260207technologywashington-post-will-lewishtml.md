<p><i><p><i>By </i><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/by/benjamin-mullin">Benjamin Mullin</a> and <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/by/katie-robertson">Katie Robertson</a></p></i></p>
<p>Will Lewis, the embattled chief executive and publisher of The Washington Post, has stepped down, the company announced Saturday.</p>
<p>Mr. Lewis said in a statement that he had made the difficult decision “in order to ensure the sustainable future of The Post.”</p>
<p>Mr. Lewis will be succeeded by Jeff D’Onofrio, formerly the chief financial officer of The Post.</p>
<p>Mr. Lewis was brought in by The Post’s owner, Jeff Bezos, at the beginning of 2024 to transform the publication and turn around years of financial losses and audience decline.</p>
<p>Katie Mettler, a former chair of the Washington Post guild, said: “I’m glad Will Lewis has been fired. I wish it had happened before he fired all my friends.”</p>
