<p>This is the guide that I wish existed when I was roughly at the midpoint of my <em>Hades 2</em> playing. Maybe this describes you?</p>
<ul> <li>You’ve cleared the final bosses a couple of times.</li> <li>You realize you still have a ton more runs in your future to power up all of your tools and complete the various story threads.</li> <li>When you’re past the first region or two, things feel hard still. You have a couple of aspects you like, but when you get a clear, it feels like luck most of the time.</li> <li>You’re trying out other aspects, and some feel unworkable.</li> </ul>
<p>That’s where this guide comes in. I want to show you how to competently use each weapon aspect and give you some build recipes you can lean on for consistent, repeatable clears.</p>

<p>If you want to understand one key to success and get to playing, it’s this:</p>
<p>Slower or high-damage attacks or specials want boons that confer percentage-based increases: Aphrodite, Apollo, Demeter, Hera, or Ares. Faster or low-damage attacks or specials want boons that add a new damage source: Zeus, Hephaestus, Hestia, or Poseidon.</p>
<p>Use your cast more. No: <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#always-be-casting">more than that</a>.</p>
<p>If you would prefer just a big ol’ list of synergies to look for, check out my <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#the-get-more-powerful-cheatsheet">Get More Powerful™ Cheatsheet</a>.</p>

<p>Just like <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-build-guide">the original <em>Hades</em></a>, I love the heck out of this game and I’ve played it a problematic amount during Early Access.</p>
<p>I’m among the first 20-odd folks to have cleared 50 Fear ever during Early Access. I’ve cleared at least 50 Fear with all aspects (including <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OXPVjbVCg4M">62 Fear!</a>), so I know <em>some</em> things.</p>
<p>An ideal build for me is one that…</p>
<ul> <li>Works with the core mechanics of the weapon aspect. There’s plenty of workable builds where you ignore the weapon. There’s nothing wrong with those! But you get a variety of weapons in this game for a reason, and all of them are potentially strong.</li> <li>Works with a handful of common boons. You can force seeing gods with keepsakes, so there’s a good chance you get a core boon from them. But after that, you might not see them much! Or you won’t get offered the duo you really need! I don’t want to leave you high and dry if that doesn’t happen.</li> <li>Doesn’t necessarily need a specific Daedalus Hammer. You might not get it! Of course, if a particular aspect and hammer combination is a spicy way to murder things, I’ll call those out.</li> <li>Once the basics are in place, the build has a couple of different avenues to scale up in power.</li> </ul>
<p>Basically the quicker and more easily a build is effectively “online,” the happier I am. Struggling through the Mourning Fields when your build isn’t effective yet or you have the ‘wrong’ hammers stinks.</p>
<p>In this house we believe in DPS. Killing things fast is the best and safest way to clear. I’ve used every build idea in this guide to clear at least 32 Fear — both routes — with the maxed Vow of Time; any of them should be strong (and fast) enough for nearly anything you’d like to do in this game.</p>
<p>When you’re starting <em>Hades 2</em>, you’re at the mercy of the game’s random number generator for the gods and boons you get offered. Because you should be experimenting with different combinations while you learn the ropes, this is for the best.</p>
<p>However, eventually you’ll want to tug the strings in your favor. Here’s how.</p>
<p>Nearly all my build suggestions will tell you to start with a specific god (“Start with Apollo on attack”) or broad class of god (“Start with a percentage-based attack boon”). <strong>Use god keepsakes in the first region</strong> to lock in your primary damage source. At the start of the run, a god keepsake guarantees that the first chamber reward will be a boon from that god.</p>
<p>My build recipes will also suggest other gods with good complementary boons or potent duos. If those gods don’t show up in the first region, take a keepsake into the second region. Your next boon reward will be from that chosen god.</p>
<p>I hardly ever take a god keepsake into the later regions, when there’s plenty of good options:</p>
<ul> <li>Need more damage? Try the Fang, Fleece, or Eye. Or Antler if you like to ride the lightning.</li> <li>Need more survivability? Try the Tooth, Bones, or the Pin.</li> <li>Need some speed? The Droplet can fix that.</li> <li>Need some extra Magick? Take the Wheel.</li> <li>Don’t need anything in particular for the second region? Take the Fig Leaf and skip a bunch of fights.</li> <li>Want to power up a Hex? Take the Moon Beam for a stack of upgrades.</li> <li>YOLO? Gamble on a free Chaos boon or an additional hammer. When leveled, taking the Experimental Hammer before Oceanus or Mount Olympus can last the remainder of the run.</li> </ul>
<p>Once you’ve taken boons from four different gods in a run, you won’t get offered any new gods for the rest of the run. Chaos, Hermes, Selene, Artemis, Athena, and Dionysus do not count towards this total. You <em>can</em> force more with keepsakes, but then you’ve got even more gods in the pool and you’ll be less likely to get any particular god later on.</p>
<p>If you’re planning on forcing another god in the second area, try to take a maximum of three different gods in the first.</p>
<p>Every standard god has “core” boons — attack, special, cast, dash, or magick recovery. The game tries <em>real</em> hard to fill those slots: you are nearly guaranteed to be offered one if a slot is empty.</p>
<p>So if you’re fishing for tier-2 boons, duos, or legendaries you might want to fill those so you stop getting offered a dash boon you don’t care about. If you can do so without unlocking unwanted duos, perfect.</p>
<p>On the flip side, if you must have a core boon like Born Gain: fill the other core slots with whatever, then Hera will likely offer Born Gain next time you see her.</p>
<p>The two Arcana cards that let you change door rewards (Enchantress) and re-roll boons (Champions) give you more control over the gods that are available and which boons you take from them, which in turn give you more power than any other Arcana you could take instead. I never turn these off.</p>
<p>Warden chambers (the doors have a skull) in early regions can <em>only</em> be boons, so those are ideal targets for rolling the door when fishing for specific gods. I’ll also roll doors to avoid a god I absolutely <em>don’t</em> want.</p>
<p>Most rolls, however, are for boon selections; another shot at a key boon lets you get your build online that much quicker. If you’re not looking for anything specific from a god, save your rolls and take the best option on screen. For important boons, I typically only roll once unless I’m desperate or I have a huge pile of rolls. Speaking of…</p>
<p>Although The Boatman arcana feels expensive at five Grasp for some gold, early money is nice! With 300 gold you can buy two boons from early shops, locking in other gods and getting your build online sooner. Even at higher fear with Vow of Debt you can buy one extra boon, a huge blessing as resources get more constrained. You can also skip early gold rewards for other things or get some goodies from Wells of Charon or Hermes Shrines.</p>
<p>That said, the true value of The Boatman is it activates The Fates in the corner for a downright luxurious ten rolls for doors, boons, or Wells. With that many rolls to start, you’ll feel more comfortable rerolling twice for key boons or changing Warden doors to different (hopefully better) gods. I turn Boatman off very, <em>very</em> rarely.</p>
<p>While rare and epic boons are certainly <em>nice</em>, if you get offered the boon you need, you take it — even if it’s common. I’ve cleared 50 Fear with level 1 common boons for my main damage. It’s fine.</p>
<p>Don’t fall into the trap of taking the highest-rarity boon on the screen, even if it doesn’t offer much long-term to your build or actively makes it worse. The correct common boon beats the wrong rare one every time.</p>
<p>Try to get a Pom or two on your major sources of damage, then don’t go out of your way for more. The bonuses for further levels — especially percentage-based boosts — won’t move the needle much.</p>
<p>The most important exceptions are:</p>
<ul> <li>Blitz from Zeus and waves from Poseidon have relatively flat level bonuses and increasing the base power is far more impactful than getting another +10% on, say, an Apollo attack boon.</li> <li>Hephaestus is like Cookie Monster but for Poms: blasts need to reduce their cooldown, and Grand Caldera has big level bonuses.</li> <li>A few boons have sorta silly Pom scaling: Aphrodite’s cast, Aphrodite’s special, Hereditary Bane from Hera, and Pyro Technique from Hestia.</li> </ul>
<p>With a mid-game incantation, you get a free Pom slice when you take a Nectar. So if it’s very early in an Underworld run, you have just one boon, you’d like a Pom on it, and there are 2 exits showing Ashes and Bones, you can roll one door into a Nectar for an early bonus level. That’s the only time I’d use a roll on minor rewards.</p>
<p>If you’re fishing for run-defining boons that aren’t core boons, look into Vow of Denial. The boons it deletes won’t be offered again during the run, which has the sneaky benefit of making it more likely that the boon you <em>do</em> want will get offered later. Also, frankly, you usually don’t care about many of the boons in any god’s pool.</p>
<p>Do <em>not</em> do this blindly, as it has considerable downsides! You will have to spend rolls if you get caught with multiple must-have boons that you don’t want to erase. Because you have to delete so many boons, legendary boons are probably off the table. Also, be wary of late-run boons — especially mystery boons. You can end up in a situation where build-bricking core boon swaps are the only thing you can choose. Check the Book of Shadows, which marks the boons you’ve deleted.</p>
<p>And before you ask: no, Vow of Denial won’t force a duo or legendary boon to appear.</p>
<p>With a later-game incantation you can salute the <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#the-oath-of-the-unseen-fear">Oath of the Unseen</a> in the courtyard, which changes the beginning of the upcoming run. I don’t want to get too deeply into the RNG seeding system but in short: if a night’s run was going to start with a certain selection of boons, it’ll now start in a randomized different way.</p>
<p>In practice, what you do is <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#use-the-god-keepsakes-early">equip a god keepsake</a> and start a run. If the core boon you want isn’t among the ones on offer within a roll or two, select Undo Night, alter the seed, and try again. You have to alter the seed every time you Undo Night. Repeat until you get your ideal boon to start, then off you go.</p>
<p>Although it’s not, like, the law, the elements for each god’s boons are a little nudge for which gods play extra-nice together.</p>

<p>Artemis offers mostly air and earth boons. Athena is all fire, and Dionysus is all water. Hermes offers a mix. You can get bonus elements from resource harvesting, Chaos, Narcissus, or Hera’s legendary.</p>
<p>Boons, hammers, arcana, or aspects can be quite particular about what they are modifying. Pay close attention to avoid surprises.</p>
<p>Pluralized “Attacks” or “Specials” or “Casts” means it applies to both basic and Omega moves. The <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#understand-the-core-boon-slots">core</a> attack (Strike) and special (Flourish) boons use this wording.</p>
<p>“Attack” or “Special” <strong>not pluralized</strong> refers to the basic moves, <em>not</em> Omega moves. The Huntress arcana is the most prominent example: it does nothing for Omega moves.</p>
<p>Attack(s) includes dash-strikes, although some hammers are only for dash-strikes or exclude dash-strikes.</p>
<p>If there’s an Omega (Ω) symbol (like “Ω Attack”), it only applies to Omega moves, not basic moves. The Omega cast is the damaging blast that occurs when an Omega cast expires.</p>
<p>Faster Omega move speed (e.g. Racing Thoughts from Hermes) and channeling speed (e.g. Revelation from Chaos) are the same: faster channeling.</p>
<p>“Strikes” is anything done with your weapon: attacks and specials, including Omegas. Well, except for Grievous Blow (Ares) which says “when struck” but that means everything you do including on-hit effects. I dunno, man.</p>
<p>Some effects last a given number of Locations (rooms) or Encounters (fights for a reward). Most of the time, they’re the same thing. Friend rooms, shops, and Chaos are Locations, not Encounters. In the Mourning Fields or Rift of Thessaly, a single Location can have multiple Encounters.</p>
<p>Without going into exhaustive detail on the damage formula, the following bits are worth knowing:</p>
<ul> <li>Multiple percentage damage boosts (eg “+50%”) are additive with each other, not multiplicative. A +80% attack boon from Aphrodite and +20% global damage boost is a +100% total increase on your attacks — effectively doubling the base power. More percentage boosts are always nice, but they’re most valuable on attacks with bigger base power.</li> <li>Added Power increases base damage <em>and</em> is further improved by relevant percentage boosts. For example, an axe swing with 70 base power, +30 power, and +100% damage will hit for 200. Power is always welcome, but it’s extra-welcome on faster attacks.</li> <li><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#flat-damage-soup">Global damage boosts</a> increase the damage of everything: weapon moves, casts, on-hit effects like Poseidon waves or Heph blasts, statuses, Hexes, all of it. These are valuable because they up the damage on, for example, a Heph blast <em>and</em> the weapon’s Omega move that triggered the blast <em>and</em> the resulting Heartthrob from the Omega move.</li> <li>Percentage damage boosts or added Power for attacks or specials apply to your weapon’s damage, not on-hit effects on those moves.</li> <li>Crits are triple damage, multiplied after most other calculations. Our axe swing above that hits for 200 will do 600 if it crits.</li> <li>When Grievous Blow or Mutual Destruction from Ares trigger, the hit does double damage, multiplied after most other calculations. This can stack with crits for a x6 multiplier on the hit.</li> </ul>

<p>That leaves just 12 grasp, which will involve hard choices and is mostly to your preference. I do mean that: I have Arcana that I favor but when I shuffle things around and use other cards, I find myself thinking “oh these are nice, actually.”</p>
<p>Here’s some brief comments on other Arcana:</p>
<ul> <li><strong>Sorceress</strong>: Take this if you’re using Omega moves. Even if you’re not, it’ll let you activate Huntress faster.</li> <li><strong>Wayward Son</strong>: Helpful to stay topped up, particularly with Strength where the healing goes farther.</li> <li><strong>Huntress</strong>: If you’re not using (many) Omega moves, almost definitely take this. Squeeze off an Omega move as soon as you enter a room to get it active. If you prime more magick than your maximum it won’t give you the damage bonus.</li> <li><strong>Eternity</strong>: A stealth damage boost for Omega-heavy aspects because you’re less likely to miss and will have to dodge less.</li> <li><strong>Persistence</strong>: The starting health and magick are good for both survivability and damage. If you need just one, Frinos or Hecuba can free up this Grasp.</li> <li><strong>Messenger</strong>: Dashing and then casting can give you that extra bit of invulnerability to dodge some tricky moves or leap into danger.</li> <li><strong>Unseen</strong>: Just-OK regeneration, 5 Grasp is expensive, and gain boons are <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#understand-the-core-boon-slots">core</a> so they’re hard <em>not</em> to get. If you’re concerned about early magick, take Hecuba before this.</li> <li><strong>Night</strong>: For aspects that alternate Omegas, potentially a huge damage boost. <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-eos-flames">Eos Flames</a> and <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-shiva-coat">the hidden Coat</a> come to mind.</li> <li><strong>Swift Runner</strong>: Faster sprinting helps you avoid a surprising amount of damage.</li> <li><strong>Origination</strong>: Pricey, but it’s the biggest global damage boost available. A gigantic power boost for Zeus, Poseidon, Hestia, and Hephaestus in particular.</li> <li><strong>Lovers</strong>: Helpful padding against Guardians, some of whom hit for ludicrous amounts of damage. This is often the first thing I drop for other things and then I miss it terribly.</li> <li><strong>Artificer</strong>: While getting three more major rewards is good, you have no control over what you get. I take this when I don’t have Boatman or to counter Vow of Forfeit at very high fear.</li> <li><strong>Excellence</strong>: Nah. Some rare boons are not worth 5 grasp, and the boost to Legendary chances is slight. God keepsakes will get your most important boons to at least rare anyway.</li> <li><strong>Queen</strong>: Ten rerolls goes a long way to getting that duo you want. I don’t go out of my way to activate this.</li> <li><strong>Divinity</strong>: Ups the general chance of epic boons from 1-in-20 to 1-in-5. Yes, yes, <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#ignore-boon-rarity-mostly">ignore boon rarity</a>, but that’s a lot of epic boons. You likely take a 1- or 2-Grasp card that you’re not totally jazzed about for this.</li> <li><strong>Judgment</strong>: Far too random for reliable clears. If you must, take the rolls &amp; cash to start, or maybe Persistence and Strength for early durability.</li> </ul>
<p>If you’re not sure what you’re going to do on a given run, Furies and Origination are <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#flat-damage-soup">the most flexible damage boosts</a> to use.</p>
<p>The layout in the image above is effectively my baseline. I take all the rolls, and the left-most column is (a) broadly useful and (b) activates Divinity. Here’s the most frequent adjustments I make:</p>
<ul> <li>For aspects that rotate through big Omegas, drop Origination for Eternity and Night, either of which conveniently turn on Moon</li> <li>For more safety with Omegas, drop Lovers for Eternity</li> <li>For a little more healing and damage, drop Lovers for Huntress <em>or</em> Night and Wayward Son</li> <li>For low-Fear speedrunning, drop Persistence for Night or Huntress</li> <li>For a faster first region at absurdly high Fear (like, really high, 58+), drop Boatman for Artificer and Night <em>or</em> Huntress</li> </ul>
<p>And, of course, a decision has to be made between…</p>
<p>They’re roughly equal and I use both — not at the same time — based on what I’m planning to do that run or if one of 'em will activate Divinity.</p>
<p>I recommend using Death until you have a handle on Guardian insta-kills, then give Strength a go when the card is leveled. A <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#flat-damage-soup">global damage boost</a> is nice, you get big value out of health recovery, you get to not think about Defiances at all, and you can summon Athena and her cracked boons whenever you want. Strength does, however, limit your options for defensive keepsakes and its placement is worse for Divinity.</p>
<p>If you’ve hit a wall with a particular Guardian, Death is going to be more effective health. Also there’s <em>many</em> ways to recover lost Defiances, especially in the Underworld. It’s also more cushion to use the White Antler to shred a region, which is very fun.</p>
<p>Early on, use Frinos. Melinoë is squishy and you need all the health you can get. The frog stays useful in the late game if you want to drop the Persistence arcana or have some extra HP padding. Also, he absorbs that pesky transformation curse from Hecate.</p>
<p>Once Toula’s powered up, her extra Defiance can be a run-saver and her damage is good too. She does, however, require micromanagement to keep her awake. Wait a beat at the room entrance for her to appear, then wake her up immediately.</p>
<p>Take Hecuba if you’re using mostly Omega moves; more magick is more damage and you can delay getting a gain or take sub-optimal ones with the doggo around. Also she sometimes stuns baddies. 14/10 great dog.</p>
<p>Gale is great to preserve your health on very high Fear without regular healing or to use the White Antler for a region. The built-in dodge chance is probably worth more effective health than you’ll get from Frinos or Toula.</p>
<p>If you don’t need any of those things, take Raki for the global crit chance. More damage is always good.</p>
<p>Get as many additional sources of damage as you can, then seek out “global” damage boosts to boost everything. For example, the Furies arcana giving +30% damage on only your weapon attack is fine, but it’s extraordinary when it’s boosting your weapon and lightning bolts and Scorch and Heph blasts.</p>
<p>If you’re coming from <em>Hades</em>, there are more global damage boosts here, including up to +100% from Arcana alone. Because of the relative glut of global damage, flat damage builds are viable more often than you’d expect.</p>
<p>Use the Furies and Origination arcana, nab a few things from each column below, then channel your inner Moon Knight and "<a href="https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/random-bullshit-go">random bullshit go!!!</a>" It’ll probably work out.</p>

<p>Hera is a great fourth god here, as Hitch is a damage multiplier against crowds and Extended Family will power up most of your flat damage.</p>
<p>Zeus will often feature prominently; global damage boosts not only increase all his flat damage, but also make it easier to trigger a…</p>
<p>Blitz from Zeus — especially at high rarity — is rarely bad, whether it’s your primary damage or you’re working it into the rotation. Blitz has outlandish scaling with rarity, solid scaling from Poms, and you can grow it even further with Double Strike and global damage boosts. Put it this way: even when I’m focusing on other things, if Blitz is in my build it nearly always lands in the top-2 damage dealers.</p>
<p>Don’t sweat Blitz’s requirement to do 120 more damage to trigger the bolt. That’s frankly not much. More damage sources can help. Conveniently, Zeus has good duos with Poseidon, Hephaestus, and Hestia. Or add Aphrodite and look for their duo Romantic Spark, letting you dash and sprint about for bonus Blitz damage.</p>
<p>If you’re going for maximum lightning and feel satisfied with three gods you got in the first region, take Hera’s keepsake in the second. Get a bunch of useless-to-you Hera boons, then dump them with King’s Ransom for a pile of poms. You’ll be all-powerful <em>and</em> an ass, the perfect Zeus cosplaying experience. Although if you’re not feeling like screwing over Hera, try…</p>
<p>Although they’d never admit it, the pairing of Zeus and Hera is tough to beat. Hera is often the best overall take for moves that want a percentage boost: the damage is good enough, there’s no range restriction, you’re halfway to Origination, and Hitch is silly against crowds. Meanwhile, a big Blitz or Storm Ring will up your damage on tankier baddies and Guardians.</p>
<p>Speaking of perfect pairing…</p>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#rub-some-dirt-on-it">Rub Some Dirt On It</a></h3>
<p>If you’ve got two gods that are vibing well together damage-wise and you’re not sure what to do next, I’d add Demeter and Hephaestus to up your odds of survival. Freeze and Snow Queen from Demeter are incredible for safety, and she has fun utility boons like Plentiful Forage or Steady Growth. Heph has excellent defensive boons such as Security System and Trusty Shield. I don’t have hard numbers but I would say 95% of my 50-Fear clears had one or both of them.</p>
<p>Bonus points if one of your damage gods is Ares, because you need a hefty 8 Earth elements to get his infusion online; Demeter’s utility boons and all of Heph’s defensive boons are Earth.</p>
<p>There are few gods I like to see <em>second</em> more than Ares, particularly if you can work him into your rotation on attack or special. The front-loaded boost from Wounds chunks down armor and one-shots weaker baddies, and you’re halfway to Origination to make up for his meager percentage boost. More importantly, Ares has insane tier-2 boons around Wounds: Grievous Blow gives <em>everything</em> a chance for double damage, and you can get some silly movement and attack speed by stacking up Plasma.</p>
<p>It gets better! Nearly all of Ares’s duo boons are good to great. Omega-heavy aspects will have a good time with Cut Above and his Heph and Apollo duos, and cast-heavy aspects love his duo with Demeter.</p>
<p>Or — this is harder to get fully online — add a Zeus core and Divine Vengeance and look for the Ares – Zeus duo Heinous Affront for massive front-loaded Wound-and-bolt damage. With Apollo’s Light Smite tacked on you’ll clear even late-region rooms in seconds.</p>
<p>If you have reasonably fast attacks or specials, Hestia is often a great take. The passive damage from Scorch starts at good, then Hestia’s Pyro Technique and <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#flat-damage-soup">global damage boosts</a> lift it to very good.</p>
<p>Aphrodite’s duo with Hestia that prevents Scorch from ticking down is terrific for hit-and-run tactics if you can keep Weak applied. One greedy example: Aphrodite’s gain and the Aphrodite – Poseidon duo will apply perma-Weak to everyone in the room.</p>
<p>If you’re finding yourself constantly at the 999 cap against bosses, add Demeter and the Demeter – Hestia duo Freezer Burn to fix that problem, clearing accumulated Scorch stacks for <em>instant double</em> damage when you apply Freeze. Pile on Scorch quick enough and you’ve got a boss eraser in just three boons.</p>
<p>If crowds are your concern, Hera is great support: Hitch and Scorch make a wicked feedback loop of pain against swarms. Also, Hestia’s Flash Fry and godsent hex upgrade (with Total Eclipse) are arguably the best room clearing tools in the game. Try the latter against Vow of Rivals Scylla and when you speak of me, speak well.</p>
<p>For aspects that use Omega moves prominently in the damage rotation, start with magick recovery so you can get right into it.</p>
<p>Although the <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#magick-recovery">ideal gain boon</a> varies by weapon or build, Zeus or Hera or Apollo can work for anything. Demeter can too if you can stand still (I can’t). Poseidon’s also great if you’re consuming lots of magick in bursts.</p>
<p>With magick sorted, see who else shows up in the first region and pivot into any of these other builds. If you’re burning lots of magick, allow me to suggest…</p>
<p>For Omega-heavy aspects, you typically want to be as efficient as possible with your magick use. But sometimes it’s more fun to throw efficiency out the window and torch your magick bar as fast as possible with things like Weed Killer from Demeter, Ocean Swell from Poseidon, Controlled Burn from Hestia, or Fine Line from Hera.</p>
<p>For starters, you can get expensive <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#hexes-of-note">Hexes</a> active in seconds, and there are some zesty ones. Or you can trigger Righteous Pike from Athena constantly.</p>
<p>Apollo and Aphrodite often show up here. Both are often good takes for chunky Omega moves, and Aphrodite’s Heartbreakers are more bonus damage from burning magick.</p>
<p>Hera and Poseidon together are solid too, as they both have strong magick recovery boons and their duo boosts Fine Line and Ocean Swell. Hestia also pairs well with Hera: Controlled Burn is a big boost to Omega specials, and their duo Incandescent Aura rewards you handsomely for recovering large amounts of magick.</p>
<p>Although I don’t often recommend builds that <em>rely</em> on a Legendary boon, Apollo’s is fun to chase. Double the Omegas is obviously useful, and the increased magick cost means more Heartthrobs, Hexes, or heavenly spears.</p>
<p>Want a gigantic health pool so you can absorb a bunch of hits and just keep going? The <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#know-your-elements">water</a> gods — Poseidon, Demeter, and Aphrodite — are here for you. Get lots of water boons from them, then add Poseidon’s infusion Water Fitness for a huge HP bump. Spiritual Affirmation from Aphrodite stacks with Water Fitness for even more health and magick, and if Hephaestus — also a clutch defensive god in his own right — is in the pool his Uncanny Fortitude can give you even more health. Aphrodite’s Weak or Demeter’s infusion or armor from Heph can reduce your incoming damage, too.</p>
<p>It’s not all about defense here: Poseidon or Aphrodite are frequently great takes on attack or special. I like to start with Poseidon on faster-hitting aspects and build towards his legendary for potent Guardian-slaying power. He’s also got good splash or Froth duos with Ares, Zeus, and Hestia.</p>
<p>Aphrodite’s infusion Wispy Wiles grants 2% dodge chance per Air element. Dodge chance is additive, so with a bunch of Air boons (Zeus and Apollo are your friends), Gale, and Hasty Retreat from Hermes you can have the majority of hits whiffing for no damage, dramatically extending your life.</p>
<p>Daze from Apollo makes even more things miss, and the best way to take advantage of a high Dodge/Daze rate is the Hestia – Apollo duo Warm Breeze, which <em>actually heals you</em> for each of those misses. With this build I’ve gotten caught in a flurry of Olympus sniper robot fire and come out of it with <em>more</em> health than I started with.</p>
<p>Maximizing Warm Breeze in this manner requires an annoyingly specific four God pool of Aphrodite, Apollo, Zeus and Hestia. For more flexibility, pumping your dodge chance with Zeus’s Air boons is a fine side quest any time Zeus and Aphrodite are in your pool, like Romantic Spark runs.</p>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#malfunctioning-pressure-cooker">Malfunctioning Pressure Cooker</a></h3>
<p>Big booms from Hephaestus are big fun. Heph has excellent-to-good blast-centric duos with Hestia, Apollo, Poseidon, Aphrodite, and Demeter. Find a use for any of them and you can assemble a bone-rattler of a blast build.</p>
<p>My favorite approach is to look for the <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#know-your-elements">other Fire gods</a>, Apollo and Hestia, who have the best blast duos.</p>
<p>With Hestia around, dig for her infusion Slow Cooker for added power on all your weapon moves. Aspects with faster movesets — think Blades, Flames, Coat — <em>love</em> added power. I especially enjoy this because any time Heph, Apollo, or Hestia show up you can grab another Fire boon and get stronger, even if the actual boon is meh or they don’t cough up a duo.</p>
<p>A comprehensive guide to combat in <em>Hades 2</em> is beyond the scope of this article. Mastery comes from practicing, learning baddie movement and attack patterns, and dying a lot. That said, here’s a few things that were not immediately obvious to me that will help you die less often.</p>
<p>While you can occasionally find an effective mash-one-button build, most aspects — even the ones that don’t <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-the-morrigan-blades">outright</a> <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-hel-skull">demand</a> <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-shiva-coat">it</a> for the aspect’s gimmick — do far more damage by mixing up your moves.</p>
<p>To optimize your damage, look for things that let you <em>do other things</em> while they’re hurting stuff for you. The specials on the <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#lim--oros-the-sister-blades">Blades</a>, <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#ygnium-the-umbral-flames">Flames</a>, and <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#xinth-the-black-coat">Coats</a> are excellent examples.</p>
<p>Casts (with a boon) and Omega casts are another prime example, and they’re always available to you. On that note…</p>
<p>If you’re struggling with combat, I am willing to guess that you aren’t using your cast enough. The movement of most baddies is slowed way down in the binding circle. Most are effectively locked in place, and many can’t attack until they’ve moved a certain distance.</p>
<p>I can’t stress this enough: the cast is a crucial mechanic and you ignore it at your peril. You should nearly always have a cast down. You can drop one almost any time, even in the middle of attack animations or while charging some Omega moves.</p>
<p>The strategic possibilities are endless: bash things in the binding circle for free damage from the Furies Arcana; pin one gaggle of baddies to keep them off you while you deal with another; stop that Wailer from chasing you down; keep a cast between you and baddies while you charge an Omega move or wind up an axe swing; drop a cast on you and Scylla to keep all the swarming fish off of you.</p>
<p>I have a bad habit of dashing and sprinting at the nearest baddie the instant I enter a room. Especially on the Surface, like half the baddies punish exactly that: you finish dashing within, say, a <a href="https://hades.fandom.com/wiki/Boozer">Boozer’s</a> surprisingly large attack range and oops now your dash is on cooldown the exact moment you need it to avoid damage. (Also, you left Toula napping back at the entrance.)</p>
<p>Save your dashes for the right moment. Speaking of…</p>
<p>Although you are invulnerable during the duration of your dash, the game punishes you for dashing <em>away</em> from things. What you want to be doing, almost without exception, is dashing <em>into</em> or <em>through</em> things. Dash through projectiles, dash into expanding shockwaves, dash through baddies and smack ‘em from behind. Especially once they’ve started an attack animation, virtually every baddie takes an eternity to turn around. (Hint: especially Eris.)</p>
<p>On a controller, the default control scheme is suboptimal. All of the face buttons (Attack, Special, Cast, Dash) need to be pressed in quick combination, and you have just one thumb. I assume.</p>
<p>Among the try-hards I play with, a common modification is moving Dash or Cast or both to bumpers. Having dedicated fingers for those makes a huge difference.</p>
<p>I played so much <em>Hades</em> that my smol brain couldn’t handle moving the Dash button. I move Attack or Special (depending on aspect) off the face and onto a bumper, a smaller change that helps a <em>lot</em> with the more button-mashy aspects like the <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#lim--oros-the-sister-blades">Blades</a> or <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#xinth-the-black-coat">Black Coat</a>.</p>
<p>Hitting things, for lack of a better word, is good. Not just because you need ’em to die. With the exception of armored baddies and Guardians, most hits from your weapons stagger baddies, interrupting their attacks and movement. Things that can’t move or attack can’t hurt you. Aggression pays off here; if you can work out how to constantly land hits, you’ll generally have an easier time.</p>
<p>To give one example, holding a Wailer in a cast so they can’t chase you down while screaming at you is fine. Lobbing a special their way to shut ’em up is better. Hitting them with a special or dash-strike <em>before</em> they can do anything at all is best.</p>
<p>Because being able to hitstun baddies is so important, the “Melting” hammers that destroy armor are always reasonable takes, particularly with Vow of Fangs active.</p>
<p>For armored baddies or bosses that you can’t hitstun, the smart play is to bait them into an attack, dodge it, then punish them. Nearly every baddie stands around doing nothing for a while after attacking, and you often don’t have a big enough health pool or overwhelming enough damage to constantly trade hits with late-run goons.</p>
<p>If you’re having trouble learning the animations, most regular baddies flash white just before attacking.</p>
<p>Unlike in <em>Hades</em> where you could destroy projectiles with your weapons, you don’t have many counters here outside of the <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#xinth-the-black-coat">Coat Omega attack</a>, Athena, or Frinos.</p>
<p>Don’t find yourself in bullet hell, losing huge chunks of health to off-screen goons. Chase down, <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#be-reasonably-aggressive">stagger</a>, and slaughter everything that shoots at you before you deal with anything else.</p>
<p>There are obstacles like columns, rocks, tables, gaps, or bushes in almost every room, and you have the advantage: you can dash through them while baddies have to go around, baddie tracking around them isn’t great, and most baddie attacks can’t go through obstacles while many of your attacks can. Stick close to corners and central obstacles to slow baddies down, have some cover, and direct their attacks away from you.</p>
<p>Nightmare eluding you? Once you have weapon aspects unlocked, here are some that are strong at low levels:</p>
<ul> <li><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-artemis-blades">Artemis Blades</a>: The parry mechanic is the same at all ranks, and the Blades’ base moveset is very good.</li> <li><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-persephone-skull">Persephone Skull</a>: Going invulnerable for a bit while doing heavy damage is handy and that mechanic is not affected by rank.</li> </ul>
<p>Alternatively, <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-staff">Melinoë Staff</a> or <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-flames">Melinoë Flames</a> are strong and flexible when ranked up, so you can use those to farm Nightmare for the others.</p>
<p>Among the hidden aspects, the <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-anubis-staff">hidden staff</a> and <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-supay-flames">hidden flames</a> are solid at Rank 1.</p>
<p>Over many hundreds of successful runs, here are the boons that really delivered.</p>
<ul> <li><strong>Anvil Ring</strong> (Hephaestus) &amp; <strong>Storm Ring</strong> (Zeus) &amp; <strong>Smolder Ring</strong> (Hestia): An awful lot of damage while you do other things.</li> <li><strong>Arctic Gale</strong> &amp; <strong>Frigid Rush</strong> (Demeter): Adding a second curse to your cast is handy, and Gusts slow baddies and projectiles too.</li> <li><strong>Arctic Ring</strong> (Demeter): Freeze is great; Freeze on an area is better. The damage is also surprisingly good. Take some magick upgrades so you don’t prime out.</li> <li><strong>Deep Dissent</strong> (Hades): Half the number of floating hourglass fuckos that show up in the final phase of Chronos. Also removes the summoned miniboss with Vow of Rivals Chronos!</li> <li><strong>Divine Dash</strong> (Athena): The GOAT returns. Many aspects <em>love</em> to be invulnerable while dash-striking.</li> <li><strong>Extended Family</strong> (Hera): If you’ve got a lot of <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#flat-damage-soup">flat damage</a>, you want this. Artemis, Athena, Dionysus, and Hermes count!</li> <li><strong>Flash Fry</strong> (Hestia): Free damage and funny chain reactions against swarms.</li> <li><strong>Glamour Gain</strong> (Aphrodite): Not the most robust gain, but it applies Weak all around you for safety and Origination.</li> <li><strong>Grievous Blow</strong> (Ares): A chance for everything to do double damage as long as you can keep Wounds applied is absurd.</li> <li><strong>Hard Target</strong> (Hermes) &amp; <strong>Enfeeblement of Cowards</strong> (Medea): There are many horrible projectiles in this game and you’d be happy if they were slower. <p>Hexes have a lot of haterz online. They’re missing out.</p>

<ul> <li><strong>Phase Shift</strong>: A couple of relatively safe seconds to wallop things is a little broken, particularly after you can use it against Chronos for a bit of time-slowing revenge.</li> <li><strong>Total Eclipse</strong>: Hefty damage for bosses, and the blue and purple upgrades grant even more damage or some safety. The godsent upgrade (Hestia) wipes out entire rooms for you.</li> <li><strong>Wolf Howl</strong>: You’re invulnerable during the rise and fall and it’s cheap to activate. A terrific “oh shit” button to avoid damage. With upgrades, the damage goes from fine to borderline silly.</li> <li><strong>Twilight Curse</strong>: This is useful without upgrades and <em>very</em> nice for rooms, especially the later regions. It even neuters some of the minibosses. The godsent upgrade (Zeus) gives it solid damage too.</li> <li><strong>Night Bloom</strong>: Even the weakest-looking revived baddie hits harder and faster than you’d expect.</li> <li><strong>Dark Side</strong>: A few seconds of invulnerability isn’t bad, particularly if you’ve got an ability with a cooldown that you’re waiting on.</li> </ul>
<p>Ok. Finally. Let’s talk weapons.</p>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>The starter weapon in <em>Hades 2</em> is your introduction to baddie management. Be thinking: which of your moves will dismantle whatever’s heading your way?</p>
<p>Swarmed or surrounded? Drop a cast to keep ‘em off you, or Omega cast to punish a group while you tend to other matters.</p>
<p>Are baddies in a clump? Hit ’em with an Omega special; it’s cheap and fast. You can smoothly transition an Omega cast into an Omega special for big damage against crowds.</p>
<p>Can you line baddies up? Try an Omega attack. It can also hit through obstacles. However, I find the aiming, hitbox, and automatic release on this to be <em>a bit</em> fussy, particularly on a controller. A dash-strike into an Omega attack is a reliable little combo against unarmored baddies, and you’re unlikely to miss that close. You can also dash in the middle of channeling the Omega attack to avoid getting hit, but it will reset the channeling.</p>
<p>If you want to torch magick, you can hold attack or special, keep holding the button, then every time you dash you’ll go right into another Omega move.</p>
<p>The regular attack combo can do some work and has decent reach, but be a little careful with the slower third hit. You can cast at any point in the attack combo without breaking it, so keep a cast down to get more use out of your cast boon and the Furies arcana.</p>
<p>Weave in regular specials — it’s fast, hits hard, and has surprisingly long range. It’s excellent at interrupting baddie attacks before you move in closer. The special cancels any lingering attack animations, so little combos like sprint → dash-strike → special, or dash-strike → attack → special → repeat, or attack → attack → special → attack work well.</p>
<p>In general I do dash-strike → attack → special as a baseline combo, weaving in Omegas as appropriate.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Trick question! You don’t have a choice.</p>
<p>If you’re concerned that the beginner’s weapon will be mediocre, don’t be. When ranked up, this is a formidable stick.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Dual Moonshot is twice the heavy-hitting specials. You want this. The aspect’s added power makes Giga Moonburst pretty fun too. Rapid Moonshot for faster Omega specials is convenient.</p>
<p>Wicked Thrasher, Mirrored Thrasher, or Rapid Thrasher are good for Omega attacks, but they’re also signs from the universe to just bonk things with your stick using regular attacks. With Wicked Thrasher basically anything on attack is fine. If you see Mirrored Thrasher early, pivot hard into Hestia, Poseidon, or Zeus on attack. Hestia’s gain is the dream.</p>
<p>When you have magick regeneration in a good place nothing will brick you, so the Experimental Hammer is worth a shot.</p>
<p><strong>Build Recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with Aphrodite on special; boost the rarity and get a Pom or two on it early. Your main combo is dash-strike → special or Omega special. Or Omega special → dash-strike → special. Add Hestia and look for Controlled Burn for another fireball. Round out your damage with whatever’s handy for your other core slots (Zeus attack is nice), a Hex, or Apollo might help you get a Heartbreaker factory up and running. This is alarmingly strong with the Dual Moonshot hammer, or the Ares – Hestia duo.</li> <li>Start with Zeus and take the highest rarity between attack or special (lean attack with a tie). With a Zeus attack, a handy combo is dash-strike → Omega attack or dash-strike → special (with some combination of damage boosts like a special boon, Huntress, or Strength) to pop Blitz. Add Aphrodite in any core slot to unlock their duo, then you can dash-strike and sprint about and things die. Or if Ares shows up early, slotting him on the open attack/special slot and assembling the Ares – Zeus duo Heinous Affront (especially with Light Smite from Apollo) is very potent. Or you might get lucky with attack hammers; Mirrored/Wicked/Rapid Thrasher with Static Shock will turn you into an efficient Blitz factory.</li> <li>Start with Hephaestus and take the highest rarity between attack or special. Lean attack in a tie. Hoard fire elements (Apollo, Hestia) or earth elements (Ares, Demeter) for Hestia or Heph’s infusion boons. Or add Zeus for Blitz, Storm Ring, Static Shock, or Master Conductor to put down some hurt. Or if you started with Heph’s special, add Poseidon on attack; they’ve got a solid duo too.</li> <li>Use Strength and Origination and consider taking Hecuba; you need magick to spare. Start with Hera on magick recovery. Spam Omegas while you also look for Fine Line. In the second region, take Athena and roll for Righteous Pike. Three Omega attacks with Fine Line will drop the spears. If Demeter shows up, add Weed Killer to drop that neatly to two Omega attacks. If you’re low on rolls and want Athena for other things, take Poseidon for Ocean Swell and his duo with Hera. Or take Zeus for some boons to sacrifice for Queen’s Ransom. Get a big percentage or Zeus on attack and get Origination online. If an early hammer has a spicy special hammer, spam Omega specials instead and look for Controlled Burn from Hestia to make them pricier.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Approach baddies and drop a cast or, magick permitting, an Omega cast. You can dash while channeling an Omega cast to dip away from danger. From there, boons and hammers will influence what you’re doing between casts. A combo like special → dash-strike → attacks or Omega attack (magick permitting) is a good starting point. In dangerous situations the regular special has decent range, so you can poke at baddies from some distance. See <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-staff">Mel Staff</a> for more discussion of the base moveset.</p>
<p>The leash with your familiar adds significant damage. Watch your positioning and keep it zapping baddies. Use Raki or Toula or Gale for best results: Raki will kinda-reliably chase after baddies, Toula is quite aggressive after you wake her up, and Gale will chase after you.</p>
<p>Double the casts is double the fun, most notably double the Omega eruptions. Winner’s Circle from Hermes is often welcome to get to the Omega cast faster. Lucid Gain from Apollo restores double.</p>
<p>If you catch baddies in overlapping casts, most casts will hit twice; the exceptions are Tidal Ring (Poseidon), Smolder Ring (Hestia), and Sword Ring (Ares). Sadly, Hostile Environment (Demeter – Ares) doesn’t pin <em>both</em> Omega casts to you, but you’ll still be a menace with a mobile Omega cast.</p>
<p>Definitely have Furies and Origination active. I also like Night for a chance at a crit on Omega casts if you’re sprinkling in other Omega moves.</p>
<p>The Dark Side hex-y is sexy here: you can cast and Omega cast normally while invulnerable.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Yes. Two casts have a lot of potential at all ranks.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>The doubling hammers (Dual Moonshot and Mirrored Thrasher), speed boosters (Rapid Moonshot and Rapid Thrasher) and Wicked Thrasher are all nice to have.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your pick of Zeus, Poseidon, Demeter, Apollo, or Hephaestus on cast. Zeus in particular can pop off on solo baddies, especially with Double Strike. Poseidon’s cast doesn’t hit twice with double casts, but it does immediately give you Froth which is easy to trigger with the Psychic Leash. Get magick recovery sorted and activate Origination between attack and special; a strong early hammer like Wicked Thrasher, Mirrored Thrasher, or Dual Moonshot might give you something to build around. Look for any of the following opportunities for more Omega/cast damage: Geyser Spout from Poseidon, Prominence Flare from Apollo (and the Apollo – Zeus duo from there), gusts from Demeter (especially with Air Quality from Zeus or Froth from Poseidon), Local Climate from Demeter, Meat Grinder from Ares (especially with the Ares – Demeter duo), Cut Above from Ares (opens a duo with a Heph core), or Fine Line from Hera.</li> <li>Start with Zeus and take the highest rarity you can get between special, attack, or cast. If you go with cast, proceed per the recipe directly above. With a <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#big-bad-blitz">big Blitz</a> on attack or special, use the Psychic Leash to help pop Blitz early while you get Origination up with your other slots. Some directions to be open to: Aphrodite and the Aphrodite – Zeus duo, Ares on the open attack/special slot and the Ares – Zeus duo, gusts from Demeter and Air Quality, Blitz duos with Poseidon or Hestia, Prominence Flare from Apollo and the Zeus – Apollo duo, or the speed/doubling hammers are terrific with Static Shock and the Heph – Zeus duo. With Zeus in the pool, don’t sleep on Lightning Lance: there are <em>many</em> situations where you’d love to drop a high-damage cast (or two!) from a relatively safe distance.</li> <li>Start with Apollo on magick recovery. As long as you <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#always-be-casting">keep casting (which you should be)</a>, it’s exceedingly difficult to run out of magick. With double the cast coverage it should be relatively safe to use Omega moves. So: spam Omegas to your heart’s content. The Night arcana is solid here. From there, put big percentages on attack and special and look for Omega boosters like Weed Killer from Demeter or Cut Above from Ares (good duo with Apollo!) or Geyser Spout from Poseidon or Fine Line from Hera. <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dBCBFh57QaA">Watch me (err, barely) take on all four Vow of Rivals in the Underworld with Circe Staff and Lucid Gain</a>.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>If you enjoy tower defense games, here’s your aspect.</p>
<p>Drop a cast or an Omega cast in between clumps of baddies to pin ’em. It’s a commitment, so do your best to target a crowd. You can move in, cast, and dash while still holding Cast to dip back out while you finish channeling.</p>
<p>The rotation from there will depend on what hammers and boons show up and how baddies are grouped. Blast tight clumps with the Omega special, or nail tidy rows of baddies with the Omega attack. In a vacuum, I’d lean on the Omega special because it’s more forgiving to aim, faster to charge, and cheaper.</p>
<p>You can smoothly channel the Omega cast or Omega special into the other Omega moves, letting you set up multi-Omega damage zones quickly. To set up a King Of Omegas Kill Zone, hold Cast → dash back → hold Special → release the Omega → dash-strike → hold Attack.</p>
<p>If you open with an Omega cast, once it starts exploding make a judgement call if it’s worth immediately dropping a regular cast: you can keep baddies stuck in your death zones, but you won’t be able to relocate your Omega cast turret until it expires. Another option is whacking unarmored baddies with regular attacks and specials to hitstun them, keeping them in place for the turrets.</p>
<p>Winner’s Circle from Hermes is great to get to the Omega cast damage faster.</p>
<p>Don’t get attached to your Omega turrets: if baddies aren’t where they’re firing, set up new ones instead of trying to draw baddies into your existing ones.</p>
<p>Because you’re cycling through Omegas, the Night arcana is great for burst damage, even though only the initial Omega (not the echoes) can crit from Night.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>I don’t love this one at the earliest ranks. The Omega echoes are really dang slow. It’s manageable without Vow of Frenzy.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>With triple the Omegas, the big Omega boosting hammers like Giga Moonburst or Mirrored Thrasher or Dual Moonshot are great takes.</p>
<p>The speed boosters — Rapid Thrasher and Rapid Moonshot — are handy for faster channeling.</p>
<p><strong>Build Recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your favorite gain between Hera, Zeus, Poseidon, Demeter, Apollo (each Omega cast pulse recovers magick!), or Hestia (poke baddies while your turrets are going to replenish). Get big percentage boosts or a big Blitz on attack and special and cycle through your Omegas. The Night arcana is solid here. Omega boosters like Weed Killer and Local Climate from Demeter, Controlled Burn from Hestia, Heartbreaker from Aphro, Cut Above from Ares, or Geyser Spout from Poseidon are worthy additions. Or try assembling a beefy Hex to cash in on all the magick you’re burning.</li> <li>Start with Hephaestus on cast. Your cast does damage while you wait for the Omega cast turret to get going, so you’ll basically always be inflicting pain. Look for a robust gain. A hammer should direct you to focus on your attack or special next; look for percentage boosts or a high-rarity Blitz on those. Lightning Lance from Zeus can give you a lot of control over where to drop casts and Omega cast turrets. For a variation, start with Poseidon on cast instead, which will set you up nicely for Geyser Spout from him later.</li> <li>Start with Demeter on cast, but I wouldn’t turn my nose down at an epic attack or special if those are on offer. Freeze baddies, then they’re sitting ducks for your Omega turrets. I like to add Poseidon for Geyser Spout, Apollo for Super Nova, or more Demeter for Weed Killer or Local Climate to amp up your Omega moves. If Ares shows up, the Ares – Demeter duo goes unbelievably hard on Momus, gluing your Omega cast turret and Omega cast add-ons like Meat Grinder to you.</li> <li>With the aspect fully leveled, start with Hephaestus, taking the highest rarity attack or special on offer. Take the special with a tie. Start filling out the requirements for Heph’s legendary; a rank 6 Momus has a 1-second delay on the turrets, doubling their damage over time. Get a robust gain, get Origination up between your attack, special, and cast, then wreck shit. I like adding Poseidon’s Geyser Spout and Winner’s Circle because must go faster. This one’s a little choose-your-own-adventure based on the gods that show up; either you power up double-speed Omega move turrets with big percentage boosts and <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#omega-maxing">Omega boosters</a>, <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#malfunctioning-pressure-cooker">assemble some blast duos</a>, or take Zeus for Static Shock and Master Conductor if you get an early Rapid Thrasher or Mirrored Thrasher.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Dash-strike near baddies to create a damage zone. Avoid the standing attack, which has a much slower animation.</p>
<p>Now work around the zone to keep baddies inside and taking damage. This is an outstanding use-your-entire-kit aspect: cast to keep things pinned, and use specials to suck nearby things in.</p>
<p>The Omega special in particular charges faster and hits harder than the other staffs, so that’s worth weaving in or building around entirely. It’ll zorp most baddies (except Guardians and such) right on top of you, a side effect that is amazing for clearing rooms but occasionally less amazing if they’re armored and shooting at you. Not helping matters: the damage ticks from the attacks don’t <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#be-reasonably-aggressive">hitstun</a>.</p>
<p>The Omega attack adds an additional, higher-power zone in an arc where you’re pointing it, excellent for choke points, enclosing waves, and large bosses. The arc from the Omega attack acts as a “wall” of sorts for baddies, kinda like your cast. If you’re quick with the inputs you can dash in, hold Attack to drop a zone, dash backwards, then tilt the stick back forwards to drop the Omega arc in the middle of the regular zone.</p>
<p>In practice the loop is at a safe-ish distance dash-strike → (time and magick permitting) hold attack for an Omega attack → cast, then do Omega or regular specials until your zone expires, dashing between specials to avoid the clumps of baddies that you’ve invited into your personal space.</p>
<p>Don’t get terribly attached to your active zone; if you can’t suck ’em in, make a new one.</p>
<p>Between the fast-hitting and large-coverage damage zones and the ability to shift the entire room into them, few aspects clear a room better. (Even if I think <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-the-morrigan-blades">Morrigan</a> <em>looks</em> cooler doing it.)</p>
<p>I mostly don’t go out of my way to do anything with the extra Lone Shades; you can set them loose between waves but I think that time is better spent setting up your next attack zone.</p>
<p>The Total Eclipse hex is fun here: your specials can bring baddies into the blast zone and help keep them there.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Omega moves are slightly more dangerous at earlier ranks, but this is a very powerful aspect from the get-go.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>All of the special-focused hammers are decent, but Dual Moonshot is the best for double the Omega specials.</p>
<p>Because it speeds up the animations for attacks, Rapid Thrasher is useful for safety.</p>
<p>Of the aspect-exclusives, Soulfilled Ankh is the best for a more forgiving zone size, and Mirrored Ankh and Pharaoh Etchings are solid too.</p>
<p><strong>Build Recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your pick of Hestia, Hera, Poseidon, or Zeus on attack. Use specials and Omega specials to hoover baddies into your attack zone and watch them melt from your on-strike effects or Hitch. Get Origination online and get magick regeneration sorted; conveniently, any of these starting gods are also good for magick. If you didn’t start Zeus, I like to add him for his special, cast, or Static Shock. Or throw a big percentage boon like Hera or Apollo or Aphrodite on special. Or add Poseidon’s Geyser Spout; the aspect boosts Omega casts too! With Zeus attack and an Aphrodite core, Romantic Spark is fun: drop an attack, bait or hoover things in, and sprint about.</li> <li>Start with your pick of Apollo or Hera or Aphrodite on special. We’re leaning into Omega specials, so get magick regeneration in a good place. Add Hestia and dig for Controlled Burn for another fireball during Omega specials; she’s also solid on attack, cast, or gain. Stack up Omega boosters or get something else spicy on your attack (see just above) and cast to round out your damage.</li> <li>Use Strength and Origination and consider taking Hecuba; you need magick to spare. Start with Hera on magick recovery. Spam Omegas while you also look for Fine Line, which you will now abuse by vacuuming enemies right on top of you and the exploding lines. In the second region, take Athena and roll for Righteous Pike. Two Omega attacks or specials with Fine Line will drop the spears. If you’re low on rolls or want Athena for other things, take Poseidon for Ocean Swell and his duo with Hera. Or take Zeus for some boons to sacrifice with Queen’s Ransom, boosting your Fine Line damage <em>and</em> solving your magick problems with a buncha Poms on Born Gain. Nab Hera, Hestia, Poseidon, or Zeus on attack. Look for a big percentage or Zeus on special, and get Origination online. Heartthrobs from Aphrodite are fun here; you’ll get one every Omega move with Fine Line, and you’re hoovering things towards you anyway.</li> </ul>
<h2><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#lim--oros-the-sister-blades">Lim &amp; Oros, the Sister Blades</a></h2>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>To maximize damage — both to baddies and your hands — the best rotation is special → dash-strike (or attack) → attack (or two) → repeat, skipping the later, slower parts of the attack combo and chucking out specials — you can attack while the knives are flying — at every opportunity.</p>
<p>That said, I don’t always use that rotation as it’s not entirely necessary. I often use the full attack combo, which is straightforward, good enough, and more forgiving as the final slash covers a bigger area. Chuck a special, then dash-strike and stab away with attacks in the back for the bonus damage from the aspect. Even that simpler rotation sounds more strategic than how I usually play: I mash attack and sometimes it’s in the back.</p>
<p>If you’re feeling dextrous and you’re quick with the inputs you can sneak in a special before the final slice for extra damage or to apply a curse. You can also dash right before the big final slice to reposition.</p>
<p>The dash-strike has minimal range but hits harder than you’d expect: get a decent boon on there and you’ll one-shot a lot of weaker baddies in the early regions.</p>
<p>I don’t often use the Omega attack with this aspect (check out <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-artemis-blades">Artemis</a> if you like it) but it does teleport you behind the target, handy for backstab damage and also for getting out of a pickle. Pay close attention to the targeting reticle so you don’t zorp into a worse situation. You can move around while charging, or dash to do a little hop and avoid projectiles or shockwaves.</p>
<p>I also don’t build around the Omega special, even though it has an outlandish selection of dedicated hammers. Here it’s the fastest and cheapest way to activate Huntress.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Yes. The Blades’ base kit is excellent, and most of your damage will come from boons or hammers anyway.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Trick Knives is subjectively my favorite hammer in the game and objectively it kicks righteous ass: you get seven hits out of a single dash-strike <em>with</em> backstab damage on top. Add Zeus or Ares on an open attack or special slot or Static Shock or Reaper Knives for a <em>real</em> good time. Don’t sleep on Reaper Knives by itself; if you’re throwing out specials at every opportunity — and you should be — the extra power adds up.</p>
<p>Speaking of extra power, Wicked Onslaught nearly doubles the base damage of the attack combo. It’s amazing.</p>
<p>Final Slice is great damage and the added reach is welcome for safety and against crowds. It’s funny with Apollo on attack.</p>
<p><strong>Build Recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Because the aspect is pretty fast, pretty strong, and has a built-in damage boost, you can roll with almost any boon on attack. Percentage-wise, Aphrodite’s the biggest number, Apollo’s range boost is helpful, Hera’s nice against crowds. For flat damage, Poseidon (good duos with Zeus or Ares) and Hestia (add Demeter for Freezer Burn) are both excellent. Mix and match some of the following things for support: Slow Cooker from Hestia or Secret Crush from Aphrodite or Back Burner and Extra Dose from Apollo for more attack damage; Ares on special and Grievous Blow to make everything stronger; Zeus on special or cast or Static Shock.</li> <li>Start with Zeus on special so you can boost the rarity and get some early poms on it. Sprinkle in specials here and there as you approach or in between bits of the attack combo to keep applying Blitz. Pick up basically anything on attack per above. Adding Static Shock and a Hephaestus core boon to unlock Master Conductor is my favorite way to go, but Zeus has good duos with Poseidon, Ares, Aphrodite, Hestia, Demeter, or Hera (if you’re feeling vindictive) so you have options.</li> <li>Start with Hephaestus on special to chunk down groups or armor early on. Take any percentage-based attack you find, particularly Apollo. Add Hestia for Slow Cooker to up your power on all the rapid attack hits and nab as many Fire elements as you can. Or add Zeus and look for Static Shock and Master Conductor. As an alternative, start with Zeus attack or special and add Heph later. Either way, you’ll want the Furies and Origination arcana to double-dip on global damage boosts. Apollo, Hestia, Demeter, and Poseidon have good blast duos with Heph if they show up.</li> <li>Start with Hera on cast, which will make up for the Blades’ relative lack of attack range and AOE. Starting with Poseidon’s cast isn’t bad either, as the many hits from your aspect will trigger Froth a lot. Based on who shows up, flesh out your attack and special with any of the above recipes.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Early on, interrupt baddies with the basic special and use dash-strikes or the attack combo to slice through weak baddies. It’s not worth taking the time to set up Riposte when a special and dash-strike kills a chunk of Erebus.</p>
<p>For beefier baddies, charge an Omega attack just before you get hit. You can parry without magick, and you can parry <em>everything</em> that can hurt you: attacks, projectiles, explosions, shockwaves, traps, thorns, fire, Chronos instakills — all fine! Parrying is great for getting out of a corner, as you’re briefly invulnerable after it happens. This is a more-experience-more-better aspect, as you can anticipate when to Parry when you’re more familiar with attack patterns.</p>
<p>Hammers or boons will influence what you do while Riposte is active. Spamming Omega attacks is one way to go: the big base damage and the relative safety of zipping about (you are invulnerable while teleporting) are hard to beat. Unfortunately, you have to do a dash-strike or standing attack to charge another Omega attack, consuming some of your precious Riposte charges. You can still get off 5 Omegas during Riposte if you’re careful.</p>
<p>With crowds, zorp around the perimeter so you don’t get surrounded. When charging an Omega attack, tap Dash (while holding Attack) to do a little hop to avoid getting hit. It resets by the channeling, but with the aspect leveled you channel incredibly fast so it’s no biggie.</p>
<p>While the Omega attack is the most efficient strike-to-damage available during Riposte, the second-most efficient is the regular special, which can hit twice, pierce multiple baddies, and crit both directions. Against big crowds or with low magick, give it a shot. Special → dash-strike → repeat is a good (and magick-free) rotation.</p>
<p>A point-blank Omega special is nearly the <em>least</em> efficient use of Riposte, but it’ll chunk stuff down quickly if you’ve got charges to burn.</p>
<p>The Riposte buff isn’t time-limited, so take a beat to drop casts to control the area.</p>
<p>When off Riposte, do attack combos, sprinkling in specials as frequently as you can. See <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-blades">Mel Blades</a> for more discussion of the regular moves on the blades. Again, boons and hammers might change up which moves you lean on more.</p>
<p>I’m fond of the Dark Side hex here. When you’re out of boosted hits after Riposte, go invulnerable for a bit while you’re waiting to Parry again. With the Godsent upgrade (Aphrodite), your damage output will be excellent during the hex.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Yes. The Parry mechanic is the same at all ranks. For Omega attack spamming, higher ranks are more convenient.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Sweeping Ambush is the killer Omega attack hammer if you’ve got the magick. If you get this, do everything you can to get the magick and enjoy instantly phasing early bosses.</p>
<p>Skulking Onslaught is second-best for big Omega attacks, and it’s pretty good if you’re just regular-stabbing.</p>
<p>Final Slice, Wicked Onslaught, or Reaper Knives are less good for huge Omega attacks, but they’ll increase your damage overall. No shame in playing like <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-blades">Mel Blades</a> — all the build recipes for that aspect work here too — except your damage output is spikier, you’re occasionally invulnerable for a second, and your attack/special slightly prefers percentage boosts because of all the crits.</p>
<p>Don’t sleep on Trick Knives. The bonus knives do not crit during Riposte, but 6 free special hits and critical dash-strikes is a <em>lot</em> of damage and you can ignore magick. Add Reaper Knives for preposterous damage output.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with Aphrodite on attack and look for solid magick regeneration like Hera or Zeus or Poseidon. Starting with Apollo (more reach) or Hera (Hitch is nice) is also valid. Honestly, that’s the meat of it: you’re looking to get your attacks as chunky as possible and have magick recovery in place for Sweeping Ambush if it shows up. If it does show up, Hearthrobs from Aphrodite (and the Aphrodite – Apollo duo) are a fun source of additional damage. Weed Killer from Demeter is excellent. If the hammers on offer favor regular attacks, get your base attack souped up with Secret Crush from Aphrodite, Extra Dose from Apollo, Static Shock from Zeus, or Slow Cooker from Hestia if Fire gods are in the pool. Add Zeus, Heph, or Ares on special for supplemental damage. Especially Ares: Grievous Blow or Mutual Destruction and a crit processing on the same strike is a 6x multiplier.</li> <li>Start with Hera, Apollo, or Aphrodite on special; Hera’s great on crowds, Apollo is consistent, Aphrodite is big number up close (and her duo with Poseidon can help). Special, then dash-strike is the basic rotation. Try to line up baddies for the piercing special. Add Apollo’s cast or sprint for Daze and Back Burner. Ares or Zeus on attack are great. If Heph or Hestia shows up, nab more fire boons and Slow Cooker from Hestia. Static Shock from Zeus is also a good take. You don’t need magick here, so prime away. This is quite fun with Trick Knives or Reaper Knives.</li> <li>This is hammer-dependent to really sing. Start with Ares on attack. If you get Sweeping Ambush, it has +400% damage built-in, so the added power from Wounds on the first hit <em>absolutely chunks</em> harder than you’ll get with anything else. Add Weed Killer from Demeter so it chunks harder. Cope with Skulking Ambush with its +150% backstab damage. Add Grievous Blow so follow-up Omega attacks can sometimes hit for double damage. Oh also you’ll need a <em>ton</em> of magick, so look for Hera, Zeus, or Poseidon for their gains. If the hammers don’t behave, you can pivot into assembling Heinous Affront with Zeus, or perhaps add Cut Above and fish for falling blade duos with Hephaestus or Apollo.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Sprint in, drop a cast on a baddie, maybe dash out of danger, charge an Omega special, fire.</p>
<p>While charging the Omega special you can tap cast (still holding special) then release special, a handy trick for approaching baddies or bosses coming off of invulnerability or if you’ve held onto the special too long. You can also cast just after releasing the special and the knives will start seeking.</p>
<p>Wanna do some sick <a href="https://youtu.be/I1d4BewyhlI?si=FP1FPNOV3ROG6DIa"><em>Wanted</em>-curve-the-bullet</a> shit? Of course you do. Aim outside the cast circle and catch baddies all around you as the daggers swoop back into the cast. Lightning Lance from Zeus, Glowing Coal from Hestia, or Howling Soul from dad let you place your cast at a distance, clutch for safety. Watch your spacing as the knives don’t have infinite range.</p>
<p>This is all, let’s say, <em>fine</em> at lower fear.</p>
<p>If you do push higher fear or use Vow of Frenzy, focusing on the Omega special is harder to recommend. First, the Omega special daggers don’t pierce baddies like regular specials, leading to crowd control issues. Second, the Omega special locks you in place while aiming <em>and</em> after releasing. Even worse, although you can dash while the daggers are flying out to avoid getting hit, if you do so early you won’t fire all the daggers <em>and</em> you lose the magick anyway.</p>
<p>In this universe, optimal play looks a lot like <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-blades">Mel Blades</a>: throw out a special, then dash-strike or do a couple of standing attacks, repeat when the special boomerangs back. The specials will do sick <em>Wanted</em>-curve-the-bullet shit towards baddies in a cast, so you can aim at one just outside and the dagger will swoop back and hit the other. Sometimes this is rules, other times your specials just won’t hit what you want. Omega casts are a useful source of damage against crowds.</p>
<p>Apollo is an excellent support god for Pan: Super Nova expands the size of your cast to make up for near-misses and Back Burner adds backstab damage.</p>
<p>If you’re doing lots of Omega specials, the Sorceress arcana is all but mandatory.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>If you’re leaning into Omega specials, I’d wait until the higher ranks. If you’re not doing Omegas, ranks make no difference.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Trick Knives is the clear winner, granting an (under)world-beating combination of damage and safety. You get a bunch of knives in the air by spamming dash-strikes, it costs zero magick, you can dash-strike outside a cast and still hit stuff (twice!), and it opens up plenty of boon combinations because you’re simultaneously doing attacks and specials.</p>
<p>If you’re focusing on Omega specials, Sudden Flurry and Hidden Knives are good. Dancing Knives is great for busy rooms, but won’t help against solo bosses. Sureshot Flurry lets you pepper baddies without bothering with a cast, an upgrade against solo baddies and a sidegrade or downgrade against crowds.</p>
<p>Reaper Knives and Melting Sickle are always helpful.</p>
<p><strong>Build Recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with Poseidon on special. Pom it as much as you can while looking for Slippery Slope or building toward his legendary. Wave Flourish gives you sorely-needed crowd control. Get magick regeneration and add another status on cast to trigger Origination. I like Hera here on both counts; Engagement Ring gives you better damage against swarms and Born Gain is manageable with a few Soul Tonics. Adding Zeus for Lightning Lance or (magick permitting) Static Shock is great fun. Gusts from Demeter are handy for triggering more damage from Froth. Zeus or Ares or Hephaestus are nice to throw on attack to sprinkle in occasionally, and Poseidon’s got nice duos with all three of them.</li> <li>Start with Hestia on special. Add Demeter on attack and fish for their duo Freezer Burn. Build big Scorch stacks with Omega specials, then pop 'em with an attack. In my experience, to reliably build up <em>loads</em> of Scorch you need some combination of a high-rarity or high-level Flame Flourish, the Hestia-Aphrodite duo, or Highly Flammable from Hestia. Or if the Total Eclipse hex is on offer, the godsent upgrade applies 999 Scorch to everything in the room instantly. If Freezer Burn is not forthcoming, take Pyro Technique. Hestia’s duos with Poseidon, Aphrodite, or Ares are fun avenues to explore too.</li> <li>Use the Night arcana. Start with your favorite gain like Hera or Zeus or Apollo. Alternate Omega casts and Omega Specials for a potent mix of area blasts and single-target damage. Get whatever big percentage boost on special for a flurry of big crits — it’s Aphrodite’s time to shine. (You can also start with a special and add a gain soon.) One approach I like is to start with Apollo’s gain or special and add the Fire gods for added power from Hestia’s Slow Cooker. Back Burner is 🔥 for this setup.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Against the weakest baddies, don’t bother with the Blood Triad. Use dash-strike → attack, which jumps to the “final” slice of the two-attack regular combo. Chuck out specials for a little extra damage. An Omega attack will also wipe out trash baddies in a blink.</p>
<p>Against tougher baddies, go for Triads for huge damage and also, not for nothing, sick style points. Between the teleportation and area coverage of Omega attacks, boomerang specials, and quick dash-strikes followed by that chunky Triad cha-ching, nothing looks cooler than Morrigan clearing a room. It’s a fact.</p>
<p>The Triad indicator is top = Omega, left = attack, right = special. The corners of the triangle with dots have been satisfied; the dot-free corners are what you need to hit still.</p>
<p>While dashing you can launch a special and buffer a dash-strike right after, so a fast Triad combo is Dash → Special → hold Attack → release to Omega. I <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#maybe-rebind-your-controls">rebind</a> Special off a face button to make this easier.</p>
<p>Because channeling the Omega attack is the riskiest bit, a more practical Triad combo is hold Attack → release to Omega → Special → attack or dash-strike (Triad) → hold Attack to repeat the loop.</p>
<p>To look super fucking rad, abuse the boomerang special to complete two or even three Triads with one special. <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bXsUwT33Qas">This slightly outdated (but still informative) video</a> and <a href="https://youtu.be/Vj1rg2mVgSg">this more recent one</a> from Loose Cage on YouTube break down the steps.</p>
<p>While reliable combos are good to know, stay flexible and adapt to complete Triads. The Omega attack throws out more slashes to the side, so scan crowds for easy targets to finish off with a dash-strike and special. When the special is covered or you only need an Omega to complete a Triad on a beefy baddie, use an Omega special to apply another Omega on the way back. If you only need the attack, a standing attack will complete the Triad and immediately apply an attack for the next one.</p>
<p>The Omegas are cheap and are primarily for triggering Triads, so this is an especially efficient magick-to-damage aspect.</p>
<p>The Sorceress arcana is effectively mandatory. I also find Eternity very helpful for timing the coolest-looking Omega attack zorp backstabs.</p>
<p>Poseidon is <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#why-am-i-not-getting-as-many-poseidon-waves-as-i-think-i-should-be-getting">somewhat restricted</a> on attack or special here. You get 2 waves on standing attacks even though they hit thrice, and specials don’t always trigger a wave per strike.</p>
<p>Worth noting: the Triad’s damage is fixed, so boons or other global damage boosts won’t boost it. If you have concerns about it not scaling up, don’t. It’s still meaningful damage even into the endgame. Well, unless…</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>This one is not a world-beater at the lowest ranks; you’re missing a <em>lot</em> of Triad damage.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Two of the aspect-exclusive hammers (or Heph’s legendary) are the only way to get more damage out of the Triad, either via more of 'em (Banshee Brand) or more damaging ones (Phantom Etchings). Both are good.</p>
<p>Sweeping Ambush gives more coverage on the Omega attack and a lot more damage overall.</p>
<p>Wicked Onslaught or Final Slice are both terrific here to juice up regular attacks. If you’re finding Triads tricky to pull off, either of these can be sufficient damage with simple dash-strike → attack combos.</p>
<p><strong>Build Recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your pick of Hestia, Apollo, or Hera on attack. Hestia’s nice for clearing smaller baddies and wearing down bigger ones before you coup de grâce 'em with a Triad. Apollo’s area coverage is helpful and his Legendary has loads of potential: pop a Triad with an Omega attack and instantly apply a second Omega for the next Triad. Hera is terrific for clearing rooms and lets you get more damage from Triads by Hitching it around. If I <em>had</em> to pick one, I’d pick Hera. For your special, Zeus or Ares are fantastic. Weed Killer from Demeter can get you more Omega attack damage. <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y_JBoCGyUps">Watch me clear the Surface at 32 Fear (Vow of Rivals 4) with Morrigan Blades and Hera’s attack</a>.</li> <li>Start with Demeter and pick whichever of attack, cast, or special. Take a high-rarity attack over the others. Frozen baddies are sitting ducks to complete the Triad, and Weed Killer is already in the pool for more damage on Omega attacks. Get magick recovery and Origination sorted, and power up whatever shows up for your open core slots.</li> <li>Start with Zeus on special. I wouldn’t turn my nose down at a high-rarity attack if that’s on offer. Omega attacks and Triads will pop Blitz immediately (assuming they survived the Triad), giving you another potent damage source to build around. Get Origination active, and toss something on attack per any of the above recipes. While this is niche, hammer dependent, and only sorta effective, have some fun with Arc Flash from Zeus and the Sinister Pinion hammer: an Omega special will apply and immediately trigger Blitz, and you can sprint around while your Omega specials give chase.</li> <li>Start with Hephaestus and pick the highest rarity between attack or special. I’d go with special in a tie. Assemble the rest of your kit with a combination of Hestia (gain, attack, cast), Poseidon (gain, cast), Apollo (attack, cast, dash), Ares (special, Grievous Blow, Cut Above, cast, dash), or Demeter (special, cast). Then you want every Heph duo you can get for more/bigger/faster blasts between Triads. Fair warning: this is very much the opposite of an ASMR build.</li> </ul>
<h2><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#ygnium-the-umbral-flames">Ygnium, The Umbral Flames</a></h2>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-flames">Aspect of Melinoë Flames</a></h3>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Charge up an Omega special, then approach baddies and hold Attack to bonk baddies with your flames. Maintain a little distance so your specials keep smacking things. Unfortunately the Omega special doesn’t hitstun, but attacks and the regular special do.</p>
<p>You can slide around, dash, and drop casts while holding Attack, so if you’re about to get hit, dash out of the way. When your specials expire back away, reload your Omega special, and repeat.</p>
<p>Regular specials have short range but can hit twice. If you’re surrounded up close or some baddie is near death and you don’t wanna refresh your Omegas, it’s your best option.</p>
<p>Consider changing your control scheme. I move Attack to a shoulder button so I can tap dash or cast on face buttons while holding down Attack.</p>
<p>Because of the built-in critical hit chance, attacks and specials <em>slightly</em> prefer big percentage boosts, but nearly anything is fine. This is a great submit-to-RNGesus aspect where you throw whatever on your core slots and it somehow works out.</p>
<p>Hexes have a fun wrinkle here: when you activate a hex your Omega specials smack baddies during any time freezes or while you’re transformed with Dark Side.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Yes. The extra critical chance from higher ranks doesn’t alter how to approach the aspect.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Hidden Helix not only boosts your Omega special but also doubles up your regular specials, a substantial damage boost.</p>
<p>Dividing Blaze or Inverted Blaze make your regular attacks pierce baddies, a helpful upgrade against crowds. With Inverted Blaze you can do funny tricks like stack up a bunch of attacks behind something and dash back, or sprint past a bunch of baddies and let your trailing flames torch 'em.</p>
<p>Otherwise, most hammers are nice bonuses and don’t change your rotation. I like Enduring Coil, Furious Blaze, Sudden Burst, and Clean Coil.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your favorite (or whoever you’d like to lock in) of Hera or Zeus or Apollo or Ares or Hestia or Poseidon on magick recovery, from which point forward you kinda have to go out of your way to run out of magick. Put your favorite percentage boost on attack and get Origination up with your special or cast. Starting with Hera attack or special and getting magick recovery next works too; you can get through a few rooms (or many rooms with Hecuba) without a magick recovery boon. <p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>The Omega attack is the play here: it charges incredibly fast, covers a ginormous area, and hits hard. As with the other axes, you can charge and then dash to avoid damage and start whirling at the end of your dash. You can also dash to cancel out of it if you’re about to get smacked.</p>
<p>Similarly, the Omega special comes out alarmingly fast. It’s also your fastest-hitting option to restore health against solo baddies while Berserk. After it’s firing, you can dash so you’re not stuck in place while the shockwaves are happening. I like to sprint up, dash-strike, hold special and release for the Omega.</p>
<p>I mostly stick to the Omega moves, dash-strikes, and specials. That said, the attack combo is satisfying to vent some aggression. It’s dangerous against bosses and armored baddies. Dash in between parts of the combo to get out of harm’s way or hit something otherwise just out of reach. You’ll have to rewire your brain, but an alternative to the dash-strike is tap attack, then dash to reposition mid-swing; you’ll have invulnerability during the dash and it hits harder than the aspect’s actual dash-strike.</p>
<p>The Berserk buff heals even with the Vow of Scars active. I speak from experience: you can play <em>real</em> sloppy with Nergal and it’s weirdly fine because you heal yourself constantly.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>The damage is there at Rank 1, but if you were banking on the Berserk buff, stay away until you rank up.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Psychic Whirlwind is the best. It’s not even close. Omega attack, then race around, dash, cast, special, Omega special, charge another Omega attack… whatever you want while everything dies around you.</p>
<p>Dashing Heave is solid here. Like with Mel Axe, you can go surprisingly far with a simple dash-strike → special rotation.</p>
<p>Giga Cleaver gets more hits out of the Omega special, which is pretty funny.</p>
<p>Of the aspect-exclusives, Iron Core is nice for survivability. And you should at least <em>try</em> Heaven Splitter once; attack, then tap dash for best results.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your pick of Apollo (insane reach) or Hera (best against crowds) on attack. Add Demeter for some Freeze and Weed Killer if you’re big on Omega attacks. Zeus or Aphrodite are excellent on special. Apollo has a bunch of nice-to-have attack bonus boons, or you can flex for his Legendary (very amusing on the Omega special) or make a bunch of Heartthrobs with Aphrodite.</li> <li>Start with Demeter on attack. You’d really like epic or heroic rarity here, but it’s not the end of the world. Baddies will stay frozen long enough to set up a second Omega attack, and Demeter’s in the pool already for Weed Killer to up her just-ok attack damage. Add Ares on cast or Meat Grinder next to unlock their duo, then cycle between Omega casts and Omega attacks, especially with Psychic Whirlwind. Cut Above from Ares is solid, or if Heph shows up he’s worth chucking on special and looking for the Heph – Demeter duo.</li> </ul>
<h2><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#ravaal-the-argent-skull">Ravaal, the Argent Skull</a></h2>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-skull">Aspect of Melinoë Skull</a></h3>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Pin baddies in a cast and pepper them with attacks as fast as you can launch them. Closer is better. Tap special to retrieve your skulls, either going through a baddie, or to the side if it isn’t safe to ram into something. Repeat. You can stun-lock unarmored baddies with continuous attacks and specials once you have the rhythm down.</p>
<p>Dash → Special lets you scoot around quickly if you need to outrun something or get into a better position.</p>
<p>The Huntress arcana goes hard here; open locations with a random Omega move to burn some magick and keep Huntress active.</p>
<p>Don’t ignore Omega moves. The Omega attack is great against crowds that aren’t clumped together tightly enough to all get hit by regular attacks. Conveniently, the blast zone covers about the same area as your cast.</p>
<p>You are invulnerable during an Omega special, so keep that in your pocket to get your skulls out of a dangerous situation.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Try it out to see if you like the playstyle or some of the more insane hammers like Possessed Array. Otherwise, this stands out more at later ranks.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#what-works-with-the-skull-hammer-possessed-array">Possessed Array</a> is a ton of additional damage, but you do need a ton of magick (or Flood Gain from Poseidon) to trigger it frequently. Scale up with all the Omega-boosting goodies like Weed Killer from Demeter or Apollo’s legendary.</p>
<p>Fetching Array lets you machine gun baddies nonstop. Stand a slight distance away to make sure you’re getting the bonus power on later skulls before the first ones return to you.</p>
<p>With Bolstered Array you can either use the added ammo for more flexibility, or chuck some out of the way and then play like normal with the added attack power for each skull that’s out of your hands.</p>
<p>Wide Grin sadly doesn’t instantly apply the aspect’s damage bonus to all your skulls with regular attacks, but it does let you fire all your skulls in an Omega attack for the cost of one. Consider leaning into that if it’s the best early option. Adding Bolstered Array later is good fun. As you can imagine, Apollo’s legendary is bananas here.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your pick of Hera (Hitch is good), Aphrodite (big number), Apollo (big booms), or Ares (big first hit) on attack. From there I like seeing Apollo for attack bonuses like Extra Dose or Back Burner; Demeter on cast for safety; or Heph or Zeus for supplemental damage on cast and special. Ares on whichever of attack or special is nice to open up Grievous Blow to help with the tankiest baddies.</li> <li>Start with Hephaestus on special. We want Heph early because we’re hunting for his legendary; when the aspect is maxed out, his legendary boosts the aspect bonus to +21 power <em>per skull</em> that’s out of your hands. Get whichever of Hera, Apollo, Aphrodite, or Demeter on attack and power up Heph’s blasts — Grand Caldera, duos with Apollo, Hestia, Demeter, Posiedon — for additional burst damage. Bolstered Array is very silly here.</li> <li>This one’s a dream build, but it’s both hammer-and-legendary-specific: Start with Apollo on attack, get a robust gain (Hera or Poseidon, probably), then start fleshing out the prerequisites for Apollo’s legendary. Add Demeter for Weed Killer. Then we pray for the Possessed Array hammer, where we get the bonuses from Exceptional Talent and Weed Killer for free, with jumbo-sized, extra-damaging double-skullsplosions whenever you pick one up.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Hit attack once or twice. Race up to baddies. Then tap special to go through them and pop your attacks. Load another attack or two, special back through the other way. Rip and tear until it is done.</p>
<p>I <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#maybe-rebind-your-controls">rebind</a> Attack or Special to shoulder buttons on a controller to make the loop easier to execute. Play around with different combinations until something feels good for you.</p>
<p>Dash → Special covers a lot of ground quickly, either to reposition or just outrun almost anything the game throws at you.</p>
<p>Once you’ve got the damage loop down, this is a dangerous but ridiculously fun aspect. The fast attack-special cycle, built-in damage boost, and AOE from the explosions makes it one of the strongest aspects in the game.</p>
<p>Approach baddies from the side or behind or in between their attacks so you don’t get whacked. Keep a cast down to control swarms. Against armored baddies, load multiple skulls for the first attack to hopefully break their armor. For bigger armored baddies and Wardens and Guardians, you have to know your openings to dive in unless you’re willing to trade damage.</p>
<p>More than any other aspect, Medea rewards your knowledge of baddie movements, attack patterns, and health pools. If you tried it at one point and hated it, give it another shot when you’ve got a little more experience.</p>
<p>Although I generally ignore Omega moves here, keep the Omega special in your pocket to retrieve skulls from a dangerous situation — you’re invulnerable for the duration. Or open rooms with an Omega attack to activate Huntress.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>The aspect’s built-in damage bonus isn’t significant until Rank 3 or so, but this is strong at any rank.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>So, um, that thing I said above about ignoring magick? Turns out Possessed Array is king here. If you see it early, get big magick regeneration sorted immediately. Flood Gain from Poseidon is great for a few seconds of insanity during the buff and you can ignore max magick otherwise.</p>
<p>Neither Colossus Driver nor Boosted Ignition are flashy, but you’ll be shocked at how much damage they add, particularly when the aspect is ranked up.</p>
<p>Special shoutout for Melting Break. Armored baddies are your nightmare here.</p>
<p>Avoid Wide Grin. It slightly delays retrieval of your ammo and you’ll find it annoying against loose crowds. I don’t take the Experimental Hammer with this aspect.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with Zeus on special. With global damage boosts from Arcana and any percentage boost on attack you should be popping Blitz immediately. For attack boons, any percentage boost or Hestia are fine. I like Ares the best: the aspect already has a huge damage boost, so more power from Wounds goes hard and the Ares – Zeus duo rules here, especially if you can <em>also</em> add Light Smite from Apollo. Demeter and Hephaestus are great additions for their various safety boons and duos with Zeus. Do everything you can to boost your attacks and lightning.</li> <li>If you’re dealing with Vow of Wards or Hordes, start with Hera on attack. Look for Hephaestus and Demeter for some defense and hoard Earth boons — conveniently mostly defensive — for a damage boost via Heph’s infusion. Like above, Zeus is hard to top for special, but Heph is also fun.</li> <li>Start with Hephaestus on the higher rarity of special or attack. Take attack in a tie. Depending on your needs, more Heph can either give beefier explosions or stout defense. Heph has good duos here with any of Hestia, Apollo, Aphrodite, Zeus, Poseidon, or Demeter so you can probably cobble together something good. My favorite is getting Hestia and Apollo for their duos and to get Fire boons for Slow Cooker from Hestia.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>This aspect initially plays like <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-skull">Mel Skull</a>: pepper baddies with regular attacks fairly close up, then tap Special to retrieve your skulls when empty. Avoid special-charging straight into a crowd of armored baddies; you can special to the side and still hoover your skulls to you.</p>
<p>About 2,000 points of <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#flat-damage-soup">flat damage</a> from Olympians fills the Sprouted gauge. When the gauge is charged, unleash an extra-long Omega Special and zip through crowds to clear the room or nab any loose skulls. As with the other Skulls, you can’t be damaged during the Omega special. Unlike the other skulls you can change direction while Omega special surfing; do loose circles around tough baddies or bosses to keep smacking them with the waves.</p>
<p>A few seconds of invulnerability from the Sprouted Omega is super valuable both to avoid getting killed and to dish out some pain in otherwise hazardous situations like traps, fire, or AOE moves. Keep an eye on the gauge and don’t get too reckless: it’s easy to get whacked if your Omega special expires at an inopportune moment. I speak from experience.</p>
<p>If you just need to get away from a dangerous situation, Dash → Special covers a lot of ground in a hurry.</p>
<p>You can’t build up your Sprouted gauge while surfing about with the Omega special. Put big percentage boosts instead of Olympian damaage on special for best results.</p>
<p>If a room is almost done but you don’t have a full gauge, you can use it for a little bonus time just to end things, or save some for the next room — your call.</p>
<p>The distribution of the aspect’s freebie levels for boons is skewed towards granting low bonuses or no bonuses at all. If you’re fishing for, say, a high-rarity <em>and</em> high-level Hephaestus start, the <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#fish-for-a-favorable-start">ability to reset the RNG seed for the start of the run</a> will be your friend.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Yes. The aspect’s ability to go invulnerable for a bit might power an early clear for you.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>The Skulls are swimming in solid special hammers to get more mileage out of the Omega. Twisting Crash is the clear standout, but Colossus Driver, Massive Driver, and Sudden Driver are all helpful.</p>
<p>Like with the other skulls, Possessed Array is an insane damage boost if you can get enough magick to trigger it constantly.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with Hephaestus on attack. A few bonus levels and a rarity bump should get you to the fastest 2-second recharge. A blast on a crowd should fill your gauge. From there, look for Hestia (cast, gain) or Aphrodite (special) or Apollo (cast, dash, special) or Poseidon (cast, gain) or Demeter (special, cast, gain) for their blast duos. Zeus’s Static Shock and duo with Heph is another direction to go. Or add Hera on special; she’s probably best-in-slot there.</li> <li>Start with your pick of Hestia, Poseidon, or Zeus on attack. Compared to a Heph start, these are a more stable drip for Sprouted. Any of them are quite happy with a couple of bonus levels to start. Hestia and Poseidon are smoother starts, but Zeus has the highest potential late-game with Double Strike or Kings Ransom (if you can sacrifice all your Hera boons). Get Origination online with your other slots and get any percentage boon on special. For a Hestia start, look for Aphrodite (any core) and Demeter (cast, special) or Ares (any core) who have fun duos with Hestia. Poseidon has good duos with Ares and Zeus.</li> <li>This is a convoluted build that dominates if you get it online. Start with Ares on attack. For reasons I do not completely understand, the added damage from applying Wounds counts as Olympian damage, so this will build your gauge from the start. Add Zeus on cast, dash, high-rarity special, or gain. Next add Apollo’s Light Smite or Zeus’s Divine Vengeance (ideally both) to unlock the Ares – Zeus duo. Once you’ve got all that (told you this was convoluted!) every time you hit something new with an attack you’ll trigger tons of bolts and Light Smite on everyone around you and fill your Sprouted gauge in a flash.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Charge and launch an Omega special, then hold Attack at close-ish range. Keep your distance or drop a cast while channeling the Omega special, because it takes a while. Dash if you’re about to get smacked while holding Attack.</p>
<p>If you’re out of magick, hold Attack and occasionally tap Special to launch it for more damage. You can’t fire another special or charge an Omega special while any special is flying about. In busy rooms it can be out for a <em>long</em> time.</p>
<p>Theoretically this weapon has an Omega attack but you have to sit still forever to fire it. That’s kind of a death sentence. With Vow of Frenzy maxed I’m not sure I’ve ever actually fired this.</p>
<p>Because the Omega special costs a small fortune to launch, building up a Hex can be a fruitful side quest. Unless you’re planning on <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#gain-and-pivot">starting with a magick recovery boon</a>, take Hecuba so you can do more than just one Omega special per room at the beginning.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Your damage output is lower than it could be but this starts reasonably strong, and rank doesn’t change the optimal builds.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>The aspect-exclusive Venerated Relic is tempting; it doubles the length of the Valkyrie buff, a gigantic quality-of-life upgrade if most of your damage is coming from the machine gun attacks. However, it is a trade-off because you can’t fire another Omega special during that longer buff.</p>
<p>I also like Leering Glance to spread attacks around some.</p>
<p>Otherwise, the various special hammers are all decent.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with Poseidon on attack, which does exactly what you want it to do with waves every strike. Get magic regeneration sorted, add Slippery Slope and another status on cast/special for Origination and you should be in good shape. I like Hera (Hitch is very good here) or Ares (good duo with Poseidon) or Aphrodite (with her duo with Poseidon) on special the most. If you can get it, Poseidon’s legendary is amazing for bossing here.</li> <li>Start with Zeus on attack. Compared to Poseidon, this is a <em>much</em> slower start but late-run can get ridiculous with Static Shock and Double Strike. Add Hera, Hestia, or Poseidon on magick recovery (they all have good duos with Zeus too) and get Origination online to amp up all your lightning.</li> <li>Start with Hestia on attack, then add Demeter on cast and look for their duo. During Valkyrie mode, build up a huge Scorch stack to nuke with Freezer Burn. If the duo is elusive, take Pyro Technique from Hestia. Another potential avenue is nabbing the other Fire gods (Apollo, Hephaestus) and hoarding Fire boons for Hestia’s infusion, Slow Cooker, to add lots of power on your rapid-fire attacks. Since Hestia’s in the pool, Controlled Burn from her is decent here.</li> <li>Start with Hera on special. The damage is good and Hitching entire rooms to open fights has obvious benefits. Get magick recovery sorted and add whichever of Zeus, Poseidon, or Hestia on attack and build your attack up from there. Hestia in particular has Controlled Burn for more damage out of all the Omega specials you’ll be doing.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Leaning on attacks or specials or both is viable here. The balance of specials and attacks you favor will depend on the hammers and boons that show up. Special → attack → attack → repeat is a reasonable starting point; you can attack while rockets are out, and the attack cancels the special’s recovery animation.</p>
<p>For special-heavy builds, pin baddies in a cast and keep a slight distance while you pepper them with rockets. Keep moving and tapping special and things will die. Unfortunately, the rocket tracking is sometimes wonky, they’ll get lost on barriers, and you can’t guarantee what or when exactly they’ll hit.</p>
<p>In comparison, the attack hits reliably and immediately. If baddies are getting in your face, punches will manage them better.</p>
<p>With the Auto-Fire setting on, you can do fast combos if you hold attack or special or both in combination; the <a href="https://hades.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Coat">Hades Wiki has more details in the “Auto-Fire Statistics” section</a>. The combos change based on your attack speed and distance and magick reserves, so it feels unstable to me but you might find it helpful.</p>
<p>Similar to <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-flames">Mel Flames</a>, you can sorta throw anything on attack/special. When I advise folks on Reddit or Discord, I’ll say any combination of Hera, Zeus, Poseidon, Ares, or Hestia on attack and special will probably work out. However, the dash-strike adds an interesting wrinkle: it hits 3 times quickly, perfect for Poseidon or Hestia or Static Shock.</p>
<p>I rarely focus on Omega moves here (check out <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-selene-coat">Selene</a> if you’re into those), but they have their uses.</p>
<p>The Omega special can focus a bunch of special rockets on a baddie, the tracking is better, and you can do other things while the rockets are flying about. I typically start waves with an Omega special to activate Huntress and get more rockets in the air, then jump into a regular attack/special rotation.</p>
<p>While charging the Omega attack, you block attacks in front of you. This is one of the rare tools that deletes projectiles. You can block almost everything, including Gramps’ dashes and scythe swings. You’ll learn the unblockable stuff real quick; it’s mostly expanding rings and ground explosions.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Try the Coat’s playstyle out, but the speed boosts aren’t noteworthy until the highest ranks.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Exhaust Riser makes the dash-strike hit six times. If you see this early, run towards Poseidon or Hestia’s attack or Static Shock and use dash-strikes liberally. This is great with Reaper Frame for the added power or Launcher Frame so you’re also constantly launching free rockets.</p>
<p>Shimmering Rockets is great for crowds, and Ripper Rockets is excellent for guardians and bigger baddies.</p>
<p>Siege Frame extends the range of your punches substantially, helpful for crowds and for some breathing room.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your pick of Hera, Poseidon, Hestia, or Zeus and take the highest-rarity special or attack. All are very good, but Poseidon is probably the easiest start and he’s terrific if you get Exhaust Riser. Add whoever shows up between Ares, Zeus, Hestia, Poseidon, or Hera on the other attack/special slot. Some particularly good combinations are Poseidon – Ares, Hera – Hestia, Poseidon – Hestia, or anyone with Zeus, especially with Static Shock.</li> <li>Start with Hestia on attack. Nab some of Hestia’s Scorch boosters while seeking out Demeter on cast or special and their duo. Exhaust Riser makes it trivial to plaster on lots of Scorch. If Freezer Burn isn’t forthcoming, take Pyro Technique to alleviate your too-much-Scorch problems.</li> <li>Start with Zeus on attack. You’d also love Static Shock. Add Aphrodite on cast or dash or gain for Origination, then look for their duo. Once you have it, dash-strike and sprint away to pop Blitz with bonus damage. As an alternative, if you see Hephaestus early, throw him on a core slot and go for Master Conductor. Dash-strike to maximize the chain lightning.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>The aspect-exclusive Hex, Sky Fall, starts good and gets better. Plan on taking the Moon Beam keepsake at some point so you can reach the juicy blue and purple upgrades. Ferocity and Prism are excellent. Cascade is solid — free damage is nice. Otherwise prioritize nodes that give you additional hits or more power.</p>
<p>Alternate Omega attacks and specials. Omega specials are more flexible since you can sweep a crowd or focus on one baddie. Meanwhile, Omega attacks have huge base power and consume more magick. Once you’ve burned enough magick for Sky Fall, light it up for more damage and to boost your next Omegas. My typical rotation is Omega special → activate Sky Fall → dash-strike → Omega attack, squeezing off a cast somewhere in the middle there.</p>
<p>While charging the Omega attack, you block attacks in front of you. This is one of the rare tools in this game that deletes projectiles. You can block almost everything, including Gramps’ dashes and scythe swings. You’ll learn the unblockables real quick; it’s mostly expanding rings, ground explosions, and Hecate’s transformation curse. The range on the Omega attack is a little lacking in my opinion, but you can glide a little bit while charging or dash and you’ll punch immediately afterwards.</p>
<p>You can drop a cast while channeling either Omega move; triggering your Hex while channeling, however, will fire the Omega move.</p>
<p>If you’re out of magick or for small or weakened baddies that aren’t worth Omegas, alternate regular attacks and specials like <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-melinoe-coat">Mel Coat</a>.</p>
<p>For damage Arcana, I like either picking Eternity &amp; Night and cycling through all the Omegas — that corner will also turn on Moon — or Origination for a damage boost for all the space lasers. Either works well in my experience.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Probably not. This aspect revolves entirely around Sky Fall, and it’s much easier to activate at the higher ranks.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Nearly every hammer has merit here. I’m happiest when I see Shimmering Rockets or Ripper Rockets. But those aren’t very flashy and you’re here for the big punches, be honest.</p>
<p>Siege Frame and Reaper Frame increase the coverage of the Omega attack so you whiff less often. World Collider is a touch impractical — it’s a long windup — but 100 more power is no joke if you’re chasing big funny numbers. My record big punch is in the 9000s and I know it’s beatable.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with the Moon Beam to immediately power up Sky Fall. The specific upgrades on offer and early hammers might influence what you do next. Look for any big percentage boost for your attack, magick recovery (Hera, Zeus, Poseidon, Demeter are great), and whatever on your special. Some attack/special combos I like are Hera/Zeus, Ares/Poseidon, Aphrodite/Poseidon, or Apollo/Ares. Anything that makes your Omega moves more expensive is welcome to access Sky Fall quicker, like Controlled Burn from Hestia or Weed Killer from Demeter or Fine Line from Hera. If the Ferocity upgrade is on your path, add Ares to unlock his Godsent upgrade, and maybe also take Athena’s keepsake and fish for Task Force so you can use it all the time. Lance of Ares with Ferocity is disgusting damage, especially with Grievous Blow or other Hex boosters like Whispered Prayer from Artemis.</li> <li>Use Origination and start with Zeus on special. You’re also fishing for Arc flash from him so repeat missiles from the Omega special insta-pop Blitz for bonus damage. Get Origination up between your cast and attack, and power up your hex as you’re able. As with Mel Coat, probably any combination of Hera, Ares, Poseidon, or Hestia will be just fine. Conveniently, each of them have boons that make Omegas more expensive.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>In between waves, sprint around to power up your Omega Boost. Slam into the first unlucky baddie you see, then drop a cast and spam attacks or specials while the Nightspawn buff is active. When Nightspawn expires, repeat the process.</p>
<p>The mix of attacks or specials you’ll favor will depend on boons and hammers. Special → attack → attack → repeat is a reasonable starting point. You can attack while rockets are out, and attacks cancel out of the lingering special animation. In dangerous situations, strafing around and firing zillions of Nightspawn rockets is valid.</p>
<p>Nightspawn doesn’t do anything for Omega moves, so I mostly ignore them here outside of occasionally charging an Omega attack to block something, or using the Omega special to focus a bunch of rockets on something before sprinting in.</p>
<p>Nightspawn also doesn’t do much of anything against solo baddies, so you can save some magick against guardians.</p>
<p>While charging the Omega attack, you block attacks in front of you. This is one of the rare tools that deletes projectiles, so keep it front of mind. You can block almost everything, including Gramps’ dashes and scythe swings. You’ll learn the unblockables real quick; it’s mostly expanding shockwaves, ground explosions, and Hecate’s sheep curse.</p>
<p>Because you’re always opening fights with an Omega move, The Huntress arcana is nice here.</p>
<p>Nitro Boost from Hermes is hugely useful for a little protection as you sprint into things. With the high power on the sprint, Vital Signs from Artemis is very funny for huge damage as you slam into freshly-spawned enemies.</p>
<p>Hera’s gain doesn’t automatically trigger with the Omega sprint, so I’d avoid it here or at least watch your magick carefully.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>Although you’re missing out on some supplemental damage from Nightspawn at the lower ranks, that doesn’t change the gameplay or desirable builds.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Most of the non-Omega-specific ones are good. I particularly like Shimmering Rockets, Siege Frame, Ripper Rockets, Launcher Frame, and Reaper Frame.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your pick of Hera, Poseidon, Hestia, or Zeus and take the highest-rarity special or attack. All are very good, but Hestia is probably the easiest start. Next up, add whoever shows up between Ares, Zeus, Hestia, Poseidon, or Hera on the other attack/special slot. Some particularly good combinations are Ares – Poseidon, Hera – Hestia, Poseidon – Hestia, Hera – Zeus, or Hestia – Zeus.</li> <li>Start with Hephaestus on special (or maybe take a high-rarity attack). Then look for Apollo or Hestia or Poseidon for the other attack/special slot. Seek out more of any of them for blast duos with Heph or more Fire boons for Slow Cooker from Hestia. Another option is adding Zeus for Blitz, Static Shock, and Master Conductor.</li> <li>Start with Hestia’s gain. Rarity doesn’t matter; even a common one will top you up as you punch and rocket things after an Omega boost. You don’t need tons of max magick either. Flesh out your attack/special per any of the above recipes from there. One fun direction is Warm Breeze: add Apollo cast or dash to unlock the duo, and also look for Aphrodite (Wispy Wiles infusion) and Zeus (more Air boons). Between a silly-high dodge chance <em>and</em> the duo healing you for it, you can end up nearly unkillable.</li> </ul>
<p><strong>Winning combo</strong></p>
<p>Launch an Omega special near some baddies, zip into the landing zone, then hold Attack for an Omega attack. Hang out as long as you can to build up the Destruction gauge with the Omega special bounces. Casts (you can drop one mid-charge) and the Omega attack’s shield will help you do this without getting too bullied — hopefully. Let 'er rip. Repeat.</p>
<p>The Omega attack covers an absurd area (even at minimal charge) and hits <em>hard</em>. You should be one-shotting most early baddies with it.</p>
<p>While charging the Omega attack, you block attacks in front of you. This is one of the rare tools that deletes projectiles. You’ll learn the unblockables real quick. Be wary of ground explosions and expanding rings. You can dash while charging to avoid damage at the cost of immediately firing the attack.</p>
<p>Against weaker baddies or things that don’t merit a gigapunch, skip the Omega loop and do special → attacks up close. If you’re not big on Omegas, the basic attack combo is strong enough to build around.</p>
<p>Avoid the dash-strike if you can help it. The animation is slow, it hits just once, and it doesn’t hit terribly hard.</p>
<p>For damage arcana, I like taking Huntress and especially Night here since the primary rotation is alternating Omegas. The occasional and hilarious boss instant death from a Destruction-boosted gigapunch is one of this game’s greatest dopamine hits.</p>
<p>The Omega moves are outlandishly expensive, so Hexes are easy to activate here.</p>
<p><strong>Should you use it at low ranks?</strong></p>
<p>If you don’t use Omegas, ranks do nothing for you.</p>
<p>The highest ranks unlock the very silliest gigapunches, but without the Counter Supreme hammer you’re rarely filling the gauge to 6 anyway.</p>
<p><strong>Hammers of note</strong></p>
<p>Most of the aspect-exclusives are solid. Elephant Rockets saves you some Destruction-building time as you can launch on Omega special up close and immediately absorb it.</p>
<p>Counter Supreme is also aces for building Destruction fast. Pro-tip: you can block the golden orbs that Chronos emits during phase transitions to build up a full Destruction gauge in a blink. He won’t know what hit him.</p>
<p>Siege Frame extends your attack and Omega attack range significantly, handy for not whiffing those slow, expensive Omega attacks. Reaper Frame also makes for bigger punches, but it’s better for regular attacks instead of the Omega, which already has huge base power.</p>
<p>I also like Rapid Frame to speed attacks up generally and World Collider or Chakra Collider for big funny numbers.</p>
<p><strong>Build recipes</strong></p>
<ul> <li>Start with your pick of Apollo, Demeter, Hera, Aphrodite, or Ares on attack. Apollo’s Omega punch can cover entire rooms, and the extra reach is comfy for regular attacks too. Demeter is nice just because frozen baddies are easy to punch. Hera’s solid if you’re struggling with Wards or Hordes. With Ares, the added power from Wounds adds up. Get magic regeneration sorted (you need a ton) and throw Hera, Aphrodite, Apollo, Ares, or Zeus on special. If Demeter’s in the pool you want Weed Killer from her, particularly if you went with Ares on attack. Anything that adds some juice to Omega moves will be welcome, especially Apollo’s legendary if you enjoy gigantic double punches. <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KJpap1AUa-o">Watch me giga-punch all 4 levels of Vow of Rivals on the Surface with Shiva Coat and Exceptional Talent</a>.</li> <li>Skip Night and use Origination and maybe Huntress. Start with your pick of Apollo, Aphrodite, or Zeus on attack. Get Origination up between cast, special, or dash and regular-attack punch stuff until it dies. It works! Apollo’s the easiest start; Zeus is the slower start but can scale the hardest with Double Strike or Hera and King’s Ransom. Ares on special is nice to unlock Grievous Blow.</li> </ul>
<h2><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#a-few-warden-midboss-tips">A few Warden (midboss) tips</a></h2>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#charybdis-rift-of-thessaly">Charybdis (Rift of Thessaly)</a></h3>
<ul> <li>To avoid his phase transition bullet hell, go to one corner of the arena. Bait the bullets towards you, then race along the perimeter to the opposite corner. You’ll outrun most of the bullets and the sub-bullets. Then dash into the stagglers. <a href="https://imgur.com/a/Fx2slmC">Dodging his bullets looks like this</a>.</li> </ul>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#spawn-of-typhon-the-summit">Spawn of Typhon (The Summit)</a></h3>
<ul> <li>Keep getting behind him. He has three moves: a ram, swipe-swipe-stomp, and a leap with an expanding shockwave, all of which miss or have more forgiving hitboxes if you get behind him. <a href="https://imgur.com/a/qRFIydy">Here’s a particularly clean Spawn of Typhon kill with Eos Flames</a>.</li> </ul>
<h2><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#some-guardian-tips">Some Guardian tips</a></h2>
<ul> <li>Dash <em>into</em> her expanding ring, and dash back out when it’s contracting.</li> <li>To avoid getting Sheep’d, dash directly <em>through</em> the projectile a few times. The left and right sides of the arena give you the most room to also dodge Hecate’s hexes. Or let Frinos absorb the ball.</li> </ul>
<ul> <li>Kill Roxie (drums) first. She’s got all the ground area-of-effect attacks and she doesn’t move so she’s easier to wail on. Bait the others away from her if you’re not in a hurry.</li> <li>It’s a hectic fight, but try not to lose track of Jetty (guitar), who has a bad habit of slinking off and then charging in or sending hard-to-spot projectiles your way.</li> <li>There’s 3 of them, so Hitch from Hera is extraordinarily helpful.</li> <li>In the final phase, keep a cast on you and Scylla and the fish swarm can’t get near you.</li> </ul>
<ul> <li>At nearly all times the safest place to be is behind him. Keep dashing straight through him.</li> <li>All the glowing circles don’t hurt you until the last moment, so you don’t have to get out of them immediately.</li> <li>When the screen is absolutely covered in glowing circles, the timing is offset: stand in the later ones, wait for the earliest ones to blow, then dash into where the earliest ones were.</li> </ul>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#chronos-tartarus">Chronos (Tartarus)</a></h3>
<ul> <li>When in doubt, stay a few paces in front of him; if he does the fast scythe boomerang it’ll loop right around you.</li> <li>Dashing straight through him is generally the way to avoid his swings and charges.</li> <li>You can dash during the killing blow of the second-stage insta-kill moves. When you pull this off, it feels amazing.</li> </ul>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#polyphemus-ephyra">Polyphemus (Ephyra)</a></h3>
<ul> <li>In general, beat on him until he moves, then dash through him immediately.</li> <li>Wait for him (or a boulder) to land, then dash <em>into</em> the shockwaves.</li> <li>Unless you have overwhelming damage, kill the barrel guys so they can’t set too much of the stage on fire.</li> </ul>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#eris-rift-of-thessaly">Eris (Rift of Thessaly)</a></h3>
<ul> <li>Stay nearly on top of her and keep dashing through her. Once she starts shooting she turns <em>very</em> slowly.</li> <li>Failing the above, hug the pillars. They absorb bullets and they’re not large so you can often attack through them.</li> <li>Try to not let her blow up the pillars with the sniper shot. Run circles around her while she’s aiming and she’ll whiff.</li> <li>During her final hit-and-run onslaught, she has fixed locations she can land. I like staying to the right of the right-hand pillar (if it’s still standing); you’re covered there for 2 out of the 3 nearby places she’ll land.</li> </ul>
<ul> <li>Each phase has a fixed pattern of small or flashy moves. Once he completes the cycle, he stands still for a bit. That’s when you beat on him. The <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iDfSTMw9qsI">patterns vary by phase, which I break down in this video</a>.</li> <li>Do your best to aim the summoned fire moves towards the edges of the arena so he doesn’t severely restrict your movement.</li> <li>For the memory game, assign numbers to the zones. Top/left is 1, middle is 2, bottom/right is 3. Say the “safe” zones (they don’t flash) out loud, like “1, then 3, then 2.” You can remember 3 or 5 numbers. If you can’t even with all of that, dash straight through the waves; only the leading edge of the flames can hurt you.</li> </ul>
<h3><a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#typhon-the-summit">Typhon (The Summit)</a></h3>
<ul> <li>The chin slam is hugely telegraphed by the horns flashing. Either move down quickly to avoid it completely, or dash right under him to i-frame it.</li> <li>For the 3rd-eye ball, move to one side, dash to avoid the first shot, and sprint to the other side to avoid the splashes. They probably won’t bounce that far. In the final phase, there’s a third splash that you’ll have to save and time a dash to completely avoid.</li> <li>During the egg portions, the arm will pound once or the tail will drop on you twice, so be ready for it. As always, dash into shockwaves once they start expanding.</li> <li>The phases are not equal in terms of health; the final phase after the second eggs he’ll have about 40% of his total health, so that’s a slog.</li> </ul>

<p>Adjust things to taste or swap in something from the next tier up. If you feel comfortable with <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#on-the-vow-of-time">more Time</a> for the middle tiers, take it and drop something irksome.</p>
<p>If you are only looking for a clear at any of these levels, do not take the unreasonably hard pacts like Void, Rivals 4, or Forfeit. Think very, very hard about lots of Hordes or Grit. Also, think hard about Debt, especially on the surface where you can get ludicrously strong by throwing money at Hermes.</p>
<p>If you are a fellow try-hard and you’d like to push beyond 32 Fear, use Vow of Frenzy at its maximum as soon as possible and just get used to faster enemies. It’s an adjustment, but you can learn. It’s also a whopping 6 Fear, which lets you avoid a lot of annoyances from other Vows. Alternatively, practice for a bit at Frenzy 2, then turn it down and the game will feel easy all of a sudden, like taking off a weighted vest.</p>
<p>Many folks think a time limit on each region sounds impossible, but I promise you, it’s not.</p>
<p>With Vow of Time maxed out, you have to play somewhat aggressively, pause when choosing doors, and take most free room offered, but five minutes per region is very possible on either route. Particularly if you don’t take the Vows that make everything take longer: Hordes, Wards, and Grit.</p>
<p>If you’re waffling, <em>trust me</em>, it’s easier to go a little faster and maybe get a little lucky than it is to handle 60% more enemies with way more health that take more hits to kill.</p>
<p>After you complete the main story, you can get three “Fates’ Whim” keepsakes that only work if you don’t <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#take-the-rolls">take any reroll Arcana</a> or <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#use-the-god-keepsakes-early">use God keepsakes</a>.</p>
<p><strong>If you’re looking for consistency, these aren’t it</strong>. On the flip side, the keepsakes are powerful and making random stuff work is fun.</p>
<p>First up, change your <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#arcana">Arcana</a>:</p>
<ul> <li>Unseen is helpful because you can’t roll for magick recovery boons; if you don’t need magick or rely on Huntress, drop it.</li> <li>Boatman is harder to justify, but boons from early shops to lock gods in or <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#understand-the-core-boon-slots">fill some core slots</a> is a tiny bit of build control. I can’t decide if I want that money or some combination of Eternity or Night orArtificer or Huntress more.</li> <li>I am not sold on Excellence. Going from Common to Rare when you’re already at level 4 <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#ignore-boon-rarity-mostly">doesn’t move the needle much</a>, and it’s harder to flesh out the requirements for legendary boons or have enough opportunities to get them with no rolls.</li> <li>Death or Strength is <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#strength-or-death">still your choice</a>, but you have no ability to reroll Athena for Renewed Faith if you bank on that with Defiances.</li> <li>If you like Judgment, don’t worry: it won’t activate the reroll Arcana on you.</li> </ul>
<p>For <strong>keepsake order</strong>:</p>
<ul> <li>Start with the Jeweled Pom. Free levels on all your boons for potentially the entire run is insane, especially with <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#flat-damage-soup">flat damage</a> builds. If you get Gigaros Dash to start, that’s a huge early boost for some aspects. Or sometimes you get Howling Soul and your casts miss all the dang time.</li> <li>Unless you need something else urgently, take the Time Piece in the second region to fill your coffers (minor rewards are good targets) for the rest of the run. You can do shenanigans like take Chaos and purge the boon for a totally free room with no curse (and Chaos is <em>exceedingly</em> dangerous without rolls).</li> <li>For later biomes I’d do the Moon Beam for a hex or whichever survival or damage keepsake you need.</li> </ul>

<p>The requirements for unlocking the hidden aspects changed with the 1.0 release, so there’s tons of correct-at-the-time-but-now-wrong information out there.</p>
<p>You need:</p>
<ul> <li>All six weapons unlocked</li> <li>All the other aspects for the weapon in question unlocked</li> <li>At least one <strong>non-Melinoë</strong> aspect for the weapon in question ranked up fully</li> </ul>
<p>The <strong>non-Melinoë</strong> bit is what changed. A Rank-5 Mel aspect won’t do it anymore.</p>
<p>Once you’ve done that, keep chatting with the NPC for the second aspect — Circe, Artemis, Moros, Charon, Medea, Selene — and they’ll give you the waking phrase. You don’t need to be using the weapon.</p>
<p>If you don’t have <a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-circe-staff">Circe Staff</a> available, you need to unlock Familiars to use that one.</p>
<p>If you just unlocked the Coat and don’t see aspects for it (but you do for other weapons), go for a run or Chaos Trial and they’ll be there next night.</p>
<p>Once you’ve applied Scorch to a baddie, it (in the code, not visually) ticks down 6 times per second removing 16.67 Scorch per tick. So it’ll remove 100 Scorch per second. For simplicity from here on out, I’m going to round and say 17 per tick.</p>
<p>With zero global damage boosts, the tick removing 17 Scorch will apply 17 damage to the baddie.</p>
<p>So: stick 100 Scorch on a baddie, and one second later it will have zero Scorch and have taken 100 damage.</p>
<p>Only the following increase the damage rate of Scorch:</p>
<ul> <li>Global damage boosts increase the damage applied per tick. They do not change how much Scorch is removed per tick. For example, if you have a +100% global damage boost active, each tick will remove 17 Scorch but do 34 damage.</li> <li>The Hestia boon Pyro Technique increases the Scorch removed per tick, and accordingly the damage applied per tick. For example if you have an epic Pyro Technique (+100% Scorch rate), each tick will remove 34 Scorch and deal 34 damage. If in addition to that you have +100% global damage, it will remove 34 Scorch and deal 68 damage per tick.</li> </ul>
<p>If your primary damage is Scorch, you want Pyro Technique, a rare <em>multiplicative</em> damage boost. With global damage increases you can get to 300 – 400 DPS Scorch fairly easily, and I’ve seen builds with upwards of 800 DPS with a high-level Pyro Technique.</p>
<p>If you’re using Scorch principally for keeping Origination online for other things or you’re building huge stacks for Freezer Burn, be wary of Pyro Technique as it removes the Scorch status faster. The Aphrodite – Hestia duo can alleviate that concern.</p>
<p>The following do <em>not</em> increase the damage rate of Scorch:</p>
<ul> <li>Applying lots of Scorch does not change the tick rate or the damage per tick.</li> <li>Multiple sources of Scorch (for example, you have both Hestia’s cast and attack) just apply more Scorch stacks to eventually tick down at the same rate.</li> <li>Air Quality from Zeus does not increase the damage per tick.</li> <li>Scorch damage ticks can’t crit.</li> <li>Scorch damage is never increased by Grievous Blow or Mutual Destruction from Ares.</li> </ul>
<p>There are hard-coded limitations on Poseidon waves for specific weapon moves. I won’t dive into every detail, but as a practical matter you’re going to see fewer waves than you’d like on these moves:</p>
<ul> <li>Morrigan Omega specials will sometimes fail to make a wave, particularly when you shotgun things with an Omega special.</li> <li>The Flames specials can fail to trigger waves each strike depending on how you’re moving, how many baddies you’re hitting in rapid sequence, or if you have hammers that result in faster strikes like Whirling Helix or Hidden Helix.</li> <li>Coat specials and Omega specials can hit close enough together to not get a wave for each hit</li> </ul>
<p>Fast multi-hit Omega moves with Arc Flash (a hit from Omega moves instantly triggers Blitz) won’t apply and pop Blitz 4 or 5 times. A baddie can only have 1 Blitz effect on them at a time. From there, the limitation is Blitz’s animation; Blitz has to appear and then disappear completely before you can apply it again.</p>
<p>With Possessed Array, when you collect a skull you lose 25 magick and trigger an Omega attack explosion.</p>
<p>Anything that adds an effect to Omega attacks or triggers in response to Omega attacks works. For example, Weed Killer from Demeter increases the damage and Fine Line from Hera will trigger. If that thing increases the magick cost of Omega attacks (e.g. Weed Killer, Fine Line, Exceptional Talent), you <em>do not</em> lose more magick. It’s always 25 per pop.</p>
<p>The magick lost to Possessed Array does not charge a hex.</p>
<p>Most things that trigger specifically when magick is spent <em>do not</em> work. Aphrodite’s Heartbreaker or Athena’s Righteous Pike are examples.</p>
<p>However, magick recovery that’s tied to using magick mostly works:</p>
<ul> <li>Born Gain from Hera will trigger normally</li> <li>The Mist Veil item from Charon wells will activate.</li> <li>Flood Gain from Poseidon sorta works. Possessed Array will activate the buff. During the buff, you can activate Possessed Array even when you’re out of magick. However, it won’t actually recover that spent magick. In practice, every few seconds you can go nuts with Possessed Array while the recovery buff is active.</li> </ul>
<p>The game’s list in the prophecy doesn’t clarify which god goes with which Hex. Here’s the pairs:</p>
<table> <tr><th>God</th><th>Hex</th><th>What’s it do?</th></tr> <tbody> <tr><td>Ares</td><td>Sky Fall (<a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-selene-coat">Selene Coat</a> exclusive)</td><td>Applies Wounds status — including 50 bonus power — on every space laser</td></tr> <tr><td>Apollo</td><td>Lunar Ray</td><td>Dramatically stronger beam</td></tr> <tr><td>Aphrodite</td><td>Dark Side</td><td>Constantly generates Heartthrobs while transformed</td></tr> <tr><td>Demeter</td><td>Phase Shift</td><td>Constant damage on all baddies during the hex</td></tr> <tr><td>Hephaestus</td><td>Wolf Howl</td><td>Another blast after you land</td></tr> <tr><td>Hera</td><td>Night Bloom</td><td>Summoned minion hits harder and faster, sometimes hilariously so</td></tr> <tr><td>Hestia</td><td>Total Eclipse</td><td>Applies 1000 Scorch on everything in the room</td></tr> <tr><td>Poseidon</td><td>Moon Water</td><td>A few seconds of constant health regeneration</td></tr> <tr><td>Zeus</td><td>Twilight Curse</td><td>The orb also drops lightning bolts around it</td></tr> </tbody> </table>
<p>When you add the appropriate god to your pool after picking your hex, the Godsent upgrades will appear, so don’t fret if you nabbed a hex early.</p>
<p>These work and seem intended, as their descriptions do not change with Vow of Scars:</p>
<ul> <li>All Defiance effects (extra lives) and the Engraved Pin keepsake (Moros) give back health as described</li> <li>Ghost Onion (Keepsake from Dora)</li> <li>Self Healing (Apollo infusion). Interestingly, this gives you back health even if the damage taken was to armor.</li> <li>Revival (Chaos blessing)</li> <li>Vampiric Cataclysm (Staff hammer)</li> <li>Berserk (<a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-nergal-axe">Nergal</a> status) &amp; Iron Core (<a href="https://www.leereamsnyder.com/hades-2-build-guide#aspect-of-nergal-axe">Nergal</a>-exclusive hammer)</li> <li>The White Antler keepsake expiring restores your remaining health</li> <li>Most things that increase your max health also give you that much additional health immediately, even a temporary increase like Fight Fight Fight from Echo</li> </ul>
<p>These ones work, but they <em>seem</em> unintended:</p>
<ul> <li>Breath of Eros (Well item)</li> <li>Bounce Back (Dionysus)</li> <li>Old Herbal Remedy (Circe); the one-time heal always gives you 50% HP, the boost to future healing is impacted by Vow of Scars</li> </ul>
<p>Unlike in <em>Hades</em>, the benefits of many keepsakes don’t get removed if you change them out:</p>
<ul> <li>Silver Wheel (the max magick bump is permanent)</li> <li>Discordant Bell (the “blessing” remains and continues to accrue when you take it off)</li> <li>Gold Purse (you keep the money if you haven’t spent it yet somehow)</li> <li>Moon Beam (your next Path of Stars will have extra upgrades if you haven’t found one yet)</li> <li>Fig Leaf (lasts three regions at rank 3, so ideally equip it before the second region)</li> <li>Crystal Figurine (you keep the Arcana you unlocked)</li> <li>Silken Sash (you keep any armor you’ve accrued)</li> <li>Experimental Hammer (you keep the upgrade until it expires)</li> <li>Transcendent Embryo (you keep the current Blessing and it stops changing)</li> <li>The three post-credits Fates’ Whim keepsakes maintain their blessing after you remove the keepsake so long as you don’t put on a God keepsake.</li> </ul>
