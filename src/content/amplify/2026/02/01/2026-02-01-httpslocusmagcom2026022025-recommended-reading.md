<h3>Welcome to the <i>Locus</i> Recommended Reading List…</h3>
<p>We saw some fabulous books come out last year and are so pleased to let you know about them! Our recommendations are compiled annually by the <em>Locus</em> reviewers, editors, and columnists; outside reviewers; and other professionals and well-known critics of genre fiction and non-fiction. This year we looked at over 1,000 titles between short fiction and long fiction.</p>
<p>Note: we <em>know</em> there will be books you loved that didn’t make it; any one of our voting group would have a different exact list, of course. The list we share is our combined sum of opinions, assessed with a great affection and care for the field. We did not see everything that came out last year (though we tried!) and there will always be books that didn’t make it. No one in the group is allowed to vote for any titles they worked on or acquired.</p>
<p>This year we had recommendations from Liza Groen Trombi, reviews editor Jonathan Strahan; <em>Locus</em> reviewers Liz Bourke, Jake Casella Brookins, Alex Brown, Paul Di Filippo, Paula Guran, Niall Harrison, Rich Horton, Paul Kincaid, Russell Letson, Archita Mittra, Ian Mond, Colleen Mondor, Abigail Nussbaum, Alexandra Pierce, Wole Talabi, Gary K. Wolfe, and Alvaro Zinos-Amaro; staffers Bob Blough, Tim Melody Pratt, and Eleanor Trombla; and outside critics James Bradley, Cheryl Morgan, and Graham Sleight. Input for the non-fiction section also came from Eugen Bacon and Farah Mendlesohn. The art book section had advice from Arnie Fenner, Karen Haber, and <em>Locus</em> senior editor Francesca Myman. Short fiction recommendations added in anthologists and reviewers John Joseph Adams, Sean Dowie, Maria Haskins, Allan Kaster, Charles Payseur, Nisi Shawl, Bogi Takács, and A.C. Wise.</p>
<p>This year we are adding a new category for Translated Novels! This decision was spurred by a conversation that has been ongoing for a while about translated novels: how to uplift more international voices and recognize the extra work that goes into translation. For those recommendations we started with a shortlist of 68 titles, and with input and assistance from Gautam Bhatia, Rachel Cordasco, Jukka Halme, Cristina Jurado, Roseanna Pendlebury, Carlos Arturo Serrano, Alex Schvartsman, and Jared Shurin, ended up with 18 recommended titles.</p>
