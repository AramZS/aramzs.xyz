<h2 data-toc-id="3448a843-bb09-4136-90eb-256bc99307c1">The very real fight behind the pizza posts</h2>
<p>The tomato pie-based banter was sweet ‚Äî but the subtext was spicier.</p>
<p>AI labs are locked in a high-stakes dash for elite engineers, with <a data-analytics-post-depth="80" data-mrf-link="https://www.businessinsider.com/top-ai-startup-companies-salaries-pay-data-openai-anthropic-perplexity-2025-7" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/top-ai-startup-companies-salaries-pay-data-openai-anthropic-perplexity-2025-7">high-end compensation packages</a> stretching into the nine-figure territory.</p>
<p>Companies including Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, OpenAI, and Musk's xAI are competing for a relatively small pool of researchers capable of building the next generation of models and infrastructure.</p>
<p>Aside from money, two key perks have emerged in the AI talent wars, according to professional AI poacher Mark Zuckerberg: <a data-analytics-post-depth="100" data-mrf-link="https://www.businessinsider.com/mark-zuckerberg-ai-researchers-want-gpus-compute-few-direct-reports-2025-7" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/mark-zuckerberg-ai-researchers-want-gpus-compute-few-direct-reports-2025-7">access to GPUs and fewer direct reports.</a></p>
<p>"People say, 'I want the fewest number of people reporting to me and the most GPUs,'" Zuckerberg said in 2025 TITV interview.</p>
<p>At the same time, the broader <a data-analytics-post-depth="100" data-mrf-link="https://www.businessinsider.com/tech-industry-amazon-microsoft-meta-google-companies-intensity-hardcore-2025-3" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/tech-industry-amazon-microsoft-meta-google-companies-intensity-hardcore-2025-3">tech industry has pulled back</a> on many of the pre-pandemic perks amid cost-cutting. Remote work has narrowed, <a data-analytics-post-depth="100" data-mrf-link="https://www.businessinsider.com/companies-hardcore-era-employee-changes-att-memo-2025-8" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/companies-hardcore-era-employee-changes-att-memo-2025-8">layoffs have gathered steam</a>, and perks like pet care stipends and expansive wellness benefits are becoming less common for new hires.</p>
<p>But there's one perk that has remained: <a data-analytics-post-depth="100" data-mrf-link="https://www.businessinsider.com/companies-splurging-on-free-lunch-perks-catering-microsoft-meta-google-2026-2" data-track-click='{"element_name":"body_link","event":"tout_click","index":"bi_value_unassigned","product_field":"bi_value_unassigned"}' href="https://www.businessinsider.com/companies-splurging-on-free-lunch-perks-catering-microsoft-meta-google-2026-2">the fancy lunch spread</a>.</p>
<p>Might as well throw in wood-fired pizza, too.</p>
