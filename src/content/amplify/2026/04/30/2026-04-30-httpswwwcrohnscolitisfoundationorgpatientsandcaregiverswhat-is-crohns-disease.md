<p>Crohn’s disease is an inflammatory bowel disease that causes chronic inflammation of the <a href="https://www.crohnscolitisfoundation.org/gastrointestinal-gi-tract">gastrointestinal tract</a>. ​If you have been diagnosed with Crohn’s disease or are looking for more information about the condition, we are here to help.</p>
<p>Living with a chronic illness can be stressful and overwhelming. We can provide you with information and resources to help you manage the <a href="https://www.crohnscolitisfoundation.org/patientsandcaregivers/what-is-crohns-disease/symptoms">physical and emotional symptoms of Crohn’s disease</a>.</p>
<p>To better understand what Crohn’s disease is, it’s important to know how it affects the body. The condition can cause inflammation anywhere along the digestive tract, often leading to Crohn’s symptoms such as abdominal pain, fatigue, diarrhea, and unintended weight loss. In some cases, a Crohn’s flare-up may include joint pain or skin irritation — symptoms that extend beyond the gut.</p>
<p>Crohn’s disease treatment options include medication, nutrition therapy, and, sometimes, surgery, which can help reduce symptoms and promote remission. Comparing your experience with other IBD symptoms can also help clarify diagnosis and treatment decisions.</p>
