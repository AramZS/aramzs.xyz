<p>“It seems like they were trying to proactively fall in line as to not then be forced to change,” one of the people said.</p>
<p>The museum pulled from its website a page called “Teaching Materials on Nazism and Jim Crow” at some point after Aug. 29, 2025, the last time the page was captured on the Internet Archive. That page provided lesson plans and resources about the connections between American <i>de jure </i>racism and the Nazi regime, including links to sites about “African American Soldiers during World War II” and “Afro-Germans during the Holocaust,” among other topics.</p>
<p>It also linked to a <a data-tracking="mpos=center&amp;mid=ar_body&amp;lindex=62&amp;lcol=" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=199ZOkkp-e8&amp;list=PLWQC3P4psZP4u2aWWiKpY0MkxLsc_TvPO&amp;index=5&amp;t=2s"><u>2018 video on the museum’s YouTube channel</u></a> featuring a conversation between a Holocaust survivor and a woman whose father was lynched in Alabama. That video is now unlisted, meaning it does not show up on the USHMM’s YouTube page but is still accessible via direct URL.</p>
<p>Leaders at the museum also renamed a one-day civic education workshop designed for college students from “Fragility of Democracy and the Rise of the Nazis” to “Before the Holocaust: German Society and the Nazi Rise to Power.” In an email, obtained by POLITICO, between a senior staff member at the museum’s Levine Institute for Holocaust Education and a staffer planning the workshop, the senior staff member said the change was necessary due to “concerns regarding how the term <i>fragility</i> may be perceived or interpreted in the current climate.”</p>
<p>Since taking office, Trump has tightened his grip on the USHMM, an independent museum that relies on both private donations and federal appropriations and is not affiliated with the Smithsonian. In an unprecedented move last year, the president <a data-tracking="mpos=center&amp;mid=ar_body&amp;lindex=63&amp;lcol=" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2025/04/29/us/politics/trump-doug-emhoff-holocaust-museum-biden.html"><u>purged from its board</u></a> several of President Joe Biden’s appointees before the end of their terms. And in the months since, he has installed his own loyalists on the board — most notably replacing Stuart Eizenstat, who helped found the museum, with GOP megalobbyist Jeffrey Miller <a data-tracking="mpos=center&amp;mid=ar_body&amp;lindex=64&amp;lcol=" href="https://www.ushmm.org/information/press/museum-welcomes-president-trumps-appointment-of-jeffrey-miller-as-new"><u>as chair last month</u></a>.</p>
