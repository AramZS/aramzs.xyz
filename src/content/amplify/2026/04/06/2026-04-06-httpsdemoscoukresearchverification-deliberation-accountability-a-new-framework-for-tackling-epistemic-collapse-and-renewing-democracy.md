<p>In this <a href="https://demos.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/VDA_Epistemic-Security_paper_October.pdf">guest report</a> journalist and founder of Bellingcat, <b>Eliot Higgins</b>, and the academic <b>Dr Natalie Martin</b>, set out a new framework to help us understand how epistemic security defines the health of a democracy, and that without it, democracies hollow out and ultimately collapse.</p>
<p>The authors introduce the <b>VDA Framework:</b> a way of diagnosing when democracy is functioning, when it is hollowing, and when it has descended into simulation.</p>
<p>The framework rests on a simple proposition:<b> democracy only works when verification, deliberation, and accountability are obvious to the people, and trusted by them.</b></p>
