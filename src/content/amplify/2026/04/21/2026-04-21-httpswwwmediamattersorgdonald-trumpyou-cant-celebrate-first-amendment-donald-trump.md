<figure><img alt="Trump with newspapers" src="https://cloudfront.mediamatters.org/styles/crop_1620x600/s3/static/D8Audio/2020/06/02/6.2.20_free_press.png?itok=YQzI7h2A"/><figcaption>Trump with newspapers</figcaption></figure>
<p>The White House Correspondents Association has decided that now is the time to link arms with President Donald Trump. After Trump <a href="https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116162119298005428">claimed</a> Monday night on Truth Social that the group had “asked me, very nicely, to be the Honoree” at the annual White House Correspondent’s Dinner, its president, CBS News White House correspondent Weijia Jiang, <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/phillewis.bsky.social/post/3mg4i34f7os2o">confirmed</a> in a press release that he had accepted its invitation.*</p>
<p>The White House Correspondent’s Dinner — an annual event in which D.C.’s top journalists rub elbows with the people they cover and an odd array of celebrities — is, at its best, an unseemly affair excused by the conceit, as Jiang put it in her press release, that it is “a dinner that celebrates the First Amendment.” Under past administrations, there was a benefit to have the president attend the event and <a href="https://rollcall.com/factbase/whca-dinner/transcript/whca-dinner-bush_43-2004-05-01">give a speech</a> in which he would inevitably <a href="https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/the-press-office/2016/05/01/remarks-president-white-house-correspondents-dinner">pay tribute</a> to the freedom of the press.</p>
<p>But as a matter of principle, you can either have a dinner that celebrates the First Amendment or you can have a dinner that features Donald Trump. You can’t have both because the president holds the freedom of the press in contempt and is using the administration to hammer reporters into compliance with his whims — and everyone knows it.</p>
<p><strong>Trump’s White House violated the First Amendment by punishing the AP.</strong> The president kicked off his second term by <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/donald-trump/freedom-press-about-more-access">barring</a> Associated Press reporters from the White House press pool and other events because the AP continued referring to the “Gulf of Mexico” instead of adopting Trump’s new “Gulf of America” moniker. When a Trump-appointed judge ordered the AP’s access restored on the grounds that this violated the First Amendment, the White House sidestepped the ruling by eliminating the press pool’s dedicated slot for wire service reporters.</p>
<p><strong>The Pentagon replaced its press corps with Trumpist propagandists.</strong> Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth, a former Fox News propagandist, has overseen the <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/pete-hegseth/pete-hegseth-replacing-pentagon-press-corps-maga-propagandists">replacement of credible reporters with MAGA shills</a>. After handing the workspaces of national news outlets to right-wing publications and barring unescorted reporters from much of the building, in September the department rolled out stringent new reporting guidelines that nearly every member of the established press corps refused to sign on the grounds that it would make them “stenographers parroting press releases.” They were subsequently replaced by far-right outlets and influencers willing to accept those terms.</p>
<p><strong>At Trump’s behest, the Justice Department targets journalists.</strong> Trump <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2025/03/14/media/trump-media-speech/index.html">ranted</a> about the “fake news” during a March 2025 speech at DOJ headquarters, instructing its employees to “watch for” their “totally illegal” behavior. Attorney General Pam Bondi subsequently <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/cbs/trump-administrations-war-press-heating#paragraph--narrative-item--3471172">rescinded</a> the previous administration’s restrictions protecting journalists from prosecutorial scrutiny. Several journalists, including former CNN host Don Lemon, were <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jan/30/don-lemon-minnesota-protest-charges">arrested</a> in January and charged with federal crimes in connection with their filming of a protest inside a Minnesota church. The same month, the FBI <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2026/02/24/washington-post-reporter-search/">conducted</a> an unprecedented search of Washington Post reporter Hannah Natanson’s home, seizing her devices as part of a leak investigation.</p>
<p>Trump has also <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/donald-trump/donald-trump-greatest-threat-our-free-speech-rights">suggested</a> that Bondi could prosecute critical reporters for “hate speech” on the grounds that “you treat me so unfairly, it’s hate,” and <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/cbs/trump-administrations-war-press-heating#paragraph--narrative-item--3471174">said</a> media pollsters whose surveys show he is unpopular “should be investigated for ELECTION FRAUD.”</p>
<p><strong>The FCC crackdown on free speech.</strong> Trump has <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/cbs/trump-calls-fcc-retribution-against-fake-news-abc-and-nbc-while-touting-great-man-who-just">frequently called</a> for the Federal Communications Commission to rescind the broadcast licenses of ABC, CBS, and NBC in retaliation for coverage he finds unfavorable. While taking those networks off the air is <a href="https://www.cbsnews.com/news/trump-broadcast-licenses-terminated-100-negative/">not actually within the FCC’s power</a>, its Trumpy chairman, Brendan Carr, is leveraging its authority to cudgel them into line, leading to the censoring of <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/donald-trump/jimmy-kimmel-and-maga-strong-arming-american-media">Jimmy Kimmel</a> and <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/cnn/paramount-mangling-cbs-and-cnn-may-be-next">Stephen Colbert</a>.</p>
<p><strong>An authoritarian plan for America’s media.</strong> Trump <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/cnn/paramount-set-take-over-wbd-trump-just-steered-major-news-outlet-hands-crony">emulates the authoritarian policies</a> of Hungary’s Viktor Orbán, who dismantled his country’s independent news media by steering critical outlets into the hands of his cronies. CBS News, the social media platform TikTok, and Paramount’s movie studios have already come under the thumb of the pro-Trump billionaires Larry and David Ellison — and CNN, along with HBO and Warner’s movie business, could soon follow after Trump’s threats of regulatory retribution influenced their sale.</p>
<p><strong>Trump’s endless lawsuits over journalism he doesn’t like. </strong><a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/cbs/year-media-oligarchs-bowed-trump#paragraph--narrative-item--3475570">ABC News</a> and <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/cbs/year-media-oligarchs-bowed-trump#paragraph--narrative-item--3475564">CBS News</a> faced lawsuits from Trump they could have won over coverage he didn’t like, but after Trump’s reelection, their parent companies capitulated and settled them. Trump is currently suing <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/17/business/media/trump-lawsuit-new-york-times.html">The New York Times</a> and several of its reporters for $15 billion over its coverage of his businesses; <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/fox-news/trumps-latest-attempt-defuse-epstein-revolt-point-supporters-common-enemies">The Wall Street Journal</a>, two of its reporters, several related entities, and its owner, Rupert Murdoch, for $10 billion over coverage of Trump’s relationship with notorious sex offender Jeffrey Epstein; and The Des Moines Register and its pollster, J. Ann Selzer, for <a href="https://www.kcrg.com/2026/01/31/judge-hears-arguments-trump-lawsuit-against-des-moines-register-j-ann-selzer/">publishing poll results</a> that showed Trump trailing in Iowa in October 2024.</p>
<p>Trump is <a href="https://news.bloomberglaw.com/litigation/trump-heads-to-discovery-phase-in-pulitzer-defamation-suit">also currently suing</a> the Pulitzer Prize Board for defamation over its <a href="https://www.pulitzer.org/winners/staffs-new-york-times-and-washington-post">2018 award</a> to the Times and the Post for their reporting on Russian interference in the 2016 election.</p>
<p>The WHCA’s Jiang should probably consult with the group’s lawyers before announcing the “awards honoring excellent journalism” she said would be given at the event — if the “wrong” honoree wins, the president attending the dinner to celebrate the First Amendment may respond with a lawsuit.</p>
<p>Trump is <a href="https://www.mediamatters.org/cbs/trump-administrations-war-press-heating#paragraph--narrative-item--3471178">well aware</a> that the media establishment is capitulating to his will.</p>
<p>“You know at some point, they give up,” he said of media owners in an interview last year with The Atlantic. “At some point they say, No más, no más.”</p>
<p>Now it’s the WHCA’s turn to surrender.</p>
<p>*Notably, Jiang did not address Trump’s assertion that he would be the dinner’s “Honoree.” That's almost certainly a lie designed to humiliate the press — Trump loves grinding them into the dirt, which is what we should expect from the forthcoming event.</p>
