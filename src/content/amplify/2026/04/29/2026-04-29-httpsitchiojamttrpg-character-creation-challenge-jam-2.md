<p><strong>The Challenge</strong></p>
<p>create a ttrpg character every day in may—that's 31 characters! use any* system(s) you want. make all your characters in one system, or showcase 31 different systems, or do anything in between. they don't even have to be systems you've actually played. make a d&amp;d character if you want and make them at level 1, or level 20. there's a <em>lot </em>of flexibility in how you want to engage in this challenge, but there's some more details below!</p>
<p>check out <a href="https://itch.io/jam/ttrpg-character-creation-challenge-jam">the first one from last march</a>!</p>
<p><strong>Guidelines</strong></p>
<ul><li>absolutely no generative ai allowed for anything at all.<ul><li>*did you see that asterisk up there? the exception to "any system" is don't use any systems that use generative ai </li><li>check out the <a href="https://itch.io/jam/ttrpg-character-creation-challenge-jam-2/topic/6182060/random-generators-list">random generators thread</a> in the jam forum for prompt generators that aren't ai instead! </li></ul> </li><li>you don't actually need to complete them literally one character a day—make a few in one day, take a break for the week, it's all good! </li><li>complete these characters to whatever extent feels right for you. that might mean only coming up with their stats, but it could also mean giving them a backstory and some art, too! in a system with levels or other advancement, you might make a fully advanced character build or just their out-of-the-box beginning stats. not all characters even need to be equally complete.<ul><li>if you <em>do </em>give your character art, make sure you have full permission to use it—that means no googling "sexy elf wizard" and grabbing the pinterest result that pops up and calling it a day. </li><li>check out the <a href="https://itch.io/c/5292825/character-art">character art collection</a> i've set up if you want assets, and suggest more for the collection in the <a href="https://itch.io/jam/ttrpg-character-creation-challenge-jam-2/topic/6182054/character-art-suggestions">jam forum thread</a>! </li></ul> </li><li>you can start plotting and preparing whenever, but please don't make any characters until may begins! </li></ul>
<p><strong>Submissions</strong></p>
<p>formats in which you can submit your characters:</p>
<ul><li>one big pdf you create at the end of the challenge </li><li>one big google doc or word document you create at the end of the challenge </li><li>a bunch of individual pdfs or word documents or filled out character sheets submitted every time you finish a character </li><li>a txt document you wrote in notepad </li><li>a bunch of txt documents you wrote in notepad </li><li>the three character sheets you actually finished before forgetting about the jam </li><li>have you realized yet that i don't especially care how the submissions are done </li></ul>
<p>consider making an itch collection or an rpggeek list of the systems you used!</p>
<p>submissions close on 6/7 [juggles hands slightly] to give everyone time to compile all their work together if they'd like to, but you're asked not to work on characters after may 31st!</p>
