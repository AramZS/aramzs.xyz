<p>October 21, 2022 – <a href="https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/news/multitaxo/topic/women-and-health/">Women</a> in the U.S. who are pregnant or who have recently given birth are more likely to be murdered than to die from obstetric causes—and these homicides are linked to a deadly mix of intimate partner <a href="https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/news/multitaxo/topic/violence/">violence</a> and <a href="https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/news/multitaxo/topic/guns/">firearms</a>, according to researchers from Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health.</p>
<p>Homicide deaths among pregnant women are more prevalent than deaths from hypertensive disorders, hemorrhage, or sepsis, wrote <a href="https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/profile/rebecca-b-lawn/">Rebecca Lawn</a>, postdoctoral research fellow, and <a href="https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/profile/karestan-koenen/">Karestan Koenen</a>, professor of psychiatric epidemiology, in an October 19 <a href="https://www.bmj.com/content/379/bmj.o2499">editorial</a> in the journal BMJ.</p>
<p>The U.S. has a higher prevalence of intimate partner violence than comparable countries, such violence is often fatal, and it frequently involves guns, Lawn and Koenen noted. They cited one <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36075083/">study</a> that found that, from 2009–2019, 68% of pregnancy-related homicides involved firearms. That study also found that Black women face substantially higher risk of being killed than white or Hispanic women.</p>
<p>Laws restricting women’s access to <a href="https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/news/multitaxo/topic/reproductive-health/">reproductive care</a> and abortion can place women at further risk, since control over a woman’s reproductive choices often plays a role in intimate partner violence, Lawn said in an October 20 U.S. News &amp; World Report article. Lenient firearms legislation can also increase the risk.</p>
<p>Koenen and Lawn called gun violence “a health emergency for pregnant women.” But they added that pregnancy-related homicides are preventable. When pregnant women have checkups, healthcare providers can identify those at risk of violence and try to help them, Lawn told CNN.</p>
