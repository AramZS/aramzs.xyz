<p>When sexual assault allegations emerged this month against Eric Swalwell, the Bay Area congressman and Democratic front-runner for governor, the response was a familiar mix: outrage for the victims, cries of a smear campaign from the accused, and shock from constituents who couldn’t believe the extent of his alleged depravity.</p>
<p>Swalwell <u><a data-post-id="d7617740-db39-4582-a2d0-1cbec4fa2458" href="https://sfstandard.com/2026/04/11/swalwell-denies-sexual-assault-allegations-da-probe/">denies the allegations</a></u> — but the more revealing question might not be whether they are true, but what the repercussions will be regardless.</p>
<p>Donald Trump was found liable for sexual abuse by a Manhattan jury and is still president. Brett Kavanaugh was confirmed as a Supreme Court justice for life despite allegations of sexual assault. Both denied the allegations and went on with their careers.</p>
<p>But that’s Washington. When prominent Bay Area men face allegations of sexual misconduct, do they actually lose anything?</p>
<p>We decided to find out. The Standard tracked the career trajectories of 70 powerful Bay Area men — executives, investors, politicians, academics, chefs, judges — who faced public allegations of sexual misconduct between 2010 and 2026.</p>
<p>In the Bay Area, it turns out, it’s pretty hard to get permanently canceled. We found that 76% of the men we reviewed are professionally active, 41% are in the same or an elevated role since the allegations were made public, and only two of the 70 were incarcerated in prison.</p>
<p>The list includes <u><a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/spacex-paid-250000-to-a-flight-attendant-who-accused-elon-musk-of-sexual-misconduct-2022-5">world-famous moguls like Elon Musk</a></u>, who is being <u><a href="https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/28059065-holland-thielen-et-al-v-spacex-complaint/">sued for sexual harassment</a></u> by eight former SpaceX employees, and lesser-knowns like John Haley, the former director of transit at Muni, who <u><a href="https://www.sfexaminer.com/news/muni-chief-steps-down-amid-growing-pressure-over-harassment-allegations/article_0de7da9a-db9f-51ef-955e-d31bd47454e7.html">retired under pressure in 2018</a></u> after his assistant accused him of <a href="https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/28059066-sabrina-suzuki-vs-city-of-san-francisco-municipal-transportation-et-al/">sexual harassment and sued</a> the San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency. Both Musk and Haley deny the allegations.</p>
<p>We analyzed the careers of men who faced allegations in court documents, news stories, and institutional records, including Title IX investigations. Only two men accepted the allegations to be true and publicly apologized at the time. The remaining 68 denied the allegations. We requested comments from all the men and included those from all who responded. We did not include allegations against local clergy, because there are too many.</p>
<p>Not all sexual allegations fit neatly in a box. California is known to have among the country’s broadest legal definitions of <u><a href="https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=243.4.&amp;lawCode=PEN">sexual assault</a></u> and workplace <u><a href="https://oag.ca.gov/workplace-sexual-harassment">sexual harassment</a></u>. The cases we examined range in severity from criminal charges for violent crimes like rape and child sexual abuse to violations of ethics, like allegations of in-office relationships or unwanted jokes that may not be illegal but ran afoul of company policies.</p>
<p>Though our analysis includes 70 men accused of wrongdoing, we’re publishing only the names of those whose allegations were made in court records or who responded to us for comment. We omitted the names of 26 men accused of wrongdoing whose conduct never made it to court.</p>
<p>This is a list of allegations — some investigated, some settled, some dismissed, some still in litigation. Most of these cases will never reach a criminal courtroom and will never be proved beyond a reasonable doubt.</p>
<p>The cost of the allegations isn’t incurred just by the men or their alleged victims. In many cases, publicly funded organizations pay a significant toll. Rick Callender, CEO of Valley Water, Santa Clara County’s public water agency, resigned last month after an independent investigation confirmed sexual harassment, according to a <u><a href="https://www.valleywater.org/news/release-executive-summaries-regarding-valley-water-ceo-rick-callender-and">report made public by the board</a></u>. The board voted to retain him as a “special adviser” and <u><a href="https://sanjosespotlight.com/valley-water-workers-want-leaders-to-resign-over-ex-ceos-contract/">continue to pay his $716,000 compensation package</a></u>. Callendar did not respond to a request for comment, but he denied the allegations and his attorney said the investigation was biased <a href="https://sanjosespotlight.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/11095228/2026-03-06-Callender-Ltr-to-SCVWD-Board.asdrc-redacted.pdf">in a </a><u><a href="https://sanjosespotlight.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/11095228/2026-03-06-Callender-Ltr-to-SCVWD-Board.asdrc-redacted.pdf">letter </a></u><a href="https://sanjosespotlight.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/11095228/2026-03-06-Callender-Ltr-to-SCVWD-Board.asdrc-redacted.pdf">to the board</a>.</p>
<p>Oakland paid more than <u><a href="https://oakland.legistar.com/LegislationDetail.aspx?ID=7352058&amp;GUID=9DD29FDC-6506-4B0A-BED7-CFB5DE107D18&amp;Options=&amp;Search=">$1.2 million</a></u> <u><a href="https://oakland.legistar.com/LegislationDetail.aspx?ID=5569775&amp;GUID=197068C7-A45D-4716-A438-897539B33160&amp;Options=ID%7CText%7C&amp;Search=sarai+crain">in settlements</a></u> between 2022 and 2025 to two employees who accused Guillermo Cespedes, the director of the city’s Department of Violence Prevention, of sexual harassment. Cespedes’s attorney denied the allegations in a statement to <u><a href="https://oaklandside.org/2025/05/09/oakland-settlement-violence-prevention-lawsuit-harassment/">Oaklandside</a></u>.</p>
<p>San Jose State University cleared its director of sports medicine, Scott Shaw, in 2009 despite complaints from more than a dozen swimmers, according to court documents. He continued treating female athletes until <u><a href="https://www.sjsu.edu/fyi/docs/Executive%20Summary-041521.pdf">new allegations surfaced in 2019</a></u>, leading to his resignation the following year. He was then investigated by the FBI and pleaded guilty to two civil rights violations. Shaw was <u><a href="https://www.justice.gov/usao-ndca/pr/former-san-jose-state-university-director-sports-medicine-sentenced-groping-female">sentenced to two years in federal prison</a></u>, and SJSU <u><a href="https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/justice-department-reaches-16m-agreement-remedy-title-ix-violations-san-jos-state-university">paid a $1.6 million settlement</a></u> for Title IX violations, according to the Department of Justice.</p>

<p>In 48 of 70 cases The Standard reviewed, the employer knew about allegations of misconduct before they became public. In some of these cases, employers took steps to protect the accused rather than the accuser — staying quiet during investigations, paying settlements under nondisclosure agreements, or punishing those who complained.</p>
<p>Of the 70 prominent men accused of sexual misconduct, more have died than have gone to prison.</p>
<figure><p class="rw-outer-content"><span>Some content could not be imported from the original document.</span> <a href="https://datawrapper.dwcdn.net/GwGMo/1/">View content ↗ </a></p></figure>
<p>According to our data, 18 men never left their jobs. Among them: the CEO of Blackberry, a $2.3 billion company, who is <a href="https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/28059587-sandhu-v-blackberry-corp/">being sued for sexual discrimination</a> in federal court by a former employee who alleges a “boys’ club” culture and wage discrimination. He and the company denied the allegations and are fighting the case in court. Also still in their roles are the CEO and chief revenue officer of Carta, who settled multiple <u><a data-post-id="b2860ae0-1e5d-4bff-b435-2bd6bdecd427" href="https://sfstandard.com/2023/10/25/carta-san-francisco-lawsuits-insider-fortune-reports/">sexual discrimination lawsuits in 2023</a></u> by employees who described unequal treatment and a culture of sexism; both denied the allegations.</p>
<p>Unsurprisingly, it’s easier to bounce back from allegations if you have a lot of money. Every venture capitalist or investor we identified either kept his job or found a new one.</p>
<p>Seven men found new jobs within a single month of their allegations becoming public.</p>

<p>The 9th Circuit opened a formal inquiry into Judge Alex Kozinski after <u><a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/prominent-appeals-court-judge-alex-kozinski-accused-of-sexual-misconduct/2017/12/08/1763e2b8-d913-11e7-a841-2066faf731ef_story.html">more than 15 women described decades of alleged harassment</a></u> — including sending pornographic images, groping, and requests to examine their bodies. He retired before the investigation concluded, then quickly pivoted to a practicing attorney. Kozinski <u><a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/prominent-appeals-court-judge-alex-kozinski-accused-of-sexual-misconduct/2017/12/08/1763e2b8-d913-11e7-a841-2066faf731ef_story.html">told The Washington Post</a></u> that he never intentionally offended his employees and denied showing pornographic images to his staff. Two years later, Kozinski was arguing cases before the same court. As of 2021, Kozinski had appeared as counsel in federal cases on behalf <u><a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/trump-hires-former-9th-circuit-judge-kozinski-twitter-court-fight-2022-07-02/">of Trump</a></u>. Kozinski did not respond to requests for comment.</p>
<p>Amit Singhal left as Google’s senior VP of search in February 2017 after an internal investigation found he had allegedly groped a colleague at a work event. He resigned quietly, with a reported $35 million exit package. Singhal <u><a href="https://mashable.com/article/uber-svp-engineering-out-after-sexual-harassment-claim-surfaces">denied the allegations.</a></u> He has since built two private foundations with a combined $74 million in assets and runs multiple VC funds. Singhal did not respond to requests for comment. Google’s parent company, Alphabet, <u><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/09/25/technology/google-sexual-harassment-lawsuit-settlement.html">settled a shareholder lawsuit</a></u> in 2020 in response to its handling of sexual harassment claims against executives, <a href="https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/28059290-alphabet-shareholder-complaint/">including Singhal</a>.</p>
<p>Perhaps no story better captures the mechanics of impunity than that of Andy Rubin.</p>
<p>Rubin invented Android, the operating system running roughly 70% of the world’s smartphones. In 2014, Google investigated an allegation that he had coerced a female employee into oral sex. Google found the claim to be credible, according to a <u><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/25/technology/google-sexual-harassment-andy-rubin.html">2018 investigation</a></u> by The New York Times; Rubin’s attorney denied the allegations, calling them part of a smear campaign. CEO Larry Page asked Rubin to resign — and <u><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/25/technology/google-sexual-harassment-andy-rubin.html">Google paid him $90 million</a></u> as he walked out the door — but he didn’t stay far from the company. Six months later, Rubin launched his next venture fund, Playground Global, and quickly raised $48 million. Google was among its investors.</p>
<p>Rubin’s ex-wife later alleged in <u><a href="https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/6180235-Rie-Andy-Rubin-complain7-2-19/">court documents</a></u> that he had maintained “ownership relationships” with women, including a photo of an email Rubin allegedly sent to one woman where he wrote, “being owned is kinda like you are my property, and I can loan you to other people.” The complaint alleged Rubin ran what appeared to be a sex ring, paying women for sexual acts and, in at least one case, offering them to other men.</p>
<p>Rubin denied the allegations. He left Playground Global in 2019 — <u><a href="https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/ryanmac/andy-rubin-playground-global-google-quiet-departure">reportedly with a $9 million payout.</a></u> Last year, <u><a href="https://www.theinformation.com/articles/android-creator-andy-rubin-launches-robotics-startup-tokyo">The Information reported</a></u> that Rubin had founded an AI robotics company in Tokyo. The article noted only that he had departed Google after “an inappropriate relationship with a subordinate.” Rubin did not respond to requests for comment.</p>
<p>Not every allegation is straightforward — and neither is its aftermath. Men’s reactions to the experience of being accused of sexual misconduct run the gamut, from vehement denials and threats of legal action to accepting responsibility and attempting to move on.</p>
<p>Venture capitalist Joe Lonsdale was sued by a Stanford undergraduate student who alleged he raped her over several months in a relationship she described as abusive. Stanford banned him from campus for 10 years. Lonsdale denied all allegations and countersued for defamation. Both lawsuits were mutually dismissed in 2015, and Stanford reversed its initial finding, citing new evidence that came out during litigation, according to <u><a href="https://stanforddaily.com/2015/11/02/stanford-reverses-campus-ban-on-joe-lonsdale/">the Stanford Daily</a></u>. Lonsdale had already founded 8VC — the fund he still runs, which has an estimated $6 billion or more in assets.</p>

<p>“Many at Stanford apologized to me after the fact for how it was handled in a biased way,” Lonsdale said in a statement to The Standard. “Although a generation ago many young women didn’t get fair treatment, today the bias tends to go in the opposite direction and we see a lot of obviously false accusations.”</p>
<p>Famed director and longtime Bay Area resident Francis Ford Coppola <u><a href="https://deadline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/coppola-pagone-complaint-sept-10.pdf">faces litigation</a></u> in Georgia from an extra on his movie set who alleges he kissed and groped her. Litigation is ongoing. A representative told The Standard, “It would be the height of journalistic dishonesty to include Mr. Coppola in a list of people accused of sexual misconduct” and said Coppola is “defending himself vigorously in court.”</p>
<p>Martina Avalos, attorney for San Francisco progressive activist Jon Jacobo, <u><a data-post-id="cf83eb18-4319-4743-86bb-a2c68d3442f7" href="https://sfstandard.com/2024/08/05/jon-jacobo-arrested-san-francisco-charges/">who was arrested</a></u> and pleaded not guilty to charges of rape and sexual battery in 2024, said her client denies the allegations. “Mr. Jacobo has not been found guilty of any wrongdoing, and legal cases shouldn’t be tried in the court of public opinion.”</p>
<p>Shahid Buttar, an attorney who was accused of sexual harassment during a 2020 congressional campaign, said he was falsely accused and denied any misconduct. He filed a defamation suit against the San Francisco Chronicle for publishing the allegations, but <u><a href="https://courthousenews.com/judge-tosses-pelosi-challengers-defamation-suit/">the case was dismissed</a></u>. In a statement to The Standard, he said, “In retrospect, I’ve grown quite grateful for my character assassination. While it publicly silenced me and effectively rigged a federal election, the concerns I raised have become increasingly widely shared in the time since then.”</p>
<p>Craig Cheslog, former East Bay director of the California Democratic Party, resigned after allegations of rape and sexual assault in 2017. “I continue to be confident a full and fair exploration of this matter, undertaken in an appropriate and fact-governed venue, would exonerate me,” Cheslog told The Standard. He never faced civil litigation or criminal charges.</p>
<p>While few of the men admitted wrongdoing or accepted responsibility for their alleged actions, some have displayed remorse and tried to rehabilitate themselves.</p>
<p><u><a data-post-id="83db0072-4c35-491a-8c78-771d21f542e2" href="https://sfstandard.com/2025/06/26/pizzaiolo-oakland-charlie-hallowell-abuse-berkeley-smashburger-station/">Charlie Hallowell,</a></u> the chef behind several East Bay restaurants<u><a data-post-id="83db0072-4c35-491a-8c78-771d21f542e2" href="https://sfstandard.com/2025/06/26/pizzaiolo-oakland-charlie-hallowell-abuse-berkeley-smashburger-station/">,</a></u> publicly apologized after allegations of sexual harassment erupted in 2017. He <u><a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/oct/12/chef-sexual-harassment-dunk-tank-pizzaiolo-oakland">admitted to the majority of the allegations</a></u>, stepped away from his businesses for two years without pay and underwent extensive therapy and reflection, according to Donna Insalaco, Hallowell’s business partner, whom he brought in post-allegations. “I have personally seen his growth, his ongoing work to repair trust, and his commitment to building a more inclusive and accountable workplace,” Insalaco told The Standard, adding that the majority of the managers at Hallowell’s restaurants are now women.</p>
<p>Tech blogger Robert Scoble had a different experience. Multiple women <u><a href="https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/doree/woman-accuses-robert-scoble-of-sexual-harassment">accused him of sexual misconduct in 2017</a></u>. Scoble <u><a href="https://www.sfchronicle.com/business/article/Tech-booster-Robert-Scoble-denies-sexual-12309578.php">denied the allegations</a></u> and was never charged or taken to court, but he told The Standard that the consequences, both personally and economically, were severe. “Even eight years later, it is hard to get jobs, and it still comes up when clients are considering working with me,” he said. “It deeply changed me. Been sober for years. I wouldn’t wish this on my worst enemy.”</p>
