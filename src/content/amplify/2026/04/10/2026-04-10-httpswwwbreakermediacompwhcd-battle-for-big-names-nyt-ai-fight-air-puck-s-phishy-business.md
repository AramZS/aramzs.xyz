<p>(Exclusive.)The Times Guild, representing 1500 employees of The New York Times, has described the current A.I. standards at The Gray Lady as “woefully inadequate” in a letter to management.</p>
<p>The letter comes <a href="https://www.thewrap.com/media-platforms/journalism/new-york-times-cuts-ties-with-writer-ai/?utm_campaign=whcd-battle-for-big-names-nyt-ai-fight-air-puck-s-phishy-business&amp;utm_medium=referral&amp;utm_source=www.breakermedia.com">following the revelation</a> that a freelance Times book reviewer had used A.I. and, in doing so, plagiarized The Guardian.</p>
<p>“At present, The Times’ standards on A.I. use are woefully inadequate,” the letter jointly addressed to <strong>A.G. Sulzberger</strong>, <strong>Meredith Kopit Levien</strong>, <strong>Joe Kahn</strong>, <strong>Kathleen Kingsbury</strong>, <strong>Marc Lacey,</strong> and <strong>Carolyn Ryan,</strong> and reviewed by Breaker, reads.</p>
<p>“They are often unclear or open to interpretation; place responsibility on writers, editors, producers, and our members, rather than on leadership, and are already frequently ignored.”</p>
<p>“We are told to use A.I. ‘ethically,’ but given little guidance on what exactly that means,” continues the letter, signed by the Times Guild Artificial Intelligence subcommittee.</p>
<p>Better protection for union members around the use of A.I. at The New York Times is one of the <a href="https://www.breakermedia.com/p/all-the-news-that-s-fit-to-bargain?utm_source=www.breakermedia.com&amp;utm_medium=newsletter&amp;utm_campaign=the-guilded-age&amp;_bhlid=c76d66edb1b40b0b46daa1a83fadcbfd4fa74233">five core priorities</a> the Guild Bargaining Committee has brought to negotiations over the past three months. In today's letter, they say that the request has fallen on deaf ears.</p>
<p>“The company’s representatives have treated our bargaining committee’s position on A.I. with scorn, crossing out our proposals or merely ignoring them. Management has fully rejected our team’s reasonable questions about how the company uses, and expects to use, A.I.”</p>
