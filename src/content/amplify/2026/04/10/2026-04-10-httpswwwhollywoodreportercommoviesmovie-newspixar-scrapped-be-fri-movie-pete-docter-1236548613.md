title: Inside Pixar’s Scrapped Movie ‘Be Fri’ and the “Devastating” Aftermath
---
<figure><figcaption>Pete Docter attends the 2019 premiere for Pixar's 'Toy Story 4.'</figcaption></figure>
<p><a data-tag="walt-disney-pictur-1" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/walt-disney-pictur-1/">Pixar</a> has been the focus of headlines over the past month as its original comedy <em><a data-tag="hoppers" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/hoppers/">Hoppers</a></em> made a splash with a great critical response and pleasing box office results, offering optimism for the <a data-tag="disney-2" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/disney-2/">Disney</a>-owned animated studio’s ability to launch an original property on the cusp of <em><a data-tag="toy-story-5" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/toy-story-5/">Toy Story 5</a></em> becoming a likely behemoth this summer.</p>
<p>But Pixar has also been the subject of recent debate surrounding the revelation of a planned movie that is unlikely to ever come to life. On the day <em>Hoppers</em> opened last month, the <em><a href="https://www.wsj.com/business/media/pixar-disney-franchises-pete-docter-80c57f9d">Wall Street Journal</a></em> published a story looking at the future of the company and confirmed that a feature called <em>Be Fri </em>(styled as <em>BeFri</em>) had been in development for years until it was scrapped in late 2023. The article noted that Pixar employees were stunned that a project that was three years in the making and involved 50 people would be killed — becoming perhaps the longest-developed Pixar movie to receive such a fate — given that the studio had been previously known for reworking troubled films, as it did with 2012 release <em>Brave</em>.</p>
<p><em>Be Fri </em>was the brain child of director Kristen Lester, known for helming Pixar’s 2019 short <em>Purl</em>. Inspired by Lester’s real-life experience involving an adolescent friendship that dissolved, <em>Be Fri </em>was to focus on two teenage girls who were once besties but find themselves drifting apart after learning that their favorite, <em>Sailor Moon</em>-style TV show is real and that they need to set out on a universe-spanning quest to save humanity. Also part of the core creative team were writer Blaise Hemingway, whose credits include the 2021 film <em>Clifford the Big Red Dog</em>, and editor Nicholas C. Smith, who retired from Pixar in 2024 after nearly 20 years with the company.</p>
<p>One former Pixar employee who worked on <em>Be Fri</em> and asked to remain anonymous tells <em>The Hollywood Reporter</em> that the project went through four iterations after various sets of notes from higher-ups and would have been ready for the <a data-tag="animation" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/animation/">animation</a> stage when Disney opted to pull the plug. A rough version of selected scenes from the film has made the rounds on social media and hints at the girls’ tension before a later sequence shows them in a car as an angry demon gives chase. The Pixar alum recalls that just after <em>Be Fri’</em>s Braintrust 3 — the company’s name for, in this case, the third of six check-ins with studio brass and focus testing throughout a film’s evolution — Disney asked for a major retooling.</p>
<p>“There was a meeting that took place after BT3, where Kristen and Blaise made the case to Disney: ‘We know you don’t like where the film is at right now. Give us six weeks, and we’ll redo the entire film,'” says the former Pixar staffer. “Kristen, Blaise, Nick and a couple board artists spent six weeks of night-and-day, seven-days-a-week, literally reformatting. Pre-production on an animated feature takes a year of storyboarding, so [they had] to compress that into six weeks for a fourth version of the film.”</p>

<p>The former staffer, who was not present for Disney’s feedback but has heard from others who were, remembers being amazed by the final footage that the <em>Be Fri</em> team created: “It was on <em>Hoppers’</em> level. It befuddles me why they passed on it, but with each round of notes, Disney just didn’t feel like little boys could see themselves in the film enough. Basically, Disney reps were like, ‘We can’t have a girl power movie.'” (Pixar’s recent titles centering on female leads include the 2024 blockbuster <em>Inside Out 2</em>, which became the highest-grossing animated movie of all time, and the Disney+ offering <em>Turning Red</em>.)</p>
<p>This decision to stop progress on <em>Be Fri</em> came in late 2023, months after <em>Toy Story</em> prequel movie <em>Lightyear</em> was a box office misfire in light of right-wing pundits causing commotion over its same-gender kiss. This was also around the same time that Pixar boss Pete Docter and the studio’s team decided to reconfigure their original sci-fi feature <em>Elio</em>, stripping <a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/elio-pixar-america-ferrera-director-queer-2-1236301860/">away the queer-coded characterization</a> of the titular lonely boy who longs to visit space. Shortly after these internal decisions, Docter spurred public debate when he <a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-features/inside-out-wild-robot-landscape-1235921320/">noted in a 2024 interview</a> that the studio should make the “most relatable films” possible, which some social media users perceived as pushing to shift away from underrepresented characters and voices.</p>
<p>“When they canceled the film itself, it was devastating,” says the former staffer, who recalls employees coming together for a memorial of sorts to honor <em>Be Fri</em>. “They had what amounted to a funeral for <em>Be Fri </em>at the studio. They had a little place where you could put notes or things that were really special to you. There was art all over the place that was really memorializing the film, and there was this outpouring of love for a project that everybody really wanted to see come to life.”</p>

<p>A different former Pixar staffer tells <em>THR</em> that some employees are disappointed that the studio seems to let concerns about public perception lead its decision-making on movies like <em>Elio</em> and <em>Be Fri.</em> “The joke around Pixar is that, for such a tall man, Pete has no spine at all,” the individual says. A representative for the studio declined to comment for this story.</p>
<p>Pixar’s team has reason to be optimistic about the state of its original projects. After the tough situation with <em>Elio</em> that followed a slow box office start for 2023’s <em>Elemental</em> (although that movie ultimately collected a small profit), <em>Hoppers</em> earned <a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-reviews/hoppers-review-pixar-disney-comedy-1236518225/">sterling reviews</a> and has surpassed $330 million at the global box office, giving hope to future originals like next year’s <em>Gatto</em>. Meanwhile, director Andrew Stanton’s <em>Toy Story 5 </em>hits theaters this June and looks primed to be another smash for the Oscar-winning franchise.</p>
<p>Over the past 12 months, the animated feature to have earned the most buzz was undoubtedly <em><a data-tag="kpop-demon-hunters" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/kpop-demon-hunters/">KPop Demon Hunters</a></em>, which originated at Sony Pictures Animation but skipped theaters and debuted on Netflix through the studio’s first-look deal with the streamer. It isn’t lost on the former Pixar staffer who worked on <em>Be Fri </em>that the Netflix movie, which is the platform’s most-watched title ever and beat out <em>Elio </em>to win this year’s animated feature Academy Award, has similar themes to the now-canceled Pixar project.</p>
<p>“The comparisons to <em>KPop Demon Hunters</em> are pretty undeniable because there were musical aspects to it, and it was a rip-roaring time,” the individual says. “I can imagine that whoever at Disney denied <em>Be Fri </em>to exist looks at <em>KPop</em> and is kicking themselves like, ‘Shit, I can’t believe Netflix is doing exactly what we wanted to do.'”</p>
