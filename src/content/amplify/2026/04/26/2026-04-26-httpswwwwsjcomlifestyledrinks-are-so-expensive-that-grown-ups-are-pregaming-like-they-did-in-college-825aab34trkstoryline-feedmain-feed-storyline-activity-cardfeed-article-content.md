<h1>Drinks Are So Expensive That Grown-Ups Are Pregaming Like They Did in College</h1>
<p>Americans are turning to the tradition to stretch their dollars—and their buzz</p>
<p>People are taking a shine to smaller bottles and drinks at home. <strong>ELIZABETH COETZEE/WSJ</strong></p>
<p>By <a href="https://www.wsj.com/news/author/laura-cooper">Laura Cooper</a></p>
<p>April 23, 2026 5:30 am ET</p>
<p>When Julie McCarthy was a college student, she learned a time-honored budgeting tactic: Downing vodka in her dorm room with friends meant spending less later at the club.</p>
<p>Now 31, she’s dusting off the strategy to combat rising prices for drinks at concerts and raves. On one recent night out, a Massachusetts concert venue was charging $20 for a tall-can of black cherry White Claw.</p>
