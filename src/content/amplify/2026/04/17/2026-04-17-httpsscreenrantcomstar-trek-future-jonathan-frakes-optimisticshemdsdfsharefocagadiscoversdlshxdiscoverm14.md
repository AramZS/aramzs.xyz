<p>Jonathan Frakes is staying optimistic about <em><strong><a href="https://screenrant.com/tag/star-trek/">Star Trek</a>'</strong></em>s future, despite the 60-year-old franchise being at a crossroads. Frakes portrayed Commander Will Riker in <em>Star Trek: The Next Generation'</em>s TV series and movies, and he returned as Captain Riker in <em>Star Trek: Picard.</em> Jonathan is also one of <em>Star Trek</em>'s most celebrated directors, helming numerous episodes and movies, including <em>Star Trek: First Contact.</em></p>
<p>Four months into <em>Star Trek'</em>s 60th anniversary year, Paramount+ has no <em>Star Trek</em> television projects in development or greenlit after the streamer canceled <em>Star Trek: Starfleet Academy. </em>On a positive note, Paramount Skydance has confirmed a <a href="https://screenrant.com/star-trek-movie-future-paramount-confirmed-cinemacon/">new <em>Star Trek </em>movie</a> is in development, although the studio offered no details during its CinemaCon presentation in Las Vegas.</p>
<p>Jonathan Frakes appeared on <a href="https://trekmovie.com/2026/04/16/exclusive-jonathan-frakes-confident-star-trek-will-resurface-decries-starfleet-academy-trolls/"><em>TrekMovie</em></a>'s <em>All Access Star Trek </em>podcast, along with Armin Shimerman and Kitty Swink, to promote April 25th's <a href="https://secure.pancan.org/site/TR/PurpleStride/PurpleStride?pg=team&amp;team_id=66979&amp;fr_id=3091">PanCan PurpleStride</a> walk to benefit the fight against pancreatic cancer. During the interview, Frakes expressed his undying optimism for <em>Star Trek'</em>s future despite feeling that <em>"none of it is encouraging" </em>due to the uncertainty about what comes next. However, Frakes reminds us that Paramount+ will still stream new <em>Star Trek </em>in 2027. Read Jonathan's quote below:</p>
<blockquote> <p>“I’m very optimistic about the future. I just wish that something was percolating now. I know that there’s talk of another movie. I don’t think it’s going to be one of the J.J. movies. It seems it’s going to be a brand-new [idea]. I know that there’s also a percolating idea about the Paul Wesley [<em>Star Trek: Year One</em>], which would be the origin of Kirk, but that’s all I’ve got. All I’ve got is rumor and innuendo, and none of it is encouraging… But in truth, there will be a Star Trek on the air through 2027. That gives us a lot of time to get something else in the oven, if you will.”</p> </blockquote>
<p>In November 2025,<em> Variety </em>reported a new <em>Star Trek </em>movie was being developed by <em>Dungeons &amp; Dragons: Honor Among Thieves</em>' filmmakers John Francis Daly and Jonathan Goldstein, which is the film Jonathan Frakes refers to. Unfortunately, the <a href="https://screenrant.com/star-trek-strange-new-worlds-starfleet-academy-sets-destroyed/">demise of <em>Star Trek: Strange New Worlds'</em> Toronto sets</a> hints that the Paul Wesley <em>Star Trek: Year One</em> spinoff isn't happening.</p>
<p>Jonathan Frakes joins the fan chorus that Paramount's having no new <em>Star Trek</em> television in production is an <em>"unfortunate"</em> way to ring <em>Star Trek</em>'s 60th year. Frakes is <em>"sure that Trek will resurface," </em>though fans worry about whether the<em> "power that [Gene] Roddenberry invested in it" </em>will endure under Paramount Skydance's new management. Read Jonathan's quote below:</p>
<blockquote> <p>“I think, sadly, to celebrate the 60th anniversary of our incredible franchise, it seemed very unfortunate that they’ve chosen this moment to not have any new Trek in production. It seems like a very unfortunate irony. I’m sure that Trek will resurface, it always has, and it always will. And the power that Roddenberry invested in it seems to have made it through six decades.”</p> </blockquote>
<p>Jonathan Frakes shared how he learned from executive producers and co-showrunners Alex Kurtzman and Noga Landau that <a href="https://screenrant.com/star-trek-starfleet-academy-canceled-tragedy/"><em>Star Trek: Starfleet Academy </em>was canceled</a> by Paramount+. <em>Starfleet Academy </em>was targeted by a hate campaign aimed at bringing down the show, despite not actually watching <em>Starfleet Academy.</em> Frakes says Kurtzman was well aware of how <em>"the trolls"</em> factored into <em>Starfleet Academy</em>'s fate:</p>
<blockquote> <p>“Yeah, isn’t that unfortunate? And that’s how [Alex] Kurtzman felt. I got a call from Alex and Noga [Landau], who was his co-showrunner on <em>Starfleet Academy</em>. They were calling people to let them know that the show was kind of ‘on ice,’ I think was the phrase they used. And it was what you just said [critiques coming from people who hadn’t watched the show], they couldn’t not mention as a factor, the trolls.”</p> </blockquote>
<p>Besides<em> "the trolls," </em>Jonathan Frakes considered the various factors that ended Paramount+'s last 10 years of new<em> Star Trek</em> series, noting the high cost of producing <em>Star Trek</em>'s modern <em>"'shoot to thrill' cinematic" s</em>tyle, compared to how <em>Star Trek</em> TV was produced decades ago:</p>
<blockquote> <p>“So some people who either didn’t like or didn’t approve or didn’t support the latest endeavors, for whatever reason. Perhaps it’s the changing of the guard at Paramount+ and CBS [Studios]. Perhaps it’s indecision. Perhaps it’s the amount of money it costs to make how beautiful the show is; the level of the production has become this sort of “shoot to thrill” cinematic phenomenon that when we did the show back in the ’80s, we counted on storytelling and acting and the occasional camera move [laughs]. It’s a different beast now, and that beast is very expensive, and as we know, it’s called show <em>business</em>.”</p> </blockquote>
<p>Jonathan Frakes directed<em> <a href="https://screenrant.com/star-trek-starfleet-academy-season-1-episode-9-ending-explained/">Star Trek: Starfleet Academy</a></em><a href="https://screenrant.com/star-trek-starfleet-academy-season-1-episode-9-ending-explained/"> episode 9</a>, which turned out to be the last episode of the current iteration of <em>Star Trek</em> that Frakes helmed. Unfortunately, Jonathan's schedule didn't allow him to direct for <em>Star Trek: Starfleet Academy</em> season 2, or <em>Star Trek: Strange New Worlds</em> seasons 4 and 5.</p>
<p>As Frakes says, Paramount+ still has <em>Star Trek</em> in the can into 2027. <a href="https://screenrant.com/star-trek-strange-new-worlds-season-4-updates/"><em>Star Trek: Strange New Worlds</em> season 4</a> will premiere later in 2026, and the second and final season of <em>Star Trek: Starfleet Academy</em>, as well as the fifth and final season of <em>Star Trek: Strange New World</em>s, are expected to bow on Paramount+ in 2027.</p>

<p>With no <em>Star Trek </em>productions in the pipeline, it's expected that <a href="https://screenrant.com/star-trek-dark-age-history-repeat/"><em>Star Trek</em> will go dark after 2027</a>, as Paramount Skydance decides on the future of Gene Roddenberry's venerable franchise. Paramount Skydance seems to be prioritizing <em>Star Trek</em> feature films, and since a new movie is in development, fans might see a new big screen outing first to mark <em>Star Trek</em>'s comeback. Whether it can happen in 2028 is anyone's guess.</p>
<p>Jonathan Frakes still hopes that <em>Star Trek: Picard'</em>s long-desired spinoff, <em>Star Trek: Legacy, </em>can happen. The odds for <em>Star Trek: Legacy </em>seem remote, but it's unknown what Paramount Skydance envisions for <em>Star Trek</em>'s TV future. Whatever the next iteration of <em><strong>Star Trek </strong></em>will entail, hopefully, Jonathan Frakes will be back behind the camera, if not donning his Starfleet uniform again as Captain Will Riker.</p>
