<p>When it comes to making music relevant to my personal interests, Mikaela Davis covers a lot of bases. But she does it in an unexpected way. For starters, she plays the harp — not the harmonica, but the big stringed instrument. She came up as a classically trained player who pivoted to a singer-songwriter career in college. Somehow, that translated to stints playing improvised music on stage with not one but two members of the Grateful Dead — Bob Weir (in his side project Wolf Bros) and Phil Lesh (with Phil &amp; Friends). Eventually, she also hooked up with the jam outfit Circles Around The Sun, which is how she met bassist Dan Horne, a co-producer of her beguiling new album, <em>Graceland Way, </em>out this Friday.</p>
<p>A native of Rochester, New York, her record has a decidedly “left coast” feel, plugging into the spirit and lineage of L.A. country rock, from O.G.’s like Gram Parsons and Linda Ronstadt to more modern masters like Jenny Lewis and Beachwood Sparks. Assisting her in the studio are guest stars Karly Hartzman of Wednesday, the singer-guitarist Madison Cunningham (featured on the stellar single <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IE1D7htLuaI">“(Looking Through) Rose Colored Glasses”</a>), keyboardist Neal Francis, accordionist James Felice of The Felice Brothers, and all-around renaissance man (and <em>Evil Speakers </em>subscriber!) Tim Heidecker. She also covers a heretofore unrecorded Cass McCombs track, “Mizmoon,” gifted by the man himself, with haunting results.</p>
<p>I’m a fan of this psych-country stunner, and I’m a fan of Davis, who was kind enough to talk with me for an hour. Along with sharing lots of fascinating lore about jamming with the two late greats from the Dead, our conversation had lots of cool tangents — on the late Neal Casal, on the genius of McCombs (including a surprising anecdote about one my personal songs of his), on being inspired by the songwriting of Tom Petty and Sheryl Crow, on the secret rituals of the harpist community, and so much more.</p>
