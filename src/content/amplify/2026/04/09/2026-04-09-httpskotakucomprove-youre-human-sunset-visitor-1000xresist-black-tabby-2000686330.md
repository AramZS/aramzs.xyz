<p><a href="https://kotaku.com/1000xresist-indie-narrative-time-travel-impressions-1851466481"><i>1000xRESIST</i></a> was one of the <a href="https://kotaku.com/goty-best-games-2024-astro-bot-dragon-age-star-wars-1851726357">surprise breakout games of 2024</a>, and developer Sunset Visitor is back with a new sci-fi adventure game that seems to be just as existential as its last. <i>Prove You’re Human</i> is coming to Steam and is part of a new publishing partnership with <a href="https://kotaku.com/slay-the-princess-review-black-tabby-games-horror-1851000305"><i>Slay the Princess</i></a> developer Black Tabby Games.</p>
<p>The title is a reference to the CAPTCHAs you’re often asked to fill out on the internet as a way of…well, proving you’re human. <i>Prove You’re Human </i>follows an AI that believes she’s human, and you must “put her in her place.” It seems like the game will get into a lot of philosophical debates about personhood and whether “copies” of people are just as legitimate as the person who they’re based on. It’s giving a little bit of Apple TV’s incredible series <a href="https://kotaku.com/severance-season-2-finale-helly-mark-gemma-3-renewal-1851771662"><i>Severance</i></a>, which also follows “copies” of humans that are essentially made to be workhorses.</p>

<p>Throughout <i>Prove You’re Human</i>, you’ll solve CAPTCHA puzzles that get more and more haunted, marrying its science fiction premise with a psychological horror tone. The game doesn’t have a release date yet, but that means you have plenty of time to go back to <i>1000xRESIST</i> if you didn’t play it in 2024. It’s available on just about every system now that PS5 and Xbox Series X/S versions came out last year.</p>
