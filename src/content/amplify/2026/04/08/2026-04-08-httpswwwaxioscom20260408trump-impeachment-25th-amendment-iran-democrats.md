<p><strong>The latest: </strong>Trump announced Tuesday night that he agreed to a two-week ceasefire just over an hour before his stated deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz.</p>
<ul><li>Trump had threatened in a Tuesday morning post on Truth Social that if the deadline wasn't met, "a whole civilization will die tonight, never to be brought back again. I don't want that to happen, but it probably will."</li><li>That statement had Democrats and even a handful of Republicans up in arms, but Vance — despite his reputation as an anti-interventionist — <a data-vars-click-url="https://x.com/prem_thakker/status/2041512015731011670?s=20" data-vars-content-id="1db7d734-afcf-48b7-9b82-859bbb600ec4" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Iran ceasefire fails to quash Dem calls for Trump's removal" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="backed up the president" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://x.com/prem_thakker/status/2041512015731011670?s=20">backed up the president</a>, saying, "We've got tools in our toolkit that we so far haven't decided to use." </li><li>A spokesperson for Vance did not immediately respond to a request for comment. </li></ul>
<p><strong>By the numbers: </strong>More than 85 House Democrats had called for President Trump to be impeached or removed via the 25th Amendment as of Tuesday evening.</p>
<ul><li>Among those were members of House Democratic leadership and prominent lawmakers such as Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (D-N.Y.) and former House Speaker Nancy Pelosi (D-Calif.).</li><li>The vast majority were progressives, however, with more moderate and swing-district Democrats mostly sticking to calls for an Iran war powers vote.</li></ul>

Melanie Stansbury (D-N.M.) said in a <a data-vars-click-url="https://x.com/Rep_Stansbury/status/2041652214821757390?s=20" data-vars-content-id="1db7d734-afcf-48b7-9b82-859bbb600ec4" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Iran ceasefire fails to quash Dem calls for Trump's removal" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="post on X" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://x.com/Rep_Stansbury/status/2041652214821757390?s=20">post on X</a>.</li><li>Said Sen. <a data-vars-click-url="https://x.com/SenMarkey/status/2041653455698227278?s=20" data-vars-content-id="1db7d734-afcf-48b7-9b82-859bbb600ec4" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Iran ceasefire fails to quash Dem calls for Trump's removal" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="Ed Markey" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://x.com/SenMarkey/status/2041653455698227278?s=20">Ed Markey</a> (D-Mass.): I'm glad there is a reported ceasefire deal with Iran. But ... Donald Trump can't simply threaten war crimes with impunity. Congress needs to get back in session now to stop this war and remove Donald Trump."</li></ul>
<p><strong>The bottom line: </strong>These efforts were extreme long-shots to begin with, and the ceasefire only further takes the steam out of them.</p>
<ul><li>But, to Republicans at least, they are a clear signal of where Democrats may go if they retake one or both chambers of Congress in November.</li><li>"If the GOP doesn't hold the House, impeachment 3.0 is coming," former Republican congressman and White House chief of staff Mark Meadows said in a <a data-vars-click-url="https://x.com/MarkMeadows/status/2041607870085558495?s=20" data-vars-content-id="1db7d734-afcf-48b7-9b82-859bbb600ec4" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="Iran ceasefire fails to quash Dem calls for Trump's removal" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="post on X" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://x.com/MarkMeadows/status/2041607870085558495?s=20">post on X</a>.</li></ul>
