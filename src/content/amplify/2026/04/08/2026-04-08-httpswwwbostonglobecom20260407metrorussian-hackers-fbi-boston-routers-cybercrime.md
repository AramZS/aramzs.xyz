  Home and office routers across US were targets of Russian hackers, Boston FBI

<p>The FBI’s Boston office and the Department of Justice led a national action to <a href="https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/justice-department-conducts-court-authorized-disruption-dns-hijacking-network-controlled">disrupt Russian government hackers</a> who infiltrated computer routers in offices, homes and small businesses in the United States and commandeered sensitive military, government, and infrastructure information, the agencies announced Tuesday.</p>
<p>Dubbed <a href="https://www.justice.gov/usao-edpa/pr/justice-department-conducts-court-authorized-disruption-dns-hijacking-network">“Operation Masquerade,”</a> the campaign neutralized the U.S. portion of a cyber crime unit operated by Russia’s Main Intelligence Directorate of the General Staff, or GRU, the agencies said in statements.</p>
<p>The Russian hackers <a href="https://www.ic3.gov/PSA/2026/PSA260407">“weaponized routers” in more than 23 states</a>, Ted E. Docks, FBI’s special agent in charge of the Boston field office, said in one of the statements.</p>
