<p>Iran tightened its grip on the Strait of Hormuz, as US peace talks remained distant.</p>
<p>Just one ship passed through the waterway Tuesday; on Wednesday, more ships tried, and Iran attacked two and reportedly seized two more. The White House <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/live/2026/04/22/world/iran-war-trump-ceasefire-talks/heres-the-latest?smid=url-share">played down the episode</a>, but it demonstrated Tehran’s continuing stranglehold, The New York Times reported.</p>
<p>Progress toward reopening the passage remains stalled: Iran’s parliament speaker said the US blockade was “bullying” and a “<a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/amp/news/2026/4/22/iran-blames-trumps-blockade-for-diplomatic-impasse-as-fragile-truce-holds">flagrant breach of the ceasefire</a>,” adding that negotiations would not resume with it in place.</p>
<p>US President Donald Trump said the blockade would continue, but he may be on the clock: Presidents can wage war without Congressional approval for 60 days, <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/22/us/politics/war-powers-act-explanation.html">a deadline which expires May 1</a>.</p>

<p>A robot beat elite human players at table tennis, a landmark victory in a competitive sport.</p>
<p>Sony Ace <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10338-5">defeated top amateurs in three matches out of five</a>, although it lost twice against pros. It follows a running robot beating the human half-marathon record, <a href="https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/a-humanoid-robot-beat-the-human-half-marathon-record-at-a-beijing-race-but-what-did-it-actually-prove/">albeit in a highly controlled environment</a>.</p>
<p>Ace had it easy to some extent — it has no legs so needn’t worry about balance, and has nine video cameras around the table, while humans tend to have only one head and two eyes. But it could herald rapid progress in robotics, much as DeepMind’s autonomous Go player did in AI in 2016. Physical AI is a growth area: Tesla announced plans <a href="https://www.ft.com/content/7ce83108-9f2d-48b4-8ce1-28865045bd67?syn-25a6b1a6=1">to boost robotics spending this year</a>.</p>

<p>Russia is expanding its military presence across West Africa, aiming to fill a vacuum left by the breaking of ties between the region and the US and Europe.</p>
<p>An investigation by The Sentry showed that Moscow was using its shadow fleet to transport heavy equipment to its African allies, <a href="https://thesentry.org/reports/doubling-down-russia-west-africa/">bypassing Western sanctions</a>. Experts have warned regional countries against cozying up to the Kremlin, which often extracts major economic concessions in exchange for assistance.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the security situation in the region has deteriorated further: The Islamist militia Boko Haram <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/suspected-boko-haram-militants-kill-20-northeast-nigeria-attacks-2026-04-22/">killed dozens in Nigeria</a> on Wednesday, adding to the hundreds of people murdered there so far this year. And Niger said it may <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-22/niger-weighs-military-conscription-to-combat-islamist-militants">introduce military conscription</a> as it looks to combat armed groups.</p>

<p>China appears to be stuck in a deflationary trap, with even the pressures sparked by the Iran war failing to boost prices.</p>
<p>Beijing has long struggled to drive domestic consumption, while <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2025/07/22/business/china-involution-competition-deflation.html">brutal competition</a> in many industries has pushed producers to cut prices, triggering a vicious cycle in which people hold off on purchases in anticipation of coming discounts.</p>
<p>And while the conflict in the Middle East temporarily warded off deflation, the <a href="https://www.ft.com/content/ca9fce70-0b59-4b8c-a16e-1177fbde131f">gains have since subsided</a> as Chinese industry continues to run below capacity, the chief economist for Asia Pacific at French bank Natixis argued. Few expect the downward pressure to let up soon. “The world’s second-largest economy <a href="https://www.eurasiagroup.net/live-post/risk-7-chinas-deflation-trap">will remain stuck in a trap of its own making,</a>” Eurasia Group predicted.</p>

<p>Tesla reported a resurgence in sales and Chinese solar exports doubled in a month, as the global energy crisis showed no signs of easing.</p>
<p>Fifty countries saw <a href="https://ember-energy.org/latest-updates/chinese-solar-exports-double-in-a-month-to-hit-record-high-amid-energy-crisis/">record Chinese solar imports in March</a>, the think tank Ember reported, especially in Asia and Africa; battery and EV sales were up as well.</p>
<p>Soaring gasoline prices in the US have renewed interest in hybrids and EVs there too, and Tesla, which had seen demand slump since the White House removed green subsidies, <a href="https://www.wsj.com/finance/teslas-1-4-billion-surprise-for-wall-street-c4799819">announced increased profits and revenues</a>. The firm also saw a European revival, with an 84% year-on-year sales increase in March, although <a href="https://www.wsj.com/finance/teslas-1-4-billion-surprise-for-wall-street-c4799819">Chinese rival BYD reported an even bigger boost</a>, with fuel price rises pushing consumers away from combustion engines.</p>

<p>The US military has reportedly started using Ukrainian anti-drone technology to protect an air base in Saudi Arabia, highlighting a broader shift in defense away from expensive solutions to low-cost threats.</p>
<p>The “Sky Map” system detects incoming attacks, often cheap Iranian Shahed drones, and <a href="https://www.reuters.com/business/aerospace-defense/us-turns-ukrainian-counter-drone-tech-after-iran-attacks-sources-say-2026-04-22/">responds with its own interceptors</a>, according to Reuters. Ukraine has become a leader in anti-drone countermeasures after four years of facing mass-produced Russian ordnance.</p>
<p>The US move follows President Donald Trump last month saying “we don’t need their help in drone defense.” Existing US anti-air systems, though capable, are expensive: A $5 million Patriot missile destroying a $50,000 Shahed is not cost-effective. “<a href="https://x.com/PalmerLuckey/status/2047051520924115225">We cannot keep throwing Ferraris at frisbees</a>,” a US lawmaker told Congress yesterday.</p>

<p>US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth fired his Navy secretary, exacerbating turmoil within the Pentagon in the midst of the Iran war.</p>
<p>John Phelan clashed with Hegseth and other senior officials over efforts to <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/22/us/politics/navy-secretary-john-phelan.html">revive US shipbuilding</a>. It follows <a href="https://www.theatlantic.com/national-security/2026/04/hegseth-removes-top-army-officer-mid-iran-war/686675/">the sacking of the Army chief of staff</a> this month, and an <a href="https://www.wsj.com/politics/national-security/hegseths-feud-with-army-secretary-spills-into-public-view-ee2e5687">increasingly public feud</a> between <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/27/us/hegseth-promotion-list.html">Hegseth and the Army secretary</a>.</p>
<p>Though Phelan’s removal is not likely to have a direct impact on the Iran war or Washington’s blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, one lawmaker called the dismissal “troubling,” saying it “sends the wrong signal to our sailors and Marines, to our allies, and to our adversaries.”</p>

<p>Multiple attacks undermined a fledgling ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon ahead of negotiations between the two countries in Washington today.</p>
<p>Lebanon said Israeli strikes targeting Hezbollah in the south of the country <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5yvn036evlo">killed at least four people</a> on Wednesday, with the Iran-backed group saying it had carried out drone attacks in response.</p>
<p>The 10-day truce is due to expire on Sunday, though hope remained that <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/04/23/lebanon-israel-hezbollah-us-talks-ceasefire-washington/bfb7d7ac-3ef0-11f1-bb46-ed564688d953_story.html">US-brokered peace talks today</a> could lead to an extension.</p>
<p>Meanwhile Beirut, caught between its adversary Israel and a powerful armed domestic group in Hezbollah, risks being squeezed out, an expert argued. The actions of Israel and Hezbollah “<a href="https://carnegieendowment.org/middle-east/diwan/2026/04/lebanon-needs-another-negotiating-strategy-with-israel">are converging in undermining the Lebanese state,</a>” a deputy director at the Carnegie Endowment’s Middle East Center said.</p>

<p>The stock market’s <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/22/these-stocks-are-signaling-the-return-of-the-markets-animal-spirits.html">animal spirits</a> are returning, CNBC wrote, further evidence that “traders are moving past the Iran war as a major threat.”</p>
<p>But elsewhere, warning signs are piling up.</p>
<p>A US-led effort to clear mines from the Strait of Hormuz <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2026/04/22/iran-hormuz-mines/">could take six months</a>, according to Pentagon documents, and <a href="https://www.ft.com/content/2d857fb7-e952-4aee-8b9c-1d170b6857b5?syn-25a6b1a6=1">volatile energy markets</a> are already “pushing the world <a href="https://www.economist.com/finance-and-economics/2026/04/21/global-energy-markets-are-on-the-verge-of-a-disaster">towards the cliff edge</a>,” The Economist wrote.</p>

Starmer has also <a href="https://www.euronews.com/2026/03/17/starmer-says-we-cant-lose-focus-on-ukraine-amid-iran-war-during-zelenskyy-visit">pushed Europe’s involvement in Ukraine</a>, and domestic distractions may limit his diplomatic and political bandwidth on the issue.</p>
