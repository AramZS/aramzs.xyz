<p>The 180,000 sq. ft. venue <a href="https://www.visitathensga.com/listing/akins-ford-arena/3777/">has a capacity</a> of more than 8,000 seats and Vance’s appearance had been heavily promoted by Turning Point, the conservative movement targeting students founded by slain right-wing activist Charlie Kirk.</p>

Caylo Seals/Getty Images</figcaption></figure>
<p>The national campus tour is <a href="https://events2022.tpusa.com/events/this-is-the-turning-point-tour-at-the-university-of-georgia">being advertised</a> as “a chance to honor Charlie’s mission and keep the fight alive. We know he wouldn’t want us to surrender or be coerced into silence. Free speech is only free if we use our voices.”</p>
<p>Tickets were also free, with students and ‘general attendees’ required to register first online, then advised they would be allowed in on a first-come, first-served basis after doors opened at 1:30 p.m.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.thedailybeast.com/jd-vance-heckled-repeatedly-in-fiery-clash-at-top-maga-event/"><figure><picture><figure><img alt="U.S. Vice President JD Vance attends This is the Turning Point Tour at the University of Georgia in Athens, Georgia, U.S., April 14, 2026." data-chromatic="ignore" src="https://www.thedailybeast.com/resizer/v2/LQYDK3J7QJA4XJ37YHYLLP4GPI.JPG?auth=2593e29990c213890f0014d1d5658ff52495d5737e61b040fdd18c68b04181d7&amp;smart=true&amp;width=300"/><figcaption>U.S. Vice President JD Vance could not fill an area in Georgia where tickets were free. Alyssa Pointer/REUTERS</figcaption></figure></picture></figure></a></p>
<p>By the time Vance came on stage, however, the arena was virtually empty. Jake Traylor, an MS NOW <a href="https://x.com/jake__traylor/status/2044176275359838346">journalist, estimated</a> the venue was only a quarter-full when Vance was talking, which would mean roughly 2,000 people turned up.</p>
<p>Footage showed the entire second half of the venue was empty, on both the floor and in the stands, with the camera crew set up at the rear.</p>

<p>Meidas Touch <a href="https://x.com/MeidasTouch/status/2044195344435818539">shared the footage</a> with the caption, “Tens of people went to see VP JD Vance at today’s Turning Point USA event.”</p>

<p>They said protesters outside the venue had signs that read “What would the real Jesus do?,” “God does not hate, Turning Point does” and “Jesus warned us about J.D. Vance.”</p>

<p>Boedy told the Daily Beast that he estimated there were around 1,200 people in the 8,000-capacity venue.</p>
<p>“The student section on floor was 1,000 chairs and they didn’t fill - maybe 900,” Boedy said. “And the others were definitely no more than 300.”</p>
<p>The Daily Beast has contacted Turning Point and reps for Vance for confirmation of crowd numbers.</p>
<p>Vance’s limited pulling power among the MAGA fanbase is in stark contrast to Donald Trump’s <a href="https://www.thedailybeast.com/trump-79-rushes-to-brag-about-poorly-attended-rally/">fascination </a>with crowd sizes.</p>
<p>Trump has frequently boasted, without solid evidence, that he draws the largest crowds in U.S. political history, while talking down the popularity of his political rivals.</p>

<p>“Nobody has spoken to crowds bigger than me,” Trump <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2024-election/trump-compares-jan-6-crowd-audience-mlk-dream-speech-rcna165894">said in 2024</a>. “If you look at Martin Luther King, when he did his speech, his great speech, and you look at ours, same real estate, same everything, same number of people.”</p>
<p>The modest crowd wasn’t the only hiccup for Vance—he was repeatedly heckled during the event.</p>
<p>As Vance was speaking, someone in the audience shouted out, “Jesus Christ does not support genocide.”</p>
