<h2>1. Virginia</h2>
<p>Abigail Spanberger did not campaign on saving democracy at the federal level. But that might turn out to be the most important thing she does as governor.</p>
<p>As Bill <a href="https://www.thebulwark.com/p/breaking-dems-remember-they-can-play-hardball-too-virginia-redistricting-referendum-midterms-congress-democrats-trump">discussed this morning</a>, Republicans in Texas—at the behest of the president—violated a bunch of norms when they embarked on a quest to gerrymander more seats for their party in the middle of an election cycle.</p>
<p>This action was illiberal and anti-democratic but—and this is the key—it was also <em>legal</em>.</p>
<p>Democrats, to their credit, responded not by rending their garments but with ruthless proportionality: Led by Gavin Newsom and Spanberger, they counter-gerrymandered.</p>
<p>This is the way.</p>
<p>The road to preserving liberal democracy does not involve unilateral disarmament or faking normality in the hopes of making the world normal again. It involves meeting force with force while <a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/senate-bill/3750/text">crafting a national</a><a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/senate-bill/3750/text">remedy for nonpartisan redistricting</a>, which would force everyone to behave according to standards of good government.</p>
<p>It’s classical deterrence theory. As some Roman general once said, “If you seek peace, prepare for war.”</p>
<p>This should be the model for the biggest piece of ruthless aggression of them all: expanding the Supreme Court.</p>
<p>Let’s get ruthless.</p>
