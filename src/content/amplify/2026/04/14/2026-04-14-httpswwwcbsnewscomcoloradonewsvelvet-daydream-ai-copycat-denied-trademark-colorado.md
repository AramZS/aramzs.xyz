<p>"We're a rock 'n' roll band," said Velvet Daydream singer and guitarist Ryder King. "We like making rock 'n' roll music. We take a lot of pride in kind of doing our own thing with it."</p>
<p>Velvet Daydream is a Colorado band through and through.</p>
<p>"I met our drummer in kindergarten," said King. "We've all been playing all the way throughout high school. We're passionate and we love doing it."</p>
<figure><img alt="velvet-daydream.jpg " src="https://assets2.cbsnewsstatic.com/hub/i/r/2026/01/21/678d5150-9f72-48a2-8341-52bb18dd2b54/thumbnail/1240x698/0842535d58492c03ce98ebc4e83d0b8a/velvet-daydream.jpg" srcset="https://assets2.cbsnewsstatic.com/hub/i/r/2026/01/21/678d5150-9f72-48a2-8341-52bb18dd2b54/thumbnail/620x349/a624fc167a3343a9037967cce04f7a68/velvet-daydream.jpg 1x, https://assets2.cbsnewsstatic.com/hub/i/r/2026/01/21/678d5150-9f72-48a2-8341-52bb18dd2b54/thumbnail/1240x698/0842535d58492c03ce98ebc4e83d0b8a/velvet-daydream.jpg 2x"/><figcaption>velvet-daydream.jpg</figcaption></figure>
<p>And they're making a name for themselves. But so is The Velvet Daydream.</p>
<p>"We got a DM from a fan who sent us this profile and was like, Hey, there's this AI band with your name," said King.</p>
<p>Velvet Daydream says the similarly titled band and its music is created by <a data-invalid-url-rewritten-http="" href="https://www.cbsnews.com/tag/artificial-intelligence/">artificial intelligence</a> and is even a verified artist on Spotify.</p>
<p>"All you to do is put in a prompt, I want my song to sort of sound like this with these sorts of lyrics, and it'll make you an album in 5 minutes," said King. "You know, these AI groups are dropping entire albums almost every month, every few weeks, and it's just completely undermining the effort that it takes real artists."</p>
<p>"This is our livelihood, this is our career path," fellow singer and guitarist Kaeden Keys said. "I don't have another job that I go to, this is it. And another band can just pop up with our name somewhere that didn't even write any of their music."</p>
<p>The issue moreso is that it's creating confusion. People don't know who the human Velvet Daydream is.</p>
<p>"I was downtown, and I told someone about the band, and they thought we were an AI group, like, oh, you're a real band," said Keys. "And that's happened several times. We've gone to play shows where the promoter will add the AI version of their music over our promo stuff."</p>
<p>"Our first album is The Velvet Daydream, too," Keys added. "All of our social media is The Velvet Daydream."</p>
<p>The band is getting ready to release a new album in March and has officially applied to trademark both The Velvet Daydream and Velvet Daydream in hopes of shutting down the AI dupe before the launch.</p>
<p>"We're just trying to do what we can to make sure it doesn't happen to anyone else, and doesn't keep happening to us," said Keys.</p>
<p>Over the weekend, Velvet Daydream told CBS Colorado both of their applications for trademarks were denied.</p>
<p>"Our next step is figuring out why we didn't get them, and likely appealing their decision," said King.</p>
