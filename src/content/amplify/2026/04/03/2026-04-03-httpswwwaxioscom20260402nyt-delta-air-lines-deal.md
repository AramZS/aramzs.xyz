<p><a data-vars-click-url="https://www.axios.com/2025/10/21/new-york-times-watch-tab-tiktok-nyt-app" data-vars-content-id="d0651cc2-547d-40c0-9b40-800df7c7e328" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="NYT and Delta Air Lines strike distribution deal" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="The New York Times" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.axios.com/2025/10/21/new-york-times-watch-tab-tiktok-nyt-app">The New York Times</a> struck a multiyear distribution partnership with Delta Air Lines that brings its journalism and products to Delta customers via the airline's in-flight experience and beyond, executives told Axios.</p>
<p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> This partnership expands the Times' reach to millions of Delta SkyMiles members globally and adds to the suite of high-end content options Delta makes available to its customers.</p>
<p><strong>How it works:</strong> The full portfolio of the Times' journalism and content — including its core news, games, cooking, sports coverage, and more — will be accessible to Delta SkyMiles members via seat-back screens and their personal devices connected to Delta's Sync Wi-Fi by T-Mobile.</p>
<ul><li>Once a flyer signs in while on-board, they can access the Times' journalism for up to 24 hours, allowing them to continue exploring the Times' content beyond their in-flight experience. </li><li>Access can be unlocked for free through Delta's Sync Wi-Fi program with no credit card required. </li><li>Delta's Sync Wi-Fi is available on nearly 1,200 aircraft. Its planes feature 160,000 setback screens. Its SkyMiles membership reaches millions of customers. </li></ul>
<p><strong>Zoom out: </strong>The deal represents a unique opportunity for the Times to get its product in front of a very premium customer and during a unique on-board environment that's void of distractions.</p>
<ul><li>"We see a really good brand affiliation between Delta and the Times," said Rebecca Grossman-Cohen, senior vice president of strategic partnerships and executive operations at the New York Times.</li><li>"For the Times to be in front of people in that kind of way is a great opportunity for us to expose our suite of products to people when they're in this really unique moment of their day."</li></ul>
<p><strong>The big picture: </strong>The partnership represents Delta's first truly global content deal. It's also its first major news content deal, said Julieta McCurry, vice president of in-flight entertainment and connectivity strategy at Delta.</p>
<ul><li>The airline already offers premium streaming content through deals with companies such as Paramount, YouTube Premium and Crunchyroll. </li></ul>
<ul><li>More entertainment partnerships will be announced later this year, McCurry said. </li><li>The deal is an expansion of a <a data-vars-click-url="https://news.delta.com/media/image/55301" data-vars-content-id="d0651cc2-547d-40c0-9b40-800df7c7e328" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="NYT and Delta Air Lines strike distribution deal" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="previous 2023 partnership" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://news.delta.com/media/image/55301">previous 2023 partnership</a> between the two companies in which Delta Sync Wi-Fi customers could access the New York Times' games content. </li></ul>
<p><strong>Context:</strong> The Times has a long history of striking enterprise partnerships to increase its exposure around the world, including a <a data-vars-click-url="https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/the-new-york-times-company-and-pressreader-announce-new-agreement-to-drive-global-distribution-and-reach-new-audiences-301947096.html" data-vars-content-id="d0651cc2-547d-40c0-9b40-800df7c7e328" data-vars-event-category="story" data-vars-headline="NYT and Delta Air Lines strike distribution deal" data-vars-item="in_content_link" data-vars-link-text="hotel, cruise and airline distribution" data-vars-sub-category="story" href="https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/the-new-york-times-company-and-pressreader-announce-new-agreement-to-drive-global-distribution-and-reach-new-audiences-301947096.html">hotel, cruise and airline distribution</a> partnership with PressReader and a smaller-scale deal with American Airlines that gives its loyalty members the ability to unlock access to the Times through their loyalty points.</p>
<ul><li>It also has distribution deals with thousands of universities, public libraries, schools and businesses around the world.</li><li>It partners with dozens of global news outlets on subscriber deals, and has unique partnerships with companies like American Express and Instacart.</li></ul>
<p><strong>The bottom line:</strong> The Delta deal is different from other Times distribution deals because it's more integrated within the Delta customer experience, Grossman-Cohen said.</p>
<ul><li>Other strategic partnerships are mostly focused on adding Times content to their existing bundles or products that they already offer.</li></ul>
