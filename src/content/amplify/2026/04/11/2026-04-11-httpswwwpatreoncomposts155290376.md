<p>Yesterday, I read <a href="https://nygroove.nyc/local-business-ai-slop/">this interview</a> with Jordan Beau, the new owner of venerable Park Slope institution Daisy's Diner. I've had an unremarkable meal or two there, which is no dig at the diner — do we typically go to these sorts of restaurants for remarkable food, or simply to relax into a red vinyl nostalgia booth for a little while? Anyway, the interview wasn't really about the food, but about the owner's loud 'n proud use of generative AI to promote it:</p>
<blockquote><p>“Leveraging AI has been a godsend, truly,” Beau said, citing an upward trajectory for the restaurant since he took over the business. “Come in and see the people in the AI videos … Do we use AI to put steam on the macaroni and cheese? Yeah. But that's my mac and cheese. You know, there's nothing AI about that mac and cheese.”</p></blockquote>
<p>I'm gonna level with you guys: "come in and see the people in the AI videos," conceptually, makes me want to die in a big way. Indeed, I included this quote before the one I really want to talk about today because yall are living in <em>Rax King's The Ring, </em>and if I make you read about "seeing the people in the AI videos," then <em>you'll </em>suffer, not me. Also, it exemplifies the fundamental, how shall I say?, missing-the-point-ness of gen AI evangelists. I don't need to see steam on a video of mac and cheese, guy. In fact, I don't need to see a video of it, period. What can mac and cheese possibly do that warrants an action shot? We may have grown inured to this porno-inflected take on food photography, but I'm old enough, just, to remember that we never used to require mac and cheese to be telegenic.</p>
<p>But that's just set dressing, and so here is Beau saying the thing I <em>really</em> want to disagree with today:</p>
<blockquote><p>“If you're worried about AI taking your job, then be smarter than AI,” Beau said. “Learn to run AI, learn to learn to operate AI, and learn to take advantage of it, and how is it going to enhance your career? Every day I have to figure out where I'm going to get X, Y and Z to maintain a competitive advantage.” </p></blockquote>
<p><em>If you're worried about AI taking your job, then be smarter than AI.</em></p>
<p>I can't hate Beau as much as I'd like, despite his saying such a loathsome thing so blithely. Neighborhood friends who spend more time at Daisy's than I do say that he's often there bustling around trying to infect customers with his bonhomie — dopey though he may be, he clearly gives something resembling a fuck about the 40-year-old business he purchased two years ago. This makes me <em>want</em> to soften towards him. <a href="https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1865/04/01.htm">Nothing human, after all, is alien to me</a>. But I've been hearing more and more variants on this idea lately, how rejecting AI means being left behind. And my retort is:</p>
<p>If an AI future is what I'm getting left behind from, then Christ, <em>just leave me behind</em>...</p>
