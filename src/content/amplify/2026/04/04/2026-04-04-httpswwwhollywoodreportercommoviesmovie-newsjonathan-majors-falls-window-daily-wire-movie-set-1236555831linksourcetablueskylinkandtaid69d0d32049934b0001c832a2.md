title: Jonathan Majors Falls Through Window of Daily Wire Movie Set

<figure><figcaption>Jonathan Majors. Rodin Eckenroth/Getty Images</figcaption></figure>
<p>A video has surfaced of <a data-tag="jonathan-majors" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/jonathan-majors/">Jonathan Majors</a> falling through a window on set of <a data-tag="the-daily-wire" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/the-daily-wire/">the Daily Wire</a> and Bonfire Legend’s untitled action movie.</p>
<p>The video, published by <em><a href="https://deadline.com/2026/04/jonathan-majors-falls-through-window-crew-strike-daily-wire-1236773038/">Deadline</a></em>, came to light after reports that crew were organizing a strike and claiming unsafe conditions. In the clip, Majors and his co-star, JC Kilcoyne, fall out of shot and out of the window, with the outlet noting that sources said Kilcoyne required stitches “all over his hands” after the fall, which was about six feet to the ground.</p>
<p>When asked for comment about the video of Majors and Kilcoyne allegedly falling through a window on set, Dallas Sonnier of Bonfire Legend told <em>The Hollywood Reporter</em>, “The actors’ fall was shorter than the failed movie careers of the now-union reps.”</p>
<p>Kyle Rankin serves as writer-director of the film; Ben Shapiro is producing for Daily Wire and Sonnier for Bonfire Legend. Travis Mills, Lillian Campbell and Sydney Aucreman are also producing.</p>
<p><em>THR</em> has reached out to Daily Wire for comment, but did not hear back by the time of publication.</p>
<p>The film serves as one of Majors’ newest projects he’s taken on following his 2023 <a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/general-news/jonathan-majors-sentence-domestic-violence-intervention-program-1235868537/">conviction for assault and harassment</a>. He was dropped from a number of films after he was convicted, which stemmed from an incident involving his ex-partner Grace Jabbari. He was later <a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/general-news/jonathan-majors-sentence-domestic-violence-intervention-program-1235868537/">sentenced</a> to a 52-week in-person domestic violence intervention program.</p>
<p>Amid the controversy, the <em>Creed III</em> actor was most notably <a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/marvel-drops-jonathan-majors-as-kang-1235391129/">dropped by Disney and Marvel Studios</a>, where he played Kang the Conqueror, who was originally slated to be the MCU’s next leading villain.</p>
