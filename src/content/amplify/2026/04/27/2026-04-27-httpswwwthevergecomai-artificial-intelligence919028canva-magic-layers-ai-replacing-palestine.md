<p>One of Canva’s new <a href="https://www.theverge.com/tech/913068/canva-ai-2-update-prompt-based-editing-availability">AI features</a> has been caught replacing the word “Palestine” in designs. The Magic Layers feature — which is designed to break flat images out into separate editable components — isn’t supposed to make visible alterations to user designs, but it was found by <a href="https://x.com/ros_ie9/status/2048454007258243362">X user @ros_ie9</a> to automatically switch the phrase “cats for Palestine” to “cats for Ukraine.”</p>
<p>The issue was seemingly limited specifically to the word “Palestine,” as @ros_ie9 noted that related words like “Gaza” were unaffected by the feature. Canva says it has now resolved the issue and is taking steps to prevent it from happening again.</p>
<p>“We became aware of an issue with our Magic Layers feature and moved quickly to investigate and fix it,” Canva spokesperson Louisa Green told <em>The Verge</em>. “We take reports like this very seriously, and we’re putting additional checks in place to help prevent this in future. We’re sorry for any distress this may have caused.”</p>

<p>Replies to the now-viral X post suggest that <a href="https://x.com/DeptOfMonkeys/status/2048501385906241776">other users</a> were able to <a href="https://x.com/AppendixBuster/status/2048557333169443238">replicate the bug</a> before Canva fixed it, though my own tests didn’t show any words — Palestine or otherwise — being altered by the feature.</p>
<p>Still, this is one heck of a blunder, especially for a platform that’s increasingly trying to compete against Adobe’s suite of AI-powered design tools. Magic Layers is a major component of Canva’s recent AI overhaul, which it claims “marks the beginning of the next era of creation.”</p>
