<p>I just spent two days at the 2026 Beijing Auto Show (Auto China 2026), and I need to tell you something: the future of the auto industry is electric and Chinese. I’m not being dramatic. Just realistic.</p>
<p>In a single hall at the show, there were more EV models on display than there are available ones in the entire United States. There are 17 halls at this show. Seventeen. And they all have more EVs than the US market.</p>
<p>The show features 1,451 vehicles, including 181 world premieres and 71 concept cars, sprawling across a record-breaking 380,000 square meters of exhibition space at two venues. It’s now the largest auto show in the world — and it’s not even close.</p>

<h2>The sheer scale is overwhelming</h2>
<p>Walking through the show, the first thing that hits you is the scale. This is not like any auto show I’ve attended in the West. The energy is different. The booths are massive. The vehicles are ambitious. And almost everything is electric or electrified.</p>
<p>Chinese automakers are competing like crazy over here, sharpening their tools while western automakers are hiding behind protectionism and falling behind. Will all of these EV programs succeed? Of course not, but the best ones will — and they will dominate.</p>
<h2>The vehicles: from concepts to production-ready EVs</h2>
<p>Every major Chinese automaker had a massive presence, and even the western brands that showed up brought their electric game.</p>

<p>Even the motorcycles were electric. The MontX (branded CLOD) electric motorcycle had a muscular, naked-bike design that drew plenty of attention. The EV revolution in China isn’t limited to four wheels.</p>
<h2>Robots everywhere — but the novelty has worn off</h2>
<p>One of the most striking aspects of the show was the sheer number of robots walking around. Humanoid robots and robot dogs were everywhere, at seemingly every other booth.</p>
<p>It makes sense for companies like <a href="https://electrek.co/2026/04/24/xpeng-gx-flagship-suv-beijing-auto-show-750km-range-58000/">XPeng, which actually builds its own humanoid robot called IRON</a> and plans to begin mass production in late 2026. But many other companies simply bought a Unitree robot and brought it to their booth as an attraction. When you see dozens of them roaming around, the novelty wears off quickly and the whole thing feels less special.</p>
<h2>The car show model lives on — with a twist</h2>
<p>In the West, the concept of car show models has been increasingly frowned upon over the last decade. At the Beijing Auto Show, it’s alive and well. While there were no bikini models, there were plenty of models standing next to cars at nearly every booth.</p>

Several car companies hired influencers who were just standing next to vehicles, responding to their “chat” in real-time. It’s a uniquely Chinese phenomenon that blends traditional auto show culture with the country’s massive livestream commerce industry.</p>
<h2>Batteries: the real story behind China’s EV dominance</h2>
<figure><img alt="" sizes="auto, (max-width: 768px) 100vw, 768px" src="https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg" srcset="https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg 1600w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=113,150 113w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=225,300 225w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=768,1024 768w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=1152,1536 1152w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=1536,2048 1536w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=263,350 263w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=140,187 140w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=750,1000 750w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=640,853 640w, https://electrek.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2026/04/battery-component-display.jpg?resize=150,200 150w"/></figure>
<p>One of the main reasons China dominates the EV world is that it also dominates batteries. At the Beijing Auto Show, there were a dozen battery manufacturers with massive booths — some as big as or bigger than the automaker booths.</p>
<p>CATL had a 1,500-square-meter “Energy Technology Experience Area” at the entrance of the exhibition hall, showcasing everything from battery cell production to its battery-swapping network and recycling systems. CATL also brought its third-generation Shenxing ultra-fast charging battery, which can charge from 10% to 98% in just six minutes.</p>
<p>CALB, EVE Energy, and several others also had enormous exhibition areas. They have all the biggest battery makers with all the different chemistries and 1,000 kW 5-minute charging capability.</p>
<p>Having close access to the world’s largest battery makers is a giant advantage of the Chinese auto industry.</p>
<h2>Tesla was missing</h2>
<p>One company that was notably absent: Tesla. I couldn’t find them anywhere at the show. Tesla has now skipped three consecutive Beijing Auto Shows — 2024, 2025, and now 2026. Other no-shows included Jaguar, Land Rover, Maserati, Subaru, and Chevrolet, though GM’s Buick and Cadillac brands were present.</p>
<p>Tesla’s absence is particularly striking given that China remains one of its most important markets and competition is intensifying from every direction. <a href="https://electrek.co/2026/04/24/xpeng-gx-flagship-suv-beijing-auto-show-750km-range-58000/">XPeng unveiled its GX flagship SUV with 750 km range and L4-ready hardware for $58,000</a>, and <a href="https://electrek.co/2026/04/24/byd-denza-z-1000-hp-luxury-drop-top-ev-hypercar-images/">BYD unveiled the Denza Z, a 1,000+ hp drop-top electric hypercar heading to Europe</a>.</p>

<p>The scale of what’s happening here is staggering. In 17 halls of exhibition space, Chinese automakers are showcasing vehicles that span every possible market segment — from $10,000 city cars to million-dollar hypercars, from off-road adventure SUVs to autonomous robotaxis, from electric motorcycles to luxury sedans that rival Rolls-Royce.</p>
<p>The battery makers are the unsung heroes. CATL, CALB, EVE Energy, and others had booths that rival the automakers themselves, and they’re pushing technology forward at a pace that makes western battery efforts look sluggish.</p>
<p>The competition among Chinese brands is fierce. Not all of these programs will succeed — that’s a certainty.</p>
<p>In fact, many of the vehicles looked similar or had little brand identity, but the ones that survive this brutal domestic competition will emerge as battle-hardened global competitors. Western automakers hiding behind tariffs and protectionism are buying time, not building advantages. They have the supply chain, the manufacturing capacity, the energy, the charging infrastructure. Unless there is a major shift in momentum, they are going to dominate the entire industry. It’s just a fact.</p>
