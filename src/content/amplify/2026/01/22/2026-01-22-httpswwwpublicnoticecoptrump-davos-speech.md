<p>Donald Trump sounded like a fascist dictator suffering from a brain bleed during his speech yesterday at Davos. It was a national embarrassment even by the lowly standards of modern American politics.</p>
<p>I had other plans for today’s newsletter and have been primarily personally concerned about the <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/whstancil.bsky.social/post/3mcxps2dims2d">increasingly terrifying scenes</a> on the streets of my city, but the president’s showing in Switzerland was so shabby that I feel compelled to share some thoughts about it.</p>
<p>First of all, despite what <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mcwwud4bps2d">cable news chyrons would have you believe</a>, Trump’s screed was chillingly aggressive. He began the Greenland part of it with a thinly veiled threat against NATO, claiming his recent coup in Venezuela proves his regime is a “much greater power than people even understand.” He contrasted his purported strength with European weakness.</p>

<h3>Complete insanity </h3>
<p>In the immediate aftermath of Trump’s speech, cable coverage seized upon his “I won’t do that” comment as evidence he’s taken armed aggression against NATO off the table. But of course he <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mcxblkjwzc2c">also ruled out strikes within Venezuela</a> until shortly before he launched them.</p>
<p>Make no mistake, Trump’s line about “unstoppable excessive force” was a threat, and the rest of what he said was meant to provide plausible deniability. He employed a similar choose-your-own-adventure rhetorical strategy on January 6 when he mentioned “fight” or “fighting” <a href="https://www.vox.com/22220746/trump-speech-incite-capitol-riot">more than 20 times</a> during a pre-insurrection speech in which he also included a few mentions of peace.</p>

<p>As if the sabre-rattling toward NATO and Greenland wasn’t enough, Trump also sneered at Canada. One day after Prime Minster Carney delivered <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mcuj4ejqlj2d">a historic speech at Davos</a> essentially announcing the demise of Pax Americana because of Trump’s malevolent erraticness, Trump <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mcwuflpqdj2a">huffed</a> that Carney “wasn’t so grateful” for all America has done for his country and said, “Canada lives because of the United States. Remember that, Mark, next time you make your statements.”</p>
<p>Perhaps just as disturbing as Trump’s aggression is his obviously disordered state of mind. He <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mcwsvixytd2u">made up</a> that “we have new steel plants being built all over the country,” <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mcwthcdcqy2d">claimed</a> that “I haven’t been able to find any wind farms in China” (the country in fact operates some of the largest ones in the world), and <a href="https://bsky.app/profile/atrupar.com/post/3mcwtzy47fs2c">vowed</a> that there will soon be prosecutions over his 2020 election loss, which he delusionally claimed was “rigged. Everybody now knows that. They found out.”</p>
<p>Trump went on to take credit for ending conflicts in countries he seemed to be unfamiliar with, saying “I settled eight other wars” including one in “Aber-baijan.”</p>
