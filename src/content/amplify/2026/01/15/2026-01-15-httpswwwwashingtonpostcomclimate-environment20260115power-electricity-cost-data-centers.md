<p>As electricity prices climb, an unusual disconnect is emeging between what regular people pay -- and what data centers pay.</p>
<p>Over the past few years, millions of Americans have seen their <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2025/11/24/power-shutoffs-surge-electric-bills/">electricity bills skyrocket</a>. Since February 2020, electricity prices have increased by an average of 40 percent across the country. In some areas, the rate is even faster — in Washington, D.C., electricity costs increased <a href="https://insideclimatenews.org/news/25092025/inside-clean-energy-electricity-rate-hikes-by-state/">93 percent</a> from July 2020 to July 2025.</p>
