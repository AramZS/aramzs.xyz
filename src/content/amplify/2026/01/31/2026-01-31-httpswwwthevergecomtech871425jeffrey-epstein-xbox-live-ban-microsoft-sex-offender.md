<p>Would it surprise you to learn Microsoft booted Epstein from its online gaming platform? <a href="https://xboxera.com/2026/01/30/jeffrey-epstein-was-banned-on-xbox-live/">As spotted by <em>XboxEra</em></a>, the latest batch of Epstein’s email shows that in December 2013, he was “permanently suspended due to harassment, threats, and/or abuse of other players.”</p>
<p>But it appears the real reason may be simpler: <a href="https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01944468.pdf">a second email</a> from Microsoft to Epstein the very same day shows he was banned because Microsoft was among the companies who agreed to purge their systems of registered sex offenders.</p>

<p>While Epstein became a registered sex offender years earlier, <a href="https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00668878.pdf">another email</a> shows he may not have joined Xbox Live until October 2012. Microsoft joined <a href="https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/sex-offender-video-game-ban-operation-game-over-ny/1975448/">New York state’s initiative to purge sex offenders from online platforms</a> where they might bump into children back in April 2012, so it seems they didn’t ban his account for over a year.</p>
<p>It’s not clear if Epstein played Xbox himself, though. In July 2014, he asks his executive assistant <a href="https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA02101088.pdf">“do we have an xbox 360 kinect?”</a> and in 2016 <a href="https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA02198968.pdf">this email</a> appears to be a discussion of buying an Xbox as a surprise birthday gift for a boy. In 2019, a message reads “Why is there a $25.24 charge on Xbox?”</p>
