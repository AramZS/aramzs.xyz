<p>In a federal lawsuit, a group of gun owners and advocacy groups argued that state bans against firearms in public places such as schools or hospitals violate the Second Amendment.</p>
<p>A federal appeals court upheld many of Maryland’s gun-control measures in a sweeping opinion that says states can ban firearms in sensitive public places such as schools and hospitals without running afoul of the Supreme Court’s recent decisions expanding the right to bear arms.</p>
