<p>Have you noticed that news stories and marketing material about Artificial Intelligence are typically illustrated with clichéd and misleading images ?</p>
<figure><img alt="A sketch of a humanoid robot in a thinking pose" src="https://betterimagesofai.org/homepageImgRobotTranspBG.png"/><figcaption>A sketch of a humanoid robot in a thinking pose</figcaption></figure>
<p>Humanoid robots, glowing brains, outstretched robot hands, blue backgrounds, and the Terminator.</p>
<p>These stereotypes are not just overworked, they can be surprisingly unhelpful.</p>
<h2>What’s the problem with current tropes?</h2>
<p>Abstract, futuristic or science-fiction-inspired images of AI hinder the understanding of the technology’s already significant societal and environmental impacts.</p>
<p>Images relating machine intelligence to human intelligence set unrealistic expectations and misstate the capabilities of AI.</p>
<p>Images representing AI as sentient robots mask the accountability of the humans actually developing the technology, and can suggest the presence of robots where there are none.</p>
<p>Such images potentially sow fear, and research shows they can be laden with historical assumptions about gender, ethnicity and religion.</p>
<p>However, finding alternatives can be difficult! That’s why <a href="https://betterimagesofai.org/whoWeAre">we</a>, a non-profit collaboration, are researching, creating, curating and providing Better Images of AI.</p>

<p>Our library is the start of a new alternative repository of stock images. They are available for anyone to use for free under <a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/">CC licences</a>, or just as inspiration for more helpful and diverse representations of AI.</p>
<p>Together we can increase public understanding and enable more meaningful conversation around this increasingly influential technology. But we need your support. <a href="https://betterimagesofai.org/contact">Please get in touch</a> if you have ideas or images, or time and funding to help commission artists and develop guidance.</p>
