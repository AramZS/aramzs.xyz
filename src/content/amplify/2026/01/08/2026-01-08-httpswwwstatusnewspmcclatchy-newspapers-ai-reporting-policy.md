<p>In mid-December, union leaders at <strong>McClatchy</strong> logged onto a <strong>Zoom</strong> call with the newspaper conglomerate’s executives, expecting a routine discussion over a new contract. Instead, according to people familiar with the discussion, they were blindsided by the company’s desire to aggressively integrate artificial intelligence into its workflow. To be sure, union leaders expected management might wish to experiment with the rapidly advancing technology. But what brass laid out went far beyond how most newsrooms are slowly adopting A.I. tools.</p>
<p>Union leaders left the meeting harboring serious concerns about McClatchy’s plans, especially given that the hedge fund-owned company has a history of finding ways to slash costs. In November, it shuttered its <strong>Washington, D.C.</strong> bureau, eliminated its entire real-time news operation, and quietly axed dozens of jobs across the organization, as <em>Status</em> <a href="https://www.status.news/p/mcclatchy-layoffs-dc-bureau">first reported</a>. The A.I. proposal put forth by management led some to believe that McClatchy was setting the stage to begin replacing human journalists with robots.</p>
