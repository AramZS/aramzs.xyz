<p>The pact with Mercosur, a bloc including Brazil and Argentina, is aimed at showing the E.U. can help maintain open global markets despite U.S. protectionism.</p>
<p>BRUSSELS — The European Union’s leaders are set to sign a landmark trade agreement with South American nations to create what they have trumpeted as the world’s largest free-trade zone.</p>
<p>This deal is more than 25 years in the making. But its culmination comes at a time when the E.U. is moving with a new, urgent sense of purpose, as President Donald Trump upends long-standing alliances and the norms of global trade.</p>
