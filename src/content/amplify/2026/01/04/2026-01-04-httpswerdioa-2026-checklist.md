<p>I'm not sure I believe in New Year's <em>resolutions</em> as such: too easy to break. But there's something powerful about a checklist, so I've been thinking about what it might look like to build one for every single day next year.</p>
<p>Here's what I have.</p>
<p>  Close my Apple Fitness rings. </p>
<p>  Go for a walk, even if it's just a mile. </p>
<p>  Eat nutritious, plant-heavy food, if possible. </p>
<p>  Read a book, even if it's just one page. </p>
<p>  Write creatively, even if it's just one sentence. </p>
<p>  Communicate with a friend, even if it's just a one-line text. </p>
<p>  Tell the people I love that I love them. </p>
<p>  Write down at least one thing I'm grateful for. </p>
<p>  Write down at least one self-affirmation. </p>
<h3>If I'm at home</h3>
<p>  Use the rowing machine, even if just for 10 mins. </p>
<p>  This does not need a checkbox, but give my buddy the biggest hug. </p>
<p>  Get to bed before 11pm. </p>
<p>Nothing bad will happen if I don't do these things. But they're a north star: a way to remind myself of what is important. I'd like them to stick.</p>
<p>If you have something similar, I'd like to read yours.</p>
