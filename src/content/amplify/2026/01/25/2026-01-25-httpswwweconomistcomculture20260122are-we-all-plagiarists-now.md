<h2>The steal industry is booming</h2>
<p>Share</p>
<figure><img alt="Two inverted commas with cltr c written on the left one and cltr V on the right one" data-nimg="1" sizes="(min-width: 960px) 700px, 95vw" src="https://marber-cdn.economist.com/foundations/latest/images/image/image-placeholder.svg"/><figcaption>Illustration: Mariaelena Caputi</figcaption></figure>
<p>Jan 22nd 2026|6 min read</p>
<p>It is a truth universally acknowledged that people get away with plagiarism a lot—even when the line they are plagiarising is “It is a truth universally acknowledged…” In 2007 chapters of <a data-analytics="in_body:link_1:para_1" href="https://www.economist.com/christmas-specials/2025/12/12/how-jane-austen-revealed-the-economic-basis-of-society">Jane Austen’s</a> “Pride and Prejudice” were sent with minor variations to 18 editors, purporting to be a “new” novel entitled “First Impressions”. Despite it containing one of the most famous lines in English literature, only one editor called out the hoaxer. “My first impression” on reading “First Impressions”, he replied with Austenian archness, was “mild annoyance” then “laughter”.</p>
