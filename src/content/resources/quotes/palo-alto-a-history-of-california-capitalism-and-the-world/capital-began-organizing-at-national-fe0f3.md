> Capital began organizing at national and global levels in earnest. What was left of Congress in 1863 passed the National Bank Act, and that decade yielded lasting finance names such as Morgan and Goldman. In 1870, some of newly unified Germany’s bankers joined to form Deutsche Bank, which quickly opened international offices to cash in on higher returns abroad.
