> Robyn Spencer explains that the insurgent Panthers made for a more appealing movement home for young women in Oakland than other militant organizations did. The party combined Maoism’s strict gender egalitarian orthodoxy with the reality of women’s community leadership, the latter of which structured the southern movement’s gender dynamics.
