> Intel’s Gordon Moore—no pessimist when it came to progress in tech production efficiency—turned down an engineer’s plan for a home computer: “The only use he could come up with at the time was, a housewife could put her recipes on it,” Moore recalled in 1993. “And I just couldn’t imagine my wife sitting there with her computer cooking dinner.”
