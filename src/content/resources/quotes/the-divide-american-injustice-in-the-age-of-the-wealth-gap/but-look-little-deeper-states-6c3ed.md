> But look a little deeper, and the state’s refusal to drop the stop-and-frisk protest cases over the course of nearly two years is highly symbolic, because there’s a key legal issue buried in the case. “The question is,” says Daniella Korotzer, who represents some of the protesters, “do the police have a right to just tell you to move no matter what? And can you be arrested if you refuse?”
